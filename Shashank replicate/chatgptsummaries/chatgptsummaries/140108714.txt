The trial court and the revisionary court had dismissed the application, and the applicant had challenged these orders. 

The applicant had filed an application under Section 311 of the Cr.P.C. to recall the complainant for cross-examination. The applicant had submitted certain documents before the trial court, which he claimed were relevant to the case and would help in establishing his innocence. However, the trial court and the revisionary court had dismissed the application, stating that the documents were not relevant to the case and there was no need to recall the complainant for cross-examination.

The applicant had challenged these orders and had filed a petition under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. before the court. The court had granted a stay order on the proceedings of the case until the petition was disposed of. 

The court examined the documents submitted by the applicant and found that they were not filed before the court. The court also found that there was nothing in the documents to recall the complainant for cross-examination. The court dismissed the petition filed by the applicant under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. and vacated the stay order. The trial court was free to pass judgment in the case.
