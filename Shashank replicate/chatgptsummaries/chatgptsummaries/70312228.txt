The court order pertains to a petition filed under Section 482 Cr.P.C. seeking to set aside an order passed by the Additional Sessions Judge, Hoshiarpur, dismissing an application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. and Section 145 of the Indian Evidence Act. The petitioners sought to use statements made before a Commission of Enquiry constituted by the Supreme Court to confront witnesses. The court held that the statement made by a witness before the Commission appointed by the Supreme Court under sub-clause (2) of Article 32 can be called for the purpose of contradiction. The court referred to various provisions of the Evidence Act and previous judgments to support its decision.

The court order states that the accused cannot confront a witness with material that was retracted during cross-examination. The provisions of the Commission of Inquiry Act, 1952 cannot be applied to the report of the Commission appointed by the Supreme Court. The court allows the petitioner's application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. and Section 145 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 to summon statements recorded before the Commission of Inquiry and provide copies to the petitioner. The order of the trial court is set aside.
