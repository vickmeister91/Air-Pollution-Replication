The court order in question pertains to a petition filed by Mohammad Ramzan Bahleem, who challenged the dismissal of his application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. by the trial court. The application was for summoning the Cash Book(Bahi) and to direct the complainant to produce the same in the Court. The trial court had dismissed the application in a routine manner, which prompted the petitioner to argue that summoning the cash book was necessary and essential for bringing the true facts on record.

The petitioner's argument was based on the fact that the cash book was a crucial piece of evidence that would help establish the veracity of the allegations made against him. He contended that the cash book would reveal the true nature of the transactions that had taken place between him and the complainant, and would help establish his innocence.

However, the court found no illegality or infirmity in the order passed by the trial court and dismissed the petition. The court further noted that the petitioner had failed to demonstrate any error or irregularity in the trial court's order, and had merely reiterated his earlier arguments.

The court's decision was based on the principle that the trial court is the best judge of the evidence that is presented before it, and that it is not the function of the higher courts to interfere with the trial court's decision unless there is a clear error or irregularity.
