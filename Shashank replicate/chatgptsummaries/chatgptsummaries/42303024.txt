The petitioner in this case had filed a petition under Section 482 Cr.P.C to quash the proceedings against him in Crime No. 360/2015, registered at Police Station Kolgawan, District Satna for an offense under Sections 363, 304-A of the I.P.C. The petitioner had claimed that there was no evidence against him to show that he was negligent or careless regarding the security of the deceased. The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, partly allowed the petition to the extent that the offense under Section 363 of the I.P.C against the petitioner is quashed, but the offense under Section 304-A of the I.P.C is not quashed.

Section 363 of the I.P.C deals with kidnapping, while Section 304-A of the I.P.C deals with causing death by negligence. The court, in its order, noted that there was no evidence to show that the petitioner had kidnapped the deceased. Therefore, the court quashed the offense under Section 363 of the I.P.C against the petitioner. However, the court noted that there was evidence to show that the petitioner was negligent or careless regarding the security of the deceased, which resulted in her death. Therefore, the court did not quash the offense under Section 304-A of the I.P.C against the petitioner.

The court, in its order, also noted that the petitioner had not challenged the charge sheet filed against him. Therefore, the court did not go into the merits of the case and only considered the arguments put forth by the petitioner in his petition under Section 482 Cr.P.C. The court also noted that the petitioner had not produced any material to show that the investigation was biased or that the charge sheet was filed with mala fide intentions.
