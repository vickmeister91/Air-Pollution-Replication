In the case of Mushtaq Ahmed Butt vs State through CBI (Wildlife), the appellant was convicted for contravening sections of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 for possession of banned shahtoosh shawls. However, the court order pertains to the appeal filed by the appellant against the conviction. The court heard arguments from both sides and concluded that while the appellant was present at the premises where the shawls were recovered, there was no evidence to suggest that he was trading or had knowledge of the banned items. The court also examined the testimonies of various witnesses to determine the appellant's possession of the banned items.

The court analyzed the testimony of prosecution witnesses in a case involving the possession of banned items. The court found that the prosecution had not been able to prove its case against the appellant, who was charged with the possession of banned items. The court set aside the impugned judgment and order on sentence and acquitted the appellant of the charges. The appellant's bail bond was cancelled, and the surety was discharged.

The court order states that TCR should be sent back along with a copy of the order, and the appeal file should be consigned to the record room. The order was announced in open court on April 5th, 2019, and was digitally signed by Sanjay Kumar Aggarwal, who is an Additional District & Sessions Judge at Tis Hazari Courts in Delhi.
