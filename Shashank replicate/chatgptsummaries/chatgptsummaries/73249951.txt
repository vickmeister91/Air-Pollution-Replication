During the hearing, Singh's legal team informed the court that he had already joined the investigation and that he was not required for custodial interrogation.

After considering the arguments put forth by both sides, the court allowed Singh's prayer for pre-arrest bail and disposed of the petition in terms of the earlier order. This means that Singh will not be arrested in connection with the corruption case, provided that he continues to cooperate with the investigation and complies with the conditions of his bail.

The granting of pre-arrest bail is a common practice in India's legal system, particularly in cases where the accused is not considered a flight risk and is willing to cooperate with the investigation. It allows the accused to avoid being taken into custody while the investigation is ongoing, which can be a significant advantage in terms of preserving their reputation and avoiding the negative consequences of being incarcerated.

In this case, it appears that the court was satisfied with Singh's cooperation with the investigation and his compliance with the conditions of his interim bail. This suggests that he is not considered a flight risk and that there is no immediate danger of him tampering with evidence or obstructing the investigation in any way.
