Roop Singh, the petitioner, filed a bail application under section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure in Forest Crime No. 3385/2014 registered by Forest Department Barman, District Narsinghpur, under sections 2, 9 & 39 of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The petitioner was accused of hunting a Sambar deer and assaulting a person who tried to stop him. 

The court allowed the bail application and directed the petitioner to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000/- with one solvent surety in the same amount to the satisfaction of the trial court for his appearance on all dates fixed in the case and for complying with the conditions enumerated under Section 437 (3) of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

The Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, is an act of the Parliament of India enacted for the protection of plants and animal species. The act provides for the protection of wild animals, birds, and plants, and for matters connected therewith or ancillary or incidental thereto. The act also provides for the establishment of national parks, sanctuaries, and other protected areas for the conservation of wildlife.

In this case, Roop Singh was accused of hunting a Sambar deer, which is a protected species under the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The act prohibits the hunting, killing, or capturing of any wild animal specified in Schedule I, II, III, and IV of the act. The Sambar deer is listed in Schedule III of the act, and hunting it is a punishable offense.

Roop Singh was also accused of assaulting a person who tried to stop him from hunting the Sambar deer. Assault is a criminal offense under the Indian Penal Code, and the accused can be punished with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both.

Roop Singh filed a bail application under section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, which provides for the grant of bail to a person who is in custody during the pendency of the trial. The section empowers the court to grant bail to the accused if it is satisfied that there are reasonable grounds for believing that the accused is not guilty of the offense for which he is charged, and that he is not likely to commit any offense while on bail.

The court allowed the bail application and directed the petitioner to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000/- with one solvent surety in the same amount to the satisfaction of the trial court for his appearance on all dates fixed in the case and for complying with the conditions enumerated under Section 437 (3) of the Code of Criminal Procedure. Section 437 (3) provides for the imposition of certain conditions while granting bail, such as the requirement to attend the court on all dates of hearing, not to leave the jurisdiction of the court without permission, and not to tamper with the evidence or influence the witnesses.
