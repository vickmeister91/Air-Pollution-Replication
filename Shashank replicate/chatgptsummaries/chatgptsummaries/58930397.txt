Mahendra Sonwane had filed an anticipatory bail application under Section 438 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, as he was anticipating his arrest in connection with a case registered at Police Station Forest Range Lanji, District Balaghat. The case was registered against him for the offence punishable under Sections 2(16)(7), 9, 39 (1)(b), 39 (3), 44(1) 39-A (b) 39-B (III), 50 51 and 52 of Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The court allowed the application and directed Sonwane to surrender himself before the Investigating Officer within ten days from the date of receipt of the certified copy of the order.

Upon his arrest, Sonwane shall be released on bail on his furnishing a personal bond of Rs. 40,000 with a solvent surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of the Arresting Authority. He shall make himself available for interrogation by a police officer as and when required and abide by the other conditions enumerated in sub-Section (2) of Section 438 of Cr.P.C.

The Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, is an Act of the Parliament of India enacted for the protection of plants and animal species. The Act provides for the protection of wild animals, birds, and plants, and for matters connected therewith or ancillary or incidental thereto. The Act has been amended several times since its enactment, and the latest amendment was in 2006.

Section 2(16)(7) of the Act defines "trophy" as any part of an animal specified in Schedule I, Schedule II, Schedule III, Schedule IV, or Schedule V, which has been kept or preserved by any means, whether artificial or natural, and includes any such part converted into any article or item. Section 9 of the Act provides for the prohibition of hunting of wild animals, birds, and plants. Section 39(1)(b) of the Act provides for the power of the Forest Officer to arrest without warrant any person who has committed a cognizable offence under the Act. Section 39(3) of the Act provides for the power of the Forest Officer to search any person, vessel, or vehicle if he has reason to believe that any wild animal, bird, or plant, trophy, or any forest produce in respect of which any offence has been committed, is concealed therein.

Section 44(1) of the Act provides for the punishment for hunting in a sanctuary or national park, which shall not be less than three years but may extend to seven years and shall also be liable to a fine. Section 39-A(b) of the Act provides for the power of the Forest Officer to seize any vehicle, vessel, or weapon used for committing any offence under the Act. Section 39-B(III) of the Act provides for the power of the Forest Officer to seize any animal, bird, or plant, trophy, or any forest produce in respect of which any offence has been committed.

Section 50 of the Act provides for the punishment for contravention of any provision of the Act or any rule, order, or permit made thereunder, which shall not be less than three years but may extend to seven years and shall also be liable to a fine. Section 51 of the Act provides for the punishment for attempting to contravene any provision of the Act or any rule, order, or permit made thereunder, which shall not be less than one year but may extend to three years and shall also be liable to a fine. Section 52 of the Act provides for the punishment for abetment of any offence under the Act, which shall be the same as the punishment for the offence itself.
