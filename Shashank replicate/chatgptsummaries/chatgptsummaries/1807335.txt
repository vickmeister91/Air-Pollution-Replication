The Jammu & Kashmir High Court recently received a petition from residents seeking the closure of industrial units manufacturing carbon in the area due to air pollution caused by the process. The petitioners claimed that the units were established without the necessary permissions and were emitting smoke and foul smells, causing health hazards to the residents. The respondents argued that they had obtained the required permissions and were not causing any harm. The court emphasized the importance of protecting the environment and the right to life under Article 21 of the Constitution and stated that the power to regulate industries under the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, was coupled with a duty to ensure sustainable development. The court ordered the concerned authorities to take action in accordance with the Act to protect the environment and the health of the residents.

The court order highlights the importance of sustainable development and environmental protection while allowing for industrial development. The State Pollution Control Board is responsible for ensuring environmental protection. The court orders an inquiry into the operation of industrial units and the installation of pollution control devices. The Deputy Commissioner is also ordered to investigate the construction of residential houses near the industrial units and whether permission was obtained. The report is to be submitted within two months. The court stresses the responsibility of all individuals to protect the environment and the duty of authorities to make the public aware of safety measures.

The court's decision is a significant step towards protecting the environment and the health of the residents.
