The case at hand involves a dispute between Mahendra Singh and the complainant over a loan that was allegedly advanced by the complainant to Mahendra Singh. In order to support his case, Mahendra Singh had filed an application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. requesting the complainant to produce account books, bank records, and income tax returns. However, the trial court rejected the application on the grounds that the complainant had not made any entry regarding the loan advanced to the accused in his income tax returns.

Mahendra Singh then filed a petition under Section 482 Cr.P.C. challenging the order passed by the trial court. The revisional court affirmed the trial court's order, stating that the complainant was not required to maintain a record of the loan advanced to the accused in his income tax returns. The court further held that the trial court had correctly rejected the application filed by Mahendra Singh under Section 91 Cr.P.C.

The court's decision in this case is significant as it clarifies the scope of Section 91 Cr.P.C. and the obligations of a complainant in maintaining records of transactions. Section 91 Cr.P.C. empowers a court to issue a summons or order to produce any document or thing that is necessary for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. However, the court's power under this section is not absolute and is subject to certain limitations.

In the present case, the court held that the complainant was not required to maintain a record of the loan advanced to the accused in his income tax returns. This decision is in line with the general principle that income tax returns are not a comprehensive record of all transactions and cannot be relied upon to establish the existence or non-existence of a particular transaction.

The court's decision also highlights the importance of maintaining accurate records of transactions. While the complainant in this case was not required to maintain a record of the loan advanced to the accused in his income tax returns, it is advisable for parties to maintain accurate records of all transactions in order to avoid disputes and legal proceedings.
