The court has granted anticipatory bail to Harbu @ Jayant Tigga, the petitioner in a case registered under sections 33 of the Indian Forest Act and section 1, 2 & 3 of Forest Conservation Act. The petitioner is accused of constructing a house over forest land, but he denies the allegations and is willing to deposit Rs. 5000 and undertake not to go over the land where the house is being constructed or any forest area during the case's pendency.

The court has ordered that the petitioner be released on bail upon arrest or surrender within four weeks, subject to certain conditions. The conditions include that the petitioner shall cooperate with the investigation, not tamper with evidence, not leave the country without the court's permission, and attend all court hearings.

The petitioner's counsel argued that the petitioner is a law-abiding citizen and has been falsely implicated in the case. The counsel further submitted that the petitioner is willing to cooperate with the investigation and has no intention of absconding.

The court considered the arguments of both parties and observed that the allegations against the petitioner are serious in nature. However, the court also noted that the petitioner has no criminal antecedents and is willing to cooperate with the investigation. The court, therefore, granted anticipatory bail to the petitioner subject to the conditions mentioned above.

In another case, the court has granted bail to the accused, Ramesh Kumar, who was arrested for allegedly assaulting a police officer on duty. The accused was charged under sections 332, 353, and 186 of the Indian Penal Code.

The court observed that the accused has been in custody for more than a month and that the investigation in the case is complete. The court further noted that the accused has no criminal antecedents and is not likely to abscond or tamper with evidence.
