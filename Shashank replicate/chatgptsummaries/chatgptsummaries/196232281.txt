Arvind Baba, the accused in a wildlife protection case, filed a bail application under Section 439 of the Cr.P.C. The prosecution alleged that the accused were in possession of the skin of a wild animal. However, the accused claimed to have been falsely implicated and had no criminal antecedents. After considering the arguments of both parties, the court allowed the bail application and ordered the accused to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 20,000 each with a solvent surety each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court.

The case against Arvind Baba and his co-accused was related to the illegal possession of the skin of a wild animal. The prosecution argued that the accused had violated the Wildlife Protection Act, 1972, which prohibits the hunting, killing, or possession of any wild animal or its parts without a valid permit. The prosecution also claimed that the accused had no valid explanation for possessing the animal skin and that they were involved in the illegal trade of wildlife.

On the other hand, the accused claimed that they were innocent and had been falsely implicated in the case. They argued that they had no knowledge of the animal skin and that it was planted on them by the police. They also claimed that they had no criminal antecedents and were willing to cooperate with the investigation.

After hearing the arguments of both parties, the court allowed the bail application filed by Arvind Baba. The court observed that the accused had no criminal antecedents and that there was no evidence to suggest that they were a flight risk or a threat to society. The court also noted that the accused had cooperated with the investigation and had no previous record of non-cooperation.

However, the court imposed certain conditions on the grant of bail. The accused were ordered to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 20,000 each with a solvent surety each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court. The court also directed the accused to cooperate with the investigation and not to tamper with the evidence or influence the witnesses. The accused were also ordered to attend all the court hearings and not to leave the jurisdiction of the trial court without prior permission.
