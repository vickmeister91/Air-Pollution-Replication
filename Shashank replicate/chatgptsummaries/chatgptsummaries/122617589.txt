In a case filed by the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board against M/S Maruthi Enterprises, the accused were found guilty of violating Section 25 and 26 of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. The accused had started construction of residential apartments without prior consent from the complainant Board. The Deputy Environmental Officer was authorized to file the complaint on behalf of the Board, and the accused No-2 was the partner of accused No-1 and responsible for managing the day-to-day affairs. The accused pleaded not guilty but failed to prove that the offense was committed without their knowledge or that they exercised all due diligence to prevent it.

The court order stated that accused No-2 was in charge and responsible for the company's conduct of business at the time of the offense. The accused had admitted to starting construction before obtaining permission from the Board. Accused No-1 is a firm, and accused No-2 is the partner of accused No-1, who was managing and in charge, responsible for accused No-1, and both were liable to be convicted. Accused No-2 was sentenced to undergo S.I. for one year six months and also had to pay a fine of Rs.25,000/-, in default to undergo S.I. for three months for the said offense.

The court order further stated that accused No-1 and 2 had been convicted for an offense under Section 44 of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, for contravention of Section 25 of the Act. Accused No-2 was sentenced to undergo S.I. for one year six months and pay a fine of Rs.25,000/-, in default to undergo S.I. for three months for the said offense. The office was directed to supply a free copy of the judgment to the accused.

In summary, M/S Maruthi Enterprises was found guilty of violating Section 25 and 26 of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, for starting construction of residential apartments without prior consent from the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board. Accused No-2, who was in charge and responsible for the company's conduct of business at the time of the offense, was sentenced to undergo S.I. for one year six months and pay a fine of Rs.25,000/-, in default to undergo S.I. for three months for the said offense. Both accused No-1 and 2 were convicted for contravention of Section 25 of the Act and accused No-2 was sentenced accordingly. The office was directed to supply a free copy of the judgment to the accused.
