The court order in question pertains to a criminal petition filed by Ravikanth Babu K against M Govardhan. Ravikanth Babu K had filed an application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. seeking direction to the complainant to produce certain documents. The Court below had passed an order dismissing the application, against which this petition was filed under Section 482 of Cr.P.C.

The crux of the matter was whether the accused person could compel the complainant to produce certain documents. The court found that there was no error in the order passed by the learned Magistrate and rejected the petition. The accused person cannot compel the complainant to produce the documents. It is for the complainant to produce the documents to prove his case against the accused person.

Section 91 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.) empowers a court to issue a summons or order to produce any document or thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. The section is intended to enable the court to obtain evidence that is necessary for the just decision of a case.
