The court order in question pertains to a petition filed under Section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C) seeking the quashing of an order passed by the Chief Judicial Magistrate in a case registered under Sections 420, 465, 467, 471, 468 & 506 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC). The petitioner had challenged the validity of the order passed by the trial court, which had summoned certain documents for comparison of signatures by a handwriting expert. The court, however, dismissed the petition, stating that the trial court had validly exercised its discretion in summoning the documents for comparison of signatures. The court noted that the trial court had summoned the documents for the limited purpose of comparison of signatures, and that it had not relied on these documents for any other purpose.

The court also observed that the provisions of Section 311 of the Cr.P.C could be invoked for the examination of witnesses, including the summoning of documents. Section 311 of the Cr.P.C empowers the court to summon any person as a witness or to produce any document or thing that it considers necessary for the just decision of the case. The court noted that the trial court had invoked this provision for the purpose of summoning the documents for comparison of signatures, and that it had not acted arbitrarily or capriciously in doing so.

The court further observed that the petitioner had not been able to demonstrate any illegality or irregularity in the order passed by the trial court. In light of these observations, the court held that the petition was liable to be dismissed.
