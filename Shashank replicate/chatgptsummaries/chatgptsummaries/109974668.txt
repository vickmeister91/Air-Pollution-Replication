The case in question involved a challenge to an order passed by the Metropolitan Magistrate in a case registered under Section 406/498A/34 IPC. The Magistrate had allowed the Investigating Officer to conduct a search at four premises and break open locks if required. The petitioner argued that the order was passed without giving reasons and without perusing the record of any summons issued by the Investigating Officer.

Upon examination of the case, the court found that the Magistrate did not mention any reason for issuing the search warrants and did not have any record of summons issued to the petitioner. This lack of reasoning and documentation was a clear violation of the law. However, since the search had already been conducted at three properties and was ongoing at the fourth, the court did not stay the execution of the search warrants.

Instead, the court directed that a copy of the order be sent to the Trial Court for compliance with the law in the future. This was an important step in ensuring that the rights of the petitioner were protected and that the law was followed in all future cases.
