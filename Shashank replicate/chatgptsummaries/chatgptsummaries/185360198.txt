The Karnataka High Court recently heard a criminal petition filed by M/s Mahalakshmi Iron Ore Mines against the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board. The petitioners claimed to have obtained consent from the respondent for mining activity under the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act,1981. However, the respondent filed a complaint alleging that the petitioners had violated the provisions of Sections 21 and 22 of the Air Act. The petitioners argued that the complaint did not disclose any violation of the Air Act and that they had obtained clearance from the Ministry of Environment and Forests.

The court considered both arguments and concluded that the activity of the petitioner, if it was illegal, prior to obtaining such consent and clearance, would be insignificant as the authorities could have withheld such consent and clearance if the petitioner had violated the conditions in the first instance. The court ordered that the complaint be quashed. The court order states that the petitioner violated conditions, but the government did not find fault with their activity and granted them clearance. The authorization obtained for the complaint is inconsistent with the complaint itself, and the excess mining of ore was permitted by the government. Therefore, the petition seeking to quash the proceedings is allowed, and the criminal prosecution is infirm and a futile exercise. The proceedings are quashed.
