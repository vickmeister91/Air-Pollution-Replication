The Punjab and Haryana High Court recently passed a judgment in a writ petition filed by M/s Maghar Washed Stone Crusher against an order passed by the Chief Environmental Engineer, Punjab Pollution Control Board. The petitioner had challenged the order on the grounds that it was arbitrary, illegal, and without jurisdiction. However, the court dismissed the petition as the petitioner had not availed the remedy of filing an appeal against the order.

The court observed that the petitioner had not challenged the order within the prescribed time limit of 30 days, and therefore, the petition was not maintainable. The court further noted that the petitioner had not provided any valid reason for the delay in filing the appeal. The court also observed that the petitioner had not exhausted the statutory remedy of filing an appeal before approaching the court.

The court, however, granted the petitioner liberty to withdraw the petition and file an appeal with an application for condonation of delay within 15 days. The court hoped that the Appellate Authority would consider and decide the appeal in accordance with the law.

The judgment highlights the importance of following the prescribed legal procedures and exhausting all available remedies before approaching the court. The court emphasized that the statutory remedies provided under the law must be exhausted before approaching the court, and failure to do so may result in the dismissal of the petition.

The judgment also underscores the significance of filing appeals within the prescribed time limit. The court further observed that the petitioner had not provided any valid reason for the delay in filing the appeal.
