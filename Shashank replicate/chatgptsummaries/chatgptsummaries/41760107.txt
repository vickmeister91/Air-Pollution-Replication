The court order in question pertains to a criminal revision case filed by R.K. Emu Farms against K. Muthu under Sections 397 and 401 of the Criminal Procedure Code. The case was filed under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881. The accused had appeared before the court and pleaded not guilty. The complainant's evidence was closed as the accused did not cross-examine the complainant.

During the course of the trial, the accused had filed a petition under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure read with 45 of the Indian Evidence Act, which was allowed. However, the accused did not pay the fees for sending the disputed cheque for comparison to the handwriting expert. Subsequently, the accused filed a memo stating that they were not willing to send the disputed cheque for comparison.

The accused then filed a petition to return the materials to the complainant/respondent, which was dismissed by the learned Judicial Magistrate, FTC, Tiruchengode. The court found no illegality in the impugned order, warranting interference, and dismissed the criminal revision petition.

The punishment for such an offence can be imprisonment for a term which may extend to two years, or with a fine which may extend to twice the amount of the cheque, or with both.
