The Madras High Court recently issued a court order pertaining to a writ petition filed under Article 226 of the Constitution of India. The petition requested the respondents to establish Bio-Diversity Management Committees in all local bodies in the State of Tamil Nadu in accordance with the Biological Diversity Act, 2002 and the Biological Diversity Rules, 2004. The court has directed the respondents to constitute these committees in all local bodies throughout Tamil Nadu within a year.

The order is significant as it seeks to ensure the protection and conservation of biodiversity in the state. The Biological Diversity Act, 2002 was enacted to provide for the conservation of biological diversity, sustainable use of its components, and fair and equitable sharing of the benefits arising out of the use of biological resources. The Act recognizes the need to involve local communities in the conservation and management of biological diversity and provides for the establishment of Biodiversity Management Committees (BMCs) at the local level.

The court order directs the respondents to establish BMCs in all local bodies in Tamil Nadu. The BMCs are to be constituted in accordance with the provisions of the Biological Diversity Act, 2002 and the Biological Diversity Rules, 2004. The BMCs are to be responsible for the conservation and sustainable use of biological resources in their respective areas. They are also to ensure the equitable sharing of benefits arising out of the use of biological resources.

The court order lists the respondents who have been served notice. The respondents include the Chief Secretary to the Government of Tamil Nadu, the Secretary to the Department of Environment and Forests, the Secretary to the Department of Rural Development and Panchayat Raj, and the Commissioner of Municipal Administration. The order directs these respondents to ensure that BMCs are constituted in all local bodies in Tamil Nadu within a year.

The establishment of BMCs in all local bodies in Tamil Nadu is a step towards ensuring the conservation and sustainable use of biological resources in the state. The BMCs will be responsible for identifying and documenting the biological resources in their respective areas. They will also be responsible for preparing People's Biodiversity Registers (PBRs) which will contain information on the biological resources and traditional knowledge associated with them.

The BMCs will also be responsible for regulating access to biological resources and ensuring that the benefits arising out of the use of these resources are shared equitably. This is particularly important as the use of biological resources can often lead to the exploitation of local communities and the loss of traditional knowledge.
