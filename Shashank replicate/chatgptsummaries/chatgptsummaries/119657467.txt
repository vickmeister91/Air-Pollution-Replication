The Rajasthan High Court recently made a significant ruling in favor of four companies that had been denied consent to operate by the Rajasthan State Pollution Control Board. The Board had refused to grant consent to operate to these companies under the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution Act) 1981 and the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. However, the court found that the Board had violated the principles of natural justice in passing this order, as it had not considered the companies' clarifications and supporting documents, and had failed to reflect an inspection report in the order.

The court's ruling was based on the fact that the Board had not given the companies a fair opportunity to present their case. The companies had submitted clarifications and supporting documents to the Board, but these had not been taken into account when the order was passed. Additionally, the Board had not reflected an inspection report in the order, which was a violation of the principles of natural justice.

The court directed the Board to reconsider the companies' applications for consent to operate and to pass an order within 10 days. This ruling is significant because it upholds the principles of natural justice and ensures that companies are given a fair opportunity to present their case before any decision is made.
