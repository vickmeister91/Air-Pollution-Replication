The court has recently issued an order in relation to two petitions filed under Section 438 Cr.P.C. for the grant of anticipatory bail in a case registered under FIR No.136 dated 5.9.2017. The case pertains to the offences under Sections 188 and 379 IPC and Section 4 of Mines Act, 1952 and Section 15 of the Environment Protection Act, 1986 at Police Station Tigaon, District Faridabad.

The court has allowed the petitions and made the interim orders granting bail to the petitioners absolute. The petitioners are required to join the investigation as and when called upon to do so and abide by the conditions of Section 438 (2) Cr.P.C.

The order is significant as it pertains to the grant of anticipatory bail, which is a provision under the Criminal Procedure Code that allows a person to seek bail in anticipation of arrest. The provision is meant to protect individuals from arbitrary arrest and detention by the police.

In this case, the court has granted anticipatory bail to the petitioners, which means that they will not be arrested by the police in connection with the case. However, the court has also imposed certain conditions on the petitioners, which they must abide by.

One of the conditions is that the petitioners must join the investigation as and when called upon to do so. This means that they must cooperate with the police in the investigation of the case. Failure to do so may result in the cancellation of their bail.

Another condition imposed by the court is that the petitioners must abide by the conditions of Section 438 (2) Cr.P.C. This provision lays down certain conditions that must be followed by a person who has been granted anticipatory bail. These conditions include that the person must not leave the country without the permission of the court, must not tamper with evidence or influence witnesses, and must cooperate with the investigation.

The court's order is significant as it highlights the importance of the provision of anticipatory bail in protecting individuals from arbitrary arrest and detention. It also emphasizes the need for individuals to cooperate with the police in the investigation of cases.

Overall, the court's order in this case is a positive development as it ensures that the petitioners are not subjected to arbitrary arrest and detention by the police. At the same time, it also ensures that the investigation of the case is not hampered by the grant of anticipatory bail.
