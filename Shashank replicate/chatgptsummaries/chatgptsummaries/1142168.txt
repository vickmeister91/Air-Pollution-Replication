While they did involve violations of the Wild Life Protection Act and Karnataka Forest Act, they did not involve any violence or harm to human life. Thirdly, the court noted that the petitioner had already cooperated with the investigation and had appeared before the investigating officer whenever required. This suggested that he was not trying to evade the law and that he was willing to face the charges against him.
