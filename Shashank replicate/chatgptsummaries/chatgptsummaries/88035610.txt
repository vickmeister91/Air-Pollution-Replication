The Orissa High Court recently issued a notice to the opposite party in a case related to the closure of a unit due to pollution concerns. The court directed the opposite party to file requisites by November 30, 2018, failing which the interim order would stand dismissed. The matter was listed for hearing on January 7, 2019. The court also stayed the order passed by the Regional Officer, State Pollution Control Board, Angul, which directed the closure of the Unit until the next date of hearing.

The petitioner in the case had submitted that if the Unit was closed, it would not be possible to install the effluent treatment plant. The court took note of this submission and stayed the closure order until the next date of hearing. The court's decision to stay the closure order is a significant relief for the petitioner, who had argued that the closure of the unit would have a severe impact on their business operations.

The case highlights the importance of balancing environmental concerns with the need for economic development. While it is essential to protect the environment, it is also crucial to ensure that businesses are not unduly impacted by regulatory measures. The court's decision to stay the closure order is a step in the right direction towards achieving this balance.

It is worth noting that the court's decision to stay the closure order is not a final decision on the matter. The court will hear the case in detail on January 7, 2019, and will make a final decision based on the evidence presented before it. It is essential for all parties involved in the case to present their arguments and evidence in a clear and concise manner to ensure that the court can make an informed decision.
