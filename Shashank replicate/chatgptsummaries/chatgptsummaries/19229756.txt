The court order in question pertains to a criminal petition filed by Rizwana Fathima and Syed Taufiq Ahmed, who sought to quash an order passed by the X A.C.M.M, Mayo Hall, Bengaluru in CC No.55094/2016. The order in question had allowed an application filed by the complainant, Smt. Vidhya Sachitanand Suvarna, seeking direction to the petitioners to produce certain documents. However, the court found that the lower court had erred in allowing the application, as it went against the law laid down by the Hon'ble Supreme Court in a previous case. Therefore, the impugned order was set aside.

The case in question involved a dispute between the petitioners and the complainant, who had alleged that the petitioners had cheated her out of a sum of money. The complainant had filed a complaint with the police, which had led to the registration of an FIR against the petitioners. The petitioners had then filed a petition before the lower court seeking to quash the FIR, which was dismissed by the court. The petitioners had then filed a revision petition before the High Court, which had also been dismissed.

The petitioners had then filed a criminal petition before the Sessions Court, which had been transferred to the X A.C.M.M, Mayo Hall, Bengaluru. The complainant had then filed an application before the court seeking direction to the petitioners to produce certain documents. The court had allowed the application, which had led to the filing of the present petition before the High Court.

The High Court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, found that the lower court had erred in allowing the application filed by the complainant. The court noted that the law laid down by the Hon'ble Supreme Court in a previous case was clear that a complainant cannot seek direction to the accused to produce documents at the stage of investigation. The court observed that the lower court had failed to take into account this law while allowing the application filed by the complainant.

The court also noted that the complainant had not made out a case for the production of the documents sought by her. The court observed that the complainant had not explained how the documents sought by her were relevant to the case. The court also noted that the complainant had not explained how the documents sought by her were in the possession of the petitioners.

The court, therefore, set aside the order passed by the lower court allowing the application filed by the complainant. The court also directed the lower court to proceed with the case in accordance with law. The court observed that the petitioners were at liberty to raise all their contentions before the lower court.
