Milaram Gond, a resident of a forest village, filed a bail application under Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 for his arrest in connection with a forest crime case. The prosecution alleged that Gond and other accused persons entered the reserve forest area, disturbed the wild habitat, cut trees, and caused damage to public property. However, Gond's counsel argued that he has not caused any loss or damage to the forest.

After hearing both sides, the court allowed Gond's bail application and directed him to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 25,000 with one surety for his appearance in court. The court's decision was based on the fact that Gond is a resident of a forest village and has no prior criminal record. Additionally, the court noted that Gond's counsel had presented a strong argument that he had not caused any damage to the forest.

The court's decision to grant bail to Gond is in line with the principles of justice and fairness. The court recognized that Gond is innocent until proven guilty and that he should not be deprived of his liberty without just cause. The court also recognized that Gond's ties to his community and lack of criminal history make him a low flight risk.
