The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Prabh Jyot Singh Jyoti under Section 439 of the Cr.P.C. for an offence punishable under sections 26(I)-A of Indian Forest Act, 1927, section 2 (15), 16-C, 9, 51 and 52 of the Wild Life Protection Act. The allegation against the applicant is that he, along with other co-accused, illegally sold and transported sagon woods to another state.

The court allowed the bail application on the grounds that the applicant is a first offender, no other case of a similar nature has been registered against him, and there is no likelihood of him absconding or tampering with the prosecution evidence. The applicant was ordered to furnish a personal bond for the sum of Rs. 1,00,000/- with two local solvent sureties and comply with certain conditions, failing which the bail would be cancelled.

The court's decision to grant bail to the accused is based on several factors. Firstly, the applicant is a first-time offender, which means that he has no prior criminal record. This is an important consideration in determining whether or not to grant bail, as it suggests that the accused is not a habitual offender and is less likely to commit further crimes.

Secondly, the court noted that no other case of a similar nature has been registered against the accused. This suggests that the accused is not a repeat offender and is less likely to commit similar crimes in the future.

Thirdly, the court found that there is no likelihood of the accused absconding or tampering with the prosecution evidence. This is an important consideration in determining whether or not to grant bail, as it suggests that the accused is not a flight risk and is less likely to interfere with the investigation.

In light of these factors, the court granted bail to the accused on the condition that he furnish a personal bond for the sum of Rs. 1,00,000/- with two local solvent sureties and comply with certain conditions. These conditions are designed to ensure that the accused does not abscond or interfere with the investigation.

The court's decision to grant bail to the accused is significant, as it highlights the importance of considering all relevant factors when determining whether or not to grant bail.
