The Act provides for the prevention, control, and abatement of environmental pollution and the protection of the environment. The petitioner in this case is apprehending arrest in connection with a case registered under Sections 15 & 19 of the Act.

Section 15 of the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 deals with the penalty for contravention of the provisions of the Act. It states that whoever contravenes any provision of the Act or any rule, order or direction issued thereunder, shall, if no other penalty is provided for that contravention under the Act, be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to five years or with fine which may extend to one lakh rupees, or with both, and in the case of a continuing contravention, with an additional fine which may extend to five thousand rupees for every day during which such contravention continues after the conviction for the first such contravention.

Section 19 of the Act deals with the power of the Central Government to give directions. It states that the Central Government may, in the exercise of its powers and performance of its functions under this Act, issue directions in writing to any person, officer or authority, and such person, officer or authority shall be bound to comply with such directions.
