The Karnataka High Court recently passed a judgment in a writ petition filed by N M Lokesh against the State of Karnataka. The petitioner had sought to quash an order passed by the concerned authority under Section 62 of the Karnataka Forest Act, which had seized certain articles belonging to the petitioner. The court noted that the seizure had taken place five years ago, and since then, the petitioner had not challenged the impugned order nor the prosecution.

The court observed that the petitioner had not taken any steps to challenge the order or the prosecution for five years, and that the delay in filing the writ petition was not satisfactorily explained. The court also noted that the petitioner had not produced any material to show that the seizure was illegal or that the articles seized belonged to him.

The court further observed that the petitioner had not availed of the remedy available to him under the law, which was to file an appeal before the learned Magistrate. The court held that the petitioner had failed to establish any grounds for quashing the order passed by the concerned authority.

The court dismissed the writ petition, stating that it was open to the petitioner to seek appropriate relief before the learned Magistrate. The court also observed that the petitioner had not made out any case for interference by the High Court in the matter.
