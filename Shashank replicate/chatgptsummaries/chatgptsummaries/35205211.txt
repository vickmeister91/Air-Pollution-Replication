Abdul Mustaq Khan, who was in custody for a forest crime under the Wild Life Protection Act, was granted bail by the court. His counsel argued that he was a reputed citizen with no criminal past and there was no evidence against him. The arrest was made solely on the basis of a statement given by a co-accused, which was not admissible as evidence. The court allowed the application for bail and directed the release of Abdul Mustaq Khan on a bond of Rs. 30,000 with one surety bond of the same amount. The order would be effective until the end of the trial, but in case of bail jump, it would become ineffective.

The case of Abdul Mustaq Khan highlights the importance of evidence in criminal cases. The court recognized that the statement given by a co-accused was not admissible as evidence and therefore, there was no basis for Abdul Mustaq Khan's arrest. The court also took into consideration the fact that Abdul Mustaq Khan was a reputed citizen with no criminal past. This shows that the court is willing to consider the character of the accused when making decisions about bail.

The court's decision to grant bail to Abdul Mustaq Khan is also significant. Bail is a legal right of an accused person, and the court must consider several factors before granting bail. These factors include the nature of the offence, the likelihood of the accused fleeing, and the likelihood of the accused interfering with the investigation. In this case, the court found that there was no evidence against Abdul Mustaq Khan and that he was not likely to flee or interfere with the investigation. Therefore, the court granted him bail.
