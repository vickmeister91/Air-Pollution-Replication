The Patna High Court has granted anticipatory bail to Kunil Chaudhary and Akhilesh Kumar, who were apprehending their arrest in a case related to illegal mining of sand. The petitioners are the owner and driver of the tractor in question. The court has ordered them to surrender before the learned court below within six weeks and furnish bail bonds of Rs.10,000 each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad.

The case in question pertains to illegal mining of sand, which is a rampant problem in many parts of India. The petitioners, Kunil Chaudhary and Akhilesh Kumar, were accused of being involved in such illegal mining activities. They had approached the Patna High Court seeking anticipatory bail, as they feared arrest by the police.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both sides, granted anticipatory bail to the petitioners. However, the court also ordered them to surrender before the learned court below within six weeks and furnish bail bonds of Rs.10,000 each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad.

This order by the Patna High Court is significant as it highlights the issue of illegal mining of sand, which is a major problem in many parts of India. The court has taken a strict stance on the matter and has ordered the petitioners to surrender and furnish bail bonds, which is a clear indication that the court is serious about tackling this issue.

It is important to note that illegal mining of sand not only causes environmental damage but also leads to loss of revenue for the government. The sand mafia, which is involved in such illegal activities, often operates with impunity and uses violence to intimidate those who try to stop them.

In this context, the order by the Patna High Court is a welcome step towards curbing such illegal activities. The court has sent a strong message to those involved in illegal mining of sand that they will not be allowed to operate with impunity.

It is also important to note that the court has granted anticipatory bail to the petitioners, which means that they will not be arrested by the police. This is a relief for the petitioners, who were apprehending arrest and were seeking protection from the court.
