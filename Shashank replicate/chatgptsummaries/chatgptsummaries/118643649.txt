The court order in question pertains to a petition filed under Section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.) against an order passed by the learned XIVth Additional Sessions Judge Indore, in Criminal Revision No. 9637125/2016. The applicant had filed an application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. to call the Income-tax return of the respondent for the year 2013-2014 and to call Santosh Arya, Sameer Sheikh, and Sandeep as defense witnesses. However, the learned Trial Court rejected the application, and the learned ASJ also rejected the applicant's revision.

The petitioner had sought to call the Income-tax return of the respondent for the year 2013-2014 as evidence in the case. However, the court observed that there was no need to call the Income-tax return of the respondent for the just decision of the case. The court held that the Income-tax return was not relevant to the case and that the petitioner had failed to establish its relevance. Therefore, the court rejected the petitioner's application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C.

The petitioner had also sought to call Santosh Arya, Sameer Sheikh, and Sandeep as defense witnesses. However, the court held that without the correct address, the court was not competent to call the witnesses only from their names. The court observed that the petitioner had failed to provide the correct address of the witnesses and had not made any effort to locate them. Therefore, the court rejected the petitioner's application to call the witnesses.

The court held that the petitioner had failed to establish the relevance of the Income-tax return and had not provided the correct address of the witnesses. Therefore, the court rejected the petitioner's petition under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C.
