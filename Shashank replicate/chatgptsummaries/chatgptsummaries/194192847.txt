In a recent court order, the Rajasthan State Pollution Control Board was directed to address a civil writ petition filed by M/S Laxmi Handicrafts. The court has instructed the petitioner to appeal under the Water (Prevention of Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, as an alternate remedy. This decision was made in order to provide a more appropriate and effective solution to the issue at hand.

The court has also directed that if an appeal is filed, it should be decided within four weeks of filing. This is a significant directive, as it ensures that the appeal process is expedited and that the issue is resolved in a timely manner. This is particularly important in cases such as this, where environmental concerns are at stake.

The writ petition filed by M/S Laxmi Handicrafts has been disposed of accordingly. This means that the court has made a final decision on the matter and that the case is now closed. The decision to dispose of the writ petition in this manner indicates that the court believes that the alternate remedy provided under the Water (Prevention of Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, is the most appropriate course of action.

Overall, this court order highlights the importance of addressing environmental concerns in a timely and effective manner. By directing the petitioner to appeal under the Water (Prevention of Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, the court has provided a clear path forward for resolving the issue at hand. Additionally, the directive to decide on the appeal within four weeks of filing ensures that the issue is resolved in a timely manner.
