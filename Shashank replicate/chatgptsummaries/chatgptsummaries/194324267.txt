The Bombay High Court has ordered the State Government to file an affidavit on the action taken against those who violated Noise Pollution Rules during the Ganpati Festival of 2018 and Eid-E-Milad held on 21st February 2018. The court found that in 12 cases, no action had been taken, and the police officer who filed the affidavit did not provide any reasons for this inaction. The court directed the Commissioner of Police to file a personal affidavit explaining why the police have expressed helplessness in enforcing the law and order. The court also considered issuing a notice of criminal or civil contempt to the police officer who filed the affidavit.

The court has ordered the AGP to communicate the order to the Commissioner of Police and forward copies of affidavits filed by Shri Singe. The Commissioner of Police is instructed to investigate the failure of the Mumbai Police to take action against violations of Noise Pollution Rules and note that the Special Leave Petition filed by the State Government has been dismissed. The petition will be placed on March 5th, 2019, at 3:00 pm for consideration of affidavits filed by the Commissioner of Police and Municipal Corporations.
