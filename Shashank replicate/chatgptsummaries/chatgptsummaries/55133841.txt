The case in question involves a criminal misc. petition filed by the petitioner, Subhash, under Section 482 Cr.P.C. against an order passed by the Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate in Criminal Case No. 783/2013. The petitioner had filed an application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. which was dismissed by the trial court. The petitioner was then accused of filing the application to delay the matter.

The High Court of Judicature for Rajasthan at Jodhpur was tasked with reviewing the order passed by the trial court. After careful consideration, the court found no illegality in the order and dismissed the criminal misc. petition.

The petitioner's application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. was dismissed by the trial court on the grounds that it was filed with the intention of delaying the proceedings. The petitioner had argued that the application was filed to obtain certain documents that were necessary for the case. However, the trial court found that the documents in question were not relevant to the case and that the application was filed with the intention of delaying the proceedings.

The petitioner then filed a criminal misc. petition under Section 482 Cr.P.C. in the High Court of Judicature for Rajasthan at Jodhpur. The petitioner argued that the trial court had erred in dismissing the application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. and that the dismissal was illegal and unjustified.

The High Court of Judicature for Rajasthan at Jodhpur carefully reviewed the order passed by the trial court and found no illegality in the order. The court noted that the trial court had considered the relevant facts and had come to a reasonable conclusion. The court also noted that the petitioner had failed to provide any evidence to support their claim that the documents in question were relevant to the case.

The court further noted that the petitioner had a history of filing applications with the intention of delaying the proceedings. The court found that the petitioner's actions were causing unnecessary delays in the case and were not in the interest of justice.

In light of these findings, the High Court of Judicature for Rajasthan at Jodhpur dismissed the criminal misc. petition filed by the petitioner. The court upheld the order passed by the trial court and found no illegality in the order.
