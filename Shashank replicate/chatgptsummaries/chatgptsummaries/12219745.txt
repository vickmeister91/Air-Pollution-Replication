The Madhya Pradesh High Court recently allowed an application filed by the Madhya Pradesh Pollution Control Board to appeal against the acquittal of M/s Gwalior Distillers Limited Rairu and others under sections 33, 43, 44 read with sections 47 and 49 of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. The court found that the trial court had not correctly appreciated evidence and material available on record and had committed errors of law. The matter was directed to be registered under the head of the Criminal Appeal.

The case pertains to the pollution caused by M/s Gwalior Distillers Limited Rairu, a distillery unit, which was found to be discharging untreated effluent into the nearby river. The Madhya Pradesh Pollution Control Board had filed a complaint against the company and its officials under various sections of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. The trial court had acquitted the accused on the ground that the prosecution had failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt.

The Madhya Pradesh High Court, however, found that the trial court had not correctly appreciated the evidence and material available on record. The court noted that the prosecution had produced sufficient evidence to prove that the accused had violated the provisions of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. The court also observed that the trial court had committed errors of law in interpreting the provisions of the Act.

The court, therefore, allowed the application filed by the Madhya Pradesh Pollution Control Board and directed that the matter be registered under the head of the Criminal Appeal. The court also directed that the appeal be heard expeditiously.
