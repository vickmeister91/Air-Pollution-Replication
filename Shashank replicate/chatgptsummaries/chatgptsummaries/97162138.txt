The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Shankarsinh Gautamsinh Sisodiya under Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. The application was filed for offences punishable under Sections 65(A)(E), 98(2) and 81 of the Gujarat Prohibition Act. The court has allowed the application and ordered the applicant to be released on regular bail on executing a personal bond of Rs.25,000/- with certain conditions.

The court has considered several factors while granting bail to the applicant. Firstly, the nature of the offence has been taken into account. The offences in question are related to the violation of the Gujarat Prohibition Act, which prohibits the manufacture, sale, and consumption of alcohol in the state. While the offence is serious, it is not of a heinous nature, and therefore, the court has deemed it fit to grant bail.

Secondly, the fact that the investigation is over has been taken into account. This means that there is no risk of the applicant tampering with evidence or influencing witnesses. The investigation has been completed, and the chargesheet has been filed. Therefore, the court has deemed it fit to grant bail.

Thirdly, the absence of antecedents has been taken into account. Antecedents refer to the criminal history of the applicant. In this case, the applicant has no prior criminal record, and therefore, the court has deemed it fit to grant bail.

The court has also imposed certain conditions while granting bail. The applicant has been ordered to execute a personal bond of Rs.25,000/-. A personal bond is a written undertaking by the accused that he will appear in court as and when required. If the accused fails to appear in court, the bond will be forfeited, and the accused will be liable to pay the amount mentioned in the bond.

In addition to the personal bond, the court has also imposed other conditions. The applicant has been ordered to cooperate with the investigation, not to tamper with evidence, not to influence witnesses, and to appear in court as and when required. The court has also directed the applicant to surrender his passport, if any, and not to leave the country without the permission of the court.

It is important to note that while granting bail, the court has directed the trial court to not be influenced by the observations made by the High Court. This means that the trial court must consider the case on its own merits and not be influenced by the fact that the High Court has granted bail to the applicant.

In conclusion, the court has granted bail to Shankarsinh Gautamsinh Sisodiya under Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, for offences punishable under Sections 65(A)(E), 98(2) and 81 of the Gujarat Prohibition Act. The court has considered the nature of the offence, the investigation being over, and the absence of antecedents while granting bail. The court has also imposed certain conditions while granting bail, and the trial court has been directed to not be influenced by the observations made by the High Court while granting bail.
