The court order pertains to a petition filed by Suresh Kumar Pathrella under Section 482 CrPC and Article 227 of the Constitution of India read with Section 91 CrPC against the order dated 03rd November, 2011 of the learned Special Judge, CBI in C.C.No.28/11. The petitioner had filed an application under Section 91 CrPC before the Special Judge for supply of copies of some of the documents. The learned ASJ vide the impugned order declined the production of those documents and also supplying of copies thereof to the petitioner. The learned ASJ relied on the judgment of Supreme Court in the case of State of Orissa Vs. Debendra Nath Padhi, AIR, 2005 Supreme Court 359 wherein it was held that Section 91 does not confer any right of production of documents to the accused to enable him to prove his defence and such a power need not to be exercised under Section 91 of the CrPC at the stage of framing of charges.

The court order states that during the pre-charge stage, the prosecution is not required to provide the accused with copies of every document gathered during the investigation, but only those that they propose to rely upon. The court also notes that the accused does not have an indefeasible legal right to every document in the police file or complaint. At the stage of framing charges, the court is only required to determine whether there is a strong suspicion that the accused has committed an offense, and not to weigh the evidence or consider the accused's defense. The court dismisses the petition.
