The court, after considering the facts and circumstances of the case, allows the petition and grants bail to the petitioner subject to satisfaction of the Trial Court. This means that Singh will be released from custody, but he will have to comply with certain conditions set by the Trial Court.

It is important to note that bail is not a right, but a privilege granted to an accused person. The court has to consider various factors before granting bail, such as the nature of the offence, the severity of the punishment, the likelihood of the accused person fleeing from justice, and the likelihood of the accused person tampering with evidence or influencing witnesses.

In this case, the court has taken into account the fact that Singh had jumped bail in the past, which suggests that he may be a flight risk. However, the court has also noted that Singh is currently in custody, which reduces the likelihood of him fleeing from justice. The court has therefore decided to grant bail to Singh, subject to certain conditions.

It is not clear from the court order what these conditions are, but they are likely to include things like surrendering his passport, reporting to the police station regularly, and refraining from contacting certain individuals or visiting certain places. These conditions are designed to ensure that Singh does not flee from justice or interfere with the investigation.
