In a recent case, the petitioners filed a petition to quash a notification issued under the Wild Life (Protection) Act 1972, which declared an area as a sanctuary for the protection of wildlife and its environment. The petitioners claimed that they were deprived of their property without compensation. However, the court found that the area had already been declared as a reserved forest before the notification and that the notification was issued in compliance with the Supreme Court's directions.

The court also found that the petitioners had not explained the delay in filing the petition and were guilty of laches. Laches is a legal term that refers to an unreasonable delay in pursuing a legal claim or right, which can result in the loss of that claim or right. In this case, the court found that the petitioners had waited too long to challenge the notification, and as a result, their claim was barred by laches.

The court went on to uphold the notification and the order passed by the Collector. This means that the area will continue to be protected as a sanctuary for the benefit of wildlife and the environment. The court's decision is significant because it reaffirms the importance of protecting wildlife and their habitats, even if it means restricting human activities in certain areas.
