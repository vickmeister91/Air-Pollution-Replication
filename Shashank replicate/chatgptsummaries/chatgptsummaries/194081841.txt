The case in question involves a petition filed by the petitioner, Sathish, under Section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code. The petition sought to set aside an order passed by the Special Court for Mahila (Fast Track), Kanyakumari Division at Nagercoil in Cr.M.P.No.270 of 2016 in Spl.C.No.1 of 2014 dated 11.07.2016. The petitioner is accused of offences punishable under Sections 376 of IPC, r/w Section 8 of POCSO Act.

The petitioner had filed a petition under Section 91 Cr.P.C. for the production of Attendance Register by the Headmaster of the school in connection with the attendance of the victim girl on the date of occurrence. However, the petition was dismissed on 16.08.2016, for non-compliance of the earlier order dated 11.07.2016.

The court allowed the petition on the condition that the petitioner pays a sum of Rs.10,000/- to the victim girl within a period of four weeks from the date of receipt of the copy of this order. The court's decision was based on the fact that the petitioner had failed to comply with the earlier order and that the production of the Attendance Register was necessary for the proper adjudication of the case.

The case highlights the importance of compliance with court orders and the need for parties to provide all relevant evidence to ensure a fair and just trial. The court's decision also emphasizes the need for parties to act in good faith and to cooperate with the court in the administration of justice.

In conclusion, the court's decision in this case serves as a reminder of the importance of compliance with court orders and the need for parties to provide all relevant evidence to ensure a fair and just trial. It also highlights the need for parties to act in good faith and to cooperate with the court in the administration of justice.
