The court order in question pertains to a criminal case that took place in Rajasthan. The petitioner had filed a petition under section 91 Cr.P.C. for summoning documents during the course of the trial. However, the revision petition filed by the petitioner was rejected by the Add. Sessions Judge, No.5 Kota, and the order of the Judicial Magistrate was affirmed.

The court directed the petitioner to submit the required document on 16.5.2011 and ordered that the trial be completed within two weeks. This was a significant decision as it ensured that the trial would be completed in a timely manner, which is crucial for ensuring justice is served.

It is worth noting that the adinterim order granted by the Co-ordinate Bench of this court dated 21.4.2011 stands vacated. This means that any previous orders or decisions made by the court are no longer valid or in effect.

Overall, this court order highlights the importance of timely completion of trials and the need for all parties involved to comply with court orders. It also serves as a reminder that court decisions can be overturned or vacated, and that parties must be prepared to adapt to changing circumstances.
