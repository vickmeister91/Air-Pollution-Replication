The Delhi Pollution Control Committee (DPCC) filed a case against M/s Bittu Tikki Wala for using polythene bags, which is prohibited under the Environment Protection Act. The accused pleaded guilty and were convicted under Section 15 of the Act. The court imposed a fine of Rs. 10,000 each and ordered Rs. 10,000 to be given to the complainant as compensation and cost of proceedings, and the remaining Rs. 10,000 to be deposited in the State Exchequer. However, the petitioner challenged the order, stating that the court had erred in interpreting the law and failed to appreciate the special law enacted for the protection of the environment. The court found merit in the petitioner's contentions and set aside the order.

The court order pertains to the case of DPCC vs. Bittu Tikki Wala. The designated court has the power to impose a fine of up to Rs. 1 lakh and imprisonment for up to five years or both. The court cannot render Section 15 of the Environment (Protection) Act nugatory. The accused persons were explained and apprised about the probable sentence and fine which may be imposed upon them. 

The order passed by the ld. MM dated 20.04.2010, sentencing both the respondents no.2 & 3 to pay a fine of Rs.10,000 each is set aside, and the matter is remanded back to the concerned trial court.
