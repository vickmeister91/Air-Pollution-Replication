The Karnataka High Court recently issued a court order in response to a writ petition filed by M/S Laxmi Narasimhaswamy Stone Crushers against the State of Karnataka and other respondents. The petitioner sought to quash a closure notice issued by the Karnataka Pollution Control Board and sought a writ of mandamus to direct the competent authority to consider its application to operate the crushing unit. 

The court found that the petitioner was a valid licensee and allowed the operation of the unit at the present site until its application was considered and disposed of by the licensing authority or for a period of four weeks from the date of the order. The court also referred to a similar case and followed the decision of the Division Bench in that case. The closure notice was quashed, and there was no order as to costs.

The court order is significant as it highlights the importance of following due process and ensuring that the rights of licensees are protected. The court found that the petitioner had a valid license to operate the crushing unit and that the closure notice was issued without proper consideration of the petitioner's application. The court's decision to quash the closure notice and allow the operation of the unit until the application is considered and disposed of by the licensing authority or for a period of four weeks from the date of the order is a clear indication that licensees have a right to operate their businesses without undue interference from regulatory authorities.
