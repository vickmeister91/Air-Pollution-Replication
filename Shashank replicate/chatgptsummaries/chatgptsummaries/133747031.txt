The Karnataka High Court recently issued a court order in response to a writ petition filed by M/S Narsinghrao Matel Industries against the State of Karnataka and other respondents. The petitioner runs a stone crushing unit under a valid license and had sought to quash a closure notice issued by the Karnataka Pollution Control Board. The court found that the case was similar to a previous writ petition and ordered that the closure notice be quashed. 

The court also directed the respondents to permit the operation of the petitioner's crushing unit at the present site until its application for relocation is considered and disposed of by the licensing authority, or for a period of four weeks from the date of the order. This order has significant implications for the petitioner and the state of Karnataka, as it allows the petitioner to continue operating their business without interruption while their application for relocation is being considered.

The court's decision to quash the closure notice was based on the fact that the petitioner had a valid license to operate their stone crushing unit. The court noted that the Karnataka Pollution Control Board had not provided any evidence to suggest that the petitioner was violating any environmental regulations or causing harm to the environment. As such, the court found that the closure notice was arbitrary and without merit.

The court's decision to allow the petitioner to continue operating their business until their application for relocation is considered is also significant. This decision recognizes the importance of allowing businesses to continue operating while their applications for relocation are being processed.
