The court order pertains to a case where the petitioner, Sabu @ Poulose, had sought anticipatory bail as the 4th accused for offences punishable under Sections 27(1)(d), 27(1)(e) of the Kerala Forest Act read with Section 9, 27,29, 31, 32, 39, 44 and 49(B) of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The court denied anticipatory bail but allowed the petitioner to surrender before the Investigating Officer for interrogation and recovery of incriminating material, if any. The petitioner could then apply for regular bail before the Magistrate or the Court concerned. The court also set a deadline for the petitioner to surrender before the Investigating Officer.

The case pertains to the alleged violation of the Kerala Forest Act and the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The petitioner, Sabu @ Poulose, had sought anticipatory bail as the 4th accused in the case. The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, denied anticipatory bail to the petitioner. However, the court allowed the petitioner to surrender before the Investigating Officer for interrogation and recovery of incriminating material, if any. The petitioner could then apply for regular bail before the Magistrate or the Court concerned.

The court observed that the allegations against the petitioner were serious in nature and involved the violation of the Kerala Forest Act and the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The court also noted that the petitioner had not cooperated with the investigation and had absconded. The court further observed that the investigation was at a crucial stage and the petitioner's custodial interrogation was necessary for the purpose of investigation.

The court, while denying anticipatory bail to the petitioner, also set a deadline for the petitioner to surrender before the Investigating Officer. The court directed the petitioner to surrender before the Investigating Officer within a period of one week from the date of the order. The court also directed the Investigating Officer to interrogate the petitioner and recover any incriminating material, if any.

The court further directed that in case the petitioner was arrested, he should be produced before the Magistrate or the Court concerned within a period of 24 hours. The court also directed that the Magistrate or the Court concerned should consider the bail application of the petitioner on its merits and in accordance with the law.
