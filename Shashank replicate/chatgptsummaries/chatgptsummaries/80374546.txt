The court order in question pertains to a bail application made by Raja Singh Bhadoriya, who has been in custody for six months in connection with a forest crime case. This is the fourth bail application made by the applicant, with all previous applications being rejected on merit. The complainant has not produced any witnesses before the trial court, which has led to the court directing the trial court to give the complainant one last opportunity to submit evidence before charge. Failing to do so may result in presumption being drawn against them.

The court has rejected the bail application made by Raja Singh Bhadoriya, and a copy of the order has been sent to the trial court and the state counsel. The order highlights the importance of producing evidence before the trial court, as failure to do so may result in the court drawing adverse inferences against the complainant.

The court's decision to reject the bail application is based on the fact that the applicant has already made three previous bail applications, all of which were rejected on merit. The court has noted that the applicant has not been able to provide any new grounds for bail, and that the previous grounds raised by the applicant have already been considered and rejected by the court.

The court has also taken into account the seriousness of the charges against the applicant, which relate to a forest crime case. The court has noted that such crimes have a serious impact on the environment and the ecosystem, and that the accused must be held accountable for their actions.

The court's decision to direct the trial court to give the complainant one last opportunity to submit evidence before charge is significant. It highlights the importance of producing evidence in a timely manner, and the consequences of failing to do so. The court has made it clear that if the complainant fails to produce evidence before charge, presumption may be drawn against them.
