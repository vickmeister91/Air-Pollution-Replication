The court order pertains to a writ petition filed by S. Chinnazhagan challenging the impugned notification issued by the District Collector of Madurai to issue a license to quarry stone in an unassessed waste land belonging to the government situated in S.No.418, Mettupatti Village, Vadipatti Taluk, Madurai District. The petitioner contended that the land is a hillock and consists of 175.45.0 Hectare land, wherein the villagers are carrying out agriculture operations over an extent of 100 acres and the agriculture is the only source of livelihood for the villagers. The Forest Department has planted trees in the rest of the land under Social Forest Scheme, there is no access to the site and the same is through the water channel only and it would be destroyed if vehicles go through it and there are various wildlife, natural medicine, etc.

The court heard the arguments of both parties and allowed the impleading petition filed by the proprietor of the firm to be heard. The court found that the revenue and as well as Forest Department, after appraisal of issues and spot inspection come forward with the proposal that the proposed auction will generate income to the Government it cannot be ignored and the bald statement of the petitioner could not be accepted. The court allowed the writ petition to be dismissed.

In another court order, the respondent has no objection to leasing the proposed quarry as the land is not a forest land and there are legal requirements in place to safeguard everyone's interests. The petitioner's objection is deemed to be purely based on personal motives and not in the interest of the public. The court cites previous judgments to emphasize that public interest litigation should not be used for personal gain or to settle personal scores. The court dismisses the writ petition and states that the petitioner has no locus standi to file the petition as it is for the government to decide whether to lease the land or not.
