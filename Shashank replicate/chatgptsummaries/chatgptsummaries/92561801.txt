The Karnataka High Court recently received a writ petition from the Hospet Raithara Sangha and other individuals against the Government of Karnataka, the Commissioner for Cane Development and Director of Sugar in Karnataka, M/S. The India Sugars & Refineries Limited, the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board, and the Deputy Commissioner of Bellary District. The petitioners sought a writ of mandamus or any other writ, direction, or order to stop the crushing operation of respondent no.3 until respondent no.4 passes the consent order under the provisions of Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 and Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974.

The court heard arguments from all parties and ordered respondent no.3 to stop the crushing operation until the violations of the Water and Air Pollution Acts were removed. The court order directs respondent no.4 to take action in accordance with the law after considering the response of respondent no.3 to the violations noted in the Inspection Report of the Pollution Control Board. The writ petition is disposed of.
