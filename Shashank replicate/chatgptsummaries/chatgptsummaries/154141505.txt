The court order in question pertains to a case where the petitioner was not considered for promotion to the post of Assistant Environmental Engineer due to the application of the 1987 Regulations, which had not been approved by the State Government. The court had earlier held that the 1987 Regulations had no force and could not be relied upon. 

The petitioner, dissatisfied with the decision, filed a fresh representation listing out his grievances. The court directed that the representation be decided on merits by the Principal Secretary, Government of Punjab, Department of Science and Technology in the light of the decisions rendered in previous cases. The Principal Secretary was directed to dispose of the representation as expeditiously as possible and in any case within three months from the date of its submission by the petitioner.

The case in question highlights the importance of following proper procedures and regulations in matters of promotion and employment. The court's decision to set aside the 1987 Regulations, which had not been approved by the State Government, underscores the need for transparency and accountability in such matters.
