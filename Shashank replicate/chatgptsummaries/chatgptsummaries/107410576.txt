The court order in question pertains to an application filed by Nisar Khan against the State of Madhya Pradesh. The application challenged the order of a Special Judge who had dismissed Khan's application under section 91, 92 of Cr.P.C. and under section 65 of the Evidence Act. The applicant had requested the preservation of call details of certain mobile numbers, which he claimed were necessary for his defense and were destroyed after one year. The Special Judge had dismissed the application, but the High Court set aside the order and directed the trial judge to send intimation to the company to preserve the call records and their locations at the relevant time. The application was disposed of accordingly.

The case highlights the importance of preserving evidence in criminal cases. In this case, the call details of certain mobile numbers were crucial to the defense of the accused, and their destruction after one year could have severely impacted the outcome of the trial. The High Court's decision to set aside the Special Judge's order and direct the trial judge to send intimation to the company to preserve the call records and their locations at the relevant time was a crucial step in ensuring that justice was served.

The case also highlights the importance of the role of the judiciary in ensuring that evidence is preserved in criminal cases. The Special Judge's decision to dismiss the application for the preservation of call details could have had serious consequences for the accused, and it was only through the intervention of the High Court that justice was served. The judiciary plays a crucial role in ensuring that the rights of the accused are protected and that justice is served in criminal cases.
