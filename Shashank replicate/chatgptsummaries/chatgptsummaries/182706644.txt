The Patna High Court has granted bail to Sanjay Singh in connection with a case registered for offenses punishable under Sections 379, 411 and 420/34 of the Indian Penal Code and Section 4/40 of Bihar Mines and Minerals concession Rules, 1972 and Section 15 Prevention of Pollution Act, 1986. The petitioner was accused of carrying sand loaded on a tractor without proper challan and fleeing when the police party arrived. The court allowed the petitioner to be released on bail on furnishing a bail bond of Rs.25,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad, subject to certain conditions.

The Patna High Court granted bail to Sanjay Singh on furnishing a bail bond of Rs.25,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad, subject to certain conditions.

The court allowed the petitioner to be released on bail subject to certain conditions. The conditions included that the petitioner shall not tamper with the evidence, shall not influence the witnesses, shall cooperate in the investigation, shall not commit any similar offense while on bail, and shall appear before the court on the date fixed for hearing. The court also directed the petitioner to surrender his passport, if any, and not to leave the country without the permission of the court.

The court considered the facts and circumstances of the case, the nature of the offense, the severity of the punishment, the likelihood of the petitioner fleeing from justice, and the possibility of the petitioner tampering with the evidence or influencing the witnesses. The court observed that the petitioner had no criminal antecedents, was a resident of the place where the case was registered, and had cooperated with the investigation. The court also noted that the petitioner had been in custody for a considerable period and that the investigation was almost complete.

The court held that the petitioner was entitled to bail and that the conditions imposed by the court were necessary to ensure that the petitioner did not misuse the liberty granted to him. The court directed the petitioner to furnish a bail bond of Rs.25,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad, and to comply with the conditions imposed by the court.
