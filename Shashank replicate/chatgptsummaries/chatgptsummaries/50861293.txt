The Delhi High Court recently heard a petition filed by Sanjay Arora, who sought the quashing of FIR No. 246/2013 registered with the Economic Offences Wing, Police Station Mandir Marg, New Delhi, for the offences punishable under Sections 420/467/468/471/406/120B IPC and the consequential proceedings emanating therefrom against him. Arora argued that the investigation had been ongoing for over five years, and despite having replied to the notices issued by the Investigating Authority, the respondent No.1 had not concluded the investigation.

The court directed the Investigating Authority to expedite the investigation in the case and file a report accordingly. The petition was disposed of. The case highlights the importance of timely investigation and the need for the authorities to expedite the process.

The petitioner, Sanjay Arora, had been under investigation for over five years, and despite having replied to the notices issued by the Investigating Authority, the respondent No.1 had not concluded the investigation. This delay had caused significant hardship to Arora, who had been unable to move on with his life due to the ongoing investigation.

The court recognized the importance of timely investigation and directed the Investigating Authority to expedite the process and file a report accordingly. This decision highlights the need for the authorities to take timely action in such cases to ensure that justice is served and the rights of the accused are protected.

The case also highlights the need for the authorities to be diligent in their investigations and to ensure that they are conducted in a fair and impartial manner. The accused has the right to a fair trial, and any delay or negligence on the part of the authorities can result in a violation of this right.

In conclusion, the Delhi High Court's decision in the case of Sanjay Arora highlights the importance of timely investigation and the need for the authorities to be diligent in their investigations. The court's direction to expedite the investigation in the case and file a report accordingly is a step in the right direction towards ensuring that justice is served and the rights of the accused are protected.
