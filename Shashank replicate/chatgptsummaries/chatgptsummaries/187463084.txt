The court recently granted bail to Ankit Mandlekar in a case registered under the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The court allowed an application for amendment and directed Mandlekar to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 1,00,000/- and one surety in the like amount for his appearance before the concerned court on all the dates of hearing during trial. The allegation against Mandlekar was that he received a sulk of pangolin from a co-accused, but apart from the co-accused's statement, there was no evidence to substantiate the accusation.

The Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, is an act of the Indian Parliament enacted for the protection of plants and animal species. The act provides for the protection of wild animals, birds, and plants, and for matters connected therewith or ancillary or incidental thereto. The act aims to protect the wildlife and their habitats, and to prevent the illegal trade of wildlife and their products.

In the present case, Ankit Mandlekar was accused of receiving a sulk of pangolin from a co-accused. Pangolins are a critically endangered species and are protected under the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The act prohibits the hunting, killing, or capturing of any wild animal, including pangolins. The act also prohibits the possession, sale, or transport of any wild animal or their products.
