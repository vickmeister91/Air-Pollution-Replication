The recent court order pertains to a writ petition filed by Laxmi Chouhan and Ashok Kumar Mishra against the Union of India, National Tiger Conservation Authority, State of Chhattisgarh, Indravati Tiger Reserve, and Deputy Director Indravati Tiger Reserve. The petitioners raised concerns about reducing the buffer zone area of Indravati Tiger Reserve and the constitution of an expert committee against the provisions of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972.

The court directed Respondent No. 1, which is the Union of India, to consider the issue and conduct necessary enquiry/survey before passing any final order. The court emphasized that the reduction of the buffer zone area of Indravati Tiger Reserve should not be done without proper consideration of the impact on the wildlife and the environment. The court also noted that the constitution of an expert committee should be done in accordance with the provisions of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972.

The court order is significant as it highlights the importance of protecting the environment and wildlife. The court has recognized the need to balance development with conservation and has directed the authorities to take necessary steps to ensure that the reduction of the buffer zone area of Indravati Tiger Reserve does not have a negative impact on the wildlife and the environment.

The court has also emphasized the need to follow the provisions of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972, while constituting an expert committee. This is important as the Act provides for the protection of wildlife and their habitats and lays down guidelines for the constitution of expert committees.

The court has disposed of the writ petition and directed the security deposit to be paid to the High Court Legal Aid Committee. This is a positive step as it ensures that the funds are utilized for the benefit of the society.
