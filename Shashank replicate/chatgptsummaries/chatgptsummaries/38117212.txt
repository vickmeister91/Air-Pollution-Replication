The case of Ravinder Khosla was brought before the High Court after his application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. was dismissed by the Judicial Magistrate Ist Class, Ludhiana. The application was to summon the record of police and civil court for cross-examination of CW1 - Nishant Batra. The trial court dismissed the application stating that it was devoid of merit and was moved to delay the trial. 

The petitioner, Ravinder Khosla, was unhappy with the dismissal of his application and sought relief from the High Court. However, the High Court found no illegality or infirmity in the impugned order and dismissed the petition. 

The case highlights the importance of following proper legal procedures and presenting evidence in a timely and appropriate manner. The trial court's decision to dismiss the application was based on the lack of merit and the potential to delay the trial. This decision was upheld by the High Court, indicating that the legal system values efficiency and fairness in the administration of justice.
