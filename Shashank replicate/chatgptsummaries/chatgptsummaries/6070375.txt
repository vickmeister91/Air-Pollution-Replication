The case against Ramswaroop was registered under various sections of the Wild Life Protection Act. The Act was enacted to provide for the protection of wild animals, birds and plants and for matters connected therewith or ancillary or incidental thereto. The Act aims to protect the wildlife and their habitats, regulate hunting, poaching, trade, and commerce in wild animals and articles derived from them. The Act also provides for the establishment of national parks, sanctuaries, and other protected areas for the conservation of wildlife.

Ramswaroop was accused of violating various provisions of the Act. Section 9 of the Act prohibits hunting of wild animals, birds, and plants. Section 2(2) defines hunting as capturing, killing, poisoning, snaring, or trapping any wild animal, and includes any attempt to do so. Section 31(ka) (kha) provides for punishment for hunting in a sanctuary or national park. Section 35 provides for punishment for damaging or destroying any boundary mark or protection work. Section 50 (ga) provides for punishment for illegal possession of any captive animal or article derived from such animal. Section 51(1) provides for punishment for contravention of any provision of the Act or any rule or order made thereunder. Section 51(ka) provides for punishment for abetment of any offence under the Act. Section 52 provides for punishment for attempts to commit any offence under the Act.
