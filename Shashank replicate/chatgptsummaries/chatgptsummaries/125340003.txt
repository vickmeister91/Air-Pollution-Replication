The court order in question pertains to a petition filed under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. by Bhartendra Pratap Singh against an order passed by the Chief Judicial Magistrate, District Sidhi (M.P.) in Forest Crime No.535/13. The applicant had filed an application under Section 457 of the Cr.P.C. for releasing the offending vehicle on interim custody on Supurdginama, which was dismissed by the learned Magistrate. The court allowed the petition and set aside the impugned order dated 13/01/2014.

The case in question involves an offending vehicle, a Tata 407 bearing registration number M.P53 - G.A./0391, which was allegedly involved in a forest crime. The applicant, Bhartendra Pratap Singh, had filed an application under Section 457 of the Cr.P.C. for releasing the vehicle on interim custody on Supurdginama. However, this application was dismissed by the Chief Judicial Magistrate, District Sidhi (M.P.).

In response, Singh filed a petition under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. challenging the order of the Magistrate. The court allowed the petition and set aside the order of the Magistrate. The offending vehicle was directed to be released on interim custody on furnishing a Supurdginama by the applicant in a sum of Rs.4,00,000/- (Rupees Four Lac Only) with one surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of Chief Judicial Magistrate, Sidhi.

The court's order also specified that the vehicle would remain in interim custody until the disposal of the criminal case arising out of Forest Crime No.535/13, as well as until the final disposal of confiscation proceedings pending in the Forest Department, if any.

Overall, the court's order in this case allows for the release of an offending vehicle on interim custody, subject to certain conditions. The order also sets out the circumstances under which the vehicle will remain in custody until the final disposal of the criminal case and any related confiscation proceedings.
