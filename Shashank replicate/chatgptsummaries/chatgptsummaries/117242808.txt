The Indian legal system provides for anticipatory bail, which is a pre-arrest bail that can be granted to a person who apprehends arrest in a non-bailable offence. In a recent case, the court granted anticipatory bail to a petitioner who was facing charges under Sections 33 of the Indian Forest Act and Section 2 of the Forest Conservation Act.

After hearing both sides, the court granted anticipatory bail to the petitioner, subject to certain conditions. The petitioner was directed to surrender in court within four weeks and would be released on bail upon furnishing a bond of Rs.15,000 with two sureties of the same amount each.
