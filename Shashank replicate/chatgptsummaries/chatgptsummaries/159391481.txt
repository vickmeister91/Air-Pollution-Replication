In a recent criminal case in the High Court of Punjab and Haryana, a petitioner requested the court to direct the trial court to decide on an application under Section 91 of the Criminal Procedure Code. This application had been pending since 2016, and the petitioner was seeking a resolution to the matter.

The court accepted the petitioner's request and issued an order directing the trial court to dispose of the application within six weeks from the date of receipt of the order. This decision was made in order to ensure that the case could be resolved in a timely manner and that justice could be served.

After the order was issued, the trial court promptly disposed of the application, bringing the case to a close. This decision was welcomed by all parties involved, as it allowed for a resolution to be reached after years of waiting.

Overall, this court order highlights the importance of timely resolution in criminal cases. By directing the trial court to dispose of the application within a set timeframe, the High Court of Punjab and Haryana was able to ensure that justice was served and that the case was brought to a close in a timely manner.
