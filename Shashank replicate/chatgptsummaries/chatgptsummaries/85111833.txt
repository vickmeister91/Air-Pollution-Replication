The Madras High Court recently passed a court order in a criminal original petition filed by M.S. Rajapandian. The petitioner had challenged the order passed by the Judicial Magistrate No. II, Walajapet, Vellore District in C.M.P. No. 1774/2018 in STC.No.55/2016 dated 05.10.2018. The petitioner had filed an application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C, which was dismissed by the court below. 

The petitioner had requested the District Registrar, Arakonam, to produce the register submitted to him by the stamp vendor to establish that the stamp paper produced in court by the respondent was not actually purchased in the year 2015. However, the court dismissed the petition, stating that the petitioner had protracted the proceedings at every stage and there was no illegality or infirmity in the order passed by the court below. 

The court directed the Judicial Magistrate No. II, Walajah, Vellore District, to complete the proceedings in STC No.55 of 2016 within two months. This court order is significant as it highlights the importance of following proper legal procedures and not protracting proceedings. 

In another court order, the Madras High Court directed the Tamil Nadu government to pay a compensation of Rs. 25,000 to a woman who was illegally detained by the police. The woman had filed a writ petition seeking compensation for the illegal detention and torture she had faced at the hands of the police. 

The court observed that the woman had been illegally detained and tortured by the police, and that this was a violation of her fundamental rights. The court also noted that the woman had suffered mental and physical trauma as a result of the illegal detention. 

The court directed the Tamil Nadu government to pay a compensation of Rs. 25,000 to the woman within four weeks, and also directed the government to take action against the police officers responsible for the illegal detention and torture. This court order is significant as it highlights the importance of protecting the fundamental rights of citizens and holding law enforcement officials accountable for their actions.
