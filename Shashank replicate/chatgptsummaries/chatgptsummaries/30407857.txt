The Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, is an act of the Indian Parliament enacted for the protection of plants and animals. The act provides for the protection of wild animals, birds, and plants, and for matters connected therewith or ancillary or incidental thereto. The act prohibits hunting, poaching, and trading in wild animals and their body parts.

Section 9 of the act provides for the establishment of a Wildlife Advisory Board to advise the central and state governments on matters relating to the protection of wildlife. Section 39 of the act provides for the power of the forest officer to arrest without warrant any person who has committed an offence under the act.
