The court order in question pertains to a revision petition filed by Akhilesh Tiwari against an order passed by the Chief Judicial Magistrate (C.J.M.) Sidhi in Forest Criminal Case No. 6784/2012. The order rejected the application under sections 451 and 457 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.) to release the vehicle Tata 407 bearing No.MP53-AA-3203 which was seized for offences under sections 9, 27, 29, 39 and 51 of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972 and sections 2, 41 and 52 of the Indian Forest Act, 1927.

The revision petition was filed on the grounds that the trial court had committed an error of jurisdiction or illegality warranting interference in this revision. However, the court found that the trial court had not committed any such error and therefore, there was no need for interference in this revision.

The court further advised the applicant to take recourse of law as permissible under section 52(3) and (4) of the Indian Forest Act and to further take recourse to file appeal or revision in accordance with law.

Section 451 of the Cr.P.C. deals with the provisions for the release of property seized under section 102 or 103 of the Cr.P.C. Section 457 of the Cr.P.C. deals with the provisions for the release of property seized under section 41 of the Indian Forest Act, 1927.

Section 9 of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972 deals with the prohibition of hunting. Section 27 of the Act deals with the punishment for the possession of any animal specified in Schedule I, II, III and IV of the Act. Section 29 of the Act deals with the punishment for hunting in a sanctuary or national park. Section 39 of the Act deals with the confiscation of any vehicle, weapon, trap, or tool used in the commission of an offence under the Act. Section 51 of the Act deals with the punishment for offences committed in contravention of the provisions of the Act.

Section 2 of the Indian Forest Act, 1927 defines the term "forest". Section 41 of the Act deals with the power to arrest without warrant. Section 52 of the Act deals with the power to seize property.
