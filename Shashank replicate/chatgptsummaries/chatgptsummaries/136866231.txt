The court order in question pertains to a criminal original petition filed under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The petitioner had borrowed a sum of Rs.4,00,000/- from the complainant, promising to pay the said amount with interest. The accused gave a post-dated cheque dated 21.03.2013 for Rs.4,00,000/- in favour of the complainant. When the said cheque was presented on 21.03.2013, the same was dishonoured on the ground of funds insufficient. The complainant issued a statutory notice dated 18.04.2013 and on the failure of the accused to repay the amount, launched a prosecution in CC.No.288 of 2013 before the learned Judicial Magistrate No.I, Salem, under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, against the accused.

The accused filed CMP No.4577 of 2014 in CC.No.288 of 2013 under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, for a direction to the complainant to produce source of documents in the year 2012 October and November for Rs.8 Lakhs possessed to him and thus render Justice. The trial Judge dismissed CMP No.4577 of 2014 on 10.12.2014, challenging which, this petition has been filed. The court dismissed the petition as it was devoid of merits.

In summary, the accused had borrowed Rs.4,00,000/- from the complainant and gave a post-dated cheque for the same amount. When the cheque was presented, it was dishonoured due to insufficient funds. The complainant issued a statutory notice and launched a prosecution under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act. The accused filed a petition under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, which was dismissed by the trial judge. The accused then filed a criminal original petition under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, which was also dismissed by the court.

The court order highlights the importance of fulfilling financial obligations and the consequences of failing to do so. The accused's attempt to challenge the prosecution by filing a petition under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure was deemed to be without merit, indicating that the court takes a serious view of attempts to evade financial obligations. The court's decision to dismiss the petition under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure reinforces the importance of upholding the law and ensuring that justice is served.

In conclusion, the court order pertains to a case of dishonoured cheque and the subsequent legal proceedings initiated by the complainant. The accused's attempt to challenge the prosecution was dismissed by the trial judge and the court, highlighting the importance of fulfilling financial obligations and upholding the law. The court's decision serves as a reminder to individuals to honour their financial commitments and to respect the legal system.
