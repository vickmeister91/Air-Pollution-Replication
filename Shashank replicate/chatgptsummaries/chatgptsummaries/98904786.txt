The court order in question pertains to a petition filed under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. by Amandeep Arora against an order passed by the III Additional Sessions Judge, Bhopal in Criminal Revision No. 390/2016. The order had affirmed the order passed by the learned Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Bhopal in Criminal Case No. 5041/2012, whereby the applicant's application filed under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. for calling the Income tax returns of firm Motilal Sharma & Company and other records of that firm from the respondent/complainant was rejected.

The petitioner, Amandeep Arora, had filed an application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. seeking the production of certain documents from the respondent/complainant, including the Income tax returns of firm Motilal Sharma & Company and other records of that firm. The application was filed in connection with Criminal Case No. 5041/2012, which was pending before the learned Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Bhopal.

The learned Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Bhopal rejected the application filed by the petitioner under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. The petitioner then filed a revision petition before the III Additional Sessions Judge, Bhopal, challenging the order passed by the learned Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Bhopal. The III Additional Sessions Judge, Bhopal, affirmed the order passed by the learned Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Bhopal, and dismissed the revision petition filed by the petitioner.

The petitioner then filed a petition under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. before the High Court of Madhya Pradesh, challenging the orders passed by the learned Courts below. The petitioner contended that the learned Courts below had erred in rejecting the application filed by him under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. The petitioner submitted that the documents sought to be produced were relevant to the case and were necessary for the just decision of the case.

The High Court of Madhya Pradesh, after considering the submissions made by the petitioner and the respondent, dismissed the petition filed by the petitioner under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. The High Court observed that the learned Courts below had not committed any mistake in rejecting the application filed by the petitioner under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. The High Court held that the documents sought to be produced were not relevant to the case and were not necessary for the just decision of the case.

The High Court further observed that the power under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. is a discretionary power and the Court has to exercise its discretion judiciously. The High Court held that the learned Courts below had exercised their discretion judiciously in rejecting the application filed by the petitioner under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C.
