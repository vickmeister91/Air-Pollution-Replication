The court recently granted anticipatory bail to Binod Kumar Singh in connection with a case registered under sections 33 (1) a, d, c of the Indian Forest Act and under Section 27/29 of Wild Life Protection Act. The petitioner denied the allegations against him and offered to deposit Rs.10,000/- with the D.F.O., Wild Life Division, Hazaribag. The court ordered that if the petitioner is arrested or surrenders within six weeks of the order, he shall be released on bail on showing proof of deposit and furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 25,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each.

The case against Binod Kumar Singh was registered under sections 33 (1) a, d, c of the Indian Forest Act and under Section 27/29 of Wild Life Protection Act. These sections deal with offences related to hunting, possession of wildlife, and damage to wildlife habitats. The petitioner, however, denied the allegations against him and claimed that he was falsely implicated in the case.

In order to secure anticipatory bail, the petitioner offered to deposit Rs.10,000/- with the D.F.O., Wild Life Division, Hazaribag. This was seen as a gesture of good faith and a willingness to cooperate with the authorities. The court took note of this and granted the petitioner anticipatory bail.

The court's order stated that if the petitioner is arrested or surrenders within six weeks of the order, he shall be released on bail on showing proof of deposit and furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 25,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each. This means that the petitioner will have to provide evidence of the deposit and also furnish a bail bond with two sureties of the same amount. The sureties will be responsible for ensuring that the petitioner appears in court when required.
