The case of P.S. Puspalatha involves a criminal original petition to set aside an order made in Crl.M.P.No.6136 of 2014 in STC No.599 of 2009 & Crl.M.P.No.6137 of 2014 in STC No.600 of 2009 on the file of Judicial Magistrate No.III, Salem. The petitioner is facing a prosecution in STC.Nos.599 & 600 of 2009 before the learned Judicial Magistrate No.III, Salem for an offense under Section 138 N.I.Act on the complaint filed by the respondent, G. Gangatharan.

The petitioner wanted to call for the letter said to have been sent by her to the Canara Bank in the year 2009 for stopping payment or inter-alia permitting her to mark a copy of the said letter through the Branch Manager of Canara Bank who was examined as DW1. The trial Court, after hearing both sides, disallowed the petition. The petitions were dismissed as devoid of merits.

It is important to note that Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act deals with dishonor of cheque for insufficiency, etc., of funds in the account. The section provides for punishment for the person who issues a cheque that is dishonored due to insufficient funds in the account. The punishment includes imprisonment for a term that may extend to two years, or with fine that may extend to twice the amount of the cheque, or with both.

The petitioner argued that the trial court had erred in disallowing the petition and that the letter was crucial to her defense.
