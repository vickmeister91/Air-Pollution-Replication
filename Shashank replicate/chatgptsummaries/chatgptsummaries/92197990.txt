Kuldeep Singh Tomer filed a criminal complaint against M/S. East West Linkers for carrying out illegal industrial activities in a residential area without consent from the Delhi Pollution Control Committee. However, the complaint was dismissed by the trial court due to a notice not being in the prescribed format and not being given 60 days prior to filing the complaint. 

The court found that the notice was in the prescribed format and the language used was sufficient to describe the offense. The order declining the cognizance was recalled and set aside, and the case was sent back to the trial court for further consideration. The court has recalled and set aside an order declining cognizance passed by a learned Magistrate. 

The Magistrate has been instructed to decide the case again based on the facts mentioned in the complaint, which alleges an offense under the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986. The revision petition has been allowed, and the trial court record is to be sent back along with a copy of the order. The file is to be consigned to the record room.
