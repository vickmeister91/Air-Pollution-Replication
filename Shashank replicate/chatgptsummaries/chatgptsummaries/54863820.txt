A petitioner, who claims to be a journalist and public-spirited citizen, filed a petition seeking directions against the State to register an FIR based on reports lodged by the petitioner alleging grave offences punishable under the Environment Protection Act, 1986, Indian Penal Code, and Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988. The allegations were that an influential politician and his relatives changed the user of land situated at Navghar bearing Survey No.266/1 to 6; 265/2, 4; 267/2, 3 (Old Survey No.67/1 to 6; 81/2, 4; 52/2, 3) which is part of CRZ-III and falls within 'no development zone'. The alleged offenders carried out huge construction and erected a hotel known as 'Seven Eleven Hotel', in flagrant violation of the provisions of CRZ Regulations, Environment Protection Act and Rules therein, the Development Control Rules and various orders passed by this Court as well as the Apex Court for protection of the environment, especially the conservation of the mangroves and the wetland. The jurisdictional police have not registered the FIR.

The court order discusses whether the police can register an FIR for offences punishable under the Environment Protection Act, 1986. The court concludes that the police can register an FIR for such offences, and that the bar against taking cognizance, except on the complaint of an authorized officer or a private person who has given a prior notice, and the power of the police to investigate into a cognizable offence operate in distinct spheres. The court also notes that if the report discloses commission of a cognizable offence, there is no other go but to register the FIR.

The court order states that if a report discloses the commission of a cognizable offence, then the police must register an FIR. The court cites paragraph 119 of a previous judgment, which emphasizes that the police must take action and cannot use discretion in such cases. The court orders the Senior Police Inspector to register the FIR and conduct an investigation in accordance with the law. The court clarifies that this order is only for the purpose of determining whether it is obligatory for the investigating agency to register the FIR and does not express any opinion on the merits of the matter. The court refuses to stay the investigation.
