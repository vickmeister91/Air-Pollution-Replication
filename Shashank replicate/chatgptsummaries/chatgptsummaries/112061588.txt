The Punjab and Haryana High Court has recently issued a court order that pertains to four writ petitions that raise concerns about pollution caused by rice shellers in the state of Punjab. The court has disposed of the petitions after a comprehensive policy was placed on record, which addresses the concerns raised by the petitioners and adequately addresses the issue of compliance with the provisions of the Environment Protection Act, 1986.

The court order is a significant step towards addressing the issue of pollution caused by rice shellers in the state of Punjab. Rice shellers are an essential part of the agricultural industry in Punjab, and they are used to remove the husk from rice grains. However, the process of rice milling generates a significant amount of dust and other pollutants, which can have adverse effects on the environment and public health.

The court has taken note of the concerns raised by the petitioners and has directed the Pollution Control Board to ensure that no rice sheller in the State of Punjab functions without compliance with the requirements of the policy in the ensuing paddy season. The Pollution Control Board has assured the court that the terms of the policy shall be strictly enforced, and no rice sheller shall be permitted to commence its operations in the ensuing season unless the Board is satisfied that they are equipped to meet the standards set out in the policy.
