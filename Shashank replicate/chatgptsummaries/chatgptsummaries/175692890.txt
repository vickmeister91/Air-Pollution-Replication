The Jharkhand High Court recently issued an order in connection with a case registered under Sections 379 of the Indian Penal Code, the Pollution Control Act, and the Indian Forest Act. The order directed three petitioners to surrender to the court within two weeks. The petitioners had been apprehending their arrest, but the court directed the court below to enlarge them on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs. 10,000 each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Hazaribag, subject to the conditions as laid down under Section 438(2) of the Cr.P.C.

The petitioners had approached the High Court seeking anticipatory bail, but the court had directed them to surrender before the court below. The court below had then rejected their bail applications, prompting the petitioners to approach the High Court once again.

The High Court, after hearing the arguments of both sides, observed that the allegations against the petitioners were serious in nature and required a thorough investigation. However, the court also noted that the petitioners had cooperated with the investigation and had not been arrested so far. The court further observed that the petitioners had roots in the society and were not likely to abscond or tamper with the evidence.

Taking these factors into consideration, the court directed the court below to enlarge the petitioners on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs. 10,000 each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Hazaribag, subject to the conditions as laid down under Section 438(2) of the Cr.P.C. The court also directed the petitioners to surrender to the court within two weeks and cooperate with the investigation.

The section lays down certain conditions that must be satisfied before bail can be granted. These conditions include the nature and gravity of the offence, the likelihood of the accused absconding or tampering with the evidence, and the need to ensure that the accused attends the trial.
