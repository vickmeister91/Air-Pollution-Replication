In 2005, a court order was issued to Rakesh Kumar, D.I.O.S. However, an application for contempt of court was filed against him for allegedly violating this order. The court found that there was a prima facie case and issued a notice to the opposite party, giving them a month to comply with the order before charges were framed. The office was instructed to send a copy of the order along with the notice.

The case was heard on January 21, 2010. During the hearing, it was revealed that the order in question was related to the appointment of a teacher. The court had ordered Rakesh Kumar to appoint a particular teacher, but he failed to do so. As a result, the court found him guilty of contempt of court.

The court then proceeded to impose a fine of Rs. 5,000 on Rakesh Kumar. Additionally, he was ordered to comply with the original court order within a month. If he failed to do so, he would face further charges of contempt of court.

This case highlights the importance of complying with court orders. Failure to do so can result in serious consequences, including fines and even imprisonment. It is important for individuals and organizations to take court orders seriously and to comply with them in a timely manner.
