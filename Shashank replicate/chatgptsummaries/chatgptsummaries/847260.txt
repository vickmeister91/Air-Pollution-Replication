The case of Hindalco Industries Ltd. challenging the orders from the Divisional Forest Officer, South Forest Division, Daltonganj, Medninagar, Palamau, has been resolved by the court. The orders directed the company not to carry out mining operations without prior approval from the Central Government under the Forest Conservation Act, 1980, and not to use or undertake repair work on the Hami-Orsa Road. The court found that the mining lease was subject to the provisions of the Forest Conservation Act, and the State Government or any other authority cannot direct that any forest land or a portion of forest land shall be used for any non-forest purposes without the prior approval of the Central Government.

The court also held that repairing or constructing a road within the forest land for the purpose of transportation of minerals to be extracted from the leasehold area shall also come within the definition of mining activities and that can also not be done without the prior approval of the Central Government. The court dismissed the writ petition.

The Forest Conservation Act, 1980, is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to protect the country's forests and wildlife. The act provides for the conservation of forests and for matters connected therewith or ancillary or incidental thereto. The act also provides for the establishment of a Central Government authority to oversee the implementation of the act.

The court's decision in this case is significant as it upholds the provisions of the Forest Conservation Act and ensures that forest land is not used for non-forest purposes without the prior approval of the Central Government.
