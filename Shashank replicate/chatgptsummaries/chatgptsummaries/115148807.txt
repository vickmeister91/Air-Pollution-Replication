The case property in question was a tractor and trolley that had been seized by the police in connection with a case registered at Police Station Kanchanpur, Dholpur. The petitioner, Devendra Singh, had filed a petition under Section 482Cr.P.C. to set aside an order passed by the Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, No.2, Badi, Dholpur, which had refused to release the tractor and trolley.

The petitioner argued that the Supreme Court has held that the vehicle should not be permitted to remain parked in the police station as it shall gather rust and shall not remain useful. The court allowed the petition and directed the trial court to release the tractor and trolley seized as case property by imposing certain conditions.

The court noted that the tractor and trolley had been seized as case property and were therefore subject to the provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure. However, the court also noted that the Supreme Court had held that the vehicle should not be permitted to remain parked in the police station as it shall gather rust and shall not remain useful.

In light of this, the court directed the trial court to release the tractor and trolley seized as case property by imposing certain conditions. These conditions included the requirement that the petitioner furnish a personal bond in the sum of Rs. 50,000 with one surety of like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court.

The court also directed the petitioner to deposit a sum of Rs. 25,000 with the trial court as a security deposit. This deposit was to be made within a period of two weeks from the date of the order.

The court further directed the petitioner to produce the tractor and trolley before the trial court within a period of four weeks from the date of the order. The trial court was then directed to release the tractor and trolley to the petitioner subject to the conditions imposed by the court.
