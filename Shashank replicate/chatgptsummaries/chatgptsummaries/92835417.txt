The court recently rejected the anticipatory bail application of Ajabul Sekh, who was apprehending his arrest in connection with a case registered under Sections 9, 49B, Ia (ii) (v) and 52 of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The court did not grant bail due to the nature of the allegations against the petitioner.

The Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, is a comprehensive law that provides for the protection of wild animals and plants and the conservation of their habitats. The Act aims to regulate hunting, poaching, and trade in wildlife and their products. It also provides for the establishment of national parks, sanctuaries, and other protected areas for the conservation of wildlife.

In this case, Ajabul Sekh had filed an anticipatory bail application apprehending his arrest in connection with a case registered under Sections 9, 49B, Ia (ii) (v) and 52 of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. Section 9 of the Act prohibits hunting of wild animals, while Section 49B prohibits the sale, purchase, or possession of any animal specified in Schedule I, II, III, and IV of the Act. Section 52 provides for punishment for contravention of the provisions of the Act.

The court, while rejecting the anticipatory bail application, took into consideration the nature of the allegations against the petitioner. The court observed that the allegations against the petitioner were serious in nature and that the petitioner was apprehending his arrest in connection with a case registered under the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The court also noted that the petitioner had not made out a case for grant of anticipatory bail.

The court's decision is in line with the provisions of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, which provides for stringent punishment for offences related to wildlife. The Act recognizes the importance of protecting wildlife and their habitats and provides for the establishment of national parks, sanctuaries, and other protected areas for their conservation.
