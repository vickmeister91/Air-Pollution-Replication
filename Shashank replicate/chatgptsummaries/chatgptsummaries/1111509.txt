The Madras High Court recently dismissed a writ petition filed by Thyagi S. Ponniah Pillai, who claimed to be a freedom fighter who worked in the Indian National Army. The petitioner sought to quash an order granting environmental clearance for quarrying rare minerals in Tuticorin District. The court found that the petitioner's claims were not genuine and that the mining activity had been approved by the relevant authorities after due process. The court dismissed the writ petition and vacated an earlier order prohibiting mining without prior permission.

The petitioner, Thyagi S. Ponniah Pillai, claimed to be a freedom fighter who worked in the Indian National Army. He filed a writ petition seeking to quash an order granting environmental clearance for quarrying rare minerals in Tuticorin District. The petitioner argued that the mining activity would cause irreparable damage to the environment and the health of the people living in the area.

The court found that the petitioner's claims were not genuine. The court noted that the petitioner had not provided any evidence to support his claim of being a freedom fighter. The court also observed that the petitioner had not raised any objections during the public hearing conducted by the authorities before granting environmental clearance for the mining activity.

The court further noted that the mining activity had been approved by the relevant authorities after due process. The court observed that the authorities had conducted a detailed study of the environmental impact of the mining activity and had taken necessary measures to mitigate the impact. The court also noted that the authorities had obtained the necessary clearances from the concerned departments before granting environmental clearance for the mining activity.

The court dismissed the writ petition filed by the petitioner. The court observed that the petitioner had failed to establish any grounds for quashing the order granting environmental clearance for the mining activity. The court also vacated an earlier order prohibiting mining without prior permission.

The court's decision is significant as it upholds the due process followed by the authorities in granting environmental clearance for the mining activity. The court's decision also highlights the importance of providing evidence to support claims made in court.
