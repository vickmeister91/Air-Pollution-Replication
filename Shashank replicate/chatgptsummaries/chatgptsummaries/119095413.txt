The order also lists six conditions that Govind must comply with, failing which the bail will be cancelled.

The first condition is that Govind must not directly or indirectly make any inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the court or to any police officer. The second condition is that he must not leave India without the prior permission of the court. The third condition is that he must surrender his passport, if any, to the court. The fourth condition is that he must not tamper with the evidence or attempt to influence or intimidate the witnesses. The fifth condition is that he must attend the trial court on all the dates of hearing fixed in this regard. The sixth and final condition is that he must not commit any offence while on bail.

The court has also observed that the allegations against Govind are not of a heinous nature and that he has no criminal antecedents. The court has further noted that the investigation in the case is complete and that the charge sheet has already been filed. The court has also taken into account the fact that Govind has been in custody for a considerable period of time and that his co-accused Aakib Khan has already been granted bail.

The court has therefore allowed the bail application and ordered that Govind be released on bail subject to the aforementioned conditions. The court has also directed him to cooperate with the investigating agency and to attend the trial court on all the dates of hearing fixed in this regard.
