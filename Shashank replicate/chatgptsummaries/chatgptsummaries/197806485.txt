The High Court of Madhya Pradesh recently dismissed a petition filed by a petitioner under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. against the order passed by the Judicial Magistrate First Class in RCT No.9808370/2012. The petitioner had filed an application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C., which was rejected by the trial court. The High Court dismissed the petition on merits and stated that the petitioner cannot file a fresh petition. The court also mentioned that the petitioner had many opportunities to lead evidence since 30.6.2015, and the application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. was filed after two years. The court cited various judgments of the Hon'ble Apex Court and stated that none of them are applicable in the present case.

The court noted that the petitioner had been callous in contesting the case and questioning the discretion of the learned trial court. The court also observed that there was an inordinate delay of two years in filing the application, which was not acceptable. The trial court was directed to decide the case expeditiously within six weeks. However, no order as to cost was given.

The dismissal of the petition by the High Court of Madhya Pradesh highlights the importance of timely filing of applications and the need to be diligent in contesting cases. The court's decision also emphasizes the need to respect the discretion of the trial court and not question it without valid reasons.
