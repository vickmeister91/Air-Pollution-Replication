The court recently granted anticipatory bail to Surendra Prasad, who was facing arrest in connection with a forest offence case. The petitioner was accused of committing a forest offence with respect to the construction of a road. The court allowed the application and directed the petitioner to surrender in the court within three weeks. On such surrender, he shall be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Dumka in connection with O.C.R. Case No. 289 of 2013 (T.R. No. 1394 of 2015), subject to the conditions as laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

The case in question pertains to an alleged forest offence committed by the petitioner in connection with the construction of a road. The petitioner had approached the court seeking anticipatory bail as he was apprehending his arrest in the case. The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, allowed the application and granted anticipatory bail to the petitioner.

The court directed the petitioner to surrender in the court within three weeks and on such surrender, he shall be released on bail on furnishing bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Dumka in connection with O.C.R. Case No. 289 of 2013 (T.R. No. 1394 of 2015), subject to the conditions as laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

The conditions laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure include that the petitioner shall make himself available for interrogation by a police officer as and when required, that the petitioner shall not, directly or indirectly, make any inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the court or to any police officer, and that the petitioner shall not leave India without the previous permission of the court.

The court also directed the petitioner to cooperate with the investigating agency and to attend the court on all the dates of hearing. The court further observed that the grant of anticipatory bail should not be construed as an expression of opinion on the merits of the case and that the trial court shall be free to decide the case on its own merits.
