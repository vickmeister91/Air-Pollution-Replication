The Andhra High Court received a letter from a practicing advocate in Vijayawada City regarding noise pollution caused by the use of air horns by buses and lorries. The court found that noise pollution existed in the city and that the A.P. Pollution Control Board had admitted to heavy noise pollution caused by air horns. The court cited rules for regulation and control of noise pollution and noted that the Governor of Andhra Pradesh had made rules regarding sound signals and restrictions on their use. The court emphasized the serious implications of noise pollution on health and established that it is a form of environmental degradation.

The court order discusses the issue of noise pollution and its harmful effects on human health, including increased heart rate and respiratory rates, hearing impairment, and chronic conditions such as hypertension and ulcers. The court cites various studies and medical evidence to support its findings. The order also highlights the right of citizens to a peaceful environment and the need for appropriate measures to control noise pollution, including the prohibition of multi-toned horns and other devices that create unduly harsh, shrill, loud, or alarming noise. The court directs the Commissioner of Police to take appropriate action to enforce the rules and guidelines issued by the A.P. Pollution Control Board and to ensure compliance with the regulations.

The court's order is a significant step towards addressing the issue of noise pollution in Andhra Pradesh. The court's recognition of the harmful effects of noise pollution on human health is an important acknowledgement of the seriousness of the issue. The court's emphasis on the need for appropriate measures to control noise pollution is also a welcome development.
