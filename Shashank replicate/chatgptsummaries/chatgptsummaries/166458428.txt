The Supreme Court of India recently acquitted Yogender Singh of violating Rule 7 of Delhi Motor Vehicle Rules and Sections 177 and 66.1/192A of the Motor Vehicles Act. The prosecution had alleged that Singh was driving a bus without wearing the prescribed uniform and overtook another commercial vehicle from the right side, violating the conditions of permit laid down by the Supreme Court of India. However, the court found that the prosecution failed to prove the charges beyond reasonable doubt, as there was no evidence of the alleged overtaking and no proof of the accused driving without a uniform. Therefore, the accused was acquitted of all charges.

The court order pertains to a traffic violation case where the accused was charged with overtaking another vehicle and driving without a uniform. The prosecution presented two witnesses who testified in support of the charges, but the defense argued that there were contradictions in their testimonies and that other witnesses should have been called. The court heard evidence from two witnesses and recorded Singh's statement, in which he denied overtaking any vehicle and claimed he had to move his vehicle to the right side of a stationary truck. The court found that the prosecution failed to prove the charges beyond reasonable doubt, as there was no evidence of the alleged overtaking and no proof of the accused driving without a uniform. Therefore, the accused was acquitted of all charges.
