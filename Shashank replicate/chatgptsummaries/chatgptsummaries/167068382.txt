The court order pertains to appeals filed by Vinod Kapoor against the orders of the Bombay High Court at Goa dismissing Civil Writ Petition No. 253 of 1999 and Civil Review Petition No. 17 of 2000. The case involves allegations that respondent no. 8 had constructed a residential bungalow without the prior permission of the Authority and in breach of the Coastal Regulation Zone notification. The court ruled that the appeals were not maintainable as the appellant had earlier challenged the same orders before the court and had withdrawn them with permission to pursue a remedy by way of review before the High Court. The court did not express any opinion on the merits of the case.

The court order states that the case is not maintainable and is dismissed. However, the court has not expressed any opinion on the merits of the case or whether the Authority or the Municipal Council had the legal right to issue notices to the respondent or take any action against the construction made by him on the land in question. The order is signed by two judges and dated October 3, 2012, in New Delhi.
