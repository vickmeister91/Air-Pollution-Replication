The case in question involves a writ petition filed by a petitioner who runs an engineering workshop. The petitioner received a notice from the Senior Environmental Engineer stating that the workshop was being conducted without obtaining the necessary consent to operate and without setting up the necessary machinery for controlling pollution. The petitioner was asked to suspend the functioning of the workshop until he obtains the requisite consent to operate from the Pollution Control Board.

The petitioner approached the court seeking relief from the notice issued by the Senior Environmental Engineer. However, the court did not find any reason to interfere with the notice as it was issued in tune with the statutory requirements. The court directed the 1st respondent to consider the application submitted by the petitioner for getting consent to operate the engineering workshop and pass appropriate orders thereon expeditiously and in accordance with the law after conducting a proper inspection regarding the remedial steps taken by the petitioner to rectify the defects.

The court's decision was based on the fact that the notice issued by the Senior Environmental Engineer was in accordance with the statutory requirements. The court also noted that the petitioner had not obtained the necessary consent to operate the workshop and had not set up the necessary machinery for controlling pollution. Therefore, the court directed the 1st respondent to consider the application submitted by the petitioner for getting consent to operate the engineering workshop and pass appropriate orders thereon expeditiously and in accordance with the law.

The court's decision is significant as it highlights the importance of complying with statutory requirements when operating a business. The court's decision also emphasizes the need for businesses to take steps to control pollution and obtain the necessary consent to operate from the Pollution Control Board.

In conclusion, the court's decision in this case serves as a reminder to businesses to comply with statutory requirements and take steps to control pollution. The court's decision also highlights the importance of obtaining the necessary consent to operate from the Pollution Control Board.
