The court order in question pertains to a case filed by Tarun Gulati against Rachna Gulati. The issue at hand is whether the trial court was right in dismissing an application under section 91 Cr.P.C. seeking direction to Vodafone Mobile Services Ltd. and Bharti Airtel Corporate to preserve and provide the call and message details of the complainant/respondent for a specific period.

The appellant, Tarun Gulati, had filed an application under section 91 Cr.P.C. seeking direction to Vodafone Mobile Services Ltd. and Bharti Airtel Corporate to preserve and provide the call and message details of the respondent, Rachna Gulati, for a specific period. The application was dismissed by the trial court, and the appellant filed an appeal against the order.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, directed the service providers to preserve the incoming and outgoing call details of the mobile numbers in question for the specified period. The court held that the appellant had a right to access the call and message details of the respondent, and the trial court had erred in dismissing the application under section 91 Cr.P.C.

The court observed that the preservation of call and message details was necessary for the proper adjudication of the case. The court noted that the call and message details would provide crucial evidence in determining the nature of the relationship between the parties and the events leading up to the filing of the case.

The court also set aside the impugned order and allowed the appeal. The court held that the trial court had erred in dismissing the application under section 91 Cr.P.C. and directed the service providers to preserve the call and message details of the mobile numbers in question.
