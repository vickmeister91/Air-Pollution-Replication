The Indian Forest Act was enacted in 1927 to consolidate the law relating to forests, the transit of forest produce, and the duty leviable on timber and other forest produce. Section 33 of the Act deals with the power of the forest officer to arrest without a warrant. This section states that any forest officer who has reason to believe that any person has committed an offense punishable under this Act or any rule made thereunder may arrest such person without a warrant.

Section 63 of the Indian Forest Act deals with the punishment for offenses under the Act. This section states that any person who contravenes any of the provisions of this Act or any rule made thereunder shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to six months or with fine which may extend to five hundred rupees, or with both.
