The court order in question pertains to a case where the petitioner had challenged the notification of the Ministry of Environment and Forest (MoEF) regarding the designation of an Eco Sensitive Zone (ESZ) in the lower side of the Mount Abu area. The Union of India had submitted that a draft notification had been published and the final notification would be issued after 120 days. 

The court directed the State to issue appropriate directives to ensure that all activities within the zone conform to the guidelines issued by the MoEF. This is a significant development as it ensures that the ecological balance of the area is maintained and that the interests of the local population are protected. The court's decision is in line with the principles of sustainable development and environmental protection.

The Union of India was also asked to file an affidavit indicating the number of objections received. This is an important step as it ensures that the concerns of all stakeholders are taken into account before a final decision is made. The court's decision to seek this information is a positive development as it ensures that the decision-making process is transparent and accountable.

The case was listed for hearing on 24th September, 2019.
