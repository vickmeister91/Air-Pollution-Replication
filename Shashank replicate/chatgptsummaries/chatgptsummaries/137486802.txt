The case in question involves a complaint filed by the applicant against the respondent under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act. The applicant had alleged that the respondent had issued a cheque that was dishonoured due to insufficient funds. The applicant had moved the complaint before the JMFC, Burhanpur, and the case was registered as Complaint Case No. 1716/2006.

During the course of the trial, the respondent had filed an application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C, seeking the production of certain documents. The JMFC, Burhanpur, had rejected the application, and the respondent had filed a revision petition before the Additional Sessions Judge, Burhanpur. The Additional Sessions Judge had allowed the revision petition and set aside the order passed by the JMFC, Burhanpur.

The applicant had then filed a revision petition before the High Court, challenging the order passed by the Additional Sessions Judge, Burhanpur. The High Court, after considering the submissions made by the parties, directed the trial court to consider whether the applicant was residing at Village Basali during the relevant period.

The High Court also gave the applicant the right to rebut the document filed by the respondent and cross-examine the complainant. The High Court observed that the trial court had not considered the evidence on record and had rejected the application filed by the respondent under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C without giving any reasons.

The High Court further observed that the trial court had not considered the submissions made by the respondent and had rejected the application filed under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C in a mechanical manner. The High Court held that the order passed by the Additional Sessions Judge, Burhanpur, was erroneous and set aside the same.

The High Court also dismissed the revision petition filed by the applicant, as it found no merit in the same. The High Court observed that the applicant had not been able to show that the order passed by the Additional Sessions Judge, Burhanpur, was erroneous or illegal.

The order passed by the JMFC, Burhanpur, in Complaint Case No. 1716/2006 was set aside, and an application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C filed by the respondent was accepted. The court directed the trial court to consider whether the applicant was residing at Village Basali during the relevant period and gave the applicant the right to rebut the document filed by the respondent and cross-examine the complainant. The revision filed by the applicant was dismissed.
