The case of Krapal vs The State of Madhya Pradesh pertains to the conviction of the applicants for offences under the Wild Life Protection Act. The applicants were sentenced to 3 years of imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 10,000. In response, the applicants filed an application for suspension of sentence and grant of bail. The court allowed the application subject to the deposit of the fine amount and furnishing of personal and surety bonds.

While the offence was serious, it was not of a violent nature. Secondly, the court took into account the fact that the applicants had no prior criminal record. Thirdly, the court considered the fact that the applicants had cooperated with the investigation and had not attempted to flee from justice.

This was to ensure that the applicants did not abscond and that they remained available for trial.
