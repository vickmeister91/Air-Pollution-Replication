The Punjab State Board for the Prevention and Control of Water Pollution filed a complaint against Raja Ram Corn Products (Punjab) Pvt. Ltd. for violating the Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. The accused company was required to apply for consent from the Board to regulate the discharge of trade effluent in the sewer, which was granted subject to certain conditions. However, the company was found to have exceeded the permissible limit of pollution in the effluent. 

The accused filed an application for dismissal of the complaint, but the court found that the provisions of the Act had not been complied with and directed the trial magistrate to proceed against the accused according to law. The trial was to be conducted expeditiously due to the matter's public importance.

The Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to prevent and control water pollution in India. The Act requires industries to obtain consent from the State Pollution Control Board before discharging any effluent into the water bodies. The consent is granted subject to certain conditions, including the permissible limit of pollution in the effluent. The Act also provides for penalties and punishment for non-compliance.

In this case, Raja Ram Corn Products (Punjab) Pvt. Ltd. was found to have violated the Act by exceeding the permissible limit of pollution in the effluent. The company had obtained consent from the State Pollution Control Board, but it had not complied with the conditions of the consent. The Board filed a complaint against the company, and the court directed the trial magistrate to proceed against the accused according to law.

The court's decision is significant as it upholds the importance of complying with environmental laws and regulations. The Act aims to protect the environment and public health by preventing and controlling water pollution. Non-compliance with the Act can have severe consequences, including penalties and punishment.

The court's direction to conduct the trial expeditiously is also noteworthy. The matter's public importance requires a speedy resolution to ensure that the environment and public health are not further compromised.
