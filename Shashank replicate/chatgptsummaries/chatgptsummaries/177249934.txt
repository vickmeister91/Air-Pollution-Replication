The case of Thanaram vs. the learned Special Metropolitan Judge (N.I. Act Cases) No.2 Jodhpur Metropolitan in criminal original case No.104/2014 for the offence under Section 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act was brought before the court. The petitioner had issued a cheque for Rs. 8,00,000/- which was to be paid in lieu of the cheque. The petitioner filed a criminal misc. petition under Section 482 Cr.P.C. against an order passed by the learned Special Metropolitan Judge (N.I. Act Cases) No.2 Jodhpur Metropolitan in criminal original case No.104/2014 for the offence under Section 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act.

The court considered the aspects of the case and stated that the complainant had not stated in his complaint that the amount paid was through the bank transaction. The court dismissed the petition as there was no requirement which could be proved for invocation of Section 91 Cr.P.C. The court's decision was based on the fact that the complainant had not provided any evidence to support his claim that the payment was made through a bank transaction. Therefore, the court found that there was no requirement for the invocation of Section 91 Cr.P.C.

Therefore, the court found that there was no requirement for the invocation of Section 91 Cr.P.C. Section 91 Cr.P.C. deals with the production of documents or other things necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code.

In this case, the court found that there was no requirement for the production of any documents or other things under Section 91 Cr.P.C. as the complainant had not provided any evidence to support his claim that the payment was made through a bank transaction.
