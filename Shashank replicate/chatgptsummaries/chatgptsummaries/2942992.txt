The court order pertains to three anticipatory bail applications filed by Mahadeo Godse, Pramod Deshmukh, and Vaibhav Salunkhe in connection with a case registered against them under Section 379 r/w 34 of I.P.C. and under Section 8 and 15 of The Environment (Protection) Act, 1986. The FIR was lodged by police constable Dadasheb Bankar, who stated that on 29/11/2018, on prior information, the police party including present informant went towards river Yerala. While they were proceeding towards the river, they saw three tractors, two JCB machines and one jeep. They also saw some sand on the road. When the police party chased the vehicles, the JCB drivers and drivers of the tractors ran away. The police party chased them. One of them was caught. His name as Yuvraj Patil. On further inquiry with him, he told the police party that the tractors belonged to Mahadeo Godse who is the applicant in ABA No.102/2019 and Pramod Deshmukh who is the applicant in ABA No.103/2019. He also informed that one of the JCBs was owned by Vaibhav Salunkhe who is the applicant in ABA No.104/2019. Based on this, the FIR was lodged. The court found no prima facie material to connect the present applicants with the alleged offence and allowed the applications.
