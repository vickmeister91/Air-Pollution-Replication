In a criminal petition filed by Nawab Jad Saad Bin Jung, the court was asked to quash the chargesheet in C.C. No. 509/2009 on the file of the Principal Civil Judge, Junior Division JMFC at Kanakapura for the offences punishable under Section 9, 27, 28, 29, 31, 39 read with Section 51 of the Wild Life Protection Act and Section 24 of the Karnataka Forest Act. The petitioner argued that the proceedings did not constitute an offence under Section 9 of the Act as the postmortem report relied on by the prosecution specified a rabbit, which is not an animal specified in any of the schedules of the Wild Life Protection Act.

After considering the arguments put forth by the petitioner, the court allowed the petition and quashed the proceedings. The court held that the postmortem report relied on by the prosecution specified a rabbit, which is not an animal specified in any of the schedules of the Wild Life Protection Act. Therefore, the proceedings did not constitute an offence under Section 9 of the Act.

This decision by the court highlights the importance of following the provisions of the Wild Life Protection Act and the Karnataka Forest Act. It also emphasizes the need for proper investigation and evidence gathering in cases related to wildlife protection. The court's decision in this case serves as a reminder to law enforcement agencies to ensure that they have sufficient evidence before proceeding with charges under the Wild Life Protection Act and the Karnataka Forest Act.
