The court order pertains to a writ petition filed by Siciliamma Joseph, a headmistress of a school in Kerala, challenging an order that held her liable for the recovery of an amount due to staff fixation revision. The petitioner had contended that the revision was made due to bogus admissions and attendance, and that she should not be held liable for the same. However, the court examined the case and found that the revision was indeed made due to bogus admissions and attendance, and upheld the order.

The court also dismissed the petitioner's alternate contentions regarding the accommodation of excess teachers and protection under 1:40 ratio, as they did not address the issue of bogus admissions. The court stated that subsequent representation made before the Deputy Director is of no consequence in deciding the division fall and the issue of liability being attempted to be recovered from the Headmistress. The court also mentioned that the protection including 1:40 ratio would not militate against the recovery proceedings.

The petitioner's prayers made before the Deputy Director cannot absolve the liability for having procured a staff fixation based on bogus admissions. The court also mentioned that no disciplinary proceedings were initiated against the petitioner and since the petitioner has already retired, no proceedings can be taken against her.

The court allowed the writ petition declaring that the petitioner cannot be proceeded with for recovery of liability after her retirement.
