In a recent court order, a petitioner had challenged a condition imposed by the court for the custody of a jeep that was seized for carrying fishes in violation of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972. The petitioner had applied for custody of the jeep, but the court had imposed a condition of furnishing a bank guarantee of Rs. 40,000. The petitioner argued that there was no provision for furnishing a bank guarantee under the Act and challenged the condition.

However, the court held that the forest officers had the power to give custody of captive or wild animals, but not vehicles. The court further stated that the condition of furnishing a bank guarantee was justified to ensure the preservation and protection of wild animals. The court dismissed the petition and upheld the condition of furnishing a bank guarantee.
