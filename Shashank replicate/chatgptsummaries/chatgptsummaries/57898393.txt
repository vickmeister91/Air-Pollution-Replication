In a recent court order, the petitioner, Neeran Singh, filed a petition under Section 482Cr.P.C. to set aside the order passed by the Court of Judicial Magistrate, Rajakhera District Dholpur, which refused to release his tractor and trolley. The petitioner's counsel argued that the tractor and trolley were registered in his name and no confiscation proceedings were pending. The court allowed the petition and directed the trial court to release the tractor and trolley, imposing certain conditions. The trial court was also directed to verify that the petitioner was the registered owner of the tractor and trolley.

The case revolves around the ownership of a tractor and trolley that were seized by the police during an investigation. The petitioner, Neeran Singh, claimed that he was the registered owner of the tractor and trolley and that there were no confiscation proceedings pending against him. However, the Court of Judicial Magistrate, Rajakhera District Dholpur, refused to release the tractor and trolley, citing the ongoing investigation.

In response, the petitioner filed a petition under Section 482Cr.P.C. to set aside the order passed by the Court of Judicial Magistrate. The petitioner's counsel argued that the tractor and trolley were registered in his name and that there were no confiscation proceedings pending against him. The counsel further argued that the continued detention of the tractor and trolley was causing undue hardship to the petitioner, who relied on them for his livelihood.

After hearing the arguments of both parties, the court allowed the petition and directed the trial court to release the tractor and trolley.
