Krishna Rao had filed a criminal petition under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, challenging the order passed by the XII Additional Chief Metropolitan Magistrate, at Bengaluru, in C.C.No.19836/2014. The order in question had dismissed the application filed by the petitioner/accused under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The petitioner had sought to call for the Bank statement relating to Account No.10838617165. 

The court allowed the petition partly and directed the learned Trial Judge to mark in evidence the certified copy of the Bank statement produced by the complainant relating to his Bank account referred to above as exhibit for the complainant. This order was passed after considering the arguments put forth by both the parties and examining the relevant provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure empowers the High Court to exercise its inherent powers to prevent abuse of the process of any court or to secure the ends of justice. The petitioner had invoked this section to challenge the order passed by the XII Additional Chief Metropolitan Magistrate, at Bengaluru, in C.C.No.19836/2014. The order in question had dismissed the application filed by the petitioner/accused under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure deals with the power of the court to issue summons or order for the production of any document or other thing necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. The petitioner had sought to call for the Bank statement relating to Account No.10838617165 under this section.

The court, after considering the arguments put forth by both the parties, observed that the Bank statement sought by the petitioner was relevant to the case and could be used as evidence. However, the court also noted that the complainant had already produced a certified copy of the Bank statement in question. Therefore, the court allowed the petition partly and directed the learned Trial Judge to mark in evidence the certified copy of the Bank statement produced by the complainant relating to his Bank account referred to above as exhibit for the complainant.
