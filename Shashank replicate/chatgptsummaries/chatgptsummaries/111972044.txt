This section lays down the conditions for granting anticipatory bail, which include that the person must cooperate with the investigation, not tamper with evidence, and not commit any further offenses while on bail.

The court's decision to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioner was based on several factors. Secondly, the court may have taken into account the fact that there had been previous litigation on the matter. This suggests that the issue of the land's status has been a point of contention for some time, and that there may be differing opinions on the matter.

Finally, the court's decision to grant anticipatory bail was likely influenced by the fact that the petitioner had agreed to follow the conditions laid down under section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure. By agreeing to these conditions, the petitioner has demonstrated a willingness to cooperate with the investigation and to abide by the law.

Overall, the court's decision to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioner in this case appears to have been based on a careful consideration of the facts and circumstances of the case.
