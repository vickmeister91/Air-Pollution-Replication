The court order in question pertains to a case filed by Nankoo @ Salamatulla & Ors. against the State of U.P. & Anr. The applicants have filed an application under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure seeking the quashing of the charge sheet arising out of Case Crime No. 1183/2015 under Sections 9/51 Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972, Police Station- Ranipur, District- Bahraich.

The applicants have argued that the charge sheet filed against them is illegal and without jurisdiction. They have also contended that the investigation conducted by the forest department officials was biased and that they were falsely implicated in the case.

After hearing the arguments of both parties, the court has directed that if the applicants surrender before the court below and apply for bail within two weeks, their prayer for bail shall be considered as early as possible, keeping in view the settled law laid down by the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India.
