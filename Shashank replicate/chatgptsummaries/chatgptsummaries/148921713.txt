The Madras High Court recently dismissed a writ petition filed by K. Tamilarasi against the District Environmental Engineer, Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board, and others. The petitioner had sought a writ of certiorified mandamus to quash a communication requesting disconnection of electricity supply for alleged violations of pollution control acts and to grant consent for carrying on the activity of spinning mill to manufacture cotton yarn. However, the court allowed the petitioner to withdraw the writ petition, and it was dismissed as withdrawn with no costs.

The court order lists the names and addresses of four individuals and organizations, including the District Environmental Engineer and the District Collector of Tiruppur, Tamil Nadu. The order is related to a case with the reference number W.P.No.2679 of 2020 and includes two additional reference numbers for WMP.Nos.3103 and 3104 of 2020. The date of the order is February 5, 2020.

The dismissal of the writ petition by the Madras High Court is significant as it highlights the importance of complying with pollution control acts and regulations. The petitioner had sought relief from the court to continue operating a spinning mill to manufacture cotton yarn despite alleged violations of pollution control acts. However, the court did not grant the relief sought and instead allowed the petitioner to withdraw the writ petition.
