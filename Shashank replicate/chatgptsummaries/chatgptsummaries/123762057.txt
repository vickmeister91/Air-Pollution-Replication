The Madras High Court recently dismissed a writ petition filed by M/s.Geetha Traders seeking a writ of mandamus to direct the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board to grant necessary orders of consent to the petitioner unit under the provisions of Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 and Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. The court stated that the plea for which consent was sought had expired long ago and the dismissal of the writ petition would not prevent the petitioner from filing a fresh application if desired.

The petitioner, M/s.Geetha Traders, had filed a writ petition seeking a writ of mandamus to direct the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board to grant necessary orders of consent to the petitioner unit under the provisions of Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 and Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. The petitioner had claimed that they had applied for the necessary orders of consent in 2015, but the same had not been granted by the Board.

The court, however, dismissed the writ petition stating that the plea for which consent was sought had expired long ago. The court further stated that the dismissal of the writ petition would not prevent the petitioner from filing a fresh application if desired. The court also observed that the petitioner had not approached the Board for renewal of the consent orders and had instead approached the court seeking a writ of mandamus.

The court's decision is in line with the provisions of the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 and Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, which require industries to obtain necessary orders of consent from the Pollution Control Board before commencing operations. The Acts also require industries to renew their consent orders periodically.

The dismissal of the writ petition by the Madras High Court highlights the importance of complying with the provisions of the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 and Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. Industries must obtain necessary orders of consent from the Pollution Control Board before commencing operations and must renew their consent orders periodically. Failure to comply with these provisions can result in legal action being taken against the industry.
