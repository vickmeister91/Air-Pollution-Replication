The court order in question was issued in response to a writ petition filed by Prabhdayal Singh Randhawa regarding the non-introduction of Environmental Education in schools and educational institutions in Punjab. The petitioner had raised concerns about the lack of awareness and education among students regarding environmental issues and the need for sustainable development. The court had issued a direction to the State of Punjab to take necessary action in this regard.

In response to the court's direction, the Punjab State Board of Technical Education and Industrial Training filed an affidavit stating that they have made Environmental Education a compulsory subject in all technical diploma courses. The board also informed the court that they have taken steps to ensure that the subject is taught effectively and that students are made aware of the importance of environmental conservation and sustainable development.

The court was satisfied with the board's response and disposed of the petition as the petitioner's grievance had been addressed. The court's decision is a significant step towards promoting environmental education and awareness among students in Punjab.
