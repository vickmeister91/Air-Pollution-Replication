The court order in question pertains to a petition filed under Section 482 Cr.P.C. The petitioner had sought to set aside an order passed by the Judicial Magistrate, Rajakheda, Dholpur, which had refused to release a tractor bearing registration number RJ 11 R7601 to the petitioner. The tractor had been seized as case property in FIR No. 233/2018, registered at Police Station Rajakheda, Dholpur, for offences under Section 379 IPC and Section 29, 51 of Wild Life (Protection) Act and Sections 41 and 42 of Rajasthan Forest Act.

The petitioner had argued that the tractor was not involved in the commission of the alleged offences and that it was being used for agricultural purposes. The petitioner had also submitted that the tractor was the only source of livelihood for the petitioner and his family and that its continued detention was causing immense hardship to them.

After hearing the arguments of both parties, the court allowed the petition and directed the trial court to release the tractor seized as case property by imposing certain conditions. The court noted that the tractor was not involved in the commission of the alleged offences and that its continued detention was causing undue hardship to the petitioner and his family.

The court directed the trial court to release the tractor to the petitioner on the condition that the petitioner furnishes a personal bond in the sum of Rs. 50,000 with one surety of like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court. The court also directed the petitioner to deposit a sum of Rs. 25,000 with the trial court as a security deposit.

The court further directed the petitioner to produce the tractor before the trial court whenever required and to cooperate with the investigation. The court also directed the trial court to ensure that the tractor is not used for any illegal activities and that it is used only for agricultural purposes.
