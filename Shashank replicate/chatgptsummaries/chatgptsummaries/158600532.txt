The court orders discussed in this summary pertain to two separate cases in Himachal Pradesh, India. The first case involves a challenge by M/s Misty Woods Hotel against a notice issued by the Himachal Pradesh State Pollution Control Board. The second case involves a violation of municipal laws and construction beyond the sanctioned plan.

In the first case, the court directed the petitioner, M/s Misty Woods Hotel, to file an affidavit stating the extent of construction permissible as per the sanctioned plan, the extent of construction actually carried out, deviation made in actual construction, the extent of construction which is otherwise compoundable, and an undertaking to demolish the construction raised in excess of the sanctioned plans and beyond compoundable limits. The court also directed the petitioner to comply with all other statutory rules/regulations/bye-laws under the provisions of the Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The court further directed that no coercive action shall be taken till the next date of hearing.

This order highlights the importance of adhering to sanctioned plans and following all statutory rules and regulations related to pollution control. The court's directive to file an affidavit and undertake to demolish any construction raised in excess of sanctioned plans and beyond compoundable limits emphasizes the need for strict compliance with regulations to prevent environmental damage.

In the second case, the petitioner had raised construction to the extent of 461.35 square meters, which is beyond the compoundable limits stipulated under the provisions of the H.P. Municipal Act, 1994 and Town and Country Planning Act, 1977. The court dismissed the writ petition with the petitioner's undertaking to be enforced in accordance with the law. The petitioner was also asked to deposit a sum of `2.00 lac as costs to be deposited with the H.P. State Legal Services Authority.

This order highlights the consequences of violating municipal laws and building beyond sanctioned plans. The court's dismissal of the writ petition and requirement for the petitioner to deposit a sum of money as costs emphasizes the seriousness of such violations and the need for strict enforcement of laws and regulations related to construction.

Overall, these court orders emphasize the importance of adhering to sanctioned plans and following all statutory rules and regulations related to pollution control and construction in Himachal Pradesh. The court's directives and requirements for affidavits and undertakings, as well as the consequences for violations, highlight the need for strict compliance with regulations to prevent environmental damage and ensure safe and legal construction practices.
