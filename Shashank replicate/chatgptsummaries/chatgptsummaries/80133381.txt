The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Dinesh Singh and Umesh Singh under Section 439 of the Cr.P.C. for an offence punishable under sections 9, 39, 51 read with section 52 of Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The allegation against the applicants is that they, along with other co-accused, were found in possession of meat of wild pig. The court allowed the bail application on strict terms without commenting on the merits of the case.

The applicants were ordered to furnish a personal bond of Rs.50,000/- each with one solvent surety each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court for securing their presence before the said Court on all the dates of hearing fixed in this regard during trial. The order also listed eight conditions that the applicants must comply with, failing which the bail would be cancelled.

The first condition listed in the order is that the applicants shall not directly or indirectly make any inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the court or to any police officer. The second condition is that the applicants shall not leave India without the prior permission of the court. The third condition is that the applicants shall surrender their passports, if any, to the court within a week of their release on bail.

The fourth condition is that the applicants shall not tamper with the evidence or attempt to influence or intimidate the witnesses. The fifth condition is that the applicants shall attend the trial court on all the dates of hearing fixed in this regard. The sixth condition is that the applicants shall not commit any offence while on bail.

The seventh condition is that the applicants shall furnish their residential address and mobile number to the investigating officer and the trial court and shall keep the investigating officer and the trial court informed of any change in their residential address or mobile number. The eighth and final condition is that the applicants shall not directly or indirectly make any contact with the complainant or the witnesses in the case.

The court order is significant as it highlights the strict conditions that are imposed on individuals who are granted bail in cases related to wildlife protection. The order makes it clear that the court takes such cases seriously and expects the accused to comply with all the conditions listed in the order. Failure to comply with any of the conditions can result in the bail being cancelled and the accused being taken into custody.

It is important to note that the court did not comment on the merits of the case while granting bail to the applicants. This means that the court did not express any opinion on whether the applicants were guilty or not guilty of the offence they were charged with. The court simply granted bail on strict terms and conditions.
