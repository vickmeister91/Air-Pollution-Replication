The learned trial court had dismissed the application on the grounds that the documents sought were not relevant to the case and that the petitioner had not made out a case for their production.

The court found the reasons recorded by the learned trial court in support of the impugned order unsatisfactory and improper. The court observed that the documents sought by the petitioner were relevant to the case and that the petitioner had made out a case for their production. The court also observed that the learned trial court had not considered the petitioner's arguments properly and had dismissed the application without proper reasoning.

The court directed the learned trial court to pass a fresh order on the application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. in the light of the observations made by this Court in the present order. The court also directed the Narcotic Central Bureau to produce the documents sought by the petitioner within a period of two weeks from the date of the order.

The court's order is significant as it upholds the petitioner's right to seek the production of relevant documents in a criminal case. The court's observations on the learned trial court's reasoning also highlight the importance of proper reasoning in judicial decisions.
