Ram Kumar, who had been in custody since February 6, 2017, filed a bail application under Section 439 of the Cr.P.C. Kumar had been arrested for hunting a spotted deer (Cheetal) by spreading naked G.I. wire and connecting it with live electricity. His co-accused, Vishal Singh, was arrested on the spot, and from his possession, the dead body of the spotted deer, G.I. wire, and a small piece of wooden pole were seized.

The court allowed the bail application and directed the applicant to furnish a bail bond of Rs.25,000/- with one surety of the same amount to the satisfaction of the JMFC concerned or trial Court for his appearance before them on the dates given by the concerned Court.

In another case, the Delhi High Court granted bail to a man accused of raping a minor girl. The accused, who was a friend of the victim's brother, had allegedly raped the girl on multiple occasions. The court noted that the accused had been in custody for over a year and that the trial was unlikely to conclude anytime soon. The court also observed that the accused had no criminal antecedents and that there was no evidence to suggest that he was likely to abscond or tamper with evidence.

The court granted bail to the accused on the condition that he would not leave the jurisdiction of the court without prior permission and that he would cooperate with the investigation. The court also directed the accused to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 and two sureties of the same amount.

In a third case, the Madras High Court granted anticipatory bail to a man accused of cheating and forgery. The accused had allegedly taken a loan from the complainant and had given him a cheque in return. However, the cheque was dishonoured, and the complainant filed a case against the accused.

The court noted that the accused had cooperated with the investigation and had already repaid a substantial amount of the loan. The court also observed that the accused had no criminal antecedents and that he was not likely to abscond or tamper with evidence.

The court granted anticipatory bail to the accused on the condition that he would cooperate with the investigation and appear before the investigating officer as and when required. The court also directed the accused to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 and two sureties of the same amount.
