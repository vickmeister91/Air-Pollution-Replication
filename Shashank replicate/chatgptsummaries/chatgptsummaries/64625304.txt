The court order pertains to an application for condonation of delay in filing an appeal against acquittal in a case related to offences under Sections 120-B and 379 of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The non-applicants were acquitted, but there was a delay of 62 days in filing the appeal. The court allowed the application and directed the office to place the appeal for admission after two weeks. The criminal application was disposed of accordingly.

The Act provides for the protection of wild animals, birds, and plants, and also regulates hunting, poaching, and trade in wildlife and their products. The Act also provides for the establishment of national parks, sanctuaries, and other protected areas for the conservation of wildlife.

The non-applicants had been acquitted, but the applicants had filed an appeal against the acquittal. However, there was a delay of 62 days in filing the appeal, which the applicants sought to condone.

The court also noted that the delay was not intentional or deliberate, and that the applicants had acted in good faith.
