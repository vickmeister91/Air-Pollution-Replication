The court order in question pertains to a case where the petitioners are facing allegations of obstructing forest officials from performing their duty of detecting offenses punishable under the Wild Life (Protection) Act. The petitioners had moved the bail bench for anticipatory bail, which was dismissed. They then approached the court to quash the FIR registered against them, citing that the forest officials did not have jurisdiction to take action under the Act. However, the court dismissed their plea, stating that the police must conduct a fair and efficient investigation into the crime, and the petitioners can seek regular bail after surrendering before the Investigating Officer or the learned Magistrate.
