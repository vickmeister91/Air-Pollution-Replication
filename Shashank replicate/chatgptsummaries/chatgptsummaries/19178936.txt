The case in question involves a petitioner who filed a petition under Section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code to set aside an order passed by the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Madurai. The petitioner was facing charges under Sections 406 and 420 IPC and requested that certain documents, such as passbooks, vouchers, account books, and other office records, which were available in the premises while he was in possession as a tenant, be produced before the Court below.

However, the Trial Court dismissed the petition on the ground that it was premature. The Court found that the petitioner was not entitled to summon the documents from the custody of respondent police, and the order passed by the Trial Court was valid. Consequently, the Criminal Original Petition was dismissed as devoid of merits.

However, the Court found that the petitioner had not made a proper case for the production of the documents. The Court also noted that the petitioner had not made any effort to obtain the documents from the police or any other source.

The Court further observed that the petitioner had not shown that the documents were in the custody of the police or that they were relevant to the case. The Court also noted that the petitioner had not shown that the documents were not available from any other source.

The Court held that the petitioner was not entitled to summon the documents from the custody of the police and that the Trial Court's order was valid. The Court also observed that the petitioner had not made a proper case for the production of the documents and that the Criminal Original Petition was devoid of merits.
