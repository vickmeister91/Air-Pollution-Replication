The court order in question pertains to a revision filed by T.N. Bhardwaj against the order of the trial court dismissing his application under Section 319 of Cr.P.C. to implead some other person as an accused, his application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. for appropriate direction to some authority to produce the documents, and his application filed under Section 228 of the Cr.P.C. to discharge him from the alleged offences.

The court has permitted the applicant to withdraw the revision till the extent of the part of the impugned order whereby his application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. has been dismissed with liberty to file the appropriate petition under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. for quashment of such part of the impugned order. The revision shall proceed further for the other part of the impugned order.

Section 319 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.) deals with the power of the court to proceed against other persons appearing to be guilty of the offence. It empowers the court to proceed against any person who is not an accused in the case, but whose presence is necessary for the proper adjudication of the case. In this case, T.N. Bhardwaj had filed an application under Section 319 of Cr.P.C. to implead some other person as an accused. However, the trial court had dismissed his application.

Section 91 of Cr.P.C. deals with the power of the court to issue a summons or order to produce a document or thing. It empowers the court to direct any person, including a public officer, to produce any document or thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. In this case, T.N. Bhardwaj had filed an application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. for appropriate direction to some authority to produce the documents. However, the trial court had dismissed his application.

Section 228 of Cr.P.C. deals with the power of the court to discharge an accused. It empowers the court to discharge the accused if, after considering the record of the case and the documents submitted therewith, and after hearing the submissions of the accused and the prosecution, the court is of the opinion that there is no sufficient ground for proceeding against the accused. In this case, T.N. Bhardwaj had filed an application under Section 228 of Cr.P.C. to discharge him from the alleged offences. However, the trial court had dismissed his application.

The revision filed by T.N. Bhardwaj against the order of the trial court had been dismissed. However, the court has permitted the applicant to withdraw the revision till the extent of the part of the impugned order whereby his application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. has been dismissed with liberty to file the appropriate petition under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. for quashment of such part of the impugned order. The revision shall proceed further for the other part of the impugned order.

Section 482 of Cr.P.C. deals with the inherent powers of the High Court. It empowers the High Court to make such orders as may be necessary to give effect to any order under the Code, or to prevent abuse of the process of any court or otherwise to secure the ends of justice. In this case, T.N.
