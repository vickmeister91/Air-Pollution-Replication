In a recent case registered under the Wild Life Protection Act, the court has allowed the first application for anticipatory bail filed by the accused. The accused were alleged to have illegally hunted wild boar. The court has directed the accused to surrender before the Arresting Authority/Investigating Officer within 15 days and they shall be released on bail on furnishing a personal bond of Rs.50,000 each with one solvent surety each in the like amount. The accused shall make themselves available for interrogation before a Police Officer as and when required and abide by the other conditions enumerated in sub-section (2) of Section 438 of Cr.P.C.

The Wild Life Protection Act is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to protect the wildlife of India. The act prohibits hunting, poaching, and trading of wild animals and their products. The act also provides for the establishment of protected areas, national parks, and wildlife sanctuaries. The act has been instrumental in protecting the wildlife of India, which is home to a diverse range of species.
