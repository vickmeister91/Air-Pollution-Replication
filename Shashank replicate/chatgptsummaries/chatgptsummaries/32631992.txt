Adrash Credit Co-Operative Society Limited has filed multiple cases against individuals in Rajasthan for non-payment of loans. The cases are being heard by the High Court of Judicature for Rajasthan in Jodhpur. The petitioners are seeking relief from the court, and each case is being heard separately. The Adrash Credit Co-Operative Society Limited has also filed multiple criminal misc petitions against various individuals in the State of Rajasthan. The respondents in each case are either the State of Rajasthan or specific individuals accused of committing offenses against the petitioner. The petitions were filed through authorized officers and managers of the petitioner, and the court will hear each case separately.

In addition, the Adrash Credit Co-Operative Society Limited has filed multiple petitions seeking to quash orders passed by the trial court and the learned Judge of the Special Court, SC/ST (Atrocities) Cases, Sirohi, Rajasthan. The petitioner-Society had initiated proceedings under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act against a private respondent who had issued a dishonoured cheque. During trial, the petitioner moved an application under Section 311 Cr.P.C., seeking relief to permit to take on record Letter of Authorization. The learned Magistrate rejected the application, and the learned revisional court dismissed the revision stating it to be not maintainable. The petitioner-Society has relied upon the judgment of Hon`ble Supreme Court in M/s. Haryana State Co-operative Supply and Marketing Federation Ltd. Vs. M/s. Jayam Textiles & Anr., reported in AIR 2014 SC 1926.

The court has set aside the judgments of the lower courts and remitted the matter back to the trial court with a direction to conduct a fresh trial, taking into consideration the authorization placed before them. The court has allowed the petitioner to produce and prove the authorization before the trial court, as procedural defects and irregularities should not be allowed to defeat substantive rights or cause injustice. The court has allowed the document in question, the Letter of Authorization, to be taken on record and exhibited during the trial.
