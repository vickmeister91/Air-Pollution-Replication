The Karnataka State Pollution Control Board was accused of violating the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 by commencing construction of a residential complex without obtaining clearance from the Ministry of Environment and Forest, Government of India, New Delhi. M/s. Keppel Puravankara Development (P) Ltd. filed a criminal petition against the board, and the case was heard in court. 

After hearing both sides, the court found that the petitioner had subsequently obtained the necessary clearance and directed them to pay a fine of Rs. 1,00,000/- within four weeks from the date of the order. The court also stated that upon payment of the fine, the proceedings against the petitioner would be closed. 

The court order is significant as it highlights the importance of obtaining clearance from the Ministry of Environment and Forest before commencing construction of any project. The Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 was enacted to protect and improve the environment and to prevent hazards to human beings, other living creatures, plants and property. The act requires that any person or organization that wishes to undertake any activity that may have an adverse impact on the environment must obtain clearance from the Ministry of Environment and Forest. 

The court order also emphasizes the need for strict adherence to environmental laws and regulations. Violations of environmental laws can have serious consequences, including fines, imprisonment, and damage to the environment. The court's decision to impose a fine on the petitioner serves as a warning to other organizations that may be tempted to violate environmental laws. 

In conclusion, the court order in the case of M/s. Keppel Puravankara Development (P) Ltd. vs. Karnataka State Pollution Control Board highlights the importance of obtaining clearance from the Ministry of Environment and Forest before commencing any construction project. The court's decision to impose a fine on the petitioner serves as a warning to other organizations that may be tempted to violate environmental laws. It is essential that all organizations and individuals adhere to environmental laws and regulations to protect the environment and prevent hazards to human beings, other living creatures, plants, and property.
