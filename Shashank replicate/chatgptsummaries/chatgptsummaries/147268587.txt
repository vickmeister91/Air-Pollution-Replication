The Jharkhand High Court recently granted anticipatory bail to Prakash Sahu and Anand Sahu, who were accused of running a crusher machine without a license and NOC from the Pollution Control Board, in violation of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1981 and various provisions of the Factories Act. The court directed the petitioners to surrender in the court below by May 13, 2014, and in that event, the court below was directed to enlarge the petitioners on bail on furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Seraikella in connection with Chowka P.S. Case No. 57 of 2013.

The case against Prakash Sahu and Anand Sahu was that they were running a crusher machine without a license and NOC from the Pollution Control Board, in violation of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1981 and various provisions of the Factories Act. The case was registered against them under Chowka P.S. Case No. 57 of 2013. The petitioners approached the Jharkhand High Court seeking anticipatory bail in the case.

The Jharkhand High Court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, granted anticipatory bail to the petitioners. The court directed the petitioners to surrender in the court below by May 13, 2014, and in that event, the court below was directed to enlarge the petitioners on bail on furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Seraikella.

The court observed that the petitioners were not involved in any other criminal case and that they were ready to cooperate with the investigation. The court also noted that the petitioners were not likely to abscond or tamper with the evidence. The court further observed that the allegations against the petitioners were not of a serious nature and that they were not likely to influence the witnesses or hamper the investigation.

The court also directed the petitioners to cooperate with the investigation and to appear before the investigating officer as and when required. The court also directed the investigating officer to complete the investigation within a period of three months from the date of the order.

The court's decision to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioners was based on the principles of justice, equity, and good conscience. The court observed that the petitioners were entitled to the benefit of anticipatory bail as they were not involved in any other criminal case and that they were ready to cooperate with the investigation.

Case No. 57 of 2013 was based on the principles of justice, equity, and good conscience. The court also directed the petitioners to surrender in the court below by May 13, 2014, and in that event, the court below was directed to enlarge the petitioners on bail on furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Seraikella. The court's decision was a balanced one, taking into account the interests of both the petitioners and the state.
