The Kerala High Court recently passed a judgment in a writ petition filed against a company carrying out industrial activities. The company had been issued a consent by the Kerala State Pollution Control Board (KSPCB), but the petitioners alleged that the inspection was not proper and that the company was situated within prohibited distance from their residences.

The court observed that the petitioners had not challenged the consent before the appellate authority and that there was no interim order granted by the court. The court ruled that the petitioners should have approached the appellate authority before approaching the court. The court also noted that the petitioners had not produced any material to show that the company was situated within the prohibited distance.

The court allowed the writ petition to be closed, leaving open the contentions of either party to be urged before the appropriate authority if necessary. The court also observed that the KSPCB had the power to revoke the consent if it was found that the company was violating the conditions of the consent.

The court's decision highlights the importance of following the proper legal procedures while challenging the actions of regulatory authorities.
