The court order in question pertains to a case where an applicant had moved an application for temporary custody of his vehicle, which had been seized for a forest crime. The applicant had challenged the order passed by the JMFC and the revision filed by the applicant was also dismissed by the court.

The court held that the provisions of confiscation in the Wild Life Protection Act are different from those in the Indian Forest Act. Therefore, the power of the Magistrate under section 451 of the Cr.P.C. shall not go away. The court further held that when the confiscation proceeding was initiated under the Indian Forest Act, then the Magistratrial Court had no power to entertain the application under Section 451 or 457 of the Cr.P.C.
