The case of Bhaira Ram involved a criminal misc. petition filed under Section 482 Cr.P.C. against the order passed by the learned Sessions Judge, Sirohi in Criminal Revision Petition No.90/2011. The petitioner was charged for the offences under Sections 9/51 of Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 and Section 4/25 of the Arms Act. The petitioner relied on the judgment passed in the matter of Nawab Jad Saad Bin Jung Vs. State of Karnataka passed by the Karnataka High Court. The petitioner's counsel pointed out that the present animal in question was a rabbit, which is not specified in any one of the schedules of Wild Life Protection Act. The court allowed the petition and quashed the orders passed by the learned courts below and discharged the petitioner from the alleged offences.
