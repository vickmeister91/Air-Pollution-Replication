The court order in question pertains to a challenge against a general search warrant issued by a Joint Civil Judge under Section 93 of the Code of Criminal Procedure against respondent No.1. The court finds that the judge committed grave illegality and patent jurisdictional error in passing the impugned order. The court explains that the pre-requisite for exercise of power under Section 93 is that the Magistrate should have reasons to believe that a person to whom summons under Section 91 has been issued or might be issued will not produce document or things required from him, then only a search warrant under this section can be issued.

The court finds that the impugned order is patently illegal and suffers from excessive exercise of jurisdiction, non-application of mind, and is a specimen of an order for which no reasons have been given. The revision is allowed, and the impugned order is set aside.

The court's decision is based on the fact that the judge who issued the search warrant did not have sufficient reason to believe that the person to whom the summons had been issued would not produce the documents or things required from him. The court finds that the judge's decision was based on an excessive exercise of jurisdiction and a lack of application of mind.
