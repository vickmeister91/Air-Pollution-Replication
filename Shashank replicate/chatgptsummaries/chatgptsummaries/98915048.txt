The court recently granted bail to Ganpat Lal Yadav, who was accused of grazing domestic animals in Kanha National Park, in connection with Forest Crime No. 3031/2009. The court ordered the applicant to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 with a solvent surety in the same amount to ensure his presence during trial and comply with the conditions of Section 437(3) of the Cr.P.C.

The case against Yadav was initiated in 2009 when he was accused of grazing domestic animals in the Kanha National Park, which is a protected area. The forest department filed a case against him under Forest Crime No. 3031/2009. Yadav was arrested and sent to judicial custody.

Yadav filed a bail application before the court, which was opposed by the forest department. The department argued that Yadav had committed a serious offence by grazing domestic animals in a protected area, and that he was likely to abscond if released on bail.

However, the court considered Yadav's arguments and granted him bail. The court observed that Yadav had been in custody for a considerable period of time, and that there was no evidence to suggest that he was likely to abscond or tamper with evidence. The court also noted that Yadav had no criminal antecedents and that he was a resident of the area.

The court imposed certain conditions on Yadav while granting him bail. Yadav was ordered to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 with a solvent surety in the same amount. He was also directed to ensure his presence during trial and comply with the conditions of Section 437(3) of the Cr.P.C. This section requires the accused to cooperate with the investigation, not to tamper with evidence, and to attend court on all hearing dates.

In conclusion, the court granted bail to Ganpat Lal Yadav, who was accused of grazing domestic animals in Kanha National Park, in connection with Forest Crime No. 3031/2009. The court imposed certain conditions on Yadav while granting him bail, including the requirement to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 with a solvent surety in the same amount and to comply with the conditions of Section 437(3) of the Cr.P.C. The court considered Yadav's arguments and observed that there was no evidence to suggest that he was likely to abscond or tamper with evidence. The court also noted that Yadav had no criminal antecedents and that he was a resident of the area.
