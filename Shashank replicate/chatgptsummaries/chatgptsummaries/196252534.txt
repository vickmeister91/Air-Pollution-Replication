The Telangana High Court recently heard a complaint from the Sarpanch of Dalemarajuvalasa Village regarding air pollution caused by brick kilns run by respondents 7 to 11. The court directed the petitioner to file an application under Section 133 Cr.P.C for abetment of nuisance before the 3rd respondent within four weeks, enclosing a copy of the court order. The 3rd respondent was directed to enquire into the matter, call for the assistance of Pollution Control Board if necessary, and take steps as necessary either for abetment or removal of nuisance within three months from the date of filing of the application. The kiln owners were to be given an opportunity to be heard before any orders were passed.

Section 133 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C) deals with the power of a Magistrate to take action against a public nuisance. The section empowers a Magistrate to take action against any person who causes or abets the commission of a public nuisance. The section also provides for the removal of the nuisance or the prevention of its recurrence.
