The State of Karnataka had filed an appeal against the acquittal of Venkatesha @ Jiya for an offence punishable under Section 87 of the Karnataka Forest Act and Section 379 IPC. However, the appellate court acquitted the accused of the aforementioned offences but confirmed the conviction for offences punishable under Sections 9, 39 (a) (b) and 51 of the Wild Life Protection Act. This certificate is mandatory for the prosecution to prove that the sandalwood billets in the possession of the accused were obtained from a lawful source. In the absence of this certificate, the court could not hold the accused guilty of the offence under Section 87 of the Karnataka Forest Act.

The court also noted that the sandalwood billets in the possession of the accused did not exceed the weight limit specified in Rule 154 of the Karnataka Forest Rules. This rule specifies the maximum weight of sandalwood billets that a person can possess without a valid permit. The court found that the accused had not exceeded this limit and therefore, could not be held guilty of the offence under Section 87 of the Karnataka Forest Act.

The court's decision also emphasizes the need to adhere to the weight limit specified in the Karnataka Forest Rules. The accused in this case had not exceeded the weight limit, which was a crucial factor in the court's decision to dismiss the appeal.
