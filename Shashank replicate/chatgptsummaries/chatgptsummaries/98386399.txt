In a recent court order, four individuals who were arrested for possession of deer meat were granted bail on the condition that they furnish a personal bond and surety for their appearance in court. The case pertains to a violation of the Wild Life Protection Act, which prohibits the hunting and possession of certain protected species.

The court found that there was no evidence to support the allegation that the individuals had hunted the deer, and that the meat was not seized from them. This lack of evidence was a key factor in the court's decision to grant bail to the accused.

The Wild Life Protection Act is a crucial piece of legislation that seeks to protect India's rich biodiversity. It prohibits the hunting, killing, and possession of certain protected species, including deer, tigers, and elephants. The act also provides for the establishment of national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and other protected areas where these species can thrive.
