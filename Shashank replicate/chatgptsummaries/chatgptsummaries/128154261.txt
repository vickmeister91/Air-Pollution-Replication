The case at hand involves a dispute over the validity of a marriage between the petitioner, R. Madanagopal, and the respondents, M. Usha and M. Pooja Shruthi. The petitioner had filed a petition under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. seeking the production of certain documents, including the negative pertaining to Ex.P2 and the marriage register for the relevant period from Guruvayur Devasan Board. The trial court had initially allowed the petition, but later dismissed it on the ground that the Ration Card, Ex.P4, was sufficient to prove the factum of marriage.

The petitioner challenged this order, arguing that the Ration Card alone was not sufficient to prove the validity of the marriage. The court agreed with the petitioner and set aside the impugned order. The court directed the lower court to send for the documents as sought in the petition from Guruvayur Devasan Board and proceed with the matter after receiving the documents.
