The court recently dismissed a petition filed by Hotel Pineview against the State of Himachal Pradesh and others. The court stated that the petitioner's contentions could be addressed through remedies provided under the National Green Tribunal Act, 2010. The petitioner was allowed to file a review petition or appeal to the Supreme Court.

The case involved a dispute between Hotel Pineview and the State of Himachal Pradesh over the construction of a hotel in the state. The petitioner had alleged that the state government had granted permission for the construction of the hotel without following the due process of law. The petitioner had also alleged that the construction of the hotel would have a detrimental impact on the environment.

The court, however, dismissed the petition on the grounds that the petitioner's contentions could be addressed through remedies provided under the National Green Tribunal Act, 2010. The court stated that the petitioner could approach the National Green Tribunal to seek redressal of their grievances. The court also allowed the petitioner to file a review petition or appeal to the Supreme Court.

The National Green Tribunal Act, 2010, provides for the establishment of a specialized tribunal to deal with environmental disputes. The tribunal has the power to hear and dispose of cases relating to environmental protection and conservation. The tribunal has the power to issue orders and directions to any person, authority or government to take measures to prevent or mitigate environmental damage.
