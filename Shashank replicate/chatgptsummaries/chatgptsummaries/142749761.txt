The court order in question pertains to an application filed under Section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code. The application sought to quash the order and criminal proceedings against the applicants in a case related to the cutting down of green trees without permission from the competent authority. This act is punishable under the Forest Act and Wild Life (Protection) Act.

The applicants had argued that the proceedings against them were baseless and that there was no evidence to support the charges. They had also claimed that the lower court had erred in taking cognizance of the case without proper investigation.

However, the court refused to quash the proceedings, stating that a prima facie case was made out against the applicants. The court noted that the allegations against the applicants were serious in nature and that there was sufficient evidence to support the charges. The court also observed that the lower court had followed due process in taking cognizance of the case.

The court directed the applicants to appear/surrender before the court below and apply for bail. The court assured the applicants that their bail applications would be considered and decided expeditiously in accordance with the law.
