The case in question involves a complaint filed by the petitioner against the respondents for an offense punishable under section 500 IPC. The petitioner has repeatedly issued summons to respondent No.2, who is the general manager of M/s. Mars Protection Group, but each time the summons have been received back unserved. The petitioner filed an application u/s.91 Cr.P.C. praying for summoning of records like attendance register etc. from M/s. Mars Protection Group, which would have revealed that respondent No.2 is an employee of the said firm. However, the application was dismissed by the ld. ACMM vide impugned order.

The petitioner then approached the court, seeking relief in the form of an order allowing the application u/s 91 of the Cr.P.C. The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, allowed the petitioner's application and directed the ld. Magistrate to issue summons to M/s. Mars Protection Group to produce the Pay Rolls, Attendance register, and list of its employees from 1.1.2009 till date.

The court's decision is significant as it allows the petitioner to obtain crucial evidence that could prove respondent No.2's employment with M/s. Mars Protection Group. This evidence could be crucial in establishing the petitioner's case against the respondents for an offense punishable under section 500 IPC.

The court's decision also highlights the importance of following due process in legal proceedings. The petitioner had followed the proper procedure by filing an application u/s.91 Cr.P.C. for summoning of records from M/s. Mars Protection Group. The ld. ACMM's dismissal of the application was therefore not in line with due process, and the court's decision to allow the application u/s 91 of the Cr.P.C. corrects this error.

In conclusion, the court's decision to allow the petitioner's application u/s 91 of the Cr.P.C. and direct the ld. Magistrate to issue summons to M/s. Mars Protection Group to produce the Pay Rolls, Attendance register, and list of its employees from 1.1.2009 till date is a significant development in the case. The decision highlights the importance of following due process in legal proceedings and ensures that the petitioner has access to crucial evidence that could prove their case against the respondents for an offense punishable under section 500 IPC.
