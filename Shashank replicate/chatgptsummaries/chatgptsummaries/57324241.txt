In a recent court order, the petitioner, Hardeep Singh, filed a misc. petition challenging the order passed by the Judicial Magistrate and affirmed by the Sessions Judge, rejecting his application under Sections 91 and 311 Cr.P.C. for summoning the concerned bank official with the record of the bank as a defence witness. The petitioner sought to lead defence evidence for proving that the cheque bounced due to misuse of blank cheques retained by the son of the complainant. 

The court found it essential to call the concerned official in the interest of justice and set aside the previous orders, directing the trial court to summon the official as a defence witness on behalf of the petitioner accused. This order is significant as it highlights the importance of ensuring that all relevant evidence is presented in court, and that the accused has the opportunity to defend themselves fully.

The case in question involved a cheque bouncing due to the misuse of blank cheques retained by the son of the complainant. The petitioner, Hardeep Singh, sought to lead defence evidence to prove this fact, but was initially denied the opportunity to do so by the Judicial Magistrate and Sessions Judge. However, the court found that it was essential to call the concerned bank official as a defence witness in the interest of justice.

This decision is significant as it highlights the importance of ensuring that all relevant evidence is presented in court, and that the accused has the opportunity to defend themselves fully. It also underscores the need for judges to carefully consider all applications made by the accused, and to ensure that they are given a fair and impartial trial.
