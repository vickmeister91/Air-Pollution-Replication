In a recent court order, a petitioner who was convicted under The Wild Life Protection Act was sentenced to one year's imprisonment with a fine of Rs. 500. However, the petitioner applied for suspension of jail sentence and grant of bail, which the court allowed subject to certain conditions. The petitioner was required to deposit the fine amount and furnish a bail bond of Rs. 50,000 with two solvent sureties. Additionally, the petitioner was directed to appear before the CJM concerned on 9/1/19 and all subsequent dates as may be fixed by him.

The court order also stated that if the petitioner is found absent on any date, the CJM can issue and execute a warrant of arrest without referring the matter to the court. This condition was put in place to ensure that the petitioner does not evade the legal process and is present for all court proceedings.

The act provides for the protection of wild animals, birds, and plants, and also regulates hunting, poaching, and trade in wildlife. The act also provides for the establishment of national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and other protected areas to conserve wildlife and their habitats.
