In a recent court order, eight individuals who were arrested for destroying plantation and damaging cement polls in a forest range in Kabirdham, Chhattisgarh were granted bail. The applicants argued that they were residing in the forest and the damage was accidental. The court allowed the bail application and directed the applicants to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 25,000 each with one surety for their appearance in court.

The case pertains to the destruction of plantation and damage to cement polls in a forest range in Kabirdham, Chhattisgarh. The eight individuals were arrested in connection with the incident and subsequently filed a bail application. In their application, they argued that they were residing in the forest and the damage was accidental. The court considered their arguments and allowed the bail application.

As part of the bail conditions, the court directed the applicants to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 25,000 each with one surety for their appearance in court. This is a common practice in bail applications, where the court requires the accused to furnish a bond as a guarantee that they will appear in court for their trial. The surety is usually a person who is willing to take responsibility for the accused and ensure that they appear in court.
