The Kerala High Court recently passed a judgment in a writ petition filed by the Secretary of Paramekkavu Devaswom against the Principal Chief Forest Conservator and Additional Principal Chief Conservator of Forests. The petitioner had sought permission to purchase an elephant from outside Kerala for the benefit of devotees, which was rejected citing Section 43(1) of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972.

The court order pertains to the interpretation of Section 43(1) of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, which prohibits the transfer of any wild animal from one state to another without the prior permission of the Chief Wildlife Warden of the state. The petitioner had argued that the provision did not apply to the purchase of an elephant from outside the state for the purpose of religious rituals and ceremonies.

The court, after considering the arguments of both parties, set aside the rejection order and directed the first respondent to reconsider the application in light of a previous judgment and take a decision within a month. The court also observed that the purchase of an elephant for religious purposes was a long-standing tradition in Kerala and that the petitioner had made a strong case for the same.

The court's decision is significant as it clarifies the scope of Section 43(1) of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, and provides relief to religious institutions seeking to purchase elephants from outside the state for religious purposes. The court's decision also highlights the importance of balancing conservation efforts with the cultural and religious practices of the people.
