The Allahabad High Court recently dismissed a petition for writ challenging an order passed by the Uttar Pradesh Pollution Control Board. The court stated that the petitioner must exhaust all alternative remedies before approaching the writ court. The order in question is appealable under Section 31 of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The petitioner is free to avail the remedy given under the Act.

The case in question was brought before the court on February 28, 2020. The petitioner had challenged an order passed by the Uttar Pradesh Pollution Control Board. The petitioner had argued that the order was arbitrary and illegal. The petitioner had also argued that the order violated the principles of natural justice.

The court, however, dismissed the petition for writ. The court stated that the petitioner must exhaust all alternative remedies before approaching the writ court.
