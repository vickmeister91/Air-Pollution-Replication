The court order in question is related to a Public Interest Litigation (PIL) filed by Neelesh Dubey against the Government of India and others. The PIL sought to restrain respondent No.6 from continuing with illegal mining activity in forest land. The court directed the Principal Secretary of the Mining Department and the Forest Department of Madhya Pradesh to reconsider the license/permission granted to respondent No.6 in light of the petitioner's plea and the decisions of the Apex Court.

The court's decision was based on the fact that the mining activity was being carried out in forest land, which is a protected area under the Forest Conservation Act, 1980. The petitioner had argued that the mining activity was causing irreparable damage to the environment and wildlife in the area.

The court directed the Principal Secretary of the respective Department to reconsider the license/permission granted to respondent No.6 and to make a final decision expeditiously after following due process and giving opportunity to all concerned. The court also directed the Principal Secretary to submit a compliance report by March 31, 2015.

The court's decision is significant as it highlights the importance of protecting forest land and the environment. The court's directive to reconsider the license/permission granted to respondent No.6 is a step towards ensuring that mining activities are carried out in a sustainable and responsible manner.

The court's decision also underscores the need for due process and the importance of giving all concerned parties an opportunity to be heard. This is crucial in ensuring that decisions are made in a fair and transparent manner.

Overall, the court's decision in this case is a positive step towards protecting the environment and ensuring that mining activities are carried out in a responsible and sustainable manner. It also highlights the importance of due process and the need to give all concerned parties an opportunity to be heard.
