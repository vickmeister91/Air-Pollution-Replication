In a criminal petition filed by G Sainath Shet against Somanath Mestha, the court has issued an order pertaining to the production of specific documents related to a loan obtained by the accused. The petitioner had filed an application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. to produce these documents, but when the witness summoned to produce them did not do so, the complainant sought to get two documents marked as evidence. The court allowed the petition and directed the marking of the documents in the case.

The matter has been pending since 2002, and the court has directed the competent authority to dispose of the matter within one year and recover the costs if any. This order is significant as it highlights the importance of producing relevant documents in a criminal case and the consequences of failing to do so.

Section 91 of Cr.P.C. empowers a court to issue a summons to produce documents or other things that are necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. In this case, the petitioner had sought the production of specific documents related to a loan obtained by the accused. However, when the witness summoned to produce the documents did not do so, the complainant sought to get two documents marked as evidence.

The court allowed the petition and directed the marking of the documents in the case. This means that the documents will be considered as evidence in the case and will be taken into account while deciding the matter.
