The Jharkhand High Court has granted anticipatory bail to Anil Kumar Yadav in connection with a case involving illegal excavation of stone chips. Yadav was directed to surrender in the Court of learned Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Hazaribagh within four weeks from the date of the order. The court has ordered that he would be enlarged on bail on depositing Rs.2,000/- as cash security and on furnishing bail bond of Rs.25,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each. Additionally, he was required to cooperate with the investigation and appear before the investigating officer as and when noticed by him.

The case against Yadav pertains to the illegal excavation of stone chips. The police had registered an FIR against him under various sections of the Indian Penal Code and the Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957. Yadav had approached the High Court seeking anticipatory bail, apprehending his arrest in the case.

The High Court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, granted anticipatory bail to Yadav. The court observed that Yadav had no criminal antecedents and was not involved in any other criminal case. The court also noted that Yadav had cooperated with the investigation and had appeared before the investigating officer whenever required.

The court further observed that the allegations against Yadav were not of a serious nature and that he was not likely to abscond or tamper with the evidence. The court, therefore, granted anticipatory bail to Yadav on the conditions mentioned above.

Anticipatory bail is a provision in the Indian legal system that allows a person to seek bail in anticipation of his or her arrest. The purpose of anticipatory bail is to protect a person from unnecessary harassment and detention by the police. The grant of anticipatory bail is subject to certain conditions, which are imposed by the court to ensure that the person does not abscond or tamper with the evidence.

In this case, the High Court has granted anticipatory bail to Yadav on the condition that he would surrender before the Court of learned Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Hazaribagh within four weeks from the date of the order. He has also been directed to deposit Rs.2,000/- as cash security and furnish a bail bond of Rs.25,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each. These conditions are aimed at ensuring that Yadav does not abscond or tamper with the evidence.

The court has also directed Yadav to cooperate with the investigation and appear before the investigating officer as and when noticed by him. This condition is aimed at ensuring that Yadav does not evade the investigation and that the investigation is conducted smoothly.

In conclusion, the Jharkhand High Court has granted anticipatory bail to Anil Kumar Yadav in connection with a case involving illegal excavation of stone chips. Yadav has been directed to surrender before the Court of learned Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Hazaribagh within four weeks from the date of the order and has been granted bail on the conditions mentioned above. The grant of anticipatory bail is subject to the condition that Yadav cooperates with the investigation and appears before the investigating officer as and when noticed by him.
