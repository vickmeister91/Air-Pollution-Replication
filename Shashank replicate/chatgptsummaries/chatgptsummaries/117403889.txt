In a recent court order, three petitioners were granted anticipatory bail in connection with a case registered under sections 2(16), 9 & 50 (C) of the Wild Life Protection Act. The petitioners were accused of killing a Nilgai within the Digwar Forest by entangling it with iron wire. The court has ordered the petitioners to deposit cash security of Rs.10,000/- each and furnish bail bond of Rs. 25,000/- each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned J.M. -1st Class, Hazaribagh, in connection with G(F) Case No.04 of 2018 subject to the conditions laid down under section 438 (2) Cr. P.C.

The case in question involves the killing of a Nilgai, a type of antelope, within the Digwar Forest. The petitioners were accused of using iron wire to entangle the animal, resulting in its death. The Wild Life Protection Act, under which the case was registered, is a law designed to protect wildlife and their habitats. It includes provisions for the prevention of hunting, poaching, and trade in wildlife and their products.

The court's decision to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioners means that they will not be arrested in connection with the case, provided they comply with certain conditions. These conditions include the deposit of cash security and the furnishing of a bail bond with two sureties. The sureties must also deposit the same amount of cash security as the petitioners.

The court's decision to grant anticipatory bail is based on the provisions of section 438 (2) of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr. P.C.). This section allows a person to seek anticipatory bail if they have reason to believe that they may be arrested for a non-bailable offence. The court may grant anticipatory bail if it is satisfied that the person is not likely to abscond or tamper with evidence, and that granting bail is necessary to prevent injustice.

In this case, the court has granted anticipatory bail to the petitioners on the condition that they comply with the provisions of section 438 (2) Cr. P.C. The court has also ordered the petitioners to cooperate with the investigation and to appear before the investigating officer as and when required. Failure to comply with these conditions may result in the cancellation of the bail.
