The Jammu & Kashmir High Court recently issued an order in the case of M/S R S Polythene Industries vs Union Territory Of J&K And Others. The petitioner's unit was closed down and premises sealed by respondent No.3 on the basis of a report that the unit was in operation in violation of pollution control laws. The petitioner's counsel argued that no opportunity of hearing was given before passing the order, which is in contravention of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974.

The court has issued a notice to the respondents returnable within four weeks and has stayed the operation of the order until the next date of hearing. This order is significant as it highlights the importance of following due process and giving an opportunity of hearing before passing any order. The court has also emphasized the need to comply with pollution control laws and regulations to protect the environment and public health.

The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 is a crucial legislation that aims to prevent and control water pollution. It provides for the establishment of pollution control boards at the central and state levels to monitor and enforce pollution control measures. The act also lays down penalties for non-compliance with pollution control laws and regulations.

In the case of M/S R S Polythene Industries, the court has rightly emphasized the need to follow due process and give an opportunity of hearing before passing any order. This is in line with the principles of natural justice, which require that no one should be condemned unheard. The court has also stayed the operation of the order until the next date of hearing, which shows its commitment to ensuring that justice is done.

The issue of pollution control is of utmost importance in today's world, where environmental degradation and climate change are major concerns. The court's order in this case is a reminder that we need to take pollution control seriously and comply with the laws and regulations in place. It is also a reminder that the judiciary plays a crucial role in ensuring that the rule of law is upheld and justice is done.
