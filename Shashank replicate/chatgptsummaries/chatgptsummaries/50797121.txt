The Delhi District Court convicted Mahesh Kumar, proprietor of Sonia Leather, for violating sections 24/25/26 & 33A of the Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. The complaint was filed by the Delhi Pollution Control Committee (DPCC) through Sh. I.K. Kapila, the then Sr. Environmental Engineer. The accused was found to have discharged sewage or trade effluent into a stream or well or sewer or on land, contravening the provisions of Section 26 of the Act. Despite being served with directions under Section 33A of the Act, the accused continued with illegal activities. The court relied on the pre-charge evidence of Dr. B.M.S. Reddy, Sr. Environmental Engineer, DPCC, and convicted the accused.

The court order states that no person shall knowingly cause or permit any poisonous, noxious or polluting matter to enter into any stream, well, sewer or land. It also restricts the establishment of any industry or process that is likely to discharge sewage or trade effluent without the previous consent of the State Board. The court found the accused guilty of violating the Water Act by engaging in water polluting activity without obtaining consent and discharging untreated effluent into a drain, causing pollution. The accused was released on probation for a period of six months and directed to pay compensation for environmental damages.

The court order pertains to a case between DPCC and Mahesh Kumar, with the case number CC 537988/2016. The order was issued by ACMM (Spl. Acts) of Central District, Delhi on January 11, 2017. The content of the order is not provided.

The Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to prevent and control water pollution. The Act prohibits the discharge of any poisonous, noxious or polluting matter into any stream, well, sewer or land. It also restricts the establishment of any industry or process that is likely to discharge sewage or trade effluent without the previous consent of the State Board. The Act empowers the State Board to take measures to prevent and control water pollution and to inspect any premises where an industry or process is carried out.
