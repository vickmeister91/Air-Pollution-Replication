The court order in question pertains to a petition filed under Article 226 of the Constitution of India. The petitioner sought a writ of mandamus to direct respondents 1 to 4 to constitute the State Pollution Control Board as per Section 4 (2) of Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1974 and Section 5 (2) of Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1981 without the presence of the 5th respondent. 

The court found that the conduct of the fifth respondent was being assailed on the ground that he or his son is carrying on business, which has a direct link with the office he holds. The court disposed of the writ petition, stating that the petitioner should take his chance before the Hon'ble Supreme Court, in case the Court is so inclined to entertain the petition.
