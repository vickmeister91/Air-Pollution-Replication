The case of K. Murugadas involves a writ petition filed under Article 226 of the Constitution of India, seeking a Writ of Certiorari to call for the records pertaining to the impugned order in Ref.No.PV2/12162/2016 dated 29.08.2016 on the file of the first respondent and quash the same as illegal. The petitioner had purchased land to an extent of nine acres in S.No.785/1C situated at Azhagiyapandipuram Village, Thovalai Taluk, Kanyakumari District. The said land has been classified as Private Forest under the Tamil Nadu Preservation of Private Forests Act, 1949, and the Tahsildar, Thovalai has issued patta to the afore-said land in his favour.

The petitioner had applied before the second respondent Committee on 21.11.2015 in accordance with the Tamil Nadu Preservation of Private Forests Act, 1949, for carrying out maintenance work in the road situated in S.No.785/1C1C4, Azhagiyapandipuram Village, Thovalai Taluk, Kanyakumari District with certain conditions. The second respondent after careful consideration of the application and based on the reports of the respondents 3 and 4 had passed an order in Mu.Mu.M1/45049/2014 dated 29.02.2016 granting permission for carrying out maintenance work in the road.

However, to the shock and surprise of the petitioner, the first respondent had issued the impugned order dated 29.08.2016 directing the second respondent to withdraw the permission order and has directed the petitioner to seek permission under the Forest Conservation Act,1980. The petitioner had repaired the existing road alone and had not formed any new road as alleged in the Advocate Commissioner's Report.

The court imposed a cost of Rs.50,000/- to be paid by the petitioner and gave certain directions to the petitioner. The petitioner was directed to apply for permission under the Forest Conservation Act,1980, and the second respondent was directed to consider the application and pass appropriate orders within a period of three months from the date of receipt of the application. The court also directed the first respondent to consider the application for permission under the Forest Conservation Act,1980, and pass appropriate orders within a period of three months from the date of receipt of the application.
