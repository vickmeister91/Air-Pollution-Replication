The Patna High Court has recently issued an order regarding the release of two individuals, Shambhu Singh and Ajit Kumar, who were apprehended in connection with a case registered for offenses punishable under Sections 379, 411, 420 and 34 of the Indian Penal Code and 4/40 of M.M.C.R and 15 of Prevention of Pollution Act. The court has ordered that the two individuals be released on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs. 25,000 each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad, subject to certain conditions.

The allegations against the petitioners are that they were carrying illegal sands by their tractors without valid challan. The court has taken into consideration the fact that the petitioners have been in custody for a considerable period of time and that the investigation in the case has been completed. The court has also noted that the petitioners have no criminal antecedents and that they are not likely to abscond or tamper with the evidence.

The court has imposed certain conditions on the release of the petitioners. The petitioners have been directed to cooperate with the investigation and to appear before the investigating officer as and when required. They have also been directed not to leave the jurisdiction of the court without prior permission and not to indulge in any criminal activity during the pendency of the trial.

The court has also directed the petitioners to furnish their mobile numbers and residential addresses to the investigating officer and to the court. The court has further directed the investigating officer to verify the residential addresses of the petitioners and to submit a report to the court in this regard.
