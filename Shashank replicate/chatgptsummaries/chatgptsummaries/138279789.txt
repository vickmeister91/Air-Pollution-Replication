The court order pertains to three anticipatory bail applications filed by Mahadeo Godse, Pramod Deshmukh, and Vaibhav Salunkhe in connection with a case registered against them under Section 379 r/w 34 of I.P.C. and under Section 8 and 15 of The Environment (Protection) Act, 1986. The FIR was lodged by police constable Dadasheb Bankar, who stated that on 29/11/2018, the police party saw three tractors, two JCB machines, and one jeep while proceeding towards river Yerala. The JCB drivers and drivers of the tractors ran away, but one of them was caught, and he informed the police party that the tractors belonged to Mahadeo Godse, Pramod Deshmukh, and Vaibhav Salunkhe.

The court found no prima facie material to connect the present applicants with the alleged offence and allowed the applications. The court order is significant as it highlights the importance of evidence in establishing a case against an accused. In this case, the court found that there was no evidence to connect the accused with the alleged offence, and therefore, granted them anticipatory bail.

The case registered against the accused was under Section 379 r/w 34 of I.P.C. and under Section 8 and 15 of The Environment (Protection) Act, 1986. Section 379 of the Indian Penal Code deals with theft, and Section 34 deals with acts done by several persons in furtherance of common intention. The Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, is an act of the Indian Parliament enacted to provide for the protection and improvement of the environment. Section 8 of the act deals with the prohibition of handling of hazardous substances, and Section 15 deals with the penalty for contravention of the provisions of the act.
