The defense argued that the hairbrushes were made from synthetic materials and not from mongoose hair. However, the court found that there was enough evidence to suggest that the hairbrushes were made from mongoose hair.

The court held that at the stage of framing of charge, only prima facie is to be seen and the court has power to sift and weigh the evidence for the limited purpose of finding out whether or not a prima facie case against accused has been made out. This means that the court only needs to determine whether there is enough evidence to suggest that the accused has committed the crime. The court does not need to determine the guilt or innocence of the accused at this stage.

Based on the evidence presented, the court concluded that there was a prima facie case against Kamaljeet Singh. The court ordered that charges be framed against the accused and the case was put up for framing of formal charge on 02.02.2010. This means that Kamaljeet Singh will face trial for violating the Wildlife Protection Act, 1972.
