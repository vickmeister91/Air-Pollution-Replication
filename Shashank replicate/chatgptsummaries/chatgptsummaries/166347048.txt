The court order in question relates to a petition filed by Chandraprakash Jain, who alleged that a brick kiln in Tahsil Dabra District Gwalior was causing pollution. The court has upheld the preliminary objection raised by the State and has directed the petitioner to avail the remedy before the National Green Tribunal under the National Green Tribunal Act, 2010. The petition has been disposed of finally in the above terms.

The court's decision to uphold the State's preliminary objection means that the petitioner must now seek a remedy before the National Green Tribunal. This tribunal was established under the National Green Tribunal Act, 2010, and is responsible for adjudicating on environmental disputes and enforcing environmental laws.

The court's decision is significant because it highlights the importance of seeking remedies through the appropriate legal channels. In this case, the petitioner had approached the court directly, but the court has directed them to seek a remedy through the National Green Tribunal instead.

The National Green Tribunal has the power to hear cases related to the enforcement of any legal right relating to the environment, and to provide relief and compensation for damages caused by environmental pollution. It is an important forum for addressing environmental issues and ensuring that environmental laws are enforced.

Overall, the court's decision in this case underscores the importance of seeking remedies through the appropriate legal channels, and highlights the role of the National Green Tribunal in adjudicating on environmental disputes and enforcing environmental laws. By directing the petitioner to seek a remedy through the National Green Tribunal, the court has ensured that the environmental issues raised in this case will be addressed through the appropriate legal channels.
