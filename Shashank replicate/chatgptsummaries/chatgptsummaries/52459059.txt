The court recently dismissed a petition filed by Prem Chand Kachhi under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C against an order passed by the JMFC, Jabalpur in Complaint Case No.11516/2013 and an order passed by the XVIII Additional Sessions Judge, Jabalpur in Criminal Revision No.18 of 2015. The applicant had moved an application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C to get various documents related to the case, but both the courts had dismissed the application. The court found no illegality or perversity in the impugned order passed by the courts below and dismissed the petition.

The case in question involved a complaint filed by the complainant against the applicant, alleging that the applicant had cheated him by taking money from him on the pretext of providing him with a job. The complainant had alleged that the applicant had taken Rs. 1,50,000 from him and had promised to provide him with a job in the railways. However, the applicant had failed to provide him with the job and had also refused to return the money.

During the course of the trial, the applicant had moved an application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C to get various documents related to the case. However, both the courts had dismissed the application, stating that the documents sought by the applicant were not relevant to the case and that the applicant had failed to establish the relevance of the documents.

Aggrieved by the orders passed by the courts below, the applicant had filed a petition under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C before the High Court of Madhya Pradesh, seeking to quash the orders passed by the courts below. The applicant had contended that the orders passed by the courts below were illegal and perverse and that they had resulted in a failure of justice.

However, the High Court of Madhya Pradesh found no merit in the contentions raised by the applicant and dismissed the petition. The court observed that the documents sought by the applicant were not relevant to the case and that the courts below had rightly dismissed the application filed by the applicant under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. The court further observed that there was no illegality or perversity in the impugned orders passed by the courts below and that the petition filed by the applicant was devoid of merit.

In conclusion, the court dismissed the petition filed by the applicant under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C against the orders passed by the courts below. The court held that the documents sought by the applicant were not relevant to the case and that the courts below had rightly dismissed the application filed by the applicant under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. The court further held that there was no illegality or perversity in the impugned orders passed by the courts below and that the petition filed by the applicant was devoid of merit.
