In a recent court order, the petitioner, Veerbal, filed a petition under Section 482Cr.P.C. to set aside the order passed by the Judicial Magistrate, Karauli, which refused to release his tractor and trolley bearing Registration No. RJ 34 RA 7960 seized as case property in FIR No. 119/2017. The petitioner relied upon the judgment of the Supreme Court in Sunderbhai Ambalal Desai vs. State of Gujarat, (2002) 10 SCC 283, to contend that the vehicle should not be permitted to remain parked in the police station as it shall gather rust and shall not remain useful.

The court allowed the petition and directed the trial court to release the tractor and trolley seized as case property by imposing certain conditions. The court held that the vehicle should be released to the petitioner on the condition that he furnishes a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 and two sureties of Rs. 25,000 each. The petitioner was also directed to deposit a sum of Rs. 25,000 with the trial court as a security deposit.

The court further directed the petitioner to produce the vehicle before the trial court as and when required and not to dispose of the same without the permission of the trial court. The court also directed the petitioner to cooperate with the investigation and attend the trial court on all dates of hearing.

The court further held that the vehicle should be released to the owner on certain conditions to ensure that the investigation is not hampered and the accused does not abscond.

The court also relied upon the judgment of the Supreme Court in State of Punjab vs. Baldev Singh, (1999) 6 SCC 172, wherein it was held that the accused has a right to get back the seized property if the same is not required for the purpose of investigation or trial.
