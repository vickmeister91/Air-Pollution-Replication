The writ petition was filed by Asok Kumar J. against the Environmental Engineer of the Kerala State Pollution Control Board and John Puthenparambil. The petitioner was aggrieved by the non-consideration of his appeal by the appellate authority under the AIR (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The petitioner had filed an appeal before the appellate authority against the order of the Environmental Engineer, which had directed him to stop the operation of his unit. However, the appellate authority had not considered the appeal on the ground that the petitioner had not deposited the requisite amount of bank guarantee.

The petitioner had contended that the requirement of bank guarantee was not mandatory and that the appellate authority had erred in not considering his appeal on this ground. The petitioner had also contended that the order of the Environmental Engineer was arbitrary and without any basis.

The court, after hearing the submissions of the parties, observed that the requirement of bank guarantee was mandatory under the AIR (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The court also observed that the appellate authority had not erred in not considering the appeal of the petitioner on the ground that he had not deposited the requisite amount of bank guarantee.

The court, however, noted that the petitioner had a right to pursue the matter before the appellate authority by depositing the requisite amount of bank guarantee. The court, therefore, closed the writ petition without prejudice to the petitioner's right to pursue the matter before the appellate authority.
