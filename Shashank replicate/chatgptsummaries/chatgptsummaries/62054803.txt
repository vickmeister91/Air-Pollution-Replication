The petitioner, Sri Kiran, had filed a criminal petition seeking anticipatory bail in a case registered against him by the Channagiri Police Station. The offences he was charged with were punishable under Section 9, 39, 44, 49(B), 51, 57 of Wild Life Act and Section 379 of IPC. The petitioner had already filed a bail application which was dismissed by the Court of I Additional Sessions Judge, Davanagere. The petitioner was apprehending arrest at the hands of the respondent-Police.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, granted anticipatory bail to the petitioner subject to certain conditions. The court ordered that the petitioner shall furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000/- and two sureties for the like sum to the satisfaction of the Investigating Officer. The petitioner was also directed to cooperate with the investigation and to appear before the Investigating Officer as and when required. The court further ordered that the petitioner shall not tamper with the evidence or influence the witnesses in any manner.

The court, while granting anticipatory bail to the petitioner, took into consideration the fact that the petitioner had no criminal antecedents and that he was a resident of the place for the past several years. The court also noted that the petitioner had cooperated with the investigation and had not absconded or evaded the process of law.

In conclusion, the court granted anticipatory bail to the petitioner subject to certain conditions.
