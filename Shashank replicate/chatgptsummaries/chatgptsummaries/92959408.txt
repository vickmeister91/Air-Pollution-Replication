The Kerala High Court recently issued a court order pertaining to a writ petition filed by Rajendran K, seeking direction to issue a license for using loudspeakers to conduct rituals in a temple in Malappuram district. The first respondent had declined permission citing objections from people in the neighborhood. However, the court found that the decision was not in accordance with the Noise Pollution (Regulation and Control) Rules, 2000 and quashed the order. The court directed the first respondent to reconsider the application within ten days, taking into account the provisions of the rule.

The writ petition was filed by Rajendran K, who sought permission to use loudspeakers to conduct rituals in a temple in Malappuram district. The first respondent, who was responsible for granting permission, declined the request citing objections from people in the neighborhood. Rajendran K then approached the Kerala High Court seeking direction to issue a license for using loudspeakers.

The court found that the decision of the first respondent was not in accordance with the Noise Pollution (Regulation and Control) Rules, 2000. The rules state that the use of loudspeakers or public address systems shall not be permitted during night hours (between 10 pm and 6 am) except in closed premises. The rules also state that the sound level of the loudspeakers or public address systems shall not exceed 10 dB(A) above the ambient noise standards for the area in which it is used.

The court observed that the first respondent had not taken into account the provisions of the rule while declining the permission. The court also noted that the first respondent had not given any reasons for declining the permission. The court held that the decision of the first respondent was arbitrary and quashed the order.

The court directed the first respondent to reconsider the application within ten days, taking into account the provisions of the rule. The court also directed the first respondent to give reasons for the decision. The court observed that the use of loudspeakers for conducting rituals in a temple is a matter of religious practice and should be allowed subject to the provisions of the rule.

The court order is significant as it upholds the provisions of the Noise Pollution (Regulation and Control) Rules, 2000. The rules were enacted to regulate and control noise pollution in the country. The rules provide for the prevention and control of noise pollution, and for the establishment of standards for ambient noise levels.

The court order also highlights the importance of considering the provisions of the rule while making decisions regarding the use of loudspeakers or public address systems.
