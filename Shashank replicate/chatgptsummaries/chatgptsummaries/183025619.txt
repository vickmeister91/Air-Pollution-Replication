The court order in question pertains to a criminal petition filed by Cheluva @ Cheluvegowda seeking bail for offences punishable under Section 379 of IPC and under Sections 9, 51, 39, 40, 44, 46 and 50 of the Wild Life Protection Act. The court heard arguments from both sides and allowed the petition, ordering the petitioner to be released on bail subject to certain conditions.

The court noted that the prosecution had not seized the ivory pieces from the petitioner's possession and that the investigation was completed and charge sheet filed. The court also noted that the alleged offences were not exclusively punishable with death or imprisonment for life.

The case at hand involves allegations of wildlife poaching and theft. The petitioner, Cheluva @ Cheluvegowda, was accused of stealing ivory pieces from a forest area and selling them. The charges against him were under Section 379 of IPC, which deals with theft, and various sections of the Wild Life Protection Act, which aim to protect wildlife and their habitats.

During the hearing, the prosecution argued that the petitioner was a habitual offender and that he was likely to abscond if released on bail. They also argued that the offences he was accused of were serious in nature and that he should not be granted bail.

The defence, on the other hand, argued that the petitioner had been falsely implicated in the case and that there was no evidence to prove his guilt. They also argued that the petitioner had cooperated with the investigation and that he was not a flight risk.

After hearing arguments from both sides, the court allowed the petition and ordered the petitioner to be released on bail subject to certain conditions. The court noted that the prosecution had not seized the ivory pieces from the petitioner's possession and that the investigation was completed and charge sheet filed. The court also noted that the alleged offences were not exclusively punishable with death or imprisonment for life.

The conditions of bail included a requirement for the petitioner to cooperate with the investigation, to attend all court hearings, and to not tamper with evidence or influence witnesses.
