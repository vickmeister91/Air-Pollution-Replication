In a recent court order, a petition filed by Ashok @ Lalitesh was dismissed by the court. Ashok was accused of committing offenses and had filed an application requesting that his thumb impression and retina be sent to the Forensic Science Laboratory to prove his identity. However, the court rejected the application, stating that it is the prosecution's responsibility to prove that Ashok and Lalitesh are the same person.

The court emphasized that the identification of the accused is a crucial aspect of any criminal trial. The prosecution must establish the identity of the accused beyond a reasonable doubt. The court noted that the identification of the accused can be done through various means, including the testimony of witnesses who have seen the accused committing the offense.

The court further stated that the use of forensic evidence is not necessary in every case. The court can rely on other evidence, such as the testimony of witnesses, to establish the identity of the accused. The court also noted that the use of forensic evidence can be time-consuming and expensive, and it should only be used when necessary.

In this case, the court found that the application filed by Ashok lacked merit. The court stated that the prosecution will establish the identity of the accused during the course of identification by witnesses. The court emphasized that the use of forensic evidence is not necessary in this case.
