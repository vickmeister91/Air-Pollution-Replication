The Punjab and Haryana High Court recently passed a judgment in a writ petition filed by M/s Dhiman Stone Crusher against an order passed by the Chief Environmental Engineer, Punjab Pollution Control Board. The petitioner had challenged the order on the grounds that it was arbitrary, illegal, and without jurisdiction. However, the court dismissed the petition as the petitioner had not availed the remedy of filing an appeal against the order.

The court observed that the petitioner had not challenged the order within the prescribed time limit of 30 days, and therefore, the petition was not maintainable. The court further noted that the petitioner had not provided any valid reason for the delay in filing the appeal. The court also observed that the petitioner had not exhausted the statutory remedy of filing an appeal before approaching the court.

The court, however, granted the petitioner liberty to withdraw the petition and file an appeal with an application for condonation of delay within 15 days. The court hoped that the Appellate Authority would consider and decide the appeal in accordance with the law.

The court's decision highlights the importance of following the prescribed legal procedures and exhausting all available remedies before approaching the court. The court emphasized that the statutory remedies provided under the law must be exhausted before approaching the court, and that the court should not be used as a substitute for the statutory remedies.

The court's decision also underscores the importance of timely filing of appeals and providing valid reasons for any delay in filing the same. The court observed that the petitioner had not provided any valid reason for the delay in filing the appeal, and therefore, the petition was not maintainable.

In conclusion, the court's decision in the writ petition filed by M/s Dhiman Stone Crusher serves as a reminder to all litigants to follow the prescribed legal procedures and exhaust all available remedies before approaching the court. The court's decision also highlights the importance of timely filing of appeals and providing valid reasons for any delay in filing the same. The court's hope that the Appellate Authority would consider and decide the appeal in accordance with the law is a reminder that the judiciary is committed to upholding the rule of law and ensuring that justice is done in accordance with the law.
