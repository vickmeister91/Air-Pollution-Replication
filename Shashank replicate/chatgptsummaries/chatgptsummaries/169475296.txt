The court recently granted anticipatory bail to Subhash Singh, who was facing arrest in connection with a forest offence registered against him. Singh's counsel argued that he was a reputed citizen with no criminal past and that his tractor trolley, which was recovered by the forest officer, was not involved in the alleged crime. The court allowed the application and directed Singh to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 25,000 with a solvent surety of the same amount. Singh was also directed to make himself available for interrogation and abide by other conditions. The bail was granted for a limited period of 60 days, during which Singh could move an application for regular bail.
