The court order in question pertains to an application filed under Section 438 of the Cr.P.C. for anticipatory bail on behalf of the applicant, Paturam. Paturam is apprehending his arrest in connection with a wildlife protection act violation. The court has granted the application and directed the police to release Paturam upon his arrest on his furnishing personal surety of Rs. 50,000 and one surety to the said amount to the satisfaction of the arresting officer. The applicant shall further abide by the conditions enumerated under Section 438(2) of the Cr.P.C.

Anticipatory bail is a provision in the Indian legal system that allows a person to seek bail in anticipation of an arrest. This provision is available to a person who apprehends arrest for a non-bailable offence. The purpose of anticipatory bail is to protect the person from unnecessary harassment and detention by the police. The provision of anticipatory bail is enshrined in Section 438 of the Cr.P.C.

In the present case, Paturam has filed an application for anticipatory bail as he apprehends his arrest in connection with a wildlife protection act violation. The court has granted the application and directed the police to release Paturam upon his arrest on his furnishing personal surety of Rs. 50,000 and one surety to the said amount to the satisfaction of the arresting officer. The applicant shall further abide by the conditions enumerated under Section 438(2) of the Cr.P.C.

The conditions enumerated under Section 438(2) of the Cr.P.C. are as follows:

1. The person shall make himself available for interrogation by a police officer as and when required.

2. The person shall not, directly or indirectly, make any inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the court or to any police officer.

3. The person shall not leave India without the previous permission of the court.

4. Such other conditions as may be imposed under subsection (3) of Section 437, as if the bail were granted under that section.

The court has directed Paturam to abide by these conditions in order to avail the benefit of anticipatory bail. The purpose of these conditions is to ensure that the person does not misuse the provision of anticipatory bail and cooperates with the investigation.
