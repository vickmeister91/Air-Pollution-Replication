In the case of Md. Asharaf Ali, the petitioner sought permission to withdraw the writ petition with the liberty to approach the State Appellate Authority under the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The court granted the petitioner's request and disposed of the writ petition with the liberty aforesaid.

The Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 is an act of the Indian Parliament that provides for the prevention, control, and abatement of air pollution. The act empowers the central and state governments to take measures to prevent and control air pollution and to establish and maintain a network of air quality monitoring stations.

In this case, the petitioner had filed a writ petition seeking relief against the pollution caused by a nearby factory. The petitioner had alleged that the factory was emitting harmful pollutants into the air, which was causing health problems for the residents of the area.

However, during the course of the proceedings, the petitioner sought permission to withdraw the writ petition with the liberty to approach the State Appellate Authority under the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The petitioner's request was granted by the court, and the writ petition was disposed of with the liberty aforesaid.

The granting of liberty to the petitioner means that the petitioner is free to approach the State Appellate Authority under the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981, for relief against the pollution caused by the factory. The State Appellate Authority is a quasi-judicial body that hears appeals against the orders of the State Pollution Control Boards.

In conclusion, the court granted the petitioner's request to withdraw the writ petition with the liberty to approach the State Appellate Authority under the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. This means that the petitioner is free to seek relief against the pollution caused by the factory through the State Appellate Authority.
