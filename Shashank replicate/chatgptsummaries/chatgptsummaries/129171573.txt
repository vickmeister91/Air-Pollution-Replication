The case of Natha Singh, who filed a criminal misc. petition under Section 482 Cr.P.C., revolves around an order passed by the learned Special Judge, SC/ST Act Cases, Hanumangarh in Session Case No.07/2014. The court had rejected the application filed by the petitioner under Section 91 Cr.PC for taking the Hindi Transcript of compact disc available on record. The petitioner contended that the CD was to be examined but the same has not been taken on record even when the redressal was sought under Section 91 of Cr.P.C.

The petitioner's argument was that the CD was crucial evidence in the case and should have been examined. However, the court had not taken it on record, which was a violation of Section 91 of Cr.P.C. The petitioner, therefore, filed a criminal misc. petition under Section 482 Cr.P.C. against the order passed by the learned Special Judge.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, allowed the application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. and directed the petitioner to produce the proper CD before the learned court below within a period of two weeks from the date of the order. This decision was a significant victory for the petitioner as it allowed him to present crucial evidence in the case.

The case highlights the importance of following due process and ensuring that all evidence is examined and taken on record. The court's decision to allow the application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. was a step in the right direction towards ensuring justice is served.

In conclusion, the case of Natha Singh serves as a reminder that the legal system must ensure that all evidence is examined and taken on record to ensure justice is served. The court's decision to allow the application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. was a positive step towards achieving this goal.
