The case at hand involves the seizure of a car belonging to P. Antony Raj, which was found to be carrying 3.5 kgs of dried deer meat. The Forest Range Officer seized the car and filed a case against Raj under the Wildlife Protection Act. Raj filed an application seeking the return of his vehicle, which was dismissed by the District Munsif-cum-Judicial Magistrate, Denkanikottai. Raj then filed a criminal revision before the Sessions Court, Krishnagiri, which was also dismissed. It is this order that Raj has challenged before the High Court.

The High Court, in its order, notes that the Magistrate only has the power to release the vehicle and not to adjudicate on the merits of the case. The court cites the Supreme Court's decision in State of Punjab v. Baldev Singh (1999) to support its decision. In that case, the Supreme Court held that the power to release a vehicle seized under the provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC) is vested only with the Magistrate and not with any other court. The High Court also cites the Supreme Court's decision in State of Haryana v. Bhajan Lal (1992) to support its decision. In that case, the Supreme Court held that the High Court should not interfere with the order of the Magistrate unless there is a manifest error of law or procedure.

The High Court, therefore, sets aside the orders of the Magistrate and the Sessions Court and directs the court below to release the vehicle to Raj on certain conditions. The court orders that Raj should execute a bond for a sum of Rs. 50,000 with two sureties for the like sum to the satisfaction of the court below. The court also orders that Raj should produce the vehicle before the court below within a week from the date of the order and that he should not dispose of the vehicle till the disposal of the case. The court further orders that Raj should cooperate with the investigation and attend the court below on all the dates of hearing.

Forest Range Officer is a significant one as it clarifies the powers of the Magistrate in cases where vehicles are seized under the provisions of the CrPC.
