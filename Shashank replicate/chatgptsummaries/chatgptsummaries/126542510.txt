The court order in question pertains to a writ petition filed by M/s Gajanan Enclave Pvt. Ltd. against Jaipur Development Authority and others. The petitioner sought to stop the establishment of a Sewage Treatment Plant (STP) at a particular location and relocate it to another suitable place. The Rajasthan State Pollution Control Board objected to the writ petition, stating that it was not maintainable under the National Green Tribunal Act, 2010.

The court found that the NGT Act provided an efficacious remedy for the petitioner and dismissed the writ petition, with liberty to approach either the Appellate Authority or the National Green Tribunal for redressal of grievances.

The case highlights the importance of understanding the legal framework within which environmental disputes are adjudicated. The NGT Act, which was enacted in 2010, provides for the establishment of a specialized tribunal to deal with environmental disputes. The Act also provides for the appointment of experts in various fields, including environmental science, to assist the tribunal in its work.

In this case, the court found that the petitioner had not exhausted the remedies available under the NGT Act before approaching the court. The court noted that the NGT Act provides for a comprehensive mechanism for the resolution of environmental disputes, including the establishment of a specialized tribunal with the power to hear and decide cases related to environmental protection and conservation.

The court's decision underscores the importance of utilizing the mechanisms provided under the NGT Act for the resolution of environmental disputes. The Act provides for a specialized forum with the expertise and resources necessary to effectively address complex environmental issues.
