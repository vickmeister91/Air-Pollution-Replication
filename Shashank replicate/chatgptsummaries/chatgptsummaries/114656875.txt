Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, empowers a court to issue a summons or order to produce any document or thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. The section is intended to enable the court to obtain evidence that is necessary for the just decision of a case. The section is not intended to be used as a fishing expedition or to harass the other party.

The court also observed that the documents were not necessary for the just decision of the case.
