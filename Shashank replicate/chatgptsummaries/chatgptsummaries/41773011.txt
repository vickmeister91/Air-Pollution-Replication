The court order in question pertains to a petition filed by Vijay Tiwari against Babli and others. The petitioner had sought direction from the court to conclude the trial of a miscellaneous case within a stipulated time frame. The petitioner's application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C had been pending since July 2014, which was causing delay in the main proceedings under Section 125 Cr.P.C.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, disposed of the petition with directions to the trial court to decide the application under Section 91 Cr.P.C expeditiously, preferably within two months. The court did not award any costs in this matter.

The petitioner, Vijay Tiwari, had filed a petition seeking direction from the court to conclude the trial of a miscellaneous case within a stipulated time frame. The petitioner's application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C had been pending since July 2014, which was causing delay in the main proceedings under Section 125 Cr.P.C.

The respondent, Babli and others, had opposed the petition and argued that the petitioner's application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C was not maintainable. They further argued that the petitioner was trying to delay the proceedings by filing such petitions.

After hearing the arguments of both parties, the court disposed of the petition with directions to the trial court to decide the application under Section 91 Cr.P.C expeditiously, preferably within two months. The court did not award any costs in this matter.

Section 91 of the Cr.P.C empowers a court to issue a summons or order to a person to produce a document or thing in his possession or control. The petitioner had filed an application under this section seeking certain documents from the respondent. The delay in deciding this application was causing delay in the main proceedings under Section 125 Cr.P.C.

Section 125 of the Cr.P.C deals with the maintenance of wives, children, and parents. The petitioner had filed a case under this section seeking maintenance from the respondent. The delay in deciding the application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C was causing delay in the main proceedings under Section 125 Cr.P.C.

The court, while disposing of the petition, directed the trial court to decide the application under Section 91 Cr.P.C expeditiously, preferably within two months. This direction was given to ensure that there is no further delay in the main proceedings under Section 125 Cr.P.C.
