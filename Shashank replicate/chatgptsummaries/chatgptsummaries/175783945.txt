The charge against the petitioner was framed under Section 26 read with Section 44 of Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1974 on 08.11.2012. The petition was dismissed, and the effect of the interim order dated 05.05.2015 was vacated.

The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act 1974 is an Act of the Parliament of India enacted to provide for the prevention and control of water pollution and the maintaining or restoring of wholesomeness of water. The Act provides for the establishment of Central and State Boards for the prevention and control of water pollution. The Act also provides for the appointment of officers and other employees necessary for the efficient discharge of the functions of the Boards.

Section 26 of the Act deals with the penalty for contravention of the provisions of the Act. The section provides that whoever contravenes any of the provisions of the Act or any order or direction issued thereunder shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three months, or with fine which may extend to ten thousand rupees, or with both. Section 44 of the Act deals with the cognizance of offences. The section provides that no court shall take cognizance of any offence under the Act except on a complaint made by the Board or any officer authorized by it in this behalf.
