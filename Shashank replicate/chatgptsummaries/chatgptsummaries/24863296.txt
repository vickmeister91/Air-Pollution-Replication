The case in question involves a petition filed by Vishamber Singh under Section 482 Cr.P.C. to set aside an order passed by the Judicial Magistrate No.1, Dholpur. The order in question refused to release Singh's vehicle, Tractor No.RJ11-RA-9380, along with its trolley. The vehicle had been seized as case property in relation to FIR No.128/2018, which was registered at Police Station Sadar, Dholpur for offences under Section 379 IPC, Sections 29/51 of the Wild Life Protection Act, and Sections 41 and 42 of the Rajasthan Forest Act.

Singh's petition was successful, and the court directed the trial court to release the tractor trolley seized as case property, subject to certain conditions. These conditions included that Singh must keep the tractor trolley intact and not change its identification. Additionally, Singh must produce the tractor trolley as and when the trial court requires it for the proposed identification of the case property. Singh must also execute a Supurdaginama/indemnity bond and bonds by two sureties to the satisfaction of the trial court. The trial court is empowered to impose any other conditions in the Supurdaginama/indemnity bond and surety bonds to be furnished by Singh and the sureties, as it deems fit.

The case highlights the importance of following proper legal procedures when dealing with seized property. In this case, Singh was able to successfully petition for the release of his vehicle and trolley by following the appropriate legal channels and meeting the necessary conditions set by the court.
