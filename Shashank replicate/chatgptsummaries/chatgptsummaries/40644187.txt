A Criminal Original Petition was filed by the petitioner to direct the II Metropolitan Magistrate to complete a pending case as soon as possible. The case involves offences under Sections 498(A), 406 & 506(i) IPC. The petitioner was examined as P.W.1 on 16.07.2015 and was not cross-examined by the accused. The accused filed a petition under Section 91 Cr.P.C calling for certain documents and the trial Court posted the same for orders from 10.02.2016.

The court directed the X Metropolitan Magistrate, Egmore, Chennai, to expeditiously complete the trial since the petitioner is coming from Assam for every hearing. The accused shall cross-examine the petitioner on a date fixed for the appearance of the petitioner, and if the accused adopts any dilatory tactics, it is open to the trial Court to remand him to custody.

The petitioner had filed a Criminal Original Petition seeking to quash the proceedings initiated against him under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act. The petitioner had issued a cheque to the respondent, which was dishonoured. The respondent had initiated proceedings against the petitioner under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act.

The petitioner contended that the cheque was issued as a security cheque and not towards any liability. The petitioner also contended that the respondent had not complied with the provisions of Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act. The court observed that the petitioner had not denied the issuance of the cheque and that the cheque was issued towards a liability.

The court also observed that the respondent had complied with the provisions of Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act. The court dismissed the Criminal Original Petition filed by the petitioner.
