The court heard arguments from both sides and ultimately disposed of the application in light of an undertaking given by the respondent's counsel to deposit the entire arrears of maintenance within seven days. The court also held that the direction for a summary enquiry into the allegation of remarriage was not unwarranted and did not commit any illegality.

The case at hand involves a dispute over maintenance payments. Section 125 of the CrPC provides for the payment of maintenance to wives, children, and parents who are unable to maintain themselves. In this case, Smt. Preeti Jatav had filed an application under Section 125(3) of the CrPC seeking maintenance from her husband. However, the proceedings were stayed by the Family Court in Morena, which directed for a summary enquiry into the allegation of remarriage.

The applicant challenged this order under Section 482 of the CrPC, which empowers the High Court to exercise its inherent powers to prevent abuse of the process of any court or to secure the ends of justice. The applicant argued that the direction for a summary enquiry into the allegation of remarriage was unwarranted and had no basis in law.

The court heard arguments from both sides and ultimately disposed of the application in light of an undertaking given by the respondent's counsel to deposit the entire arrears of maintenance within seven days. The court also held that the direction for a summary enquiry into the allegation of remarriage was not unwarranted and did not commit any illegality.
