The court has granted bail to Gurjansingh Meravi, who had been in custody since April 8, 2017, in connection with a case registered under the Wild Life Protection Act. The applicant was accused of hunting a Gutri, a Schedule-III animal, with a gun. The court allowed the bail application, stating that the applicant was an innocent person and there were no criminal antecedents. The applicant was directed to furnish a bail bond of Rs. 30,000 and comply with the provisions of Section 437(3) of the Cr.P.C.

The case against Gurjansingh Meravi was registered under the Wild Life Protection Act, which is a comprehensive law that provides for the protection of wild animals and plants. The Act prohibits hunting, poaching, and trading in wild animals and plants. The Act also provides for the establishment of protected areas, such as national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, where hunting and other activities are strictly prohibited.

The court found that the applicant was accused of hunting a Gutri, which is a Schedule-III animal under the Act. Schedule-III animals are those that are not endangered but are protected under the Act. The court noted that the applicant had used a gun to hunt the animal, which is a serious offense under the Act.

However, the court also found that the applicant was an innocent person and had no criminal antecedents. The court noted that the applicant had been in custody for a considerable period of time and that there was no evidence to suggest that he was a flight risk or that he would tamper with the evidence. The court also noted that the applicant had cooperated with the investigation and had not been involved in any other criminal activity.

In light of these factors, the court allowed the bail application and directed the applicant to furnish a bail bond of Rs. 30,000. The court also directed the applicant to comply with the provisions of Section 437(3) of the Cr.P.C., which requires the applicant to attend all court proceedings and not to commit any offense while on bail.
