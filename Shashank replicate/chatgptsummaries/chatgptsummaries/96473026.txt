The People's Committee for Constitutional Rights and another party filed a Public Interest Litigation seeking a Mandamus to evict illegal encroachers from forest land under the jurisdiction of the DFO, Sonitpur East Division, Sonitpur, Assam, and to stop any permanent construction made inside the forest under the Sarba Siksha Abhijan Mission, District Sonitpur, Assam. The State Government filed an affidavit stating that appropriate action is being taken in accordance with the law to protect the forest and its flora and fauna.

The court noted that the present petition is not an adversarial litigation between the parties and that the respondents are not averse to protecting the forest. The court directed the respondents to identify the persons entitled to protection under the Scheduled Tribes and other Traditional Forest Dwellers (Recognition of Forest Rights) Act, 2006 and to evict only those who are in occupation without such rights being unauthorized. The court also directed the respondents to obtain permission under Section 2 of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 for the construction of the school building and to make an appropriate application to the Central Government.

The court order states that encroachers must be evicted in accordance with the law, and the respondents must verify if there are any pending orders from the Supreme Court. The State Government must make an appropriate application to the Central Government within three months regarding the school building, and the Central Government will take appropriate action based on the application. The petition is disposed of.
