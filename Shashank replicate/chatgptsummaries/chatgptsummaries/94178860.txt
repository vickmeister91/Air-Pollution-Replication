The court order in question pertains to a case where the petitioner was found in possession of 2 kg of meat of a wild boar and his licensed gun was seized by forest officials. The petitioner filed a petition to quash the order passed by the Judicial First Class Magistrate II, Hosdurg, which declined his request for interim custody of the gun. The court allowed the petition to the extent that if the petitioner applies to renew his gun license, the learned Magistrate shall consider the application and permit the petitioner to submit the licensed gun to the competent authority for renewal. The Forest official shall produce the gun before the authorities and return it to the learned Magistrate for verification and disposal of the property as per law after completion of the trial.
