The Patna High Court recently granted anticipatory bail to Ram Chandra Yadav, who was accused of owning a tractor that was seized by the police for carrying sand illegally extracted from the Sone river. The court noted that there was no allegation that Yadav had knowledge of the illegal use of the tractor and that he had no criminal antecedents. Yadav was granted bail on the condition that he present himself before the police or court when required and that his bail bonds would be cancelled if he failed to appear on two consecutive occasions.

Yadav had approached the Patna High Court seeking anticipatory bail, stating that he had no knowledge of the illegal use of his tractor and that he was being falsely implicated in the case. He also submitted that he had no criminal antecedents and that he was willing to cooperate with the police in the investigation.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, observed that there was no allegation that Yadav had knowledge of the illegal use of his tractor. The court also noted that Yadav had no criminal antecedents and that he was willing to cooperate with the police in the investigation.

The court, while granting anticipatory bail to Yadav, imposed certain conditions. Yadav was directed to present himself before the police or court when required and to cooperate with the investigation. The court also directed Yadav to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 and two sureties of the like amount.
