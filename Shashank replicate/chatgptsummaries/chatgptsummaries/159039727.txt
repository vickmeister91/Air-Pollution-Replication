The court order in question pertains to a case filed by M/s Sangam Stone Crushing Co. against the State of Punjab and others. The petitioner challenged the orders/directions passed by the Punjab State Pollution Control Board revoking the consent granted to the petitioner and calling for the cancellation of registration of the petitioner-unit. The court directed the appellate authority to examine the appeals preferred by the petitioner-unit against the impugned directions/orders passed by the Punjab State Pollution Control Board in accordance with the law. The interim directions already issued with regard to maintenance of status quo would enure in favor of the petitioner till the disposal of the appeals by the appellate authority.

The case in question involves a dispute between M/s Sangam Stone Crushing Co. and the Punjab State Pollution Control Board. The petitioner had been granted consent to operate a stone crushing unit by the Board, but this consent was later revoked by the Board. The Board also called for the cancellation of the petitioner's registration, which would effectively shut down the petitioner's business.

The petitioner challenged these orders/directions in court, arguing that they were illegal and arbitrary. The court agreed with the petitioner and directed the appellate authority to examine the appeals preferred by the petitioner-unit against the impugned directions/orders passed by the Punjab State Pollution Control Board in accordance with the law.
