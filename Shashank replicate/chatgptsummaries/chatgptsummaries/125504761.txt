In a recent court order, the case of possession of illegal wildlife skins was brought to a close. The prosecution argued for maximum punishment under the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972, while the defense argued for leniency due to the convict's age and long period of judicial custody. After considering both arguments, the court upheld the sentence of three years of simple imprisonment and a fine of Rs.1 lakh.
