Pepsico India Holdings Pvt Ltd, a subsidiary of the global food and beverage giant PepsiCo, filed a writ petition against the Gujarat Pollution Control Board's order to close their manufacturing unit in Jhagadia, Bharuch. The order was issued on the grounds that the unit was operating without a valid consent to operate under the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, and the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The Board had also alleged that the unit was discharging untreated effluent into the nearby river, which was causing pollution and posing a threat to public health.

Pepsico India Holdings Pvt Ltd challenged the order on several grounds, including that the Board had not given them a proper opportunity to be heard before passing the order. They also argued that the order was arbitrary, unreasonable, and violative of their fundamental right to carry on their business. During the hearing of the case, the Board stayed the order, which allowed the unit to continue its operations.

However, the petitioner withdrew the writ petition with permission to file a fresh one if necessary. The court expects the Board to reconsider the matter and pass an appropriate, reasoned order if necessary. This means that the Board will have to review its decision and provide a detailed explanation for its order, taking into account all the relevant facts and legal provisions.

The case highlights the importance of environmental regulations and the need for businesses to comply with them. The pollution caused by industrial units can have serious consequences for public health and the environment, and it is the responsibility of regulatory bodies to ensure that such units operate in a manner that is safe and sustainable. At the same time, it is also important for businesses to be given a fair opportunity to present their case and to have their concerns addressed before any adverse action is taken against them.
