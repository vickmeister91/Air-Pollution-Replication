The Patna High Court has granted bail to Jitendra Kumar, who was apprehensive of his arrest in connection with a case registered against him for offences punishable under Sections 379, 411, 420, 34 of the Indian Penal Code and Sections 4/40 of Bihar Mines and Minerals Concession Rules, 1972, including Section 15 of Prevention of Pollution Act, 1986. The allegation against him was that he was the owner-cum-driver of one of the seven tractors loaded with sand that were intercepted. The court ordered his release on bail on furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 25,000 with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad, subject to certain conditions.

The case against Jitendra Kumar was registered under various sections of the Indian Penal Code and the Bihar Mines and Minerals Concession Rules, 1972, including Section 15 of Prevention of Pollution Act, 1986. The allegation against him was that he was the owner-cum-driver of one of the seven tractors loaded with sand that were intercepted. The court granted him bail on furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 25,000 with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad, subject to certain conditions.

The conditions of the bail include that Jitendra Kumar shall not tamper with the evidence or influence the witnesses, shall cooperate with the investigation, shall not leave the country without the permission of the court, and shall attend the court on all the dates of hearing. The court also directed him to surrender his passport, if any, to the Investigating Officer and not to leave the jurisdiction of the court without prior permission.

The court considered the fact that Jitendra Kumar was not named in the FIR and was arrested only on the basis of his identification by the witnesses. The court also noted that he had no criminal antecedents and was not likely to abscond or tamper with the evidence. The court further observed that the investigation was at an initial stage and the charges against him were yet to be framed.

The court also took into account the fact that the offence alleged against Jitendra Kumar was bailable in nature and that he had been in custody for more than a month. The court held that the grant of bail would not prejudice the investigation or the trial and that the conditions imposed would ensure that he would not misuse his liberty.
