The Gujarat High Court recently granted anticipatory bail to Suthar Mansinghbhai Purabhai in connection with an FIR registered against him for violating the Gujarat Prohibition Act. The court considered the nature of allegations, the role attributed to the accused, and the law laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court before granting the bail. The court also imposed several conditions on the applicant, including a personal bond of Rs. 10,000 and not leaving the district of Banaskantha until the filing of the charge-sheet.

The court order pertains to an application for anticipatory bail filed by Suthar Mansinghbhai Purabhai. The applicant was accused of violating the Gujarat Prohibition Act, which prohibits the manufacture, sale, and consumption of alcohol in the state. The FIR was registered against him for allegedly possessing and transporting liquor without a valid permit.

The court considered the nature of allegations against the applicant before granting the bail. The court observed that the allegations against the applicant were not of a serious nature and did not involve any violence or threat to public order. The court also noted that the applicant had no criminal antecedents and was a first-time offender.

The court also considered the role attributed to the accused in the alleged offence. The court observed that the applicant was not the main accused in the case and had only been named as a co-accused. The court noted that the main accused had already been arrested and was in custody.

The court also considered the law laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court before granting the bail. The court observed that the Hon'ble Apex Court had held that anticipatory bail should be granted in cases where the allegations are not of a serious nature and the accused is not likely to abscond or tamper with evidence.

The court granted anticipatory bail to the applicant and directed him to cooperate with the investigation, make himself available for interrogation, and not obstruct or hamper the police investigation. The court also imposed several other conditions on the applicant, including a personal bond of Rs. 10,000 and not leaving the district of Banaskantha until the filing of the charge-sheet.

The court clarified that the observations made while granting bail should not influence the trial court's decision. The court observed that the trial court would have to independently evaluate the evidence and decide whether the accused was guilty or not.

In conclusion, the Gujarat High Court granted anticipatory bail to Suthar Mansinghbhai Purabhai in connection with an FIR registered against him for violating the Gujarat Prohibition Act. The court considered the nature of allegations, the role attributed to the accused, and the law laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court before granting the bail. The court also imposed several conditions on the applicant, including a personal bond of Rs. 10,000 and not leaving the district of Banaskantha until the filing of the charge-sheet. The court clarified that the observations made while granting bail should not influence the trial court's decision.
