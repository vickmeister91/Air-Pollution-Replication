The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Arvind under Section 439 of the Cr.P.C. Arvind had been in custody since September 26, 2017, in connection with a violation of the Wildlife Protection Act. The prosecution alleged that Arvind and his co-accused had hunted a deer and partitioned its flesh. Arvind's counsel argued that he was innocent and falsely implicated in the case.

The court heard arguments from both sides and ultimately allowed Arvind's bail application. The court directed him to furnish a bail bond of Rs. 30,000 and comply with the provisions of Section 437(3) of the Cr.P.C. This section requires the accused to appear before the court as and when required and not to leave the country without the court's permission.
