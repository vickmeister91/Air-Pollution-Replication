In a recent court order, two individuals who had filed a criminal petition seeking release on bail in a case related to the cultivation of ganja were granted their request. However, the court also imposed certain conditions that the petitioners must adhere to. These conditions include executing a personal bond and regularly attending court hearings.

The prosecution had alleged that the petitioners had cultivated ganja on a large scale and were involved in its distribution. The defense, on the other hand, had argued that the petitioners were innocent and had been falsely implicated in the case. The defense had also argued that the prosecution had failed to produce any concrete evidence to support their allegations.

After considering the arguments of both sides, the court had found that there was no prima facie evidence to suggest that the petitioners were involved in the cultivation and distribution of ganja. The court had also noted that the prosecution had failed to produce any evidence to support their allegations under the Karnataka Forest Act and Wild Life Protection Act.

In light of these findings, the court had allowed the petition and ordered the petitioners to be released on bail. However, the court had also imposed certain conditions to ensure that the petitioners did not abscond or interfere with the investigation. These conditions included executing a personal bond and regularly attending court hearings.
