In a recent court order, it was stated that the case between Niraj Mishra and the Union of India should proceed before the National Green Tribunal. This decision was made due to the reliefs claimed in the petition and the provisions in the National Green Tribunal Act, 2010. The court directed the Registry to transfer the proceeding to the National Green Tribunal in Bhopal.

The case in question involves environmental concerns and the impact of certain activities on the environment. The petitioner, Niraj Mishra, had filed a petition seeking relief from the Union of India for the damage caused to the environment due to certain activities. The court order acknowledges the importance of addressing environmental concerns and the need for a specialized forum to deal with such matters.

The National Green Tribunal was established in 2010 under the National Green Tribunal Act, with the aim of providing effective and expeditious disposal of cases relating to environmental protection and conservation of forests and other natural resources. The tribunal has jurisdiction over all civil cases involving substantial questions relating to the environment.

The court order recognizes the importance of the National Green Tribunal in dealing with environmental matters and directs the Registry to transfer the proceeding to the tribunal in Bhopal. This decision is in line with the provisions of the National Green Tribunal Act, which provides for the transfer of cases from other courts to the tribunal.

The transfer of the case to the National Green Tribunal is expected to expedite the proceedings and ensure that the matter is dealt with by a specialized forum. The tribunal has the expertise and experience to deal with environmental matters and is better equipped to address the concerns raised in the petition.
