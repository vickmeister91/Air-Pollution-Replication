The Allahabad High Court has dismissed a petition to quash a summoning order against M/S Sakaar Hari Ent Udhyog and others in a case related to air pollution. The court found that there was prima facie evidence against the accused and that the magistrate had not acted illegally in issuing the order. The accused were directed to surrender before the court within four weeks and apply for bail, after which no coercive action would be taken against them. If they did not surrender within the given time, authorities would be free to take action against them.

The case in question relates to air pollution caused by the accused's industrial activities. The Uttar Pradesh Pollution Control Board (UPPCB) had conducted an inspection of the accused's premises and found that they were violating various environmental norms. The UPPCB had issued a show-cause notice to the accused, but they had failed to respond. Subsequently, the UPPCB had filed a complaint against the accused under various sections of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) and the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974.

The accused had challenged the summoning order before the Allahabad High Court, arguing that there was no evidence against them and that the magistrate had acted illegally in issuing the order. However, the court rejected their arguments and upheld the summoning order. The court noted that the UPPCB's inspection report prima facie indicated that the accused were violating environmental norms and causing air pollution. The court also observed that the magistrate had followed due process in issuing the order and that there was no illegality in it.

The court directed the accused to surrender before the magistrate within four weeks and apply for bail. The court clarified that if the accused surrendered and applied for bail, no coercive action would be taken against them. However, if they failed to surrender within the given time, authorities would be free to take action against them. The court also directed the magistrate to dispose of the bail application expeditiously.
