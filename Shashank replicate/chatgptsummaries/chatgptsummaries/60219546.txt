The High Court of Madhya Pradesh recently dismissed a petition filed by an accused/petitioner under section 482 Cr.P.C. against an order passed by the Court below on 04/08/2018. The order in question dismissed an application filed under section 91 Cr.P.C. The High Court found no illegality or jurisdictional error in the order impugned and dismissed the petition on 17-12-2018.

The petitioner had filed an application under section 91 Cr.P.C. seeking the production of certain documents from the respondent. The Court below dismissed the application, stating that the documents sought were not relevant to the case. The petitioner then filed a petition under section 482 Cr.P.C. in the High Court, challenging the order of the Court below.

The High Court, after hearing both parties, found no merit in the petitioner's arguments. The Court noted that the documents sought by the petitioner were not relevant to the case and that the Court below had correctly dismissed the application. The High Court also found no illegality or jurisdictional error in the order impugned and dismissed the petition.

The dismissal of the petition by the High Court means that the order of the Court below stands. The petitioner will not be able to obtain the documents sought through the application filed under section 91 Cr.P.C. The decision of the High Court is final and binding on the parties involved.
