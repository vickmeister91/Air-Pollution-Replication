The court order in question pertains to a writ petition filed by a quarrying firm challenging an interim order passed by a learned Single Judge. The order directed the firm to stop quarrying activities for three months and obtain clearance from the National Board for Wild Life before resuming operations. The firm argued that the order was passed without the authority of law and that the Eco Sensitive Area around the sanctuary extended only to a radius of 1 km. However, the court found no infirmity in the order and dismissed the appeal, stating that quarrying has serious deleterious effects on the environment and that clearance from the National Board for Wild Life was necessary before resuming operations.
