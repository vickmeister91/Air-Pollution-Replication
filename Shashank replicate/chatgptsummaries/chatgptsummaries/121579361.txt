The Jharkhand High Court has granted anticipatory bail to Arun Yadav in connection with a case involving the seizure of a tractor loaded with 100 cft sand. The court considered the submissions of both the petitioner and the state and found that the petitioner had not committed any offense as alleged. The court ordered that if Yadav is arrested or surrenders within two weeks of the order, he shall be released on bail on furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned S.D.J.M., Khunti. Yadav was also ordered to pay a tax amount of Rs. 320 plus a fine amount of Rs. 320, totaling Rs. 640.

The case against Yadav involved the seizure of a tractor loaded with 100 cft sand. The petitioner had been accused of violating the provisions of the Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, and the Jharkhand Minerals (Prevention of Illegal Mining, Transportation and Storage) Rules, 2017. The petitioner had argued that he had not committed any offense as alleged and that the seizure of the tractor was illegal.

The court considered the submissions of both the petitioner and the state and found that the petitioner had not committed any offense as alleged. The court noted that the petitioner had produced documents to show that he had obtained the sand legally and that he had paid the necessary taxes and fees. The court also noted that the petitioner had not been involved in any illegal mining or transportation of minerals.

The court ordered that if Yadav is arrested or surrenders within two weeks of the order, he shall be released on bail on furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned S.D.J.M., Khunti. The court also ordered Yadav to pay a tax amount of Rs. 320 plus a fine amount of Rs. 320, totaling Rs. 640. The court noted that the petitioner had already paid the tax amount and that the fine amount would be paid within a week of the order.
