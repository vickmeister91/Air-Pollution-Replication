The court order in question is related to a public interest litigation that was filed against the establishment and operation of brick kilns in the districts of Baghpat and Meerut in Uttar Pradesh. The petitioners alleged that these brick kilns were being operated in violation of the Uttar Pradesh Brick Kilns (Site Criteria for Establishment) Rules, 2012. The court has directed the Pollution Control Board to take necessary steps to ensure that their statutory duties and obligations are duly performed. 

The Pollution Control Board has been directed to conduct a survey of all the brick kilns operating in the districts of Meerut and Baghpat. The purpose of this survey is to identify any brick kiln that is operating without the consent or permission of the Board and in breach of the 2012 Rules. The Board has been instructed to take appropriate action against any such brick kiln in accordance with the law. 

The court has disposed of the writ petition with no order as to costs. This means that the petitioners will not be required to pay any costs associated with the litigation. The court's decision is a significant victory for the petitioners, who had been fighting against the establishment and operation of these brick kilns for some time. 

The establishment and operation of brick kilns in violation of the Uttar Pradesh Brick Kilns (Site Criteria for Establishment) Rules, 2012 can have serious environmental consequences. Brick kilns are known to emit large amounts of particulate matter, which can cause respiratory problems and other health issues.
