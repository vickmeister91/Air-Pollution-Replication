The case involved a petitioner/accused who was facing trial under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act before the JMFC in Rewa. The petitioner had filed an application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. for calling the details of Cheque No.598729 dated 18/07/2012 from Union Bank of India, Branch Saman, Rewa, which was dismissed by the learned JMFC on 27/01/2016. The petitioner had filed another application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. on 18/01/2017 for calling the details of A/c No.425002010047806 from Union Bank of India, Branch Saman, with regard to dated 11/12/2012, which was dismissed by the learned trial Court by order impugned dated 12/07/2017.

The petitioner had filed a revision under Section 397 read with Section 401 of Cr.P.C. challenging the order impugned dated 12/07/2017. The revision was dismissed by the court, which found no irregularity, illegality or perversity in the order impugned. The court held that the petitioner had failed to make out any case for interference in the order impugned.

The court's decision was based on the fact that the petitioner had failed to provide any evidence to support his claim that the details of the cheque and bank account were necessary for his defense. The court noted that the petitioner had not explained how the details of the cheque and bank account were relevant to his defense, and had not provided any reason why he could not obtain the information through other means.

The court also noted that the petitioner had not challenged the order of the learned JMFC dismissing his application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. for calling the details of Cheque No.598729 dated 18/07/2012 from Union Bank of India, Branch Saman, Rewa. The court held that the petitioner's failure to challenge the order of the learned JMFC was fatal to his case, as it meant that the order had attained finality.

In conclusion, the court dismissed the revision filed by the petitioner/accused, finding no irregularity, illegality or perversity in the order impugned. The court held that the petitioner had failed to make out any case for interference in the order impugned, as he had not provided any evidence to support his claim that the details of the cheque and bank account were necessary for his defense, and had not challenged the order of the learned JMFC dismissing his application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. for calling the details of Cheque No.598729 dated 18/07/2012 from Union Bank of India, Branch Saman, Rewa.
