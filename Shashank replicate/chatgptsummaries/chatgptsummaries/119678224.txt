The Patna High Court has granted anticipatory bail to Abhishek Kumar Singh and Pradeep Kumar in connection with a case filed against them for alleged offenses under Sections 379, 411, 420/34 of the Indian Penal Code and Section 4/40 BMMCR & 15 Pollution Act. The petitioners had claimed that they had no involvement in the loading of sand onto tractors and that the drivers had come to the office for payment.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, observed that the allegations against the petitioners were not supported by any material on record. The court also noted that the petitioners had no criminal antecedents and were ready to cooperate with the investigation.

The court ordered the petitioners to be released on anticipatory bail upon surrender or arrest, subject to certain conditions. The conditions included a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 and cooperation in the trial. The court also directed the petitioners to appear before the investigating officer as and when required and not to tamper with the evidence or influence the witnesses.

The case against the petitioners was filed after a complaint was lodged by the District Mining Officer alleging that the petitioners were involved in illegal sand mining and transportation. The complaint alleged that the petitioners had illegally loaded sand onto tractors and transported it to various places.

The petitioners, however, denied the allegations and claimed that they were innocent. They argued that they were not involved in the loading of sand onto tractors and that the drivers had come to the office for payment.
