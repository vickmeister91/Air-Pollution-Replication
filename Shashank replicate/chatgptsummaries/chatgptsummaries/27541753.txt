In a recent court hearing, a case was presented regarding the Wild Life Protection Act of 1972. The court found that no inquiry had been conducted pursuant to a notification, which raised concerns about the enforcement of the act. In response, the court referred to a previous case and disposed of the matter with a direction to the Collector, Puri to complete the inquiry within six months.

The Wild Life Protection Act of 1972 is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to protect wildlife and their habitats in India. The act provides for the establishment of national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and conservation reserves, as well as the regulation of hunting and trade in wildlife. However, the effectiveness of the act depends on its enforcement, which requires regular monitoring and inquiry into potential violations.

In this case, the court found that no inquiry had been conducted pursuant to a notification, which suggests a lack of enforcement of the act. The court referred to a previous case, which had established the importance of conducting inquiries in cases of potential violations of the act. The court emphasized that such inquiries are necessary to ensure the protection of wildlife and their habitats.

To address the issue at hand, the court directed the Collector, Puri to complete the inquiry within six months. This direction is significant as it emphasizes the importance of timely and effective enforcement of the act. The court's decision also serves as a reminder to other authorities responsible for enforcing the act to take their responsibilities seriously and to ensure that inquiries are conducted promptly and thoroughly.

In addition to the direction to complete the inquiry, the court also ordered an urgent certified copy of the order to be issued. This order is significant as it ensures that the decision is communicated promptly and effectively to all relevant parties. It also serves as a record of the court's decision, which can be used to hold authorities accountable for their actions or inactions.

Overall, the court's decision in this case highlights the importance of effective enforcement of the Wild Life Protection Act of 1972.
