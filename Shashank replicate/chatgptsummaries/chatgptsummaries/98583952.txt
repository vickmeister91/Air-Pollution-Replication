The Wild Life Protection Act is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to protect wildlife and their habitats. The Act prohibits the hunting, killing, or capturing of any wild animal, as well as the trade in any part or product derived from a wild animal. The Act also provides for the establishment of protected areas, such as national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, where wildlife can thrive without interference from humans.
