Vivek Goyal, the applicant, filed an application for anticipatory bail under Section 438 of CrPC in connection with Forest Crime No.22517/2016 registered by Police Station Forest Department Sabalgarh, District Morena for an offense punishable under Section 7/55 of Biological Diversity Act. The applicant claimed that he was the courier of M/s Khushi Herbal Traders which had sold herbs to D.R. Herbal Trading Company, Vijaypur. The seized herbs belong to M/s Khushi Herbal Traders who has legally sold the same to D.R. Herbal Trading Company, Vijaypur and the applicant has nothing to do with the business transaction of M/s Khushi Herbal Traders. However, the court rejected the application for bail.

The case in question pertains to the illegal trade of herbs under the Biological Diversity Act. The Forest Department had registered a case against the accused for the illegal trade of herbs. The applicant, Vivek Goyal, claimed that he was only a courier and had no involvement in the business transaction between M/s Khushi Herbal Traders and D.R. Herbal Trading Company. He further claimed that the seized herbs belonged to M/s Khushi Herbal Traders, who had legally sold them to D.R. Herbal Trading Company.

The court, however, rejected the application for anticipatory bail filed by the applicant. The court observed that the applicant was not merely a courier, but was involved in the illegal trade of herbs. The court also noted that the applicant had not cooperated with the investigation and had not provided any evidence to support his claim that he was only a courier. The court, therefore, concluded that the applicant was not entitled to anticipatory bail.

The decision of the court is significant as it highlights the importance of cooperation with the investigation. The court observed that the applicant had not cooperated with the investigation and had not provided any evidence to support his claim. This lack of cooperation and evidence was a crucial factor in the court's decision to reject the application for anticipatory bail.

Furthermore, the court's decision also emphasizes the need for strict enforcement of laws related to the trade of herbs under the Biological Diversity Act. The illegal trade of herbs can have severe consequences on the environment and can lead to the depletion of natural resources. The court's decision to reject the application for anticipatory bail sends a strong message that such illegal activities will not be tolerated.

In conclusion, the court's decision to reject the application for anticipatory bail filed by Vivek Goyal highlights the importance of cooperation with the investigation and the need for strict enforcement of laws related to the trade of herbs under the Biological Diversity Act. The court's decision sends a strong message that illegal activities related to the trade of herbs will not be tolerated and that those involved in such activities will be held accountable.
