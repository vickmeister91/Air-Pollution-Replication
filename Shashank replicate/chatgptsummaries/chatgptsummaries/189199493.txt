The case of K. Sureshkumar vs. the Judicial Magistrate, Fast Track Court No.1, Erode, revolves around a Criminal Original Petition filed by the petitioner to set aside an order passed in C.M.P.No.2134 of 2015 in S.T.C.No.210 of 2014. The petitioner had requested for the call lists and SMS messages that were sent from the mobile phone of the complainant from 2010 to 2014. However, the court dismissed the petition as it did not disclose how the SMS details and call details of the complainant were necessary for the just decision of the case and would invade into the privacy of an individual, guaranteed by Article 21 of the Constitution of India.

The petitioner's request for the call lists and SMS messages of the complainant was made in relation to a case that was being heard in the Judicial Magistrate, Fast Track Court No.1, Erode. The court had previously passed an order in C.M.P.No.2134 of 2015 in S.T.C.No.210 of 2014, which the petitioner sought to set aside. The court's decision was therefore in line with the constitutional provisions that protect the privacy of individuals.
