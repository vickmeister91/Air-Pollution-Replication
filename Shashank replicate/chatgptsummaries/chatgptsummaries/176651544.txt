The Delhi District Court has convicted M/S. Electroplating Works and Mange Ram for violating the Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 by discharging untreated effluent into a drain leading to the river Yamuna. The complaint was filed by the Delhi Pollution Control Committee, and the accused pleaded not guilty. The court found them guilty under sections 43 and 44 of the Act and ordered their conviction on October 4, 2016. The court also noted that the accused violated sections 24, 25, and 26 of the Act.

The court order pertains to a case where a company was found guilty of discharging trade effluent without any treatment facility, violating sections 24/25/26 of the Water Act, 1974. The court convicted the accused and released them on probation for a period of six months, with a personal bond and surety, and directed them to pay a sum of Rs. 1,000/- as a cost of proceedings and Rs. 9,000/- to the complainant as compensation for environmental damages. The court also restricted the establishment of any industry or operation that is likely to discharge sewage or trade effluent into a stream or well or sewer or on land without the previous consent of the State Board.

The court's decision is a significant step towards protecting the environment and preventing pollution. The Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to prevent and control water pollution. The Act provides for the establishment of pollution control boards at the central and state levels and lays down the standards for the quality of water that can be discharged into water bodies.

The court's decision to convict the accused and order their probation, along with the payment of compensation for environmental damages, sends a strong message to industries and individuals that polluting the environment will not be tolerated.
