The court order in question pertains to a case where a petitioner had issued a cheque of Rs. 20,00,000/- to the respondent, which had bounced. The petitioner had filed an application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. to summon the Income Tax Returns of the respondent to assess his financial capacity. The court observed that the proceedings under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act are quasi civil proceedings having penal consequences and that fishing roving enquiry in the cross-examination is not permitted. 

The court noted that the petitioner cannot delay the trial by not cross-examining the complainant. However, to balance the equities and ensure fair play, the petitioner can examine, as defence witnesses, the official of the Ward of the Income Tax Department and the official of the Bank to produce the statement of accounts of the complainant. The court disposed of the petition accordingly.
