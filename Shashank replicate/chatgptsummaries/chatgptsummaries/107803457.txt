The court order in question relates to a criminal petition filed by Sri S M Niranjanmurthy against the Range Forest Officer and others. The petitioner had sought to quash the proceedings initiated against him for non-cognizable offenses without prior authorization mandated under Sec.155(2) of Cr.P.C. The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, allowed the petition and quashed the proceedings against the petitioner.

The petitioner had argued that the proceedings initiated against him were in violation of the provisions of Section 155(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure (Cr.P.C.), which mandates that no police officer shall investigate a non-cognizable offense without the order of a Magistrate of the first class or such other Magistrate as empowered by the State Government. The petitioner had contended that the police had initiated the proceedings against him without obtaining the necessary authorization from the Magistrate, and therefore, the proceedings were illegal and without jurisdiction.

The court, after considering the arguments of both parties, agreed with the petitioner's contentions and held that the proceedings initiated against him were illegal and without jurisdiction. The court observed that the police had not obtained the necessary authorization from the Magistrate before initiating the proceedings against the petitioner, and therefore, the proceedings were in violation of the provisions of Section 155(2) of the Cr.P.C.

The court, however, clarified that the complainant had the liberty to proceed against the petitioner in accordance with the law after compliance with the requirement under Section 155(2) of the Cr.P.C. This means that the complainant can initiate fresh proceedings against the petitioner after obtaining the necessary authorization from the Magistrate.
