The court order pertains to a criminal petition filed by Yogesh K. Ural seeking anticipatory bail in the event of his arrest in a criminal case filed against him for offenses punishable under Sections 408, 468, 471, 381, 420, 506 r/w 34 of IPC. The case is registered on the basis of a complaint lodged by the managing director of a company alleging that Yogesh K. Ural and others cheated the company to an extent of Rs.100 crores. The court granted anticipatory bail to Yogesh K. Ural subject to certain conditions.

The case against Yogesh K. Ural and others was registered on the basis of a complaint lodged by the managing director of a company alleging that they had cheated the company to the tune of Rs.100 crores. The offenses alleged against them are punishable under Sections 408, 468, 471, 381, 420, 506 r/w 34 of IPC. Yogesh K. Ural filed a criminal petition seeking anticipatory bail in the event of his arrest in the case.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, granted anticipatory bail to Yogesh K. Ural subject to certain conditions. The conditions imposed by the court include that Yogesh K. Ural shall cooperate with the investigating agency, shall not tamper with the evidence, shall not leave the country without the permission of the court, and shall furnish a personal bond of Rs.1 lakh along with two sureties of the like amount.

The court observed that the allegations against Yogesh K. Ural are serious in nature and involve a huge amount of money. However, the court also noted that Yogesh K. Ural has no criminal antecedents and has been cooperating with the investigating agency. The court further observed that the investigation in the case is at a preliminary stage and the role of Yogesh K. Ural is yet to be ascertained. In such circumstances, the court deemed it fit to grant anticipatory bail to Yogesh K. Ural subject to the aforementioned conditions.

Anticipatory bail is a provision in the law that allows a person to seek bail in anticipation of his arrest. It is granted by the court to a person who apprehends arrest in a non-bailable offense. The purpose of anticipatory bail is to protect the person from unnecessary harassment and detention by the police. However, the grant of anticipatory bail is subject to certain conditions that the court may impose to ensure that the person does not misuse the liberty granted to him.
