The court order in question pertains to a case where Madhusudan Floor Mills Pvt. Ltd. and others have filed an application under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. challenging the order passed by J.M.F.C., Gwalior in Criminal Case No.10550 of 2009. The applicants had filed an application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. for production of the Income Tax Returns of the respondent of the year 2009-2010 and 2010-2011, which was rejected by the J.M.F.C. The applicants had sought the production of these documents in order to establish their case against the respondent.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, dismissed the application and affirmed the order passed by J.M.F.C. in Criminal Case No.10550 of 2009. The court held that the applicants had failed to establish any ground for the production of the Income Tax Returns of the respondent. The court further held that the production of these documents would not be relevant to the case and would only cause unnecessary delay.

The court observed that the power under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. is discretionary and should be exercised judiciously. The court held that the applicants had failed to establish any ground for the production of the Income Tax Returns of the respondent and that the J.M.F.C. had rightly rejected their application.

The court also observed that the production of Income Tax Returns is not an absolute right and can be subject to certain conditions. The court held that the applicants had failed to establish any such conditions and that their application was rightly rejected by the J.M.F.C.
