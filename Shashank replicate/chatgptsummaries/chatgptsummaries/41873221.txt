The court order in question pertains to an application for bail under Section 439 of the Cr.P.C in connection with a case registered under Sections 9, 39, 50, 51 and 52 of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The applicant had been in judicial custody since 14-12-2017 and was alleged to have been in possession of leopard skin and nail. The court, after considering the facts and circumstances of the case, the period of incarceration already undergone by the applicant, the nature of allegations and the absence of previous antecedents, allowed the application and granted bail to the applicant upon furnishing a personal bond of Rs. 50,000/- with one solvent surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court.
