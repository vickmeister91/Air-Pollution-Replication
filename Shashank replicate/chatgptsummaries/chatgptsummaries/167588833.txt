The court order in question pertains to a case where the petitioner is accused of issuing a bounced cheque for Rs. 6 lakhs. The petitioner filed two applications, one to send the disputed documents for comparison with the admitted document and the other to call for the records from the Syndicate Bank. However, the trial court dismissed both applications and stated that the court can compare the signatures by naked eye under Section 73 of the Indian Evidence Act.

The petitioner contended that the court should have allowed an expert to compare the signatures. The court allowed the Criminal Original Petition and the Criminal Revision Case and directed the trial court to call for records from the bank and send the disputed document for comparison by a scientific expert at the cost of the petitioner.
