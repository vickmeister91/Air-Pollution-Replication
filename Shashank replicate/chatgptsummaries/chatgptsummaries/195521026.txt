The court order in question pertains to a complaint case against Gopal Singh, a Junior Engineer at the relevant time in R.E.O., Jamui. Singh was accused of executing the work of construction of road on the land of Mahapur reserve forest by pitching the stone and boulder brought from the reserved forest, which was in violation of Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 until obtainment of prior permission from the Government of India. 

Singh had filed an application for discharge from the complaint case under Section 245 of the Cr. P.C. However, the court rejected the application, stating that there was sufficient material to proceed against the petitioner. The court found no merit in the application and dismissed it.

The Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to protect forests and wildlife in India. The act prohibits the use of forest land for non-forest purposes without prior permission from the central government. The act also mandates that any diversion of forest land for non-forest purposes must be compensated by afforestation on an equivalent area of non-forest land.
