The court order in question relates to a criminal revision case between Rakesh Singh and the State of Madhya Pradesh. The applicant had filed an application under Section 91 of the Criminal Procedure Code (CrPC), which was dismissed by the Special Judge in a proceeding held under the Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (NDPS) Act. The revision petition was dismissed as no error was committed by the court below.

The applicant, Rakesh Singh, had filed an application under Section 91 of the CrPC, which empowers a court to issue a summons or order for the production of any document or thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. Singh had sought to bring on record certain panch-nama prepared by the Patwari and certain other documents, which he claimed were relevant to the case.

However, the Special Judge rejected Singh's application, finding that the documents were not required for the purposes of the proceeding. The court held that the panch-nama and other documents were not necessary to establish the guilt or innocence of the accused, and that their admission would only serve to delay the proceedings. The court also noted that the documents were not admissible under the NDPS Act, as they had not been prepared in accordance with the provisions of the Act.

Singh subsequently filed a revision petition before the High Court, challenging the order of the Special Judge. However, the High Court dismissed the petition, finding that no error had been committed by the court below. The court held that the Special Judge had correctly applied the law and had not acted arbitrarily or capriciously in rejecting Singh's application. The court also noted that the revision petition was devoid of any merit and was filed only to delay the proceedings.
