The court order in question pertains to a case where the petitioner, who is the mother of the registered owner of a vehicle involved in a forest offence, had applied for interim custody of the vehicle under Section 451 Cr.P.C. The request was disallowed by the learned Magistrate and the order was confirmed in revision by the learned Sessions Judge. The petitioner challenged both the orders, but the court found no reason to interfere in the orders passed by the courts below.

The court stated that a property can only be released under Section 451 Cr.P.C by the court to the right person who can claim custody and just because the petitioner is the mother of the registered owner, she cannot claim the vehicle. The court further explained that the purpose of Section 451 Cr.P.C is to ensure that the property is not misused or disposed of during the pendency of the case and that it is returned to the rightful owner after the case is disposed of. Therefore, the court cannot release the property to someone who is not the rightful owner.

However, the court suggested that the petitioner can obtain authorization or power of attorney from her son and make a fresh application for custody. This would establish her as the rightful person to claim custody of the vehicle and the court would be able to release the vehicle to her.

The court's decision is based on the principle of law that a person who is not the rightful owner of a property cannot claim custody of it. This principle is important to ensure that the property is not misused or disposed of during the pendency of the case and that it is returned to the rightful owner after the case is disposed of.

In this case, the petitioner is the mother of the registered owner of the vehicle, but she is not the rightful owner of the vehicle. Therefore, the court cannot release the vehicle to her. However, the court has suggested a way for the petitioner to establish herself as the rightful person to claim custody of the vehicle.
