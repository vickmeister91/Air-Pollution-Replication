The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by two individuals, Baburam and Rajendra, under Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The applicants had been in custody since June 15, 2018, in connection with a case registered under the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972. The prosecution alleged that the applicants were found hunting three Monitor Lizards by axe. However, the applicants claimed that they were innocent and falsely implicated in the case due to some enmity with the forest guard.

After hearing arguments from both parties, the court allowed the bail application and directed the applicants to furnish a bail bond of Rs. 20,000 each with a solvent surety each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court for their appearance before it on the dates given by the concerned court.
