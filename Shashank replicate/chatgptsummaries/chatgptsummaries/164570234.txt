The court order in question relates to a petition filed by Nand Lal, who was seeking regular bail in a case registered against him under Sections 9 and 51 of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The petitioner had been in custody since February 7, 2017, and seven kilograms of black buck meat were recovered from him.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, allowed the petition and granted bail to the petitioner, subject to the satisfaction of the trial court. The court observed that the petitioner had been in custody for a considerable period of time and that there was no likelihood of him absconding or tampering with the evidence.

The court also took into consideration the fact that the petitioner had no previous criminal record and that he was a first-time offender. The court further noted that the petitioner had cooperated with the investigating agency during the course of the investigation and had also made himself available for questioning whenever required.

The court, while granting bail to the petitioner, imposed certain conditions on him. The petitioner was directed to furnish a personal bond in the sum of Rs. 50,000 with one surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court. The petitioner was also directed to surrender his passport, if any, and to not leave the country without the prior permission of the trial court.

The court also directed the petitioner to cooperate with the investigating agency and to attend the trial court on all the dates fixed for the hearing of the case. The court warned the petitioner that in case of any violation of the conditions imposed, the bail granted to him would be liable to be cancelled.

The court, after considering the arguments of both the parties, allowed the petition and granted bail to the petitioner, subject to the satisfaction of the trial court. The court imposed certain conditions on the petitioner while granting him bail and warned him that in case of any violation of the conditions imposed, the bail granted to him would be liable to be cancelled.
