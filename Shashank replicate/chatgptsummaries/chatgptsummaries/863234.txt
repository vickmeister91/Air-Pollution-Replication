In a recent court order, two individuals were granted bail in a case related to offenses under the Wild Life (Protection) Act and the Electricity Act. The court directed the release of the petitioners on bail with effect from a specified date and ordered the Judicial First Class Magistrate to release them on executing a bond and providing sureties. The Wild Life (Protection) Act is a legislation enacted by the Indian government to protect wild animals and plants and their habitats. The Act prohibits hunting, poaching, and trading of wild animals and plants, and also provides for the establishment of protected areas for the conservation of wildlife. The Electricity Act, on the other hand, is a legislation that regulates the generation, transmission, distribution, and use of electricity in India.

The principle of presumption of innocence holds that a person is considered innocent until proven guilty. The CrPC also provides for the imposition of certain conditions on the grant of bail, such as the requirement to provide sureties and to not leave the country without the permission of the court.
