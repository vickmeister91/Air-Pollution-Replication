In a recent court order, 17 individuals who were arrested in connection with a case registered under various sections of the Wild Life Protection Act and the Prevention of Damage to Public Property Act were granted bail. The applicants had filed a bail application claiming that they were falsely implicated and that they had been in possession of government forest lands for 35 years, seeking a lease of the same.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, allowed the bail application and directed the applicants to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 25,000 each with one surety in the like sum. The court also imposed certain conditions on the applicants, including that they shall not leave the country without the permission of the court, shall not tamper with the evidence, and shall cooperate with the investigation.

The case pertains to the alleged illegal occupation of government forest lands by the applicants. The prosecution had alleged that the applicants had encroached upon the forest lands and had caused damage to public property. The applicants, on the other hand, had claimed that they had been in possession of the lands for 35 years and had been seeking a lease of the same from the government.

The court, while granting bail to the applicants, observed that the investigation in the case was still at a preliminary stage and that the applicants had been in custody for a considerable period of time. The court also noted that the applicants had no criminal antecedents and that they were not likely to abscond or tamper with the evidence.
