The court order in question pertains to a case where Radha Kishan Cements Ltd. challenged a communication from the Forest Department directing them to hand over possession of lease areas for mining limestone. The court found that the lease had been granted in contravention of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, and dismissed the petition. The court also noted that the company was a sick industry and recommended for winding up. Another petition by the company seeking possession of additional land for mining operations was also dismissed as the company had no right to do mining operations in the area without approval from the Central Government under the Forests (Conservation) Act, 1980.

The Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, is a crucial piece of legislation that seeks to protect forests and wildlife in India. The act requires that any diversion of forest land for non-forest purposes must be approved by the Central Government. The act also mandates that any lease or license granted for mining or other non-forest activities must be in accordance with the provisions of the act.

In the case of Radha Kishan Cements Ltd., the court found that the lease for mining limestone had been granted in contravention of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980. As a result, the court dismissed the company's petition challenging the Forest Department's communication directing them to hand over possession of the lease areas. The court's decision is significant as it upholds the provisions of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, and reinforces the importance of protecting forests and wildlife in India.
