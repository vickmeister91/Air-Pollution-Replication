The prosecution report stated that Nayak had encroached on 25,000 sq. ft of forest land and was constructing a house without permission. Nayak's counsel argued that the forest land had ceased to be protected under Section 30(b) of the Indian Forest Act, but the government advocate argued that the land remained protected until de-notified by the state government.

The court dismissed Nayak's application, stating that it was for Nayak to prove that the area where he was constructing the house was not reserved or prohibited, and that the court could not make a roving enquiry. The court allowed Nayak to raise all points before the trial court at the appropriate stage.
