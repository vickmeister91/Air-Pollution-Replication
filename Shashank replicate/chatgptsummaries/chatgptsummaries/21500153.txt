The Karnataka State Pollution Control Board was the respondent in a criminal petition filed by V.S. Badari. The petitioner was accused of not disconnecting electricity supply to a quarry after the expiry of its license, which was in violation of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The petitioner argued that he was stationed at a different location and had nothing to do with the quarry or the electricity supply. 

The court found that there was no evidence to suggest that the petitioner had committed any infraction. The court directed that he be compensated if it was found that he had been wrongly prosecuted. The court also ordered the lower court to expedite the proceedings. 

The case highlights the importance of ensuring that individuals are not wrongly prosecuted. The court recognized that the petitioner had no involvement in the quarry or the electricity supply and therefore should not have been prosecuted. The court's decision to compensate the petitioner if he was wrongly prosecuted is a step towards ensuring that individuals are not unfairly targeted by the legal system. 

The court's order also emphasizes the need for strict adherence to environmental laws. The Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to protect the environment and public health. The court's decision to hold the petitioner accountable for not disconnecting electricity supply to the quarry after the expiry of its license is a reminder that environmental laws must be followed.
