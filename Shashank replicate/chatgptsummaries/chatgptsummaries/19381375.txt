The recent court order pertains to a series of petitions filed by the State of Rajasthan and others against the Collector of Bundi and another. The issue at hand is related to the alteration of boundaries of the Ramgarh Wildlife Sanctuary. The Ramgarh Wildlife Sanctuary is located in the district of Bundi in Rajasthan. It is spread over an area of 252.79 square kilometers and is home to a variety of flora and fauna. The sanctuary was established in 1982 with the aim of protecting the wildlife and their habitats. The sanctuary is an important part of the ecosystem of the region and is a major tourist attraction.

The issue at hand is related to the alteration of the boundaries of the sanctuary. The Collector of Bundi had issued orders altering the boundaries of the sanctuary, which were challenged by the State of Rajasthan and others. The petitioners argued that the Collector had no power to alter the boundaries of the sanctuary and that the impugned orders were illegal and arbitrary.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, held that the Collector had no power to alter the boundaries of the sanctuary. The court observed that the power to alter the boundaries of a sanctuary lies with the State Government and not with the Collector. The court further held that the impugned orders passed by the Collector were liable to be set aside as they were illegal and arbitrary.

The court also observed that the matter was remanded to the Collector to reconsider afresh the objections filed by the respondents in terms of Section 19 of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972. Section 19 of the Act provides for the constitution of a committee to hear objections and make recommendations to the State Government regarding the alteration of the boundaries of a sanctuary. The court directed the Collector to reconsider the objections filed by the respondents in light of the recommendations of the committee.
