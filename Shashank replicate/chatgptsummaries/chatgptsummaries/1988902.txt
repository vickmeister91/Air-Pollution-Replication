In the case of Sitaram and Another versus the State of U.P. and Another, the petitioners had filed an application under Section 482 Cr.P.C. seeking to quash the charge sheet and summoning order against them. The court order, passed on 2nd February 2010, pertains to this case.

The court observed that there were no sufficient cogent grounds for quashing the entire proceedings and directed the applicants to appear before the court concerned and apply for bail within one month. The court also provided certain conditions for granting relief up to the stage of framing of charges.

The petitioners had sought to quash the charge sheet and summoning order against them on the grounds that the allegations made against them were false and baseless. They had argued that the charges were politically motivated and had been filed to harass them.

The court, however, did not find merit in their arguments and held that there were no sufficient cogent grounds for quashing the entire proceedings. The court directed the applicants to appear before the court concerned and apply for bail within one month.

The court also provided certain conditions for granting relief up to the stage of framing of charges. The court directed that the applicants shall not tamper with the evidence or influence the witnesses in any manner. They shall also cooperate with the investigating agency and shall not leave the country without the permission of the court.

The court further directed that the applicants shall furnish a personal bond and surety bond in the sum of Rs. 50,000 each. They shall also deposit their passports with the court and shall not apply for a new passport without the permission of the court.

The court order is significant as it highlights the importance of following due process of law and cooperating with the investigating agency. The court has provided certain conditions for granting relief up to the stage of framing of charges, which are aimed at ensuring that the applicants do not tamper with the evidence or influence the witnesses in any manner.

The court has also directed the applicants to appear before the court concerned and apply for bail within one month. This is in line with the principles of natural justice and ensures that the applicants have an opportunity to defend themselves.
