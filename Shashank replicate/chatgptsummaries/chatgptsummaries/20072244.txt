The court order in question pertains to a case where Mr. Subba Rao was accused of not paying a sum of Rs. 38,57,343/- to The Water Base Limited for prawn feed products purchased on credit. The accused had issued a cheque for Rs. 4,07,500/- towards the payment, which was returned unpaid due to insufficient funds. The complainant had filed a case against the accused under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act.

The trial court had found the accused guilty and sentenced him to three months of simple imprisonment and directed him to pay compensation of Rs. 8,15,000/-. The accused had filed an appeal, and during the pendency of the appeal, he had filed a petition stating that the blank signed cheques given by him as security towards supply of prawn feeds had been misused by the complainant.

The appellate court had directed the accused to deposit a sum of Rs. 1,50,000/- within 20 days towards the compensation awarded by the trial court. The accused had filed a revision against this order, and the court modified the order, suspending the sentence passed by the trial court and directing the accused to execute a bond for a sum of Rs. 10,000/- with one security for a like sum. The court also directed the appellate court to dispose of the appeal on or before 31.01.2013.
