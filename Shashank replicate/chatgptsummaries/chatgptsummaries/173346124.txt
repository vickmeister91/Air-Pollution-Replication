The recent court order pertains to a writ petition filed by M/s. Chettinad Cement Corporation Ltd. seeking a mining lease for Patta lands measuring 32.61.0 hectares in Ariyalur District, Tamil Nadu. The petitioner had approached the court seeking a direction to the third respondent to execute a mining lease in their favor. The court has directed the third respondent to execute a mining lease in favor of the petitioner subject to the condition that no mining activity will be carried out until an environmental clearance certificate is obtained.

The petitioner, M/s. Chettinad Cement Corporation Ltd., had submitted an application for a mining lease for Patta lands measuring 32.61.0 hectares in Ariyalur District, Tamil Nadu. The application was submitted to the third respondent, the Director of Geology and Mining, Tamil Nadu. However, the third respondent had not taken any action on the application for a considerable period of time. The petitioner had approached the court seeking a direction to the third respondent to execute a mining lease in their favor.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, directed the third respondent to execute a mining lease in favor of the petitioner subject to the condition that no mining activity will be carried out until an environmental clearance certificate is obtained. The court also directed the petitioner to obtain the necessary environmental clearance certificate within a period of six months from the date of the order.

The court's decision is significant as it balances the interests of the petitioner and the environment. The court has recognized the importance of environmental clearance certificates and has made it a condition for the execution of the mining lease. This ensures that the mining activity will not have a detrimental impact on the environment.

The court's decision also highlights the importance of timely action by government authorities. The third respondent had not taken any action on the petitioner's application for a considerable period of time. The court's direction to execute the mining lease in favor of the petitioner is a reminder to government authorities to act promptly and efficiently.
