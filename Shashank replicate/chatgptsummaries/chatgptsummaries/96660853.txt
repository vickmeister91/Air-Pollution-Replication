The court order in question pertains to a criminal petition filed by Mahadeva against the State of Karnataka and four other individuals. The petition sought to quash an order passed by the III Additional District and Sessions Judge, Mandya, which rejected an application filed under Section 311 r/w Section 91 of Cr.P.C.

The prosecution had sought to recall PW35 for further cross-examination and to direct the Investigating Officer to produce the case diary dated 24.05.2014 to ascertain whether PW35 visited the spot of the incident after registering the case or prior to that. The trial court rejected the application, and the petitioner filed the criminal petition before the High Court.

The High Court dismissed the petition, finding no legal infirmity in the trial court's order. The court held that the trial court had exercised its discretion judiciously and had not committed any error in rejecting the application. The court also noted that the petitioner had not made out a case for interference in the exercise of the trial court's discretion.

The court observed that the power to recall a witness under Section 311 of the Cr.P.C. is discretionary and should be exercised judiciously. The court further noted that the purpose of recalling a witness is to enable the court to arrive at a just decision in the case. The court held that the trial court had considered the relevant factors and had come to a reasoned conclusion in rejecting the application.

The court also observed that the power to direct the production of documents under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. is also discretionary and should be exercised judiciously. The court held that the trial court had considered the relevance of the document sought to be produced and had come to a reasoned conclusion in rejecting the application.

The court further noted that the petitioner had not made out a case for interference in the exercise of the trial court's discretion. The court held that the petitioner had not shown that the trial court had acted arbitrarily or capriciously in rejecting the application.
