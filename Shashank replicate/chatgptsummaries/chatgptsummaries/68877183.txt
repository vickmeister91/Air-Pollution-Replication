The court order in question pertains to a petition filed by Yagyaraj Singh under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. against the order passed by the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Sidhi in Forest Crime No. 471/25. The applicant had filed an application under Section 457 of the Cr.P.C. for interim custody of the offending vehicle Tractor No. MP17D/5441 and Trolley No. MP17D/5442, which was dismissed by the Chief Judicial Magistrate. The court held that the Chief Judicial Magistrate had committed illegality in dismissing the application and set aside the impugned order. The offending vehicle was directed to be released on interim custody on certain terms and conditions.

The case at hand involves a forest crime, and the applicant had sought interim custody of the offending vehicle, which was dismissed by the Chief Judicial Magistrate. The applicant then filed a petition under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. challenging the order of the Chief Judicial Magistrate. The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, held that the Chief Judicial Magistrate had committed illegality in dismissing the application for interim custody of the offending vehicle.

The court observed that the Chief Judicial Magistrate had not considered the relevant provisions of the Cr.P.C. while dismissing the application. The court noted that Section 457 of the Cr.P.C. provides for the procedure for interim custody of property, and the Chief Judicial Magistrate had not followed this procedure while dismissing the application. The court held that the Chief Judicial Magistrate had committed illegality in dismissing the application and set aside the impugned order.

The court then directed that the offending vehicle be released on interim custody on certain terms and conditions. The court directed that the applicant shall furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 and two sureties of Rs. 25,000 each. The court also directed that the applicant shall deposit a sum of Rs. 1,00,000 as security for the release of the offending vehicle. The court further directed that the offending vehicle shall not be used for any illegal activity and shall be produced before the court as and when required.
