A recent court order in Bihar has set aside summon and warrant of arrest issued against a petitioner and a company for violating the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act. The case was filed by the Bihar State Pollution Control Board, and the petitioner's counsel argued that the court had issued the orders without taking cognizance of the offense, which was illegal.

The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act was enacted in 1974 to provide for the prevention and control of water pollution and the maintaining or restoring of wholesomeness of water. The act empowers the central and state pollution control boards to take measures for the prevention and control of water pollution.

In this case, the Bihar State Pollution Control Board had filed a case against the petitioner and a company for violating the act. The board had alleged that the petitioner and the company had discharged untreated effluent into a nearby river, which had caused pollution and harm to the environment.

The petitioner's counsel had argued that the court had issued summon and warrant of arrest without taking cognizance of the offense, which was illegal. The counsel had contended that the court had not followed the due process of law and had not given the petitioner an opportunity to be heard.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both sides, found that the orders were indeed issued without taking cognizance of the offense and set them aside. The court directed the court concerned to proceed with the matter in accordance with the law.
