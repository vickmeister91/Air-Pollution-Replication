The court order pertains to an application for anticipatory bail filed by Manoharsinh @ Rocky Girivarsinh Chawda in connection with an FIR registered against him for violating the Prohibition Act. The court has granted anticipatory bail to the applicant, considering the nature of the offense and the law laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court.

The applicant had approached the court seeking anticipatory bail in connection with an FIR registered against him for violating the Prohibition Act. The court, after considering the nature of the offense and the law laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court, granted anticipatory bail to the applicant.

The court observed that the applicant had a right to seek anticipatory bail under Section 438 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. The court also noted that the Hon'ble Apex Court had laid down certain guidelines to be followed while granting anticipatory bail in the case of Sushila Aggarwal and Others v. State of NCT of Delhi and Another.

The court further observed that the applicant had a right to seek anticipatory bail if he had reason to believe that he might be arrested for a non-bailable offense. The court noted that the applicant had satisfied the conditions laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court in the case of Siddharam Satlingappa Mhetre v. State of Maharashtra.

The court granted anticipatory bail to the applicant, subject to certain conditions. The applicant was directed to cooperate with the investigation and abide by certain conditions, including furnishing a personal bond of Rs. 10,000 and not leaving the country without the permission of the Trial Court.

The court also observed that the Investigating Agency had been given the right to apply for police remand of the applicant, but the applicant had the right to oppose such an application on merits. The court clarified that its observations while granting bail should not influence the trial court's decision.
