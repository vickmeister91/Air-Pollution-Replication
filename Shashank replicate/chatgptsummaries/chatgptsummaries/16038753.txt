Gupta had filed an application under Section 91 of the Code seeking production of certain documents from Bansal, which was dismissed by the trial court. The case revolves around a private complaint filed by Manish Bansal against Sunil Gupta. Gupta had filed an application under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure seeking production of certain documents from Bansal. However, the trial court dismissed Gupta's application, which led him to file a petition under Section 482 of the Code seeking to quash the order passed by the JMFC.

Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure empowers a court to issue a summons or order for the production of any document or other thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. In this case, Gupta had sought the production of certain documents from Bansal, which he believed were necessary for his defense.

However, the trial court dismissed Gupta's application, stating that the documents sought by him were not relevant to the case. The court found that the documents sought by Gupta were not necessary for the purposes of the trial and that their production would only delay the proceedings.

Gupta, dissatisfied with the trial court's decision, filed a petition under Section 482 of the Code seeking to quash the order passed by the JMFC. Section 482 of the Code empowers the High Court to exercise its inherent powers to prevent abuse of the process of any court or to secure the ends of justice.

The High Court, after hearing both parties, found no error in the trial court's decision to dismiss Gupta's application under Section 91 of the Code. The court held that the documents sought by Gupta were not relevant to the case and that their production would only delay the proceedings. The court also noted that Gupta had not made out a case for quashing the order passed by the JMFC.

In conclusion, the High Court dismissed Sunil Gupta's petition under Section 482 of the Code seeking to quash an order passed by the JMFC in a private complaint case filed against him by Manish Bansal.
