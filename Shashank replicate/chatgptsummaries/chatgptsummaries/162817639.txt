The case in question involves a petitioner who challenged an order passed in criminal revision No.76/16 by IIIrd ASJ, Indore. The order dismissed the revision petition preferred by the petitioner against the order dated 23.12.2016 passed in criminal case No.31652/13 by JMFC, Indore dismissing the application of the petitioner under section 91 of the Cr.P.C for calling certain documents related to income of the respondent/complainant. The petitioner had prayed for quashing the order dated 30.05.2017 passed in criminal revision No.76/16.

The learned revisional Court dismissed the revision petition as not maintainable. The petitioner argued that the order was invalid, illegal, and improper, and that there were grounds to interfere with the impugned order. However, the court dismissed the petition, stating that the order was valid, legal, and proper, and that there was no ground to interfere with the impugned order.

The petitioner had sought to call certain documents related to the income of the respondent/complainant under section 91 of the Cr.P.C. However, the JMFC, Indore had dismissed the application. The petitioner had then filed a revision petition against this order, which was dismissed by the IIIrd ASJ, Indore. The petitioner had then challenged this order in the present case.

The court noted that the petitioner had not challenged the order of the JMFC, Indore directly, but had instead filed a revision petition against the order of the IIIrd ASJ, Indore. The court held that this was not maintainable, as the petitioner should have challenged the order of the JMFC, Indore directly. The court also noted that the petitioner had not provided any grounds for interference with the impugned order.

The court further held that section 91 of the Cr.P.C. empowers a court to issue a summons or order to produce a document or thing if it is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. However, the court noted that the petitioner had not made out a case for the production of the documents in question. The court held that the petitioner had not shown how the documents were necessary or desirable for the purposes of the criminal case in question.

In conclusion, the court dismissed the petition, holding that the order dated 30.05.2017 passed in criminal revision No.76/16 was valid, legal, and proper, and that there was no ground to interfere with the impugned order. The court also held that the petitioner should have challenged the order of the JMFC, Indore directly, and that the petitioner had not made out a case for the production of the documents in question under section 91 of the Cr.P.C.
