The petitioner had sought modification of the condition by which he was directed to produce a bank guarantee of Rs. 1.5 lakh, stating that he was a poor man and could not afford to deposit the amount.

However, the court dismissed the petition, stating that the conditions enumerated in the previous order did not warrant interference. This decision was based on the fact that the petitioner had failed to provide any new evidence or arguments that would justify a modification of the previous order.

The case against Beer Bahadur Singh was registered under various sections of the IPC, Wild Life Protection Act, and Indian Forest Act. The charges against him included hunting and killing of wild animals, possession of illegal weapons, and destruction of forest land.
