The case at hand involves a dispute over the ownership of a vehicle that was allegedly purchased by the petitioner, Satya Narayan, from a registered owner who had taken a loan from the complainant and had not paid the installments. The Assistant Police Inspector, Economic Offences Branch, Nagpur, issued a notice under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. directing the petitioner to produce the vehicle before the respondent on a specified date. The petitioner challenged the validity of the notice, arguing that he was a bona fide purchaser and could not be made to suffer for the misdeeds of the registered owner.

The court, however, dismissed the writ petition, stating that the notice was valid and the petitioner had failed to show any provision of law that prohibited the respondent from issuing the notice for the purpose of investigation on a criminal complaint. The court noted that the notice was issued under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C., which empowers an investigating officer to issue a notice to any person in possession of any document or thing that may be required for the purpose of any investigation under the Code.

The court further observed that the petitioner had failed to produce any evidence to show that he was a bona fide purchaser of the vehicle. The court noted that the petitioner had not produced any document to show that he had purchased the vehicle from the registered owner, nor had he produced any evidence to show that he had paid the full consideration for the vehicle. The court also noted that the petitioner had not produced any evidence to show that he had made any efforts to verify the ownership of the vehicle before purchasing it.

The court held that the petitioner could not be allowed to take advantage of his own wrong and that he could not be permitted to retain the vehicle that was allegedly purchased from the registered owner who had defaulted on the loan. The court observed that the complainant had a right to recover the amount due to him and that the investigating officer had a duty to investigate the matter and recover the vehicle if it was found to be in the possession of the petitioner.
