The court order in question relates to a petition filed by the accused, Deepak, under section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.). The petition was filed against the rejection of his application under section 91 of the Cr.P.C. for summoning police records related to the loss of his cheque book. The court found no error in the rejection of the application but stated that such an application can be considered by the trial court during the defense stage. The petition was disposed of with this observation.

Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. empowers the High Court to exercise its inherent powers to prevent abuse of the process of any court or to secure the ends of justice. In this case, Deepak had filed a petition under this section against the rejection of his application under section 91 of the Cr.P.C. Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. empowers a court to issue a summons or order to produce any document or thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Cr.P.C.

Deepak had sought police records related to the loss of his cheque book. However, his application was rejected by the court. The court found that the police records were not necessary or desirable for the purposes of the investigation or trial. Deepak then filed a petition under section 482 of the Cr.P.C. against the rejection of his application.

The court, while disposing of the petition, observed that there was no error in the rejection of Deepak's application. However, the court also stated that such an application can be considered by the trial court during the defense stage. This means that if Deepak is able to establish during the trial that the police records are necessary or desirable for his defense, he can make an application to the trial court for summoning the police records.

The court's observation is in line with the principles of natural justice. The accused has the right to a fair trial and the right to defend himself. If the accused is able to establish that certain documents or things are necessary or desirable for his defense, the court should consider his application for summoning such documents or things.

In conclusion, the court order in question pertains to a petition filed by the accused, Deepak, under section 482 of the Cr.P.C. against the rejection of his application under section 91 of the Cr.P.C. for summoning police records related to the loss of his cheque book. The court found no error in the rejection but stated that such an application can be considered by the trial court during the defense stage. The court's observation is in line with the principles of natural justice and ensures that the accused has the right to a fair trial and the right to defend himself.
