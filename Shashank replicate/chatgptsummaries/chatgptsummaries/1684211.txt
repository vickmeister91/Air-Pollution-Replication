The court order in question pertains to a civil writ petition filed by Sher Singh in public interest against respondents No. 1 to 4. The petitioner sought a writ of mandamus directing them to take action against respondents No. 5 to 8, who were draining chemical water from dye units into land owned by the Gram Panchayat. The petitioner alleged that the land's fertility had been adversely affected, and agriculture operations had become impossible.

The Haryana State Pollution Control Board filed an Action Taken Report stating that six units responsible for draining water onto the land owned by the Panchayat were issued show cause notices for closure. The court directed the Pollution Control Board to prevent draining of effluents by the units that are already shut down and may be operating illegally, as well as from other units that are violating the statutory norms on the subject.
