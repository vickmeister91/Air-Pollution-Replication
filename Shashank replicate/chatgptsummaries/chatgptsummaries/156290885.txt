The case of Narsingh Lal vs. Chief Metropolitan Magistrate No. 16, Jaipur Metro, revolves around a petition filed by the petitioner, Narsingh Lal, against the order passed by the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate No. 16, Jaipur Metro. The order dismissed his application under Section 91 of CrPC. The petitioner's counsel argued that the court below erred in dismissing the application since the conveyance deed required to be produced was necessary, and the description of the document was not available with the petitioner. However, the court found that the required particulars were omitted from the application, and it was open to the petitioner to file a certified copy of the document after obtaining it, which was not done. Therefore, the petition was dismissed.

Section 91 of the Criminal Procedure Code (CrPC) empowers a court to issue a summons or order to produce a document or thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. The section provides that any court may, at any stage of such proceedings, summon any person as a witness or order any person to produce any document or thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of such proceedings.

In this case, the petitioner had filed an application under Section 91 of CrPC, seeking the production of a conveyance deed. The court below dismissed the application on the ground that the required particulars were omitted from the application, and it was open to the petitioner to file a certified copy of the document after obtaining it, which was not done.

The petitioner's counsel argued that the conveyance deed was necessary for the purposes of the proceedings, and the court below erred in dismissing the application. However, the court found that the petitioner had not provided the necessary particulars in the application, and it was open to him to file a certified copy of the document after obtaining it, which he had not done.

The court's decision in this case highlights the importance of providing all necessary particulars when filing an application under Section 91 of CrPC. It also emphasizes the need to comply with the court's orders and directions, including the production of necessary documents or things.

In conclusion, the court's decision in the case of Narsingh Lal vs. Chief Metropolitan Magistrate No. 16, Jaipur Metro, serves as a reminder to litigants and their counsel to ensure that all necessary particulars are provided when filing an application under Section 91 of CrPC. It also underscores the importance of complying with the court's orders and directions, including the production of necessary documents or things.
