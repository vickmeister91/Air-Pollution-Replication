The Karnataka State Pollution Control Board issued a closure notice to M/S. City Light Industries, which prompted the company to file a writ petition against the State of Karnataka. The petition challenged the closure notice and sought a direction to consider their application for a lease to run their stone crushing unit in a safer zone. The petitioner also requested permission to continue the stone crushing unit in the present location until such time the application is considered.

The court heard the petition and directed the respondent authorities to consider the petitioner's application in accordance with the Karnataka Regulation of Stone Crushers Act. The court ordered the authorities to pass necessary orders within 15 days. However, the petitioner was not granted permission to carry on their stone crushing activity in the present location until then.

The court's decision was based on the fact that the petitioner's application for a lease to run their stone crushing unit in a safer zone was pending. The court recognized the importance of the Karnataka Regulation of Stone Crushers Act, which regulates the stone crushing industry in the state. The act aims to prevent pollution caused by stone crushing units and to promote the safe operation of such units.

The court's decision was a balanced one, taking into account the interests of both the petitioner and the state. The court recognized the need for the petitioner to continue their business while their application for a lease in a safer zone was being considered. At the same time, the court upheld the importance of the Karnataka Regulation of Stone Crushers Act in ensuring the safe operation of stone crushing units and preventing pollution.
