The Patna High Court has issued an order directing the Chief Secretary of the State of Bihar to form a committee comprising all three departments to oversee the execution of work related to drainage and removal of bio waste in the city of Darbhanga. The court has instructed the committee to submit a report on the progress of the work. The court has also directed the authorities to file their affidavits placing on record the decision taken by the committee within 8 weeks and listed the matter for hearing on 27.04.2020.

The court order is a result of a petition filed by concerned citizens of Darbhanga, who had raised concerns about the poor state of drainage and the accumulation of bio waste in the city. The petitioners had sought the intervention of the court to ensure that the authorities take necessary steps to address the issue.

The court has taken note of the seriousness of the issue and has directed the Chief Secretary to form a committee comprising all three departments, namely the Public Health Engineering Department, the Urban Development and Housing Department, and the Rural Development Department. The committee will be responsible for monitoring the execution of work related to drainage and removal of bio waste in the city of Darbhanga.

The court has also directed the committee to submit a report on the progress of the work to the court. This will ensure that the court is kept informed about the progress made by the authorities in addressing the issue. The court has further directed the authorities to file their affidavits placing on record the decision taken by the committee within 8 weeks.

The court order is a significant step towards addressing the issue of poor drainage and the accumulation of bio waste in the city of Darbhanga. The court has taken a proactive approach in ensuring that the authorities take necessary steps to address the issue. The formation of a committee comprising all three departments will ensure that the issue is addressed in a comprehensive manner.
