The Jharkhand High Court has granted anticipatory bail to Md. Shams Tabrej and Irfan Shahid Shams, who were apprehending their arrest in connection with a complaint case registered under the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act and the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act. The court directed the petitioners to surrender in the court within four weeks and furnish a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 each with two sureties of the like amount each. The court also ordered the petitioners to deposit Rs. 30,000 each on the date of surrender, which would be subject to the result of the case.

The case pertains to a complaint filed against the petitioners for allegedly violating the provisions of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act and the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act. The complaint was filed by the Jharkhand State Pollution Control Board (JSPCB) alleging that the petitioners had set up a stone crusher unit in violation of the environmental norms.

The petitioners had approached the High Court seeking anticipatory bail apprehending their arrest in connection with the complaint. The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, granted anticipatory bail to the petitioners subject to certain conditions.

The court directed the petitioners to surrender in the court within four weeks and furnish a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 each with two sureties of the like amount each. The court also ordered the petitioners to deposit Rs. 30,000 each on the date of surrender, which would be subject to the result of the case.

The court observed that the petitioners had no criminal antecedents and were ready to cooperate with the investigation. The court also noted that the petitioners had already obtained the necessary clearances from the concerned authorities for setting up the stone crusher unit.

The court further observed that the allegations against the petitioners were not of a serious nature and that the JSPCB had not been able to produce any material to show that the petitioners had caused any harm to the environment.

The court also took note of the fact that the petitioners had already deposited an amount of Rs. 5 lakhs with the JSPCB as a bank guarantee towards the environmental compensation.

In light of these facts, the court granted anticipatory bail to the petitioners subject to the conditions mentioned above.
