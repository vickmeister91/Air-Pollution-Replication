The case in question involves a Criminal Original Petition filed by the petitioners under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. The petitioners sought to set aside an order passed by the IX Additional Special Judge for CBI Cases in Chennai in Crl.M.P.No.3244 of 2014 dated 17th September 2014. The petitioners were seeking to obtain documents referred to in Ex.P.88 Valuation Report, which they claimed were public documents.

The court considered the arguments put forth by the petitioners and found that the documents in question were indeed public documents. As such, the court allowed the petition and directed the court below to follow the usual procedure for obtaining public documents.

The court's decision in this case is significant as it reaffirms the importance of public access to information. The court recognized that the documents in question were public documents and therefore should be made available to the petitioners. This decision is in line with the principles of transparency and accountability, which are essential for a functioning democracy.

Furthermore, the court's decision highlights the importance of following proper procedures when seeking access to public documents. The court directed the court below to follow the usual procedure for obtaining public documents, emphasizing the need for adherence to established legal procedures.

Overall, the court's decision in this case is a positive development for transparency and accountability in the legal system. By recognizing the importance of public access to information and emphasizing the need for proper procedures, the court has taken an important step towards ensuring that justice is served in a fair and transparent manner.
