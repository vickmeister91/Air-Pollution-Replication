M/s. Shakti Bricks, a registered brick kiln industry, filed a writ application seeking protection from private parties who were allegedly obstructing their business activities. The petitioner had obtained necessary permissions to carry on business, but the private parties were creating illegal impediments. The District Administration and police authorities were supposed to protect the petitioner's right to carry on business, but they had not taken any action. 

The court heard arguments from both sides, including intervenors, and ultimately ordered the Superintendent of Police, Bhadrak, to provide adequate police protection to the petitioner for carrying on their trade or business. The court order states that the petitioner is entitled to carry on his business as he has obtained necessary permissions and there is no legal impediment. The police are directed to provide necessary assistance to ensure that the petitioner's business is not impeded by any obstruction on the public road. 

The court emphasized that no individual or group has the right to take the law into their own hands and impede the lawful business of any citizen. The writ petition is allowed with the aforementioned directions. 

The court's decision is a significant victory for M/s. Shakti Bricks, as it ensures that their business activities will not be obstructed by private parties. The court's order also highlights the importance of police protection for businesses that have obtained necessary permissions to carry on their trade.
