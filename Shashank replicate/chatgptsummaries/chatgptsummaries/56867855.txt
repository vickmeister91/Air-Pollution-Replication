The Gujarat High Court recently passed a court order in response to a writ petition filed by Kheti Vikas Seva Trust and another party against the State of Gujarat and four other parties. The petitioners sought a writ of mandamus or any other appropriate writ, order or direction directing the respondent authorities to take effective steps against the respondent companies who have failed to comply with the conditional environmental clearance. 

During the hearing, the court was taken through various environmental clearance certificates and conditions imposed on the respondent companies. The court noted that the petitioners had not approached the Ministry of Environment and Forest or the Green Tribunal for their grievances and that an efficacious remedy is provided under the provisions of the National Environment Appellate Act 1997 and the National Green Tribunal Act 2010.

The court order states that under the National Green Tribunal Act, 2010, petitioners can present their grievances and evidence, and the concerned authority or tribunal can pass appropriate orders after examining the evidence. The present appeal in the form of a Public Interest Litigation is not maintainable, and the petition is dismissed. The petitioners have an efficacious remedy under the provisions of the Act and can avail of it in accordance with the law. There shall be no order as to costs.

The court order highlights the importance of following the proper legal procedures when seeking redressal for environmental grievances. The National Green Tribunal Act, 2010 provides for an efficacious remedy for such grievances, and it is important for petitioners to approach the appropriate authorities or tribunals for relief. 

The court also emphasized the need for companies to comply with the conditions imposed on them during the environmental clearance process. Failure to comply with these conditions can have serious environmental consequences, and it is the responsibility of the authorities to take effective steps against such non-compliance. 

Overall, the court order serves as a reminder of the importance of environmental protection and the need for all stakeholders to work together to ensure that our natural resources are preserved for future generations. It is important for companies to comply with environmental regulations, and for citizens to approach the appropriate authorities for redressal of environmental grievances. The National Green Tribunal Act, 2010 provides an efficacious remedy for such grievances, and it is important for all stakeholders to be aware of their rights and responsibilities under this Act.
