The court order in question pertains to a writ petition filed by Satyendra Prasad Gupta, who runs a small-scale industry manufacturing aluminium and brass utensils in Gaya, Bihar. The Bihar State Pollution Control Board had issued a letter directing Gupta to close down his unit for operating without obtaining consent and taking NOC from the Board. Gupta's counsel argued that several other small-scale industries in the area were allowed to run without any such permission.

The court, after hearing both sides, directed Gupta to avail the statutory remedy provided under Section 31 of Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981, within a month. The court also disposed of the writ petition with this observation.

The court's decision is significant as it highlights the importance of obtaining necessary permissions and consents before starting any industrial activity.
