In a recent court order, the court declined to entertain a petition filed by AAA Guest House against an order passed by the Tribunal. The court stated that the petitioner's contentions could be addressed through remedies provided under the National Green Tribunal Act, 2010. The petitioner was permitted to file a review petition or appeal before the Supreme Court.

The case involved a dispute between AAA Guest House and the Tribunal over an order passed by the Tribunal. The petitioner had challenged the order on the grounds that it was arbitrary, illegal, and without jurisdiction. The petitioner had also contended that the order violated the principles of natural justice and was passed without giving the petitioner an opportunity to be heard.

However, the court held that the petitioner's contentions could be addressed through remedies provided under the National Green Tribunal Act, 2010. The court observed that the Act provides for a comprehensive mechanism for the resolution of environmental disputes. The Act provides for the establishment of the National Green Tribunal, which has the power to hear and decide disputes relating to environmental issues.

The court further observed that the Act provides for a hierarchy of remedies, which includes the filing of review petitions and appeals before the Supreme Court. The court held that the petitioner could avail of these remedies to challenge the order passed by the Tribunal.
