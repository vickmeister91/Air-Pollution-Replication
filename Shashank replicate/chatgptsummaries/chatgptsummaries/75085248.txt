The court order in question pertains to a petition filed under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. for quashing an order passed by the Sessions Judge, Karera, Distt. Shivpuri. The petitioner's tractor trolley was seized by forest officials for carrying illegal sand, and an application for release of the vehicle was dismissed by the Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Karera. The criminal revision filed against this dismissal was also dismissed.

The petitioner argued that the impugned order was illegal as it overlooked the principle laid down in Madhukar Rao's case. The court allowed the petition and directed that the vehicle be returned to the petitioner on the petitioner's furnishing a bank guarantee to the tune of Rs.1,00,000/- to be kept alive during the pendency of the criminal case and an undertaking that he shall not alienate the vehicle till the decision of the criminal case and shall produce it before the Court as and when required.
