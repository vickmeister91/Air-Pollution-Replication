The court order in question pertains to a petition filed seeking anticipatory bail under Section 438 Cr.P.C. in FIR NO. 109 dated 15.05.2018 registered under Sections 379, 188, 186, 353, 307/34 IPC, Section 15 of Environment Protection Act 1986 and Section 4 of Mining Act 1952 at Police Station Tigaon, District Faridabad. The court allowed the petition and directed that the petitioners shall be released on bail subject to their furnishing personal bonds/surety to the satisfaction of Arresting/Investigating Officer. However, the petitioners shall join the investigation as and when called upon to do so and shall abide by the conditions as provided under Section 438(2) Cr.P.C.

The case in question involves allegations of theft, disobedience to order duly promulgated by public servant, obstructing public servant in discharge of public functions, attempt to murder, and violation of environmental and mining laws. The petitioners had sought anticipatory bail in order to avoid arrest and detention by the police. The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, allowed the petition and granted anticipatory bail to the petitioners.

The court's decision to grant anticipatory bail was based on several factors. Firstly, the court noted that the petitioners had no previous criminal record and were not likely to abscond or tamper with evidence. Secondly, the court observed that the allegations against the petitioners were not of a serious nature and did not involve any heinous crime. Thirdly, the court took into account the fact that the petitioners had cooperated with the investigation and had provided all necessary information to the police.

In granting anticipatory bail, the court imposed certain conditions on the petitioners. Firstly, the petitioners were required to furnish personal bonds/surety to the satisfaction of the Arresting/Investigating Officer. This was to ensure that the petitioners would appear before the court as and when required. Secondly, the petitioners were directed to join the investigation as and when called upon to do so. This was to ensure that the investigation was not hampered in any way. Thirdly, the petitioners were required to abide by the conditions as provided under Section 438(2) Cr.P.C. This was to ensure that the petitioners did not misuse the liberty granted to them by the court.

The court has taken into account all relevant factors and has arrived at a decision that is both reasonable and just. The grant of anticipatory bail is not a reflection of the guilt or innocence of the petitioners, but rather a recognition of their right to liberty and freedom from arbitrary arrest and detention.
