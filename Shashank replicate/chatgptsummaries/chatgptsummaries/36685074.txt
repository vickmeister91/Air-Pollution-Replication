The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Shankar @ Shankarshah, who was arrested on 06/12/2016 for possession of the skin of a Panther, which is a violation of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The applicant's counsel argued that he is innocent and has no criminal antecedents, and that the trial will take considerable time. The court allowed the bail application and ordered the release of the applicant on a personal bond of Rs.50,000/- with a solvent surety in the like amount to ensure his presence before the trial court on all hearing dates and compliance with the conditions enumerated in sub-section (3) of Section 437 of Cr.P.C.

The case of Shankar @ Shankarshah is one that highlights the importance of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, which seeks to protect the country's wildlife and their habitats. However, the court's decision to grant bail to Shankar is also significant. The court has recognized that Shankar is innocent until proven guilty, and has allowed him to be released on bail while the trial is ongoing. This decision is in line with the principles of justice and fairness, and ensures that Shankar's rights are protected.

The court's decision to order Shankar's release on a personal bond of Rs.50,000/- with a solvent surety in the like amount is also noteworthy. This condition ensures that Shankar will appear before the trial court on all hearing dates, and will comply with the conditions enumerated in sub-section (3) of Section 437 of Cr.P.C. This section lays down the conditions that must be fulfilled by a person who has been granted bail, and includes provisions such as reporting to the police station at specified intervals, refraining from committing any offence while on bail, and surrendering his passport if he has one.
