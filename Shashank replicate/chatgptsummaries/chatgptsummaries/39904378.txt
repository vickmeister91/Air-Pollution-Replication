The Allahabad High Court recently dismissed a writ petition filed by Indrajeet Puri against the State of Uttar Pradesh through the Secretary Control of Pollution and another. The petition was filed to question the correctness of the order passed by the Regional Officer, Uttar Pradesh Pollution Control Board, Saharanpur, which directed the petitioner to close down industrial operation in a residential area being in violation of the directions issued by the Government of Uttar Pradesh and also by the court in a petition for writ.

The petitioner had argued that the order passed by the Regional Officer was arbitrary and illegal, and that it violated the principles of natural justice. The petitioner also contended that the order was passed without giving him an opportunity to be heard, and that it was based on incorrect facts and assumptions.

However, the court found no merit in the petitioner's arguments and dismissed the writ petition. The court observed that the Regional Officer had passed the order after considering all the relevant facts and circumstances of the case, and that the order was in accordance with the law and the directions issued by the Government of Uttar Pradesh and the court in a previous petition for writ.

The court also noted that the petitioner had not produced any evidence to show that the order was passed without giving him an opportunity to be heard, or that it was based on incorrect facts and assumptions. The court held that the petitioner had failed to make out a case for interference in the matter, and that the writ petition was liable to be dismissed.

The court's decision is significant as it reaffirms the importance of complying with the directions issued by the Government of Uttar Pradesh and the court in matters relating to pollution control. The court's decision also highlights the need for industrial operators to ensure that their operations are in compliance with the relevant laws and regulations, and that they do not cause harm to the environment or to the health and safety of the people living in the vicinity.
