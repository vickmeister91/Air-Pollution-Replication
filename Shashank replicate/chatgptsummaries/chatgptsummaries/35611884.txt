The Patna High Court has granted anticipatory bail to Umesh Kumar, who was accused of illegal mining and transportation of sand. The bail was granted subject to the condition that he surrenders before the court within six weeks and furnishes bail bonds of Rs.10,000 with two sureties. The court will verify his criminal antecedents before accepting the bail bond.

The case against Umesh Kumar was related to illegal mining and transportation of sand. The police had registered a case against him under various sections of the Indian Penal Code and the Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957. The police had alleged that Umesh Kumar was involved in illegal mining and transportation of sand from the Gandak river in Bihar.

Umesh Kumar had approached the Patna High Court seeking anticipatory bail. His counsel had argued that he was innocent and had been falsely implicated in the case. The counsel had also argued that Umesh Kumar was a law-abiding citizen and had no criminal antecedents.

The Patna High Court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, granted anticipatory bail to Umesh Kumar. The court observed that the allegations against him were not of a serious nature and that he was entitled to bail. However, the court also imposed certain conditions on the bail.

The court directed Umesh Kumar to surrender before the court within six weeks and furnish bail bonds of Rs.10,000 with two sureties. The court also directed the police to verify his criminal antecedents before accepting the bail bond. The court further directed Umesh Kumar to cooperate with the police in the investigation and not to tamper with the evidence.

The grant of anticipatory bail to Umesh Kumar by the Patna High Court is significant as it highlights the importance of the right to bail. The right to bail is a fundamental right guaranteed under the Indian Constitution. It is a right that ensures that a person is not deprived of his liberty without due process of law.
