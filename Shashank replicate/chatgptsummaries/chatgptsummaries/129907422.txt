The case at hand involves a writ petition filed by a former member of the Tamil Nadu State Legislative Assembly seeking a writ of mandamus directing the respondents to lay a road from Pudhurpalapatti to Valkuzhipatti. The proposed road was to be laid in the Kollimalai hills, which are part of Eastern Ghats and have 16 reserve forests. The petitioner argued that the road was necessary for poor villagers and tribals to access hospitals, schools, and other public places.

However, respondent No.5, who is the District Forest Officer, raised concerns about the ecological sensitivity of the area and the need to comply with various laws, including the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, and the Hill Area Preservation Act Committee. The Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, is a central legislation that regulates diversion of forest land for non-forestry purposes. The Hill Area Preservation Act Committee is a state-level committee that regulates development activities in hill areas.

The court noted that the proposed road would pass through reserve forests and that the respondents had not obtained the necessary clearances under the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, and the Hill Area Preservation Act Committee. The court also noted that respondent No.5 had raised objections to the proposed road on ecological grounds.

The court directed respondents No.1 and 3 to take appropriate action in accordance with the law after considering all the materials available on record, including the objections raised by respondent No.5. The court also directed the respondents to ensure that the proposed road did not violate any of the provisions of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, and the Hill Area Preservation Act Committee.

The court's decision highlights the importance of complying with environmental laws and regulations when undertaking development activities in ecologically sensitive areas. The court recognized the need to balance the interests of development with the need to protect the environment and the rights of local communities.
