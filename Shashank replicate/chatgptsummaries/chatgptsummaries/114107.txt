The court order pertains to a bail application filed under Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure by two accused persons in a case related to the shooting of an elephant in a reserve forest. The accused were charged under Sections 9 and 39 of the Wild Life Protection Act and Section 27(1)(e)(iv) of the Kerala Forest Act. The court dismissed the bail application as there was no change in circumstances since the previous dismissal of a similar application.

The case in question involves the shooting of an elephant in a reserve forest, which is a protected area under the Wild Life Protection Act and the Kerala Forest Act. The accused were charged under Sections 9 and 39 of the Wild Life Protection Act, which deal with hunting and poaching of wild animals, and Section 27(1)(e)(iv) of the Kerala Forest Act, which deals with the possession of weapons in a forest area without a license.

The accused had filed a bail application under Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, which allows for the grant of bail to a person who is in custody pending trial. The court had previously dismissed a similar bail application filed by the accused, and the current application was filed on the grounds of changed circumstances.

However, the court found that there was no change in circumstances since the previous dismissal of the bail application. The court noted that the accused had not provided any new evidence or arguments to support their claim for bail.
