Naresh Narula, who was accused of possessing 96 Shahtoosh Shawls made from the wool/hair of Tibetan Antelope, which is banned under Schedule-I of the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972, has been acquitted of charges under Section 49/49B of the Act. The prosecution failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt, and the defense argued that the case property was planted on the accused. The court considered the relevant provisions of the Act and the testimony of witnesses before acquitting the accused. The court also noted that failure to comply with the provisions of the Code of Criminal Procedure in respect of search and seizure does not vitiate the trial under the Act.

The court order further states that failure to comply with the provisions of Cr.P.C in respect of search and seizure does not vitiate the trial, but conducting a search and seizure in violation of statutory safeguards would be violative of reasonable, fair and just procedure. The court found that there were certain lacunas in the present case, and the prosecution failed to prove its case against the accused beyond reasonable doubt. Therefore, the accused is acquitted for the offence punishable under Section 51 of Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972 for the contravention of Section 49 / 49 B (1) of the Wildlife (Protection) Act. The case property, if any, is to be confiscated to the state.

The accused has been directed to furnish bail bonds in the sum of Rs.20,000/- in terms of Section 437 A of Cr.P.C. The file will be consigned to Record Room after due compliance.
