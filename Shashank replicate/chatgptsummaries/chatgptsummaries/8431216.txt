The court has granted bail to Prakash Narre in a forest offence case under the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. Narre was accused of being involved in the killing of a tiger and a pangolin. The prosecution alleged that Narre was taking the tiger skin on a motor-cycle to a person for some rituals when it was snatched away by a co-accused. The court considered Narre's limited role in the crime and the fact that he had been in custody since November 2016, and granted him bail on a personal bond of Rs. 40,000 with one solvent surety.
