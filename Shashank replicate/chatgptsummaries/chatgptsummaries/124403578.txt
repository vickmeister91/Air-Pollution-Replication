The Patna High Court recently granted anticipatory bail to Lalchand Chaudhary, who was apprehensive of being arrested for alleged offenses under various sections of the Indian Penal Code and the Bihar Mines and Minerals Concession Rules, 1972, and the Prevention of Pollution Act, 1986. The petitioner claimed to have been falsely implicated and had no connection with the subject truck or sand. The court ordered his release on bail of Rs. 10,000 with two sureties and imposed certain conditions, including cooperation with the investigation and physical presence in court during the trial.

The case involved allegations of illegal sand mining and transportation in Bihar. The state government had launched a crackdown on illegal sand mining and transportation, and several cases had been registered against individuals and companies involved in these activities. Lalchand Chaudhary was one of the individuals who had been named in these cases.

The petitioner had approached the Patna High Court seeking anticipatory bail, as he feared that he would be arrested by the police. He claimed that he had been falsely implicated in the case and had no connection with the subject truck or sand. He also argued that he had a clean record and was not involved in any illegal activities.

The court considered the petitioner's arguments and observed that the allegations against him were not supported by any concrete evidence. The court also noted that the petitioner had cooperated with the investigation and had appeared before the police whenever he was summoned. The court, therefore, granted anticipatory bail to the petitioner and ordered his release on bail of Rs. 10,000 with two sureties.

The court also imposed certain conditions on the petitioner, including cooperation with the investigation and physical presence in court during the trial.
