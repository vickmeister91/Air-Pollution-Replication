The court has granted regular bail to Gaya Prasad, who had been in custody since April 13, 2016, for allegedly hunting wild boar and preparing meat in his house in violation of the Wild Life Protection Act. The applicant had no criminal record and was a permanent resident of Rampuri village. The court allowed his release on bail upon furnishing a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 with two solvent surities to ensure his presence before the trial court on all hearing dates.

The Wild Life Protection Act is a law that aims to protect wildlife and their habitats. It prohibits hunting, poaching, and trading of wildlife and their products. Violation of this law can result in imprisonment and fines. In this case, Gaya Prasad was accused of hunting wild boar and preparing meat in his house, which is a violation of the Wild Life Protection Act.

Gaya Prasad had been in custody since April 13, 2016, and had no criminal record. He was a permanent resident of Rampuri village. The court considered these factors and granted him regular bail. Regular bail is granted when the accused is released from custody during the pendency of the trial. The accused is required to furnish a personal bond and solvent surities to ensure his presence before the trial court on all hearing dates.

The court's decision to grant bail to Gaya Prasad is significant as it highlights the importance of considering the accused's background and circumstances while deciding on bail. The court took into account Gaya Prasad's lack of criminal record and his status as a permanent resident of Rampuri village.
