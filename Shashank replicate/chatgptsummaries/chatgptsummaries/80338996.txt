The court order in question pertains to a case where Fr. Siby Njavallikunel is the first accused in a case of violating the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972. The petitioner seeks to quash proceedings against him on the grounds that no offense is involved as per the report submitted by the Range Officer. The court examines whether the Range Officer is competent to file a complaint under Section 55 of the Act and concludes that the petitioner and other accused can raise this issue in the court below at the time of framing charges against them. The court allows the petitioner and other accused to plead discharge in absentia and take all plea/defense before the learned Magistrate in the course of framing charges and at the time of trial.
