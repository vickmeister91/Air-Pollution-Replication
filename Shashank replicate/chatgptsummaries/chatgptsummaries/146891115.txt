The Patna High Court has recently issued an order regarding the release of two accused individuals, Sonu Choudhary and Nirmal Kumar, who were charged under various sections of the Indian Penal Code, the Bihar Mines and Mineral Control Regulation Act, and the Pollution Act. The court has granted them bail on a bond of Rs. 10,000 each, along with two sureties of the same amount. The order also directs the accused to surrender before the lower court within four weeks from the date of receipt or production of a copy of the order.

The accused were arrested by the police and charged under various sections of the Indian Penal Code, including criminal conspiracy, cheating, and forgery, as well as under the Bihar Mines and Mineral Control Regulation Act and the Pollution Act.

The accused had filed a bail application before the Patna High Court, which was heard by Justice Anil Kumar Upadhyay. After hearing the arguments of both the prosecution and the defense, the court granted bail to the accused on certain conditions.

The court has directed the accused to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 10,000 each, along with two sureties of the same amount. The sureties must be local residents and should be able to furnish their identity proof. The accused have also been directed to surrender their passports, if any, and to not leave the country without the permission of the court.
