The court order pertains to a bail application filed by Sohanlal Biyaram Bisnoi under Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, for offences punishable under Sections 65 E, 116 B and 81 of Prohibition Act. The court has considered the nature of the offence, completion of investigation, and lack of antecedents, and has granted regular bail to the applicant on certain conditions, including a personal bond of Rs. 25,000 and surrendering his passport.

The case involves the violation of the Prohibition Act, which prohibits the manufacture, sale, and consumption of alcohol in certain areas. The applicant, Sohanlal Biyaram Bisnoi, was charged with offences under Sections 65 E, 116 B and 81 of the Act. Section 65 E deals with the possession of liquor in excess of the prescribed limit, Section 116 B deals with the punishment for abetment of an offence, and Section 81 deals with the punishment for contravention of any rule made under the Act.

The court has taken into consideration the nature of the offence and the fact that the investigation has been completed. The court has also noted that the applicant has no antecedents and is not likely to abscond. Based on these factors, the court has granted regular bail to the applicant.

However, the court has imposed certain conditions on the grant of bail. The applicant has been directed to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 25,000 and to surrender his passport. The court has also directed the trial court to complete the case within three months. The applicant has been restrained from leaving the revenue limits of District Mehsana.

The court has made it clear that its observations at this stage should not influence the trial court's decision. The trial court will have to consider the evidence and decide the case on its merits. The grant of bail does not mean that the applicant is innocent. It only means that he will be released from custody pending the trial.
