The court order pertains to a writ petition filed by Saharanpur Mines Management Services Pvt. Ltd. The petitioner had sought relief against an order passed by the Uttar Pradesh Pollution Control Board (UPPCB) directing the closure of its stone crushing unit. The UPPCB had found that the unit was operating without the necessary consent under the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981.

The court order states that the petitioner must file an appeal with the appellate authority under the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The appellate authority has been constituted since April 14, 2017, and the petitioner is free to make an application before it for expediting the hearing. The writ petition is disposed of accordingly.

The Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981, is a legislation enacted by the Indian Parliament to provide for the prevention, control, and abatement of air pollution. The Act empowers the Central and State Pollution Control Boards to take measures to prevent and control air pollution. The Act also provides for the establishment of appellate authorities to hear appeals against orders passed by the Pollution Control Boards.

In this case, the UPPCB had directed the closure of the petitioner's stone crushing unit as it was found to be operating without the necessary consent under the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The petitioner had challenged this order by filing a writ petition in the court.

The court order directs the petitioner to file an appeal with the appellate authority under the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The appellate authority has been constituted since April 14, 2017, and the petitioner is free to make an application before it for expediting the hearing. The writ petition is disposed of accordingly.
