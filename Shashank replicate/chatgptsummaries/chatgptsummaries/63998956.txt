The Karnataka High Court recently issued a court order in response to a writ petition filed by M/S Arkavathi Kumudavathi Nadi against the State of Karnataka. The petitioner sought a writ in the nature of mandamus to direct the authorities not to alienate or divert Hesaraghatta Natural Grasslands and to declare the lands as protected areas under the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The court ordered that status quo be maintained until a final decision is taken by the State Government and conveyed to the petitioner. The petition was disposed of with no order as to cost.

The court order is significant as it pertains to the protection of natural grasslands in the state of Karnataka. The petitioner had argued that the Hesaraghatta Natural Grasslands were an important ecological resource and that their protection was necessary to ensure the survival of several species of flora and fauna. The petitioner had also argued that the lands were being threatened by various development activities, including the construction of a film city and a science city.

The court order is a victory for the petitioner and for those who have been advocating for the protection of natural resources in the state. The court's decision to maintain status quo until a final decision is taken by the State Government is a positive step towards ensuring that the Hesaraghatta Natural Grasslands are not destroyed or degraded. The court's decision also sends a strong message to developers and other stakeholders that the protection of natural resources is a priority for the state.

The court's decision to declare the Hesaraghatta Natural Grasslands as protected areas under the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972 is also significant. This will ensure that the lands are protected from any further development activities that could harm the ecological balance of the area. The Wild Life Protection Act, 1972 is a comprehensive legislation that provides for the protection of wild animals and plants and their habitats. The act also provides for the establishment of protected areas for the conservation of wildlife.

The court's decision is also important as it recognizes the importance of public participation in environmental decision-making. The court has allowed the petitioner or any other person with a genuine public interest to approach the court when a decision is taken by the State Government.
