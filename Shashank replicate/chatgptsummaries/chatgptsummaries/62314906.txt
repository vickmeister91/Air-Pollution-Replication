The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Santosh Pardi under section 439 of the Cr.P.C. for a case registered against him in 2010 for poaching a black buck and violating the Indian Wild Life Protection Act and the Arms Act. The court allowed the bail application on the grounds that the trial is not likely to conclude in the near future and prolonged pre-trial detention is against the concept of liberty. The applicant was directed to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 1,00,000 with two solvent and local sureties of Rs. 50,000 each. The court also imposed six conditions for the applicant to comply with, failing which the bail order would stand cancelled.

The case against Santosh Pardi dates back to 2010 when he was accused of poaching a black buck and violating the Indian Wild Life Protection Act and the Arms Act. The case has been pending for over a decade and the trial is not likely to conclude in the near future. In light of this, Santosh Pardi filed a bail application under section 439 of the Cr.P.C. seeking release from pre-trial detention.

The court, after considering the facts and circumstances of the case, allowed the bail application. The court observed that prolonged pre-trial detention is against the concept of liberty and that the trial is not likely to conclude in the near future. The court directed the applicant to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 1,00,000 with two solvent and local sureties of Rs. 50,000 each.

In addition to this, the court also imposed six conditions for the applicant to comply with. These conditions include that the applicant shall not leave the jurisdiction of the court without prior permission, shall not tamper with evidence or influence witnesses, shall attend court on all dates of hearing, shall not commit any offence while on bail, shall surrender his passport, and shall report to the investigating officer once a month.

The court made it clear that if the applicant fails to comply with any of these conditions, the bail order would stand cancelled. The court also directed the applicant to cooperate with the investigating agency and to attend all proceedings before the trial court.
