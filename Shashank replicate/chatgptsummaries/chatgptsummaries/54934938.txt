The Karnataka High Court recently dismissed a writ petition filed by Ramesh Kumar against the Managing Director of BESCOM and the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board. The petitioner had challenged the order of BESCOM to disconnect the electric connection to his property in Kasavanahalli, Bengaluru. The court observed that the matter was now being considered by the National Green Tribunal and therefore, the writ petitions were not maintainable. The court dismissed the writ petitions with liberty to the petitioner to seek redressal of the grievance in accordance with the law.

The case pertains to the disconnection of electric connection to Ramesh Kumar's property in Kasavanahalli, Bengaluru. The petitioner had challenged the order of BESCOM to disconnect the electric connection to his property. The petitioner had contended that the disconnection of electric connection was illegal and arbitrary. The petitioner had also contended that the disconnection of electric connection was in violation of the principles of natural justice.

The court observed that the matter was now being considered by the National Green Tribunal and therefore, the writ petitions were not maintainable. The court observed that the National Green Tribunal was seized of the matter and was considering the issue of disconnection of electric connection to the petitioner's property. The court observed that the National Green Tribunal was the appropriate forum to decide the issue and therefore, the writ petitions were not maintainable.
