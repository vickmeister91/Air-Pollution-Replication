In a recent court order, three individuals who were arrested for possession of tiger bones and nails have been granted bail. The case pertains to a violation of the Wild Life Protection Act, and the applicants have been in custody since May 2019. The court has allowed their release on bail upon furnishing a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 each with one surety. However, the applicants must comply with certain conditions, including cooperating in the trial and not committing a similar offense.

The Wild Life Protection Act is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to protect India's rich biodiversity. It prohibits the hunting, poaching, and trade of endangered species, including tigers. The possession of tiger bones and nails is a serious offense under the Act, and those found guilty can face imprisonment and hefty fines.

In this case, the applicants were arrested for possession of tiger bones and nails, which is a clear violation of the Wild Life Protection Act. However, it is important to note that the applicants have no criminal past, and this is their first offense. The court has taken this into consideration while granting them bail.

Bail is a legal provision that allows an accused person to be released from custody while awaiting trial. It is a fundamental right enshrined in the Indian Constitution, and every person is entitled to it unless there are compelling reasons to deny it. In this case, the court has deemed it fit to grant bail to the applicants, subject to certain conditions.

The conditions imposed by the court are aimed at ensuring that the applicants do not abscond or tamper with evidence. They must furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 each with one surety, which means that they must provide a guarantee that they will appear in court when required. They must also cooperate in the trial and not commit a similar offense while on bail.
