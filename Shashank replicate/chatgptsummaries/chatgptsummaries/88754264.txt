The court order in question pertains to a Criminal Original Petition filed by G. Murugan against A. Mani. The petitioner had earlier filed a petition under Section 91 of the Criminal Procedure Code, seeking certain documents, which was rejected by the Trial Court. The present impugned order was passed in a petition seeking the same documents, and the court found no infirmity in the order justifying the Court's decision that the document sought may not be required for proper adjudication of the case. The court dismissed the Criminal Original Petition and closed the connected Criminal Miscellaneous Petition.

To understand the court order in detail, it is important to understand the background of the case. G. Murugan had filed a petition under Section 91 of the Criminal Procedure Code, seeking certain documents from A. Mani. However, the Trial Court had rejected the petition, stating that the documents sought were not relevant to the case and were not required for proper adjudication of the case.

Subsequently, G. Murugan filed a Criminal Original Petition seeking to set aside the order passed by the Metropolitan Magistrate Fast Track IV Court in Chennai. The petitioner argued that the documents sought were crucial to the case and that the Trial Court had erred in rejecting the petition.

However, the court found no infirmity in the order passed by the Trial Court. The court observed that the documents sought were not relevant to the case and were not required for proper adjudication of the case. The court also noted that the petitioner had failed to provide any new grounds or reasons for seeking the same documents again.

Based on these observations, the court dismissed the Criminal Original Petition and closed the connected Criminal Miscellaneous Petition. The court's decision was based on the principle that a litigant cannot seek the same relief repeatedly without providing any new grounds or reasons.
