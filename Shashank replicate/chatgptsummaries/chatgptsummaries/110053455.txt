The court order pertains to a case where the petitioner, B.L. Agrawal, had filed a petition under Article 226 of the Constitution of India to quash the order of Special Judge (Prevention of Corruption Act) Raipur, in Closure Case No.06/2010 vide order dated 05.10.2017. The petitioner was accused of misusing his position as a public servant and amassing wealth disproportionate to his income from lawful sources. The Income Tax Department had conducted a raid and found evidence of cash deposits, jewelry, and investments in movable and immovable assets. The petitioner was exonerated from departmental proceedings and by the Income Tax Department, but the respondent filed an application for further investigation.

The court held that further investigation is permitted under Section 173(8) of Cr.P.C. and dismissed the petition. The court observed that the word 're-investigation' was a misnomer and that the correct term was 'further investigation'. The court also noted that the order passed by the Special Judge did not direct further investigation, but only agreed with the reasons mentioned in the application. The court further stated that quashing the FIR at this stage was not possible as there was material to disclose that a cognizable offence had been committed.

The court order highlights the importance of following due process of law and ensuring that investigations are conducted in a fair and impartial manner. The court's decision to allow further investigation under Section 173(8) of Cr.P.C. is in line with the principles of natural justice and the rule of law. The court's observation that the word 're-investigation' is a misnomer and that the correct term is 'further investigation' is also significant as it clarifies the legal terminology used in such cases.
