The court order in question pertains to a criminal revision filed by S. Kannan against M. Mohan Kumar, proprietor of J.K. Industries, for non-payment of dues. The complainant had filed a case against the accused under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, which deals with dishonour of cheques. The accused had filed a petition under Section 91 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.) seeking the court to issue summons to sales tax authorities and to produce certain documents to establish his defense.

Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. empowers a court to issue summons or order the production of any document or thing for the purpose of an investigation or trial. The accused in this case had sought to produce certain documents from the sales tax authorities to establish his defense. However, the learned Judicial Magistrate No.II, Coimbatore, dismissed the petition filed by the accused.

The accused then filed a revision against the order passed by the Judicial Magistrate No.II, Coimbatore. A revision is a legal remedy available to a party aggrieved by an order passed by a lower court. The revision petition is filed before the higher court, which has the power to examine the legality and correctness of the order passed by the lower court.

The court, in this case, dismissed the revision filed by the accused and confirmed the order passed by the Judicial Magistrate No.II, Coimbatore. The court held that the accused had failed to establish the relevance and necessity of the documents sought to be produced from the sales tax authorities. The court also observed that the accused had not made any effort to obtain the documents from the sales tax authorities before filing the petition under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C.

The court further held that the accused had not made out a case for interference in the order passed by the Judicial Magistrate No.II, Coimbatore. The court noted that the accused had not challenged the order on the ground of jurisdiction or illegality. The court also observed that the accused had not made any submission on the merits of the case.

The court's decision in this case highlights the importance of establishing the relevance and necessity of documents sought to be produced before a court. It also emphasizes the need for parties to make efforts to obtain the documents from the relevant authorities before seeking the court's intervention.

In conclusion, the court order in question pertains to a criminal revision filed by the accused against an order passed by the Judicial Magistrate No.II, Coimbatore. The accused had sought to produce certain documents from the sales tax authorities to establish his defense, but the court dismissed the petition filed by the accused. The court's decision underscores the importance of establishing the relevance and necessity of documents sought to be produced before a court and the need for parties to make efforts to obtain the documents from the relevant authorities before seeking the court's intervention.
