The court order pertains to a petition filed by Gurmeet Ram Rahim under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The petitioner sought the quashing of an order passed by the Special Judicial Magistrate, CBI Haryana at Panchkula in FIR No.RC1(S)/2015SCU.V/SCII/CBI/New Delhi dated 07.01.2015 under Sections 120-B/326/417/506 of the Indian Penal Code, 1860, Police Station SCII/CBINew Delhi. The petitioner had filed an application under Section 91 read with Section 207 Cr.P.C. before the trial Court, which was dismissed.

The petitioner contended that all the documents collected by the investigating agency during the course of investigation have not been supplied to the accused/petitioner only on the ground that those documents have not been relied upon by the prosecution, whereas the same is violative of the right of fair trial. The court examined whether the Court can look into the defence material on record can be summoned at the instance of defence at the stage of framing of charge.

The court order discusses the accused's request for documents collected by the investigating agency during the investigation. The court states that the accused has a right to documents that are part of the final report, but not to documents collected during the investigation. The court also notes that the accused has already been provided with all documents relied upon by the investigating agency.

The court cites previous judgments to support the view that the accused has no right to produce any material at the time of framing the charge, but in rare cases, the court may look into the material produced by the defense. The court dismisses the accused's application but allows the accused to avail the appropriate remedy if necessary.

In summary, the court order pertains to a petition filed by Gurmeet Ram Rahim seeking the quashing of an order passed by the Special Judicial Magistrate. The petitioner had filed an application for documents collected by the investigating agency during the investigation, which was dismissed. The court states that the accused has a right to documents that are part of the final report, but not to documents collected during the investigation. The court dismisses the accused's application but allows the accused to avail the appropriate remedy if necessary.
