The court order pertains to a case involving the construction of a highway in Jharkhand, India. The National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) has been ordered by the court to comply with mandatory requirements under the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 and Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 before commencing work on the project. The court has also asked NHAI to take a decision on the infusion of funds and execution of a tripartite agreement with lenders, as well as the valuation report submitted by the state on structures on the Right of Way at Bundu. The matter has been posted for hearing on June 14, 2018.

The case has been postponed to June 14th at 2:15 PM as an unfixed case. The Member (Project) of NHAI and the Managing Director of Respondent No. 6 are required to be present to assist the court on the next date.
