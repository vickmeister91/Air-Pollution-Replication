The Patna High Court has granted anticipatory bail to Piyush Ranjan, who was accused of running a nursing home without taking requisite permission from the Bihar State Pollution Control Board with regard to the Bio-Medical Waste Management and Water and Air Pollution. The court has ordered that he be released on anticipatory bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs.10,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Sub Divisional Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad in Complaint case no. 5 of 2018, subject to the conditions as laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

The case against Piyush Ranjan was filed under the provisions of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981, and the Bio-Medical Waste Management Rules, 2016. The complainant alleged that Piyush Ranjan was running a nursing home without obtaining the necessary permissions from the Bihar State Pollution Control Board. The complainant further alleged that the nursing home was generating bio-medical waste, which was being disposed of in an unscientific manner, thereby causing pollution of the environment.

Piyush Ranjan had approached the Patna High Court seeking anticipatory bail in the case. The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, granted anticipatory bail to Piyush Ranjan. The court ordered that Piyush Ranjan be released on anticipatory bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs.10,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Sub Divisional Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad in Complaint case no. 5 of 2018, subject to the conditions as laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

The court also laid down certain conditions for the grant of anticipatory bail. The conditions included that Piyush Ranjan shall cooperate with the investigation, shall not tamper with the evidence, shall not leave the country without the permission of the court, and shall appear before the court as and when required. The court also directed Piyush Ranjan to surrender his passport, if any, to the learned Sub Divisional Judicial Magistrate, Aurangabad.

They have further stated that the allegations against Piyush Ranjan are baseless and that he has been falsely implicated in the case.

On the other hand, the complainant has expressed disappointment with the decision of the court. The complainant has stated that the court should have taken into account the seriousness of the offence and the potential harm caused to the environment. The complainant has further stated that the court should have denied anticipatory bail to Piyush Ranjan and that he should have been arrested and prosecuted for his actions.
