The court order in question pertains to a bail application under Section 439 of the Criminal Procedure Code in connection with Forest Crime No. 470009/01 registered at Police Station Forest Range Rukhad Distt. Seoni for the offence under Sections 2, 9, 39, 48A, 49B, 50, 51, 52 and 57 of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The applicants were accused of possessing the skin and body parts of a tiger for selling purposes.

The court allowed the bail application and directed the applicants to furnish a bail bond of Rs. 30,000/- each with one surety each of the same amount to the satisfaction of the committal/trial Court to appear before the court on the dates given by the concerned Court.

The Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, is a comprehensive legislation that provides for the protection of wild animals and plants and for matters connected therewith or ancillary or incidental thereto. The Act aims to ensure the protection of wild animals and plants and to regulate their hunting, possession, and trade.

The accused in this case were found to be in possession of the skin and body parts of a tiger, which is a protected species under the Act. The possession of such items is illegal and attracts severe penalties under the Act.

The court, while granting bail to the accused, directed them to furnish a bail bond of Rs. 30,000/- each with one surety each of the same amount. The court also directed the accused to appear before the committal/trial court on the dates given by the concerned court.

The court must consider the nature and gravity of the offence, the antecedents of the accused, the likelihood of the accused fleeing from justice, and the likelihood of the accused tampering with evidence.

In this case, the court considered the nature and gravity of the offence and the fact that the accused were found to be in possession of the skin and body parts of a tiger, which is a protected species under the Act. The court also considered the fact that the accused were not likely to flee from justice or tamper with evidence.
