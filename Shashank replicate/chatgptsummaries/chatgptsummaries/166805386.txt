In a recent court order, three individuals who were arrested in connection with an offense under Section 9/51 of the Wild Life Protection Act have been granted bail. The bail application was filed under Section 439 Cr.P.C and the court has ordered the accused to furnish a personal bond of Rs.50,000/- each with two sureties of Rs.25,000/- each for their appearance before the court on all dates of hearing.

The Wild Life Protection Act is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to protect and conserve wildlife in India. It prohibits hunting, poaching, and trading of wildlife and their products. Section 9/51 of the Act deals with punishment for hunting or poaching in a sanctuary, national park, or reserve forest. The offense is punishable with imprisonment for a term of three to seven years and a fine of not less than Rs.10,000/-.

In this case, the accused were arrested for allegedly hunting and poaching in a protected area. The court has granted them bail, which means that they will be released from custody until the trial is completed. However, the bail comes with certain conditions that the accused must fulfill. They must furnish a personal bond of Rs.50,000/- each with two sureties of Rs.25,000/- each. This means that they must provide a written undertaking that they will appear before the court on all dates of hearing. They must also provide two people who will act as sureties and guarantee that the accused will appear before the court.
