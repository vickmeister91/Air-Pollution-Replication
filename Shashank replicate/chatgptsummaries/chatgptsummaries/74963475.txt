The Jharkhand High Court has granted bail to two accused, Jagarnath Sharma and Mani Thakur, who were charged with various offenses including causing the death of four people due to illegal mining activities. The court directed them to be released on bail upon furnishing a bond of Rs. 10,000 each with two sureties of like amount to the satisfaction of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Koderma.

The case pertains to illegal mining activities in the Koderma district of Jharkhand, which resulted in the death of four people. The accused were charged with various offenses including culpable homicide not amounting to murder, causing grievous hurt by act endangering life or personal safety of others, and criminal conspiracy.

The accused had filed a bail application before the Jharkhand High Court, which was opposed by the prosecution. The prosecution argued that the accused were involved in illegal mining activities, which had resulted in the death of four people. The prosecution further argued that the accused were a flight risk and may tamper with the evidence if released on bail.

However, the defense counsel argued that the accused had been in custody for a considerable period of time and that there was no evidence to suggest that they were involved in the illegal mining activities. The defense counsel further argued that the accused were not a flight risk and that they had cooperated with the investigation.

After hearing the arguments of both the prosecution and the defense, the Jharkhand High Court granted bail to the accused. The court directed them to be released on bail upon furnishing a bond of Rs. 10,000 each with two sureties of like amount to the satisfaction of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Koderma.

The court also imposed certain conditions on the accused. The accused were directed to surrender their passports, if any, and to not leave the country without the permission of the court. The accused were also directed to cooperate with the investigation and to not tamper with the evidence.
