The Jharkhand High Court has issued an order for Manjit Prakash to surrender to the court within two weeks in connection with a case registered under various sections of the Indian Penal Code and other acts. The court has directed that if he surrenders or is arrested, he should be released on bail upon furnishing a bond of Rs. 10,000 with two sureties of the same amount each, subject to certain conditions.

The case against Manjit Prakash pertains to allegations of illegal mining and transportation of coal. The petitioner had been accused of constructing a brick kiln in a protected or reserved forest area and stealing coal from the area. However, the court noted that no stolen coal was found on the premises.

The order of the court is significant as it highlights the issue of illegal mining and transportation of coal in the state of Jharkhand. The state has been grappling with this issue for a long time, with reports of illegal mining and transportation of coal being rampant in the region.

The court's order is a step towards curbing this illegal activity and ensuring that those who engage in such activities are held accountable for their actions. The order also sends a strong message to others who may be involved in similar activities that they will not be able to escape the law.

The court's decision to release Manjit Prakash on bail is also significant. It shows that the court is willing to consider the circumstances of the case and grant bail if it deems it appropriate. The conditions attached to the bail bond also ensure that Manjit Prakash will not be able to abscond or tamper with evidence.

Overall, the court's order is a positive development in the fight against illegal mining and transportation of coal in Jharkhand. It shows that the judiciary is committed to upholding the law and ensuring that those who engage in illegal activities are held accountable for their actions.
