The Madras High Court recently issued a court order in response to a petition filed by Sethu Vaidyanathan, a member of the People for Animals Trust. The petition sought action against the illegal killing of peacocks for extracting oil from their flesh. The petitioner had complained that despite writing to forest officials, no action was taken. The forest department denied the allegations but assured the court that they would be vigilant.

The court order directed the forest authorities to be more vigilant and prevent the illegal killing of peacocks. The court expressed hope that the national bird is preserved and protected. The writ petition was disposed of with no costs.
