The court order pertains to a criminal revision case involving Keshav Netam, who was found guilty of possessing the skin of a wild animal (leopard) illegally. He was sentenced to one year of rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 5000 under the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The applicant filed a revision against the judgment of conviction and order of sentence, but the court found no illegality or infirmity in the impugned order and dismissed the revision.

The court observed that there has been a significant decline in the number of wild animals due to illegal hunting, and the minimum sentence was appropriate for the offense committed. The court's decision highlights the importance of protecting wildlife and the need for strict enforcement of laws to prevent their illegal hunting and trade.

The Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, is a comprehensive legislation that aims to protect and conserve wildlife in India. It provides for the establishment of national parks, sanctuaries, and other protected areas for the conservation of wildlife. The Act also prohibits the hunting, killing, or capturing of any wild animal, including their skins, without a valid permit.
