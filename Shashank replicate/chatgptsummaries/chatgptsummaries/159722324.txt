The court order in question pertains to a criminal revision case (CRR-831-2017) between Ranjeet and the State of Madhya Pradesh. The applicant had filed an application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. before the charges were framed, which was rejected. The applicant then filed another application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C., which was also rejected by the trial court.

Section 91 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.) empowers a court to issue a summons or order to produce a document or thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. The section also allows the court to direct any person in possession of such a document or thing to produce it before the court.

In this case, the applicant had sought the production of certain documents under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. before the charges were framed. However, the application was rejected by the court. The applicant then filed another application under the same section, which was also rejected by the trial court.

The counsel for the applicant then sought permission to withdraw the revision with liberty to challenge the order rejecting the application filed under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. The court granted the permission and dismissed the criminal revision as withdrawn.

The order highlights the importance of Section 91 of Cr.P.C. in criminal proceedings. The section allows the court to obtain necessary documents or things that may be crucial to the investigation or trial of a case. However, the court may reject an application under this section if it is not satisfied that the document or thing sought is necessary or desirable for the purposes of the case.
