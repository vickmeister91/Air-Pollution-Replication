The Jharkhand High Court has granted anticipatory bail to Ramdas Mahto and Santosh Bediya, who were facing arrest in connection with a case registered under various sections of the Indian Penal Code, Mines and Minerals Act, Jharkhand Minor Minerals Concession Rule, and Prevention of Control of Air Pollution Act. The petitioners claimed that they were the owners of a crusher that had been closed due to non-grant of license and environment clearances, but the informant had raided the same and registered the case showing stock of stone chips and stone dust which was actually less in quantity and kept since long due to closure of mines.

The court directed the petitioners to surrender in the court below within four weeks from the date of the order. In the event of their arrest or surrender, the court below shall enlarge the petitioners on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs. 25,000/- each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of Judicial Magistrate, Ranchi, subject to certain conditions.

The case involved allegations of illegal mining and violation of environmental laws. The petitioners claimed that they were innocent and that the informant had falsely implicated them in the case. They argued that the stock of stone chips and stone dust found at their crusher was actually less in quantity and had been kept since long due to the closure of mines.

The court considered the arguments of both parties and granted anticipatory bail to the petitioners. The court directed the petitioners to surrender in the court below within four weeks from the date of the order. In the event of their arrest or surrender, the court below shall enlarge the petitioners on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs. 25,000/- each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of Judicial Magistrate, Ranchi, subject to certain conditions.

The court also noted that the petitioners had no criminal antecedents and were not likely to abscond or tamper with evidence. The court further observed that the allegations against the petitioners were not of a serious nature and that they were entitled to the benefit of bail.

The court imposed certain conditions on the petitioners, including that they shall not leave the country without the permission of the court, shall not tamper with evidence, shall cooperate with the investigation, and shall attend the court on all dates of hearing.
