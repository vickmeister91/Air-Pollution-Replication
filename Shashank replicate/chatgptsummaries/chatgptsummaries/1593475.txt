The Andhra Pradesh High Court recently issued a court order in response to a writ petition filed against the Visakhapatnam Municipal Corporation for converting a park into a garbage dumping yard. The court order cites the Municipal Solid Wastes (Management and Handling) Rules, 1999, which mandate the proper management and disposal of municipal solid waste. The court strongly deprecates the Corporation's actions and directs them to restore the park and maintain it properly. 

The court order is a significant step towards ensuring that municipal bodies comply with the rules and regulations regarding the management and disposal of municipal solid waste. The order also directs the A.P. Pollution Control Board to monitor the maintenance of the park and initiate criminal proceedings against the Corporation if they violate the rules. This is a crucial step towards ensuring that the Corporation is held accountable for its actions and that it takes the necessary steps to restore the park to its original state.

The court order is a clear indication that the judiciary is taking a proactive role in ensuring that municipal bodies comply with the rules and regulations regarding the management and disposal of municipal solid waste. The order also directs the Board to issue circulars to all Municipal Bodies in the State for compliance with the rules.
