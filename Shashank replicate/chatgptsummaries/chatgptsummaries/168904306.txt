The court order in question pertains to a revision filed by the accused in a murder case challenging the dismissal of their application under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure to summon certain documents from the respondent. The accused had contended that the custody of the documents was necessary for a fair trial as there was a chance of alteration and manipulation of the records. The respondent had opposed the application, stating that it was filed at a belated stage and was vexatious. The Trial Court had dismissed the application, and the accused had challenged the order.

The court, in its ruling, held that the accused had not demonstrated the necessity or desirability of the documents sought for and that the Trial Court had rightly dismissed the application. The court noted that the accused had failed to provide any evidence to support their claim that the documents were necessary for a fair trial. The court also observed that the accused had not explained why they had not sought the documents earlier in the proceedings.

The court further noted that the respondent had opposed the application on the grounds that it was filed at a belated stage and was vexatious. The court found that the respondent's objections were valid and that the Trial Court had rightly dismissed the application.

The court's ruling highlights the importance of providing evidence to support any claims made in court. It also emphasizes the need to file applications in a timely manner and to avoid vexatious litigation.

In conclusion, the court order in question affirms the Trial Court's decision to dismiss the accused's application under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure to summon certain documents from the respondent. The court found that the accused had not demonstrated the necessity or desirability of the documents sought for and that the Trial Court had rightly dismissed the application. The ruling serves as a reminder of the importance of providing evidence to support claims made in court and of avoiding vexatious litigation.
