The court order pertains to a challenge against a communication by the Divisional Forest Officer, Faridabad requesting the Commissioner, Faridabad, the Administration Haryana, Urban Development Authority and the District Town Planner, Faridabad to forward a list of areas closed under Sections 4 & 5 of the Punjab Land Preservation Act, 1900. The challenge is based on the fact that these areas have been declared as "forest" by the court and, therefore, penal action is required to be taken for any non-forest activities under the Forest Conservation Act, 1980.

The court examines the legal effect of the notifications under Sections 4 & 5 of the Act and concludes that for the purposes of the Forest Conservation Act, these areas shall be treated as forest and for use of it for non-forestry purpose, it would be necessary to comply with the provisions of the FC Act. The court notes that the Forest Conservation Act prohibits certain activities in forest areas, including clearing or breaking up land, quarrying, cutting trees, and setting fires. The court also references a previous case regarding mining activity in the area and notes that the Environmental Pollution Control Authority found evidence of violations of court orders during a visit to the area.

The court dismisses the applicant's claims that the restrictions do not apply to their land. The court order emphasizes the importance of protecting forests and the need to comply with the Forest Conservation Act. The court's decision reinforces the legal status of these areas as forests and the need to take appropriate measures to protect them.
