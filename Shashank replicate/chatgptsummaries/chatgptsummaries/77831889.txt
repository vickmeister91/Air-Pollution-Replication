The court order in question pertains to a petition filed under Article 226 of the Constitution, which seeks to declare a notification issued by the Haryana State Pollution Control Board as ultra vires of the Constitution. The petitioner, a computer operator, alleges that the notification blocks avenues of promotion for computer operators to the post of Deputy Superintendent. 

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, finds no legal infirmity in the action of the respondent and dismisses the petition. The court notes that the notification in question was issued in accordance with the rules and regulations governing the promotion of employees in the Haryana State Pollution Control Board. The court also observes that the petitioner has not been able to demonstrate any violation of his fundamental rights or any other legal provision.

The court further notes that the petitioner is entitled to the Assured Career Progression Scheme, which provides for automatic promotion of employees based on their length of service and performance. The court observes that the scheme is a welfare measure aimed at providing job security and career progression to employees, and that it is not intended to create a right to promotion.

The court's decision is based on a careful analysis of the relevant legal provisions and precedents. The court observes that the promotion of employees is a matter of policy and discretion, and that the courts should not interfere with the decisions of the executive unless they are shown to be arbitrary, discriminatory or illegal.

In conclusion, the court order in question dismisses the petition filed by the petitioner and upholds the validity of the notification issued by the Haryana State Pollution Control Board. The court notes that the petitioner is entitled to the Assured Career Progression Scheme, but that this scheme does not create a right to promotion. The court's decision is based on a careful analysis of the relevant legal provisions and precedents, and reflects the principle that the promotion of employees is a matter of policy and discretion.
