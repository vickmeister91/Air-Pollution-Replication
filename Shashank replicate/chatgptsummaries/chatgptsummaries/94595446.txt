The court has granted anticipatory bail to Shashi Chatomba, who was apprehending his arrest in connection with a case of illegal mining of iron ores in a forest area. The petitioner was directed to be released on bail upon surrender or arrest, on furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 with two sureties of the same amount each, subject to conditions laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

The case pertains to illegal mining of iron ores in a forest area, which is a violation of environmental laws. The petitioner, Shashi Chatomba, was apprehending his arrest in connection with the case and had approached the court seeking anticipatory bail. The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, granted anticipatory bail to the petitioner.

The court directed the petitioner to be released on bail upon surrender or arrest, on furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 with two sureties of the same amount each. The bail was granted subject to conditions laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure. These conditions include that the petitioner shall cooperate with the investigating agency, shall not tamper with the evidence, shall not leave the country without the permission of the court, and shall attend the court on all the dates of hearing.

The court's decision to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioner is significant as it provides him with protection against arrest in connection with the case. The petitioner can now approach the court for regular bail and can also cooperate with the investigating agency in the case.

The case of illegal mining of iron ores in a forest area is a serious violation of environmental laws. The court's decision to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioner does not mean that he is absolved of the charges against him. The investigating agency can continue with the investigation and can file a charge sheet against the petitioner if there is sufficient evidence against him.

In conclusion, the court has granted anticipatory bail to Shashi Chatomba, who was apprehending his arrest in connection with a case of illegal mining of iron ores in a forest area. The bail was granted subject to conditions laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The court's decision to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioner provides him with protection against arrest in connection with the case. However, the investigating agency can continue with the investigation and can file a charge sheet against the petitioner if there is sufficient evidence against him.
