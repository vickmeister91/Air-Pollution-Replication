The case of Prabhash Barai involved a criminal misc. petition filed under Section 482 Cr.P.C. against the order passed by Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate, Jaitaran District Pali. The petitioner had been arrested in connection with FIR No.101/2018 of Police Station Jaitaran for various offences. He was released on bail and then moved an application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. to summon the call details of certain mobile numbers. However, the trial court dismissed the application, stating that the call details were not required for investigation.

The petitioner then filed a criminal misc. petition under Section 482 Cr.P.C. against the order passed by the trial court. The High Court held that the petitioner, being an accused, had no right to move an application before the trial court for summoning any document under Section 91 Cr.P.C. before the stage of framing charges. The court dismissed the petition with the observation that the petitioner was free to move appropriate representation before the Investigating Authority or the higher authorities for seeking fair and impartial investigation in the matter.

The case highlights the limitations of an accused in seeking documents under Section 91 Cr.P.C. before the stage of framing charges. The High Court's decision emphasizes that an accused cannot use Section 91 Cr.P.C. to obtain documents before the stage of framing charges. This is because the accused does not have the right to seek documents at this stage of the proceedings.

The court's decision also highlights the importance of seeking fair and impartial investigation in criminal cases. The petitioner was advised to move appropriate representation before the Investigating Authority or the higher authorities for seeking fair and impartial investigation in the matter. This emphasizes the importance of ensuring that investigations are conducted in a fair and impartial manner, and that the rights of the accused are protected.

Overall, the case of Prabhash Barai highlights the importance of understanding the limitations of an accused in seeking documents under Section 91 Cr.P.C. before the stage of framing charges. It also emphasizes the importance of seeking fair and impartial investigation in criminal cases, and the need to protect the rights of the accused.
