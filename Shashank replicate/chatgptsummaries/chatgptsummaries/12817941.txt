The court has granted anticipatory bail to Sandeep Mahto in connection with a wildlife protection case. The petitioner was accused of sharing the flesh of a deer that died after being chased by stray dogs. However, Mahto denies the allegations and has no criminal history. The court has imposed certain conditions for granting anticipatory bail.

The court has ordered that Mahto must surrender within four weeks and deposit Rs. 2,000 as cash security. Additionally, he must furnish a bail bond of Rs. 25,000 with two sureties. These conditions are meant to ensure that Mahto cooperates with the investigation and appears in court when required.

Anticipatory bail is a provision in Indian law that allows a person to seek bail in anticipation of being arrested. This provision is meant to protect individuals from arbitrary arrest and detention. However, the court may impose certain conditions while granting anticipatory bail to ensure that the accused cooperates with the investigation and appears in court when required.

In this case, the court has granted anticipatory bail to Mahto on the condition that he surrenders within four weeks and deposits cash security and furnishes a bail bond with two sureties. These conditions are reasonable and are meant to ensure that Mahto cooperates with the investigation and appears in court when required.
