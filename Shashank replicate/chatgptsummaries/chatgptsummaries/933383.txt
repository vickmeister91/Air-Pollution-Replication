The court order in question pertains to a complaint case filed under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act. The accused petitioners had filed a petition stating that the complaint case was not maintainable at Jagatsingpur as no cause of action had arisen within the jurisdiction of the said Court. The accused petitioners argued that a complaint case can be instituted for commission of offence under Section 138 of the N. I. Act where the cheque in dispute was issued and/or at the place where the cheque was presented for collection or at the place where the cheque was not honoured or at the place where the creditor's /debtor's office or residence is located or at the place from which legal notice was issued.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, held that a part of the cause of action arose at Paradeep inasmuch as the cheque was given at Paradeep in respect of transactions made at Paradeep. The court dismissed the case and granted liberty to the parties to raise all their submissions, both factual and legal, in the complaint case.
