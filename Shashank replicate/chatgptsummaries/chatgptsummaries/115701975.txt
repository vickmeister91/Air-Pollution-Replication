Anticipatory bail is a provision in the Indian legal system that allows a person to seek bail in anticipation of being arrested. This is usually done when a person fears that they may be arrested on false or frivolous charges. The purpose of anticipatory bail is to protect the rights of the accused and prevent them from being subjected to unnecessary harassment and detention.

In this case, the accused had applied for anticipatory bail after an FIR was registered against him under various sections of the Prohibition Act. The Prohibition Act is a law that prohibits the manufacture, sale, and consumption of alcohol in certain states in India. The accused was apprehensive that he may be arrested on false charges and hence applied for anticipatory bail.
