The Karnataka High Court issued a court order on July 30, 2014, in a case involving the U.K. District Consumers and Citizen Welfare Association and several other petitioners against the State of Karnataka and various government officials and companies. The details of the case and the parties involved are listed in the court order.

The court order pertains to a writ petition filed by residents of Uttara Kannada District in Karnataka, complaining of violation of their fundamental rights and environmental protection laws. The petition seeks a writ of mandamus and injunction against various respondents involved in the transportation and stacking of iron and manganese ore in the district.

The court notes that some of the prayers have been complied with, and the remaining grievances can be addressed by the National Green Tribunal established under the National Green Tribunal Act, 2010. Therefore, the court orders the transfer of the writ petition to the National Green Tribunal.

The court order pertains to a writ petition filed with the National Green Tribunal, which was established under the National Green Tribunal Act of 2010. The order is signed by two judges.
