The petitioner, Amandeep Arora, had filed an application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. seeking the production of certain documents from the respondent/complainant, including the Income tax returns of firm Motilal Sharma & Company and other records of that firm. The application was rejected by the learned Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Bhopal in Criminal Case No. 5043/2012.

The petitioner then filed a revision petition before the III Additional Sessions Judge, Bhopal, challenging the order passed by the learned Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Bhopal. The III Additional Sessions Judge, Bhopal, affirmed the order passed by the learned Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Bhopal, and dismissed the revision petition.

The petitioner then filed a petition under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. before the High Court of Madhya Pradesh, challenging the orders passed by the learned Courts below. The High Court of Madhya Pradesh dismissed the petition, stating that the learned Courts below did not commit any mistake in rejecting the applicant's application filed under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C.

Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. empowers a Court to issue a summons or order to produce any document or thing for the purpose of any inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. The section provides that any Court may, at any stage of any inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code, summon any person as a witness or order any person to produce any document or thing.

In the present case, the petitioner had sought the production of certain documents from the respondent/complainant under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. The learned Courts below had rejected the petitioner's application on the ground that the documents sought were not relevant to the case.

The High Court of Madhya Pradesh, in its order, observed that the learned Courts below had considered the petitioner's application and had come to the conclusion that the documents sought were not relevant to the case. The High Court further observed that the petitioner had not been able to show any error or illegality in the orders passed by the learned Courts below.

The High Court, therefore, dismissed the petition filed by the petitioner under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. The court held that the learned Courts below had not committed any mistake in rejecting the petitioner's application filed under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C.
