The order directed the accused to produce a scooter in dispute before the court and allowed the complainant to take it away on furnishing security. The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, held that the provisions of Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure did not apply to accused persons. Therefore, the order passed by the magistrate was untenable. The court also held that the magistrate had no power to grant relief that was not prayed for in the complaint. As a result, the impugned order was set aside.

The court directed the trial court to proceed with the hearing of the complaint and dispose of it by judgment and order not later than 30-3-1984. This means that the trial court must hear the case and make a decision by the specified date.
