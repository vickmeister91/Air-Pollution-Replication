The court order pertains to a case where Hotel Sapphire & Restaurant challenged a notice issued by the H.P. State Pollution Control Board. The petitioner had raised construction beyond the sanctioned plan and compoundable limits, which was found to be unauthorized by the court. The court directed the petitioner to file an affidavit stating the extent of construction permissible as per the sanctioned plan, the extent of construction actually carried out, deviation made in actual construction, the extent of construction which is otherwise compoundable as per the existing Municipal laws, and to comply with all other statutory rules/regulations/bye-laws under the provisions of the Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The court also directed that no coercive action shall be taken till the next date of hearing.

The court found that the construction carried out by the petitioner was unauthorized and beyond the compoundable limits stipulated under the provisions of the H.P. Municipal Act, 1994 and Town and Country Planning Act, 1977. The petitioner was required to furnish an undertaking to the court that in the event of the vires of the amendment incorporated in the Himachal Pradesh Town and Country Planning Act being struck down or otherwise, the construction raised in excess of the sanction plans and beyond compoundable limits shall be demolished or closed. The writ petition filed by the petitioner was dismissed with the petitioner's undertaking to be enforced in accordance with the law. The petitioner was required to deposit a sum of Rs. 2.00 lac as costs to be deposited with the H.P. State Legal Services Authority. All interim orders stood vacated.

The court order highlights the importance of adhering to sanctioned plans and compoundable limits while carrying out construction activities. The court has directed the petitioner to comply with all statutory rules and regulations under the provisions of the Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. This emphasizes the need for businesses to be mindful of environmental regulations while carrying out their operations.

The court's decision to require the petitioner to furnish an undertaking to demolish or close the construction raised in excess of the sanction plans and beyond compoundable limits in the event of the vires of the amendment being struck down or otherwise, is a significant step towards ensuring that businesses do not flout regulations. State Legal Services Authority, is a deterrent against businesses flouting regulations.
