The court order in question pertains to a case filed by Sri Mohamed Farooq against Smt Jamsheeda Banu. The petitioner had filed a petition under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure seeking permission to summon documents from an institution that was not a party before the court. The family court had dismissed the application, stating that the petitioner could take back the documents from the institution and present them to the court. The high court upheld the family court's decision, stating that the petitioner could not approach the court without exhausting all remedies available to him.
