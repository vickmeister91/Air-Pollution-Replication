The Karnataka High Court recently dismissed a writ petition filed by Vivek Wash Tech against the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board. The court stated that an alternative remedy was available under Section 33B of The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. The petitioner was given the liberty to approach the Appellate Authority if they chose to do so.

The case involved Vivek Wash Tech, a company that had been issued a notice by the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board for violating pollution control norms. The company had filed a writ petition in the Karnataka High Court challenging the notice. The company argued that the notice was issued without following due process and that the pollution control board had not given them an opportunity to be heard.

The Karnataka High Court, however, dismissed the writ petition, stating that an alternative remedy was available under Section 33B of The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. This section provides for an appeal to the Appellate Authority against any order or decision of the pollution control board. The court held that the petitioner could approach the Appellate Authority if they chose to do so.

The court's decision is significant as it highlights the importance of following due process and exhausting all available remedies before approaching the court. The court's decision also underscores the need for companies to comply with pollution control norms and regulations.

The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to prevent and control water pollution. The act provides for the establishment of pollution control boards at the state and central levels. These boards are responsible for enforcing pollution control norms and regulations and ensuring compliance by industries and other entities.

The act also provides for penalties and punishments for violations of pollution control norms. The penalties can range from fines to imprisonment, depending on the severity of the violation. The act also provides for the closure of industries that repeatedly violate pollution control norms.
