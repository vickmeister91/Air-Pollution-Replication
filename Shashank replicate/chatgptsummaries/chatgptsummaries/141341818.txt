The court order in question pertains to a criminal petition filed by Sri C Sonne Gowda against Sri B Babu Rao. The petitioner sought to quash an order passed by the II Additional Civil Judge and JMFC, Kolar, in C.C. No. 134/2007, which directed the petitioner to produce certain documents in a prosecution for an offence under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act.

Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act deals with the dishonour of cheques for insufficiency of funds in the account of the drawer. The section provides for criminal liability for the drawer of the cheque, if the cheque is dishonoured and the drawer fails to make the payment within the stipulated time.

In the present case, the accused had been charged with an offence under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, and had sought certain documents from the complainant to establish the existence of the transaction between the complainant and the accused, and about the existence of the liability.

The petitioner, however, had refused to produce the documents, and had filed a petition before the court seeking to quash the order directing him to produce the documents.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, rejected the petition filed by the petitioner, stating that the documents sought by the accused were necessary to find out the existence of the transaction between the complainant and the accused, and about the existence of the liability.

The court observed that the accused had a right to defend himself, and that the documents sought by him were relevant to the case. The court further observed that the production of the documents would not cause any prejudice to the petitioner, and that the petitioner had failed to show any valid reason for not producing the documents.

The court, therefore, directed the petitioner to produce the documents as directed by the II Additional Civil Judge and JMFC, Kolar, failing which the court would take appropriate action against the petitioner.
