Ajay Singh's second application for bail under Section 439 of the Cr.P.C. in Forest Crime No.28060/2002 registered by Regional Tiger Strike Force, Sagar under Sections 2, 9, 39, 44, 48-A, 49-B, 51 and 52 of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 was dismissed by the court. The petitioner had been in custody since 08.06.2017 and had failed to point out any material change in circumstance entitling him to bail.

The case against Ajay Singh was registered under various sections of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972. The act was enacted to provide for the protection of wild animals, birds and plants and for matters connected therewith or ancillary or incidental thereto. The act provides for the establishment of national parks, sanctuaries and closed areas for the protection of wild animals, birds and plants. It also provides for the regulation of hunting and trade in wild animals, birds and plants.

Ajay Singh had been in custody since 08.06.2017 and had filed a second application for bail under Section 439 of the Cr.P.C. The court dismissed his application as he had failed to point out any material change in circumstance entitling him to bail. The court observed that the petitioner had been in custody for a considerable period of time and had failed to show any change in circumstance that would warrant his release on bail.

The court also noted that the case against the petitioner was registered under various sections of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972. The act provides for stringent punishment for offences related to hunting and trade in wild animals, birds and plants. The court observed that the offences alleged against the petitioner were serious in nature and warranted a thorough investigation.

The court further observed that the petitioner had failed to cooperate with the investigation and had not provided any assistance in the recovery of the alleged contraband. The court noted that the recovery of the alleged contraband was a crucial aspect of the investigation and the petitioner's non-cooperation had hindered the investigation.

The court also observed that the petitioner had a previous criminal record and had been involved in similar offences in the past. The court noted that the petitioner's previous criminal record was a relevant factor to be considered while deciding his bail application.
