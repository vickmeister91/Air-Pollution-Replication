In a recent court order, a petition filed by Ashok Kumar seeking the removal of encroachment from the river bed of Kamla was dismissed. The court cited that the jurisdiction to deal with such issues rests with the National Green Tribunal and found no reason to interfere with the matter.

The petitioner, Ashok Kumar, had approached the court seeking the removal of encroachment from the river bed of Kamla. He argued that the encroachment was causing environmental damage and was a threat to the safety of the people living in the area. He also claimed that the encroachment was hindering the flow of the river and causing flooding in the nearby areas.

However, the court found that the jurisdiction to deal with such issues rests with the National Green Tribunal. The National Green Tribunal is a specialized court that deals with environmental disputes and has the power to issue orders for the protection and conservation of the environment.

The court noted that the National Green Tribunal had already taken cognizance of the matter and was actively monitoring the situation. The court also observed that the petitioner had not approached the National Green Tribunal before filing the petition in the court.

The court further stated that it did not find any reason to interfere with the matter and dismissed the application. The court's decision was based on the principle of judicial restraint, which requires courts to exercise caution and restraint in interfering with matters that fall within the jurisdiction of specialized courts.

The court's decision highlights the importance of specialized courts in dealing with complex and technical issues related to the environment. The National Green Tribunal has been established to provide a forum for the resolution of environmental disputes and to ensure the protection and conservation of the environment.

In conclusion, the court's decision to dismiss the petition filed by Ashok Kumar seeking the removal of encroachment from the river bed of Kamla highlights the importance of specialized courts in dealing with environmental disputes. The court recognized that the jurisdiction to deal with such issues rests with the National Green Tribunal and exercised judicial restraint in dismissing the application. The decision underscores the need for a specialized court to address complex and technical issues related to the environment and to ensure the protection and conservation of the environment.
