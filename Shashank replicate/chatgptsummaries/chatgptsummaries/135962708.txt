However, the court rejected this argument and held that Forest Rangers are empowered to lay a complaint before the competent court under Section 55(b) of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972.

The court held that the Forest Range Officer was well within his rights to register a case against Gunaseelan for possession of animal articles and trophies without a valid license. The court also noted that the prosecution was not barred by limitation as the complaint had been filed within the prescribed time limit.

If it is found that Gunaseelan did not have a valid certificate of possession or was not entitled to such possession by inheritance, he could be held liable for possession of animal articles and trophies without a valid license.
