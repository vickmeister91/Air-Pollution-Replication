In a recent court order, the Karnataka High Court addressed a writ petition filed by Sri S V Sathyanarayana Reddy against the State of Karnataka and other authorities. The petitioner sought permission to operate a stone crusher unit under the Karnataka Regulation of Stone Crushers Act, 2011. The court found that the petitioner was a licensee and had a valid license to run the unit. 

The court also noted that a similar case had been decided by a Division Bench of the court, and therefore, the court followed the same decision. The court quashed the closure notice issued by the Karnataka Pollution Control Board and allowed the petitioner to operate the unit at the present site till such time its application is considered and disposed of by the licensing authority, or for a period of four weeks from the date of the order.

This court order is significant as it upholds the rights of licensees to operate stone crusher units in accordance with the law. The court's decision to follow a previous ruling by a Division Bench also provides clarity and consistency in the application of the law.

It is important to note that the court's decision does not mean that stone crusher units can operate without regard for environmental regulations. The court's order specifically allows the petitioner to operate the unit until its application is considered and disposed of by the licensing authority, or for a limited period of four weeks. This suggests that the court expects the licensing authority to carefully consider the petitioner's application and ensure that all necessary environmental regulations are met before granting permission for the unit to operate.
