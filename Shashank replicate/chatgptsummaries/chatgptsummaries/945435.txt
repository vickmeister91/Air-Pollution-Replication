In a recent case, the petitioner challenged the rejection of an application under Section 91 of the Cr. P.C. by the Addl. Chief Judicial Magistrate No. 2, Jaipur District, Jaipur. The police had requested the original copy of an agreement submitted in a civil suit to ascertain whether it was forged or not. The application was dismissed on the ground that the power to give a copy of the document lies with the High Court. However, the court directed the Addl. Chief Judicial Magistrate No. 2 to hand over the original copy of the agreement to the police within two weeks and keep a certified copy in the file of the civil suit. The police was directed to keep the document in safe custody and produce it before the court when asked for.

The case highlights the importance of Section 91 of the Cr. P.C., which empowers a court to issue a summons or order for the production of any document or other material that may be necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. In this case, the police required the original copy of the agreement to ascertain whether it was forged or not. The court recognized the importance of the document and directed the Addl. Chief Judicial Magistrate No. 2 to hand over the original copy to the police.

The court also recognized the importance of keeping a certified copy of the document in the file of the civil suit. This is important because it ensures that the document is not lost or destroyed and can be produced before the court when required. The court directed the police to keep the document in safe custody and produce it before the court when asked for. This ensures that the document is not tampered with or destroyed and can be used as evidence in the case.

The case also highlights the importance of the role of the High Court in issuing copies of documents. The court dismissed the application on the ground that the power to give a copy of the document lies with the High Court. This is because the High Court is the custodian of all records and documents of subordinate courts. The court recognized the importance of following the proper procedure and directed the police to obtain a copy of the document from the High Court.

In conclusion, the case highlights the importance of Section 91 of the Cr. P.C. and the role of the High Court in issuing copies of documents. The court recognized the importance of the document and directed the Addl. Chief Judicial Magistrate No. 2 to hand over the original copy to the police. The court also recognized the importance of keeping a certified copy of the document in the file of the civil suit and directed the police to keep the document in safe custody and produce it before the court when asked for. The case serves as a reminder of the importance of following proper procedures and ensuring that documents are not lost or destroyed.
