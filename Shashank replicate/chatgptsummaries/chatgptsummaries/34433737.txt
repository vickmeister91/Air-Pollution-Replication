In a recent court order, two petitioners who were arrested in connection with an FIR for offences under Section 379 of IPC, Section 29/51 of Wild Protection Act and Sections 41 and 42 of Rajasthan Forest Act have been granted bail. The counsel for the petitioners argued that the offences were triable by the Magistrate and that recovery had already been made. The court allowed the bail application and ordered the accused to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000/- with two sureties of Rs. 25,000/- each for their appearance before the court on all dates of hearing.

The case involved allegations of theft of wildlife and forest produce, which are serious offences under the Indian Penal Code and the Wild Protection Act. The accused were arrested and produced before the court, which remanded them to judicial custody. The petitioners then filed a bail application, which was opposed by the prosecution on the grounds that the offences were serious and that the accused were likely to abscond if released on bail.

However, the counsel for the petitioners argued that the offences were triable by the Magistrate and that recovery had already been made. The counsel also submitted that the accused were not likely to abscond and that they were willing to cooperate with the investigation. The court considered the arguments of both the parties and granted bail to the accused.

The court ordered the accused to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000/- with two sureties of Rs. 25,000/- each for their appearance before the court on all dates of hearing. The court also imposed certain conditions on the accused, such as not leaving the jurisdiction of the court without prior permission, not tampering with the evidence, and cooperating with the investigation.
