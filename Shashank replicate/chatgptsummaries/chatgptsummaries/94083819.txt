The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Partha Pratim Chakraborty, who had been detained in connection with a case under Section 420 IPC R/W Section 51 of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972. The petitioner had been accused of possessing a golden Gecko, which is an animal falling under Part-II of the Schedule-I of the Wild Life (Protection) Act.

The court, after considering the facts and circumstances of the case, allowed the petitioner to be enlarged on bail of Rs. 20,000/- with one suitable surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of the learned CJM, Sivasagar. The court noted that the seized article had already been recovered from his possession and further detention may not be necessary in the interest of investigation.

The case against the petitioner was based on the allegation that he had been found in possession of a golden Gecko, which is a protected species under the Wild Life (Protection) Act. The petitioner had been arrested and detained in connection with the case, and had subsequently filed a bail application before the court.

The court, after hearing arguments from both sides, noted that the petitioner had already been in custody for a considerable period of time. The court also noted that the seized article had already been recovered from his possession, and that there was no need for further detention in the interest of investigation.

The court further observed that the petitioner had no previous criminal record, and that he was not likely to abscond or tamper with evidence if released on bail. The court also took into account the fact that the petitioner had cooperated with the investigation, and had provided all necessary information to the investigating authorities.

Based on these considerations, the court allowed the petitioner to be enlarged on bail of Rs. 20,000/- with one suitable surety of the like amount to the satisfaction of the learned CJM, Sivasagar. The court also imposed certain conditions on the petitioner, including that he should not leave the jurisdiction of the court without prior permission, and that he should cooperate with the investigation.
