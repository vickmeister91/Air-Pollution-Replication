The Kerala High Court recently heard a case (WP(C).No. 18558 of 2016 (T)) brought by M/S. Sree Sakthi Paper Mills Ltd against the State of Kerala, the Kerala State Pollution Control Board, the Environmental Engineer, the Municipal Council of Eloor, the Eloor Municipality, and the Kadungallur Grama Panchayath. Additional parties were added to the case by court order. The court heard the case on June 21, 2016, and exhibits were presented by both the petitioner and the respondents.

The case pertains to an industrial unit challenging an order passed by the Pollution Control Board directing the closure of the unit. The court has heard arguments from both sides and is considering objections raised by the petitioner and additional respondents. One of the objections raised is regarding the maintainability of the writ petition as an alternate remedy is available under Section 33B(c) of the Water (Prevention and control of Pollution) Act, 1974.

The court has granted an interim order staying further proceedings. The order lists various annexures submitted by both parties, including copies of notices, replies, and reports.
