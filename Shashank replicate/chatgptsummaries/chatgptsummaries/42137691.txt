The court order in question pertains to a challenge by D's Casa Hotel against a notice issued by the H.P. State Pollution Control Board. The petitioner had been directed by the court to file an affidavit stating the extent of construction permissible as per the sanctioned plan, the extent of construction actually carried out, deviation made in actual construction, the extent of construction which is otherwise compoundable, the extent of construction which is otherwise not compoundable, and to comply with all other statutory rules/regulations/bye-laws. The petitioner filed the affidavit as directed by the court.

The court order requires the petitioner to provide an undertaking that they will demolish any construction that exceeds the sanctioned plans or compoundable limits if the amendment incorporated in the Himachal Pradesh Town and Country Planning Act is struck down. The petitioner must also comply with all other statutory rules and regulations. The court notes that the petitioner's construction exceeds the sanctioned plan by 250% and is beyond the compoundable limits stipulated under the H.P. Municipal Act and Town and Country Planning Act. The court also mentions that similar petitions have been filed for violations of municipal laws and construction exceeding sanctioned plans.

The petitioner seeks to withdraw the writ petition, which is permitted subject to depositing a sum of Rs. 2 lakhs as costs to be deposited with the H.P. State Legal Services Authority. The court orders the writ petition to be disposed of as withdrawn.
