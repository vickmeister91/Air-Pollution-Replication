In a recent court order, the issue at hand was the demand for production, inspection, or supply of copies of documents seized by the police during the investigation of a case. The accused had requested these documents, but they were not supplied to them under Section 207 Cr. P.C. The court held that even at the stage of consideration of framing of charge, the accused is entitled to demand production of a document if it has any bearing on the question of framing of charge, although the prosecution may not rely on the same.

The court's decision was based on the principle of natural justice, which requires that both parties have access to all relevant information. The court noted that the accused has a right to know the evidence against them and to have an opportunity to challenge it. This right is essential to ensure a fair trial and to prevent wrongful convictions.

The court also directed the investigating agency to produce the remaining 31 seized cheques for inspection by the accused persons or their lawyers. This order was made to ensure that the accused have access to all relevant evidence and can prepare their defense accordingly.
