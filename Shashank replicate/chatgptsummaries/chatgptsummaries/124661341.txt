The court order in question pertains to a criminal petition filed by Sri A.N. Subbaraju against Sri Dinesh M. Bhatia. The petitioner had sought to set aside an order passed by the XIII ACMM Court, Bangalore, rejecting his application under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The petitioner had sought the production of certain documents that were in the custody of the complainant under mortgage.

The XIII ACMM Court had rejected the petitioner's application on the grounds that it was filed at a belated stage. The court found that the petitioner had not made out any grounds to interfere with the order passed by the XIII ACMM Court. The petitioner had argued that the documents in question were crucial to the case and that their production was necessary for a fair trial.

The court, however, found that the petitioner had not made out a case for the production of the documents. The court noted that the petitioner had not explained why he had not sought the production of the documents earlier in the proceedings. The court also noted that the petitioner had not shown how the documents were relevant to the case.

The court observed that the petitioner had not made out a case for the production of the documents under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The court noted that Section 91 allows a court to issue a summons to produce a document if it is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code of Criminal Procedure.

The court held that the petitioner had not shown how the documents were necessary or desirable for the purposes of the trial. The court also held that the petitioner had not shown how the documents were relevant to the case. The court, therefore, dismissed the petition.

The court's decision highlights the importance of timely filing of applications under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The court noted that such applications should be filed at the earliest possible stage in the proceedings. The court also emphasized the need for the petitioner to make out a case for the production of the documents.
