On 6 October 2010, the court issued an order in the case of Laxmansinh vs State. The petitioner expressed their willingness to approach the respondent-Collector and make a representation/application for the purpose of granting benefit in accordance with the policy of the State Government. The respondent-Authority was directed to consider and decide the same in accordance with the law within a period of two months from the date of receipt of such representation/application.

The court order further stated that until such decision is taken and communicated to the petitioner, status quo as on that day with regard to possession will be maintained, only if the same is not in breach of provision of the Forest Act as well as Wild Life Protection Act. The present petition stands disposed of.

The court order in the case of Laxmansinh vs State is significant as it highlights the importance of following the law and adhering to government policies. The petitioner's willingness to approach the respondent-Collector and make a representation/application for the purpose of granting benefit in accordance with the policy of the State Government is a positive step towards resolving the issue at hand.

The court's direction to the respondent-Authority to consider and decide the same in accordance with the law within a period of two months from the date of receipt of such representation/application is a clear indication that the court expects the respondent-Authority to act in a timely and efficient manner.

The court's decision to maintain status quo as on that day with regard to possession is a fair and just decision, as it ensures that the petitioner's rights are protected until a final decision is made. However, it is important to note that this decision is subject to the provisions of the Forest Act as well as Wild Life Protection Act.
