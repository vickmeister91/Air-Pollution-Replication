The Karnataka State Pollution Control Board issued a closure notice to M/S Lakshmaiah Brothers Pvt Ltd, a stone crushing unit, under Section 31(A) of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution Act, 1981. The petitioner sought a writ of certiorari to quash the closure notice, stating that they were operating under a valid license issued by the competent authority. The court order pertains to this writ petition filed by the petitioner against the State of Karnataka and other respondents.

The court found that the orders impugned in the writ petition, grounds against it, and the relief sought were the same as in a previous writ action. Therefore, the court followed the decision of the Division Bench in that writ petition and allowed the petitioner to operate the stone crushing unit at the present site till such time its application is considered and disposed of by the licensing authority, or for a period of four weeks from the date of the order.

The court's decision was based on the fact that the petitioner had a valid license to operate the stone crushing unit. The court also noted that the petitioner had not violated any of the conditions of the license. The court found that the closure notice was issued without any basis and was therefore illegal.
