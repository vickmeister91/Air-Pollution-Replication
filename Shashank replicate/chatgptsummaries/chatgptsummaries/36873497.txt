The first court order pertains to a writ petition filed by M/s.Velan Crusher under Article 226 of the Constitution of India. The petitioner sought a Writ of Certiorarified Mandamus to call for the records of the impugned order dated 18.12.2018 passed in Appeal Nos.32 and 33/2018 by the Appellate Authority, Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board. The petitioner also sought to quash the order dated 19.03.2018 bearing No.F.0770PDK/OS/DEE/TNPCB/22/W&A/2018 of the second respondent and further direct the respondents 1 and 2 to grant consent to operate a stone crushing unit at S.Nos.81/3B, 81/4, 81/17, 73/3C, 73/3E, 73/4, 73/3D, 81/18B, 81/18C, Panampatti Village, Iluppur Taluk, Pudukottai District, to the petitioner.

The petitioner claimed that they had installed adequate pollution control measures in their unit to contain air and water pollution and were 500 meters away from the approved habitation. The petitioner had inadvertently not applied for consent from the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board in 2013, but had applied for it after receiving a show cause notice in 2018. The TNPCB rejected the grant of consent on the grounds that four other crushing units were located within a 1 km radius of the petitioner's unit. The petitioner appealed to the Appellate Authority of the TNPCB, which was dismissed.

The court allowed the writ petition, quashing the impugned orders and directing the TNPCB to consider the application for grant of consent of the petitioner-Unit and pass appropriate orders on merits and in accordance with law. The court held that the TNPCB had not considered the relevant factors while rejecting the petitioner's application for consent. The court also noted that the petitioner had installed adequate pollution control measures and was located 500 meters away from the approved habitation. The court directed the TNPCB to consider the application for grant of consent of the petitioner-Unit and pass appropriate orders on merits and in accordance with law.
