In a recent court order, the conviction and sentence of three petitioners were upheld under Section 51(1) of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972. The petitioners were found guilty of carrying 13 yellow monitor lizards and digging instruments. The court rejected the argument put forth by the petitioners that there was an interpolation in the initial complaint/damage report and that the prosecution failed to connect them with the commission of the offense.

The court found that the evidence presented by the prosecution was sufficient to establish the guilt of the petitioners beyond a reasonable doubt. The court also noted that the petitioners failed to provide any evidence to support their defense. Therefore, the court upheld the conviction of the petitioners.

However, the court modified the sentence of the petitioners. The period of rigorous imprisonment was reduced from 3 years to 1 year for all three petitioners due to mitigating circumstances. The court considered the fact that the petitioners were first-time offenders and that they had no previous criminal record. The court also took into account the fact that the petitioners had cooperated with the investigation and had shown remorse for their actions.

The court's decision sends a strong message that wildlife crimes will not be tolerated. The Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to protect India's rich biodiversity. The act provides for the protection of wild animals and plants and their habitats. It also regulates hunting, poaching, and trade in wildlife.
