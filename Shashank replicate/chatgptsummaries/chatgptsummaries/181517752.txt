The High Court of Madhya Pradesh has recently admitted a revision for hearing in the case of Raja @ Teer Singh vs The State of Madhya Pradesh. The applicants in this case have been convicted for an offence under the Wild Life (Protection) Act and have been sentenced to undergo R.I. for one year and a fine of Rs. 500/- each with default stipulation. However, the court has allowed the applicants to be released on bail subject to deposit of fine amount, if not already deposited, and furnishing a personal bond in the sum of Rs. 50,000/- each with one surety each in the like amount. 

The court has also directed the applicants to appear and mark their presence before the Trial Court on 19.09.2019 and shall continue to do so on all such future dates, as may be given in this behalf, during pendency of the matter. The matter has been listed for final hearing in due course. 

The case pertains to an offence under the Wild Life (Protection) Act, which is a crucial legislation aimed at protecting the wildlife in the country. The Act provides for the protection of wild animals and plants and also regulates hunting, poaching, and trade in wildlife and its products. The Act has been enacted to ensure that the wildlife in the country is protected and conserved for future generations.
