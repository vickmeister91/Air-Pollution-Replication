The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Shivdutt Mehtar under Section 439 of the Cr.P.C. Mehtar has been in custody since 29th December 2014 in connection with Crime No. 22404/02 of Forest Act and Criminal Case No. 1651/14 registered in the Court of JMFC, Ambah, district Morena (M.P.) for the offence punishable under Sections 9 read with Section 51 of Wild Life Protection Act. Mehtar is accused of killing a peacock, which is a bird listed in the Schedule 1 of the Wild Life Protection Act.

The court allowed the bail application and directed Mehtar to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 40,000 with one surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court for securing his presence before the said court on all the dates of hearing fixed in this regard during trial. The order also lists four conditions that Mehtar must comply with, failing which the learned Trial Court would be at liberty to reconsider the question of bail.

The first condition is that Mehtar shall not tamper with the evidence or influence the witnesses in any manner whatsoever. The second condition is that he shall not leave the territorial limits of the district Morena without the prior permission of the trial court. The third condition is that he shall attend the trial court on all the dates of hearing fixed in this regard unless prevented by any reasonable cause. The fourth condition is that he shall not commit any offence while on bail.
