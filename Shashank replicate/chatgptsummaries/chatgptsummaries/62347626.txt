The court has granted anticipatory bail to Sanjeev Kumar Singh in connection with a forest conservation case. The petitioner had approached the court apprehending his arrest and claimed to be falsely implicated in the case. The court, after hearing the arguments of both the parties, granted anticipatory bail to the petitioner.

The court directed the petitioner to surrender within four weeks and furnish a bail bond of Rs. 25,000 with two sureties of the same amount. The petitioner was also required to deposit Rs. 10,000 in the court, which would not prejudice his case during the trial.

The court observed that the petitioner had made out a case for anticipatory bail as he had no criminal antecedents and was not likely to abscond or tamper with the evidence. The court also noted that the petitioner had cooperated with the investigation and had appeared before the investigating officer whenever required.

The court further observed that the allegations against the petitioner were not of a serious nature and that the petitioner had a good chance of being acquitted in the trial. The court also took into consideration the fact that the petitioner was a resident of the area and had a family to support.

The court, while granting anticipatory bail to the petitioner, directed him to cooperate with the investigation and not to leave the country without the permission of the court. The court also directed the petitioner to attend the court on all the dates of hearing and not to indulge in any criminal activity.
