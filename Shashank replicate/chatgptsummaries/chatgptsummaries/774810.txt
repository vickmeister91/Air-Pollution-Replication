The court order pertains to a case where a private complaint was filed against the accused for offences punishable under sections 420 and 406 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC). The accused contended that the court had no power to issue a search warrant for the production of a car. However, the court rejected the contention and allowed the petition. The accused then filed a petition to quash the proceedings under Section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C).

The court held that the Magistrate had the power to issue summons to the accused for the production of the vehicle under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. The court also discussed various statutory provisions that compel a person to produce information or evidence that may incriminate themselves. The court observed that such provisions are inconsistent with the protection against self-incrimination.

The court further held that the provisions of the Cr.P.C. do not allow the court to issue a summons or warrant against an accused person for the production of incriminating evidence during the investigation stage. In this case, an order for the production of an incriminating vehicle cannot be sustained and is therefore quashed. The petition is allowed.
