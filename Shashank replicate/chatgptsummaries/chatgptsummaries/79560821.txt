The court has granted anticipatory bail to Kuware Yadav and Daduram Yadav in connection with Forest Crime No. 7646/06 registered at Forest Range Umariya, District Umariya for the offences punishable under Sections 2, 9, 50, 51 of the Wild Life Protection Act. The court allowed the bail application considering that the co-accused had already been arrested and various articles had been seized, and there were no criminal antecedents of the present applicants. The applicants were directed to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 each with one solvent surety of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the Arresting Officer and abide by the conditions enumerated in sub-section 2 of Section 438 of the Cr.P.C.

The case in question pertains to a forest crime registered at Forest Range Umariya, District Umariya. The applicants were also directed to abide by the conditions enumerated in sub-section 2 of Section 438 of the Cr.P.C. This section lays down the conditions that must be followed by a person who has been granted anticipatory bail. These conditions include that the person must make himself available for interrogation by the police officer as and when required, must not leave India without the permission of the court, and must not directly or indirectly make any inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the court or to any police officer.
