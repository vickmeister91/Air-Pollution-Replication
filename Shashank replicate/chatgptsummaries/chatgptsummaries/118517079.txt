The Karnataka High Court recently passed a judgment in a criminal petition filed by Sri K Shamaraju, Managing Director of Maruti Power Gen. (India) Pvt. Ltd. The petition sought to quash the proceedings in C.C.No.760/2015 on the file of Civil Judge and JMFC, at Sakaleshpura, for alleged violations of the Forest Conservation Act and the Karnataka Forest Act. The petitioner had obtained all requisite permissions to implement a hydroelectric project, but was accused of violating the aforementioned acts.

The court allowed the petition and quashed the proceedings, citing violations of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. The State was given liberty to take action in accordance with the law, if permitted. This judgment is significant as it highlights the importance of following due process and obtaining all necessary permissions before undertaking any project.
