The Supreme Court recently disposed of an appeal in the case of B. Raghupathy vs Union of India. The case pertained to the violation of pollution control acts and buffer zone violations. The National Green Tribunal had previously heard the case, but the Supreme Court found that it had not adequately addressed the issues at hand. As a result, the appellant is now free to file a review petition and challenge the order if the review petition is dismissed.

During the proceedings, the court listed the names of the counsels representing both parties. This information is important for transparency and accountability purposes, as it allows the public to know who is representing each side in the case.

The case of B. Raghupathy vs Union of India is significant because it highlights the importance of enforcing pollution control acts and buffer zone regulations. These regulations are in place to protect the environment and public health, and it is crucial that they are upheld.

The Supreme Court's decision to dispose of the appeal and allow the appellant to file a review petition is a positive step towards ensuring that these regulations are enforced. It shows that the court is committed to upholding the law and protecting the environment.
