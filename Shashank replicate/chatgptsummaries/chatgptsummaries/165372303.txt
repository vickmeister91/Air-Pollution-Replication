The court order in question is related to a case between Dorbar Shnong Mynthning and Jhadc. The case revolves around a piece of land that is essentially a forest land with hundreds of trees of different varieties standing on it. During the proceedings, it was revealed that the land in question is a part of the community land of the Dorbar Shnong Mynthning, which is a traditional institution of the Khasi tribe in Meghalaya.

The dispute arose when Jhadc claimed ownership of the land and sought to register it in their name. The Dorbar Shnong Mynthning, on the other hand, claimed that the land was their community land and that they had been using it for generations. The dispute was taken to court, and the learned single judge had given a judgment in favor of Jhadc, allowing them to register the land in their name.

However, the Dorbar Shnong Mynthning appealed against the judgment and sought interim relief from the court. The court accepted their prayer for interim relief and granted a stay of the operation of the judgment of the learned single judge insofar as the same pertains to the registration of the land. This means that Jhadc cannot register the land in their name until the final judgment is given in the case.

The court also ordered that the hearing of Title Suit No.7 of 2007 and Title Suit No. 10 of 2007, which are pending before the Presiding Officer, Subordinate District Council Court, shall be concluded within a period of three months from the date of receiving a copy of this order. This means that the case will be heard and decided within three months, and the final judgment will be given.

The court order is significant because it upholds the rights of the traditional institution of the Khasi tribe in Meghalaya. The Dorbar Shnong Mynthning is a traditional institution that has been in existence for generations and has been managing the community land of the Khasi tribe. The court's decision to grant interim relief and stay the operation of the judgment of the learned single judge insofar as the same pertains to the registration of the land is a recognition of the rights of the traditional institution and the community.

The court's order also ensures that the case will be heard and decided within a period of three months, which is a significant step towards resolving the dispute. The dispute has been ongoing for several years, and the court's order to conclude the hearing within three months will bring much-needed closure to the case.
