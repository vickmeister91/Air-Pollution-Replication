The court order in question relates to two bail applications filed by a group of individuals who were accused of hunting and consuming a Schedule III animal, the Sambar, which is protected under the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. After hearing arguments from both sides, the court allowed the bail applications and directed the applicants to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 25,000 each with one surety in the like sum for their appearance in court.

The Act classifies animals and plants into various schedules, with Schedule I providing the highest level of protection and Schedule VI providing the lowest. The Sambar, which is a large deer found in the Indian subcontinent, is classified as a Schedule III animal under the Act.
