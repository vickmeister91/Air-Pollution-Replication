The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Lakhveer Singh, who was arrested for hunting deer and other protected animals. The forest department found Singh and his accomplices with the meat and skeleton of deer, black bucks, and chinkara, along with weapons and gas cylinders. The state opposed the bail application, stating that hunting of scheduled animals is prohibited and essential for maintaining the ecosystem. The court dismissed the application and granted liberty to reapply after eight weeks.

The case of Lakhveer Singh highlights the issue of illegal hunting and poaching of protected animals in India. The Act also provides for the establishment of national parks, sanctuaries, and other protected areas for the conservation of wildlife.
