The High Court of Judicature at Madras has recently issued a court order pertaining to two criminal original petitions filed by Vincent Suresh Babu against the Inspector of Police, CBCID, Coimbatore. The petitioner had requested to summon the Chief Manager or authorized officer from Canara Bank SAF Branch to produce documents under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. However, the request was rejected by the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Coimbatore.

The High Court of Judicature at Madras has set aside the order of the Chief Judicial Magistrate, Coimbatore, and directed the petitioner to complete cross-examination on the same day. The court observed that the proceedings may not get unjustifiably prolonged if a time frame is fixed.

The court order is significant as it highlights the importance of following the legal procedures and ensuring that the rights of the petitioner are protected. The court has emphasized that the petitioner has the right to summon witnesses and produce evidence in support of his case. The court has also emphasized that the Chief Judicial Magistrate should have considered the request of the petitioner and allowed him to summon the Chief Manager or authorized officer from Canara Bank SAF Branch to produce documents under Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

The court has also observed that the proceedings should not get unjustifiably prolonged and a time frame should be fixed to ensure that the case is disposed of in a timely manner.
