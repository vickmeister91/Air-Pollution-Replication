The Patna High Court recently issued a ruling in a case involving a plastic scrap factory owner, Imamullah, who had filed a writ petition seeking the quashing of a case registered against him for causing noise pollution. The case was registered based on a written complaint by a sub-electoral officer, and the police investigated the case and submitted a charge sheet against Imamullah under non-cognizable offenses.

The court clarified that when police investigate a non-cognizable offense and submit a report, it will be treated as a complaint, and the magistrate can take cognizance of appropriate offenses if materials disclose the commission of such an offense. The court also noted that Section 188 of the Indian Penal Code was not applicable to the case and that the magistrate could not take cognizance of an offense under Section 188 of the Indian Penal Code without a written complaint from a public servant.

The court also explained the penalty for contravention of the provisions of the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986. The Act provides for the protection and improvement of the environment and the prevention of hazards to human beings, other living creatures, plants, and property. The Act also provides for the establishment of authorities for the implementation of the Act and the regulation of activities that may cause environmental pollution.

The court order states that a complaint must be made by a public servant in writing for a court to take cognizance of any offense under the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986. In this case, no such complaint was made, so the order taking cognizance of the offense is set aside. The court also clarifies that the learned Magistrate lacks jurisdiction to take cognizance of the offense under Section 188 of the Indian Penal Code.
