In a criminal petition filed under Section 482 of Cr.P.C, Smt. Gayathri Vikram sought to quash the order passed by the learned Principal Civil Judge (Jr. Dvn) and JMFC at Kanakapura in C.C. No.543/2009. The order was passed to take cognizance for the offence under Section 24(F) (G) (GG) (H) and 73 (D) of the Karnataka Forest Act, 1963, Section 2 of the Forest Conservation Act, 1980 and Rule 144 of the Forest Rules, and ordering to register the case. The petitioner argued that the alleged offences were non-cognizable offences and the procedure contemplated under Section 155 (2) of Cr.P.C. was not followed.

The court considered the arguments presented by the petitioner and observed that the offences alleged were non-cognizable offences. The court also noted that the procedure contemplated under Section 155 (2) of Cr.P.C. was not followed. Therefore, the court allowed the petition and quashed the proceedings initiated against the petitioner.
