The Madras High Court recently passed a court order in a case where the second accused in a complaint filed by the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board had invoked the inherent powers of the court to have the complaint quashed. The complaint was filed against the accused for violating conditions subject to which Consent under Section 26 of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 had been given. The accused, who is the Managing Director of a company, argued that he cannot be held liable for an offence committed by one of the units of the company. 

The court held that the legal infirmity in not making the company an accused is curable and that the company has to be made an accused. The court also observed that a Managing Director is in charge of and responsible to the company for the conduct of the business of the company. The petition was dismissed, and leave to appeal to the Supreme Court was refused.
