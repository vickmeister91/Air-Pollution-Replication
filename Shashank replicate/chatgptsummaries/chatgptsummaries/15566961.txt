The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 is a law that aims to protect women from domestic violence. It provides for the protection of women from physical, sexual, verbal, emotional, and economic abuse by their husbands or other family members. The Act also provides for the right of women to reside in their matrimonial homes and for the maintenance of women and children.

Section 29 of the Act provides for an appeal against an order passed by a Magistrate under the Act. The appeal can be filed before the Sessions Court or the Court of Metropolitan Magistrate. The appeal has to be filed within 30 days from the date of the order.

In this case, the appellant had filed an appeal under Section 29 of the Act against an order passed by the Metropolitan Magistrate Traffic Court-2. The order pertained to the dismissal of the appellant's application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C seeking various documents related to the respondent.

Section 91 of Cr.P.C provides for the power of the court to issue a summons or order to produce a document or thing. The section states that any court may, at any stage of any inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code, summon any person as a witness or order any person to produce any document or thing in his possession or under his control.

In this case, the appellant had sought various documents related to the respondent under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. The trial court had dismissed the application, which the appellant challenged in the present appeal. The court allowed the appeal and directed the trial court to call for the documents as sought for in the application filed under Section 91 of Cr.P.C.

The court order also directed the trial court to request the necessary documents under Section 91 of Cr.P.C from the relevant authority.
