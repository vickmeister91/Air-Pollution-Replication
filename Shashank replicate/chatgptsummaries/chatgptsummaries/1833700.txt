The court order in question pertains to a writ petition filed by Reliance Industries Limited against the State of Haryana and others. The challenge was against the order passed by the District Magistrate, Kaithal, suspending the no objection certificate (NOC) granted to the petitioner. The grounds for suspension were that the petitioner had not taken permission from the Government of India for using forest land, had not obtained completion certificates from the National Highway Authority and the District Town Planner.

The petitioner argued that there was no provision under the Petroleum Rules, 2002, for suspending an NOC once granted. The court agreed with the petitioner and quashed the impugned order. However, the court observed that the district authorities should not issue conditional/provisional NOCs as there is no provision for such certificates under the Act or the rules.

The court's decision was based on the fact that the suspension of the NOC was not in accordance with the provisions of the Petroleum Rules, 2002. The court held that once an NOC is granted, it cannot be suspended unless there is a provision for such suspension under the Act or the rules. In this case, there was no provision for the suspension of an NOC once granted.

The court also observed that the district authorities should not issue conditional/provisional NOCs as there is no provision for such certificates under the Act or the rules. The court held that the issuance of such certificates is not in accordance with the provisions of the Act or the rules and should be avoided.
