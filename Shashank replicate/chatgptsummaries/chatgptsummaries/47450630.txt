The case in question involves a petitioner named Md. Nadim Alam who had requested the quashing of criminal proceedings against him for violating the Water and Air Pollution Control Acts. The petitioner claimed that he had applied for a 'no objection certificate' from the Jharkhand State Pollution Control Board and was falsely implicated in the case. However, upon investigation, it was found that the petitioner was running a crusher machine without consent from the Pollution Control Board and had violated the provisions of the Acts.

The court, after considering the evidence presented, rejected the petitioner's request for quashing the proceedings. The decision was based on the fact that the petitioner had clearly violated the provisions of the Acts and had not obtained the necessary permissions to operate the crusher machine. The court also noted that the petitioner's claim of having applied for a 'no objection certificate' was not supported by any evidence.

The Water and Air Pollution Control Acts are important pieces of legislation that aim to protect the environment and public health. These Acts regulate the discharge of pollutants into the air and water and impose penalties on those who violate their provisions. The Jharkhand State Pollution Control Board is responsible for enforcing these Acts in the state of Jharkhand.

In this case, the petitioner had violated the provisions of the Acts by operating a crusher machine without obtaining the necessary permissions from the Pollution Control Board. The court's decision to reject the petitioner's request for quashing the proceedings sends a strong message that violations of environmental laws will not be tolerated.

It is important for individuals and businesses to comply with environmental laws and obtain the necessary permissions before undertaking any activities that may have an impact on the environment. Failure to do so can result in serious consequences, including fines and criminal proceedings.
