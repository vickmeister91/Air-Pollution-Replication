In a recent court order, M/s MSPL Limited has been directed to undertake fencing, protection, and regeneration of the safety zone area and afforestation of degraded forest land at the project cost. The order was issued in response to a writ petition filed by the company against the State of Karnataka and others. The court has also ordered the company to not cause damage to the fauna of the area and to demarcate the area at the project cost.

However, the court has rejected the company's request to quash the First Information Report (FIR) and has ordered the Deputy Conservator of Forests to inspect and survey the area in the presence of the petitioner and the Controller of Mines, Indian Bureau of Mines. This inspection will help investigate alleged violations and ensure that the company is complying with the court's orders.

The court has also held that the fourth respondent is not empowered to suspend the mining license without substantiating the violation of conditions. This means that the company's mining license cannot be suspended without proper evidence of violations.
