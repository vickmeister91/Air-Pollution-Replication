The court order pertains to three different interlocutory applications filed in the case of T.N. Godavarman vs Union of India and others. The first application concerns illegal mining in Rohtas region of Bihar and the court has directed the State of Bihar to file an affidavit and status report within eight weeks regarding the implementation of recommendations made by the Central Empowered Committee. The second application pertains to the Piparwar Open Cast Project in Jharkhand and has been dismissed by the court. The third application concerns a report by the Central Empowered Committee in Application No. 1411 of 2017 filed before it by the National Highways Authority of India and the court has directed the parties to file their responses.

In the first application, the court has taken cognizance of the issue of illegal mining in Rohtas region of Bihar. The Central Empowered Committee had made certain recommendations regarding the implementation of measures to curb illegal mining in the region. The court has directed the State of Bihar to file an affidavit and status report within eight weeks regarding the implementation of these recommendations. This is a positive step towards curbing illegal mining in the region and ensuring that the environment is protected.

The second application pertains to the Piparwar Open Cast Project in Jharkhand. The court has dismissed this application, which means that the project can continue as per the existing regulations and guidelines. This decision is in line with the principle of sustainable development, which seeks to balance economic development with environmental protection.

The third application concerns a report by the Central Empowered Committee in Application No. 1411 of 2017 filed before it by the National Highways Authority of India. The court has reviewed the report and has accepted Recommendation No. 1, which permits the diversion of forest land subject to permission granted by authorities under the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, fulfillment of conditions laid therein, and payment of Net Present Value and compensatory afforestation in the area identified for this purpose. This is a positive step towards ensuring that development projects are implemented in an environmentally sustainable manner.

The court has given the Union of India and the State of Tamil Nadu time to take instructions regarding Recommendation Nos. 2 and 3 and has listed the application for April 9, 2018. This indicates that the court is taking a cautious approach towards the implementation of Recommendations Nos. 2 and 3 and is seeking to ensure that all stakeholders are consulted before any decision is taken.
