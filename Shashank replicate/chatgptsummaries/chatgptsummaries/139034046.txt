The court order pertains to a criminal appeal filed by Sri K. Muniraju against a judgment of conviction and order of sentence passed by the Learned XXI ACCM, Bengaluru City. The case involves a complaint filed by Sri B.S. Manjunath against the accused for the offence punishable under Section 138 of N.I. Act, contending that the accused had taken a loan of Rs.2,50,000/- from the complainant and issued a cheque that was dishonoured due to insufficient funds. The trial court held the accused guilty and sentenced him to pay a fine of Rs.2,60,000/- and in default, undergo S.I. for ten months. The accused filed an appeal challenging the judgment and order of sentence.

The court found that the complainant had proved that the accused issued the cheque in question for discharge of his legal liability and rejected the accused's defence that the cheque had been misused by the complainant through his brother. The court also rejected the accused's application seeking permission to adduce the evidence of Branch Manager, Bank of India, Bangalore Main branch. The court held that the accused had failed to establish that he did not sign the cheque in question and did not take any steps to rebut the presumption available to Ex.P.1. The court also rejected the accused's application under Section 311 of Cr.P.C. to call the Manager to give his evidence, as the accused had not utilized the opportunity given by the trial court for examination of the witness.

The Criminal Appeal filed under Section 374 of Cr.P.C. was dismissed. The court upheld the judgment of conviction and order of sentence in C.C.No.7901/2012. The accused was found guilty of the offence punishable under Section 138 of N.I. Act and was sentenced to pay a fine of Rs.2,60,000/- and in default, undergo S.I. for ten months.
