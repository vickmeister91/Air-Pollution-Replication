The Jharkhand High Court recently granted anticipatory bail to Zakir Khan, who was apprehending arrest in connection with a case registered under various sections of the Indian Penal Code and other Acts. The petitioner was accused of running a crusher and stocking stone chips and dust without proper authorization. The court directed the petitioner to surrender within four weeks and furnish a bail bond of Rs. 25,000 with two sureties of the same amount. The petitioner was also required to deposit Rs. 32,000 before the trial court at the time of surrender, which would be subject to the result of the case.

The case against Zakir Khan was registered under various sections of the Indian Penal Code and other Acts, which included Section 379 (theft), Section 420 (cheating), Section 120B (criminal conspiracy), and Section 34 (common intention). The petitioner was accused of running a crusher and stocking stone chips and dust without proper authorization. The prosecution alleged that the petitioner had stolen the stone chips and dust from the government land and was selling them in the market without any legal permission.

The petitioner, Zakir Khan, approached the Jharkhand High Court seeking anticipatory bail, apprehending his arrest in connection with the case. The petitioner contended that he was innocent and had been falsely implicated in the case. He further submitted that he had not committed any offence and had been running the crusher with proper authorization from the concerned authorities.

After hearing the arguments of both the parties, the Jharkhand High Court granted anticipatory bail to Zakir Khan. The court directed the petitioner to surrender within four weeks and furnish a bail bond of Rs. 25,000 with two sureties of the same amount. The petitioner was also required to deposit Rs. 32,000 before the trial court at the time of surrender, which would be subject to the result of the case.

The court observed that the petitioner had no criminal antecedents and was a resident of the locality. The court further noted that the petitioner had cooperated with the investigation and had not absconded from the law. The court also took into consideration the fact that the petitioner had been running the crusher with proper authorization from the concerned authorities.

The court, while granting anticipatory bail to the petitioner, imposed certain conditions. The court directed the petitioner to cooperate with the investigation and not to tamper with the evidence. The court also directed the petitioner not to leave the country without the permission of the court.
