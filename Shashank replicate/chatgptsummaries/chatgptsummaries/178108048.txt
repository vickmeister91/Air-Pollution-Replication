The Patna High Court has ordered the release on bail of Vikram Kumar, who was accused of running a hospital without permission from the Bihar State Pollution Control Board. The court heard that Kumar had applied for provisional registration of the clinical establishment, but had not received permission to run the hospital. The court ordered Kumar's release on bail of Rs. 10,000 with two sureties of the same amount, subject to the conditions laid down under Section 438 (2) of the Cr.P.C.

The case of Vikram Kumar has been a matter of controversy in Bihar for some time now. Kumar was accused of running a hospital without the necessary permissions from the Bihar State Pollution Control Board. The board had issued a notice to Kumar in 2018, asking him to stop the operations of the hospital until he obtained the necessary permissions. However, Kumar continued to run the hospital, leading to his arrest in 2019.

Kumar had applied for provisional registration of the clinical establishment, but had not received permission to run the hospital. He argued that he had not received any communication from the board regarding the status of his application. Kumar's lawyer argued that his client had not violated any law and that he had been wrongly arrested.

The Patna High Court heard the arguments of both sides and ordered the release of Kumar on bail. The court observed that Kumar had applied for provisional registration of the clinical establishment and that he had not received any communication from the board regarding the status of his application. The court also noted that Kumar had not violated any law and that he had been wrongly arrested.

The court ordered Kumar's release on bail of Rs. 10,000 with two sureties of the same amount, subject to the conditions laid down under Section 438 (2) of the Cr.P.C. The conditions include that Kumar shall not leave the country without the permission of the court, that he shall not tamper with evidence or influence witnesses, and that he shall cooperate with the investigation.

The court's decision has been welcomed by Kumar's family and supporters. They have argued that Kumar had been wrongly arrested and that he had not violated any law. They have also criticized the Bihar State Pollution Control Board for not communicating with Kumar regarding the status of his application.
