The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Chandubhai Shankerbhai Patel under Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. Patel had been charged with offences under Sections 65(A)(E), 98(2), 99 and 116 of the Prohibition Act. The court has allowed the application and ordered the release of the applicant on regular bail on certain conditions.

The court considered various aspects of the case before making its decision. Firstly, it noted that Patel was not named in the FIR. Secondly, it took into account the fact that the investigation was almost concluded. Finally, the court considered the fact that Patel had been in jail since 18.04.2019.

The court also took into consideration the law laid down by the Hon'ble Apex Court in the case of Sanjay Chandra Vs. Central Bureau of Investigation. In this case, the Supreme Court had held that bail should be granted unless there are compelling reasons to deny it. The court noted that there were no compelling reasons to deny bail to Patel.

The court imposed certain conditions on Patel's bail. Firstly, he was required to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000. Secondly, he was required to furnish two sureties of like amount. Thirdly, he was required to surrender his passport to the court. Fourthly, he was required to cooperate with the investigation and attend all court hearings. Finally, he was required to not tamper with evidence or influence witnesses.

The court's decision to grant bail to Patel was based on a careful consideration of the facts of the case and the law. The court noted that bail is a rule and jail is an exception. The court also noted that the purpose of bail is to ensure that the accused attends court hearings and cooperates with the investigation. The court was satisfied that Patel would comply with the conditions of his bail and that he would not abscond or tamper with evidence.
