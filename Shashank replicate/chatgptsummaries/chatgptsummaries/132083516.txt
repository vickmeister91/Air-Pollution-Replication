The case revolves around the acquisition of land for industrial development in Rajasthan. The petitioner, Mahipal Singh, has alleged that the respondents, including the State of Rajasthan and others, illegally and arbitrarily altered the nature of the land to facilitate its allotment to RIICO. The Rajasthan State Industrial Development and Investment Corporation (RIICO) is a government agency responsible for promoting industrial development in the state.

The petitioner has argued that the land in question was originally classified as agricultural land and was not suitable for industrial development. However, the respondents allegedly altered the nature of the land to make it suitable for industrial use and then allotted it to RIICO. The petitioner has claimed that this was done in violation of the law and without following due process.

The court has taken note of the petitioner's allegations and has remitted the matter to the District Collector, Alwar. The District Collector has been directed to undertake an exercise in the backdrop of the legal provisions involved and take an appropriate decision in accordance with the law. The court has emphasized that the decision must be taken in accordance with the law and must not be arbitrary or illegal.

The court has also directed the concerned district authority to complete the exercise within four months from the date of receipt of a certified copy of the order.
