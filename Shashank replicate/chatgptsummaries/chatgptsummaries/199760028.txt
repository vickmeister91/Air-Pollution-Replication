The court order in question pertains to a petition filed under Section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.) for quashing an order passed by the 1st Additional Sessions Judge, Gwalior in Cri.Rev.No.147/2013. The petitioner had filed an application under Section 156(3) of Cr.P.C. upon which Crime No.84/2012 under Sections 420, 120-B of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) and under Section 25, 27 of the Arms Act had been registered. After completion of the investigation, the police had filed the E.R. (Final Report).

The applicant had filed an application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. for summoning notes of the case diary and copies of the license filed on behalf of the accused persons before the learned Magistrate. The same was dismissed by the learned Magistrate holding that on the basis of the complainant, the case was registered at P.S., Gwalior and after investigation, the E.R. has been filed and also there is no reason to supply the copy of the document.

The petitioner had challenged this order before the 1st Additional Sessions Judge, Gwalior in Cri.Rev.No.147/2013, which was also dismissed. The petitioner then approached the High Court of Madhya Pradesh, Gwalior Bench, with the present petition under Section 482 of Cr.P.C.

The High Court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, dismissed the petition as it found no illegality or impropriety committed by the Court below. The Court observed that the petitioner had not made out any case for interference in the impugned order. The Court further held that the Magistrate had rightly dismissed the application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. as the petitioner had failed to show any reason for the same.
