The Calcutta High Court has recently disposed of a writ petition that was filed in 2001 regarding air pollution in a particular locality. The petition was filed by a group of individuals who were concerned about the deteriorating air quality in their area. The West Bengal Pollution Control Board had taken certain steps to address the issue and had directed improvements to be made. However, the petitioners were not satisfied with the steps taken by the Board and had approached the court seeking relief.

After hearing the arguments of both parties, the court disposed of the petition and directed the petitioners to file a representation with the Board if they still had any grievances. The court stated that the Board would consider the representation objectively and would conduct an inspection of the area if necessary. The Board would then pass an appropriate order within three months of filing the representation after hearing the affected persons.

The court did not give any order regarding costs, which means that both parties would bear their own costs.
