In a recent court order, Tilakdhari Singh Gond and three others were granted bail after being accused of killing a wild boar in violation of the Wild Life Protection Act. The defense argued that the accused were innocent and falsely implicated in the crime. The court allowed the bail application and ordered the accused to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 each and a solvent surety in the same amount for their appearance in court and compliance with the conditions under Section 437(3) of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

The Wild Life Protection Act is a law in India that aims to protect wildlife and their habitats. It prohibits the hunting, killing, or capturing of any wild animal without a permit from the government. The act also provides for the establishment of protected areas, such as national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, where hunting and other activities that harm wildlife are strictly prohibited.

In this case, the accused were charged with killing a wild boar, which is a protected animal under the Wild Life Protection Act. The prosecution argued that the accused had intentionally killed the animal without a permit, and therefore, they should be punished for their actions.

However, the defense argued that the accused were innocent and had been falsely implicated in the crime. They claimed that the accused were not present at the scene of the crime and had no involvement in the killing of the wild boar. The defense also argued that the prosecution had failed to provide any concrete evidence to support their case.

After hearing both sides, the court granted bail to the accused. The court noted that the accused had no previous criminal record and were not likely to flee from justice. The court also observed that the prosecution had failed to provide any concrete evidence to support their case.

The court ordered the accused to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 each and a solvent surety in the same amount for their appearance in court and compliance with the conditions under Section 437(3) of the Code of Criminal Procedure. This section provides for the release of an accused person on bail, subject to certain conditions, such as the requirement to appear in court when required and not to commit any further offenses while on bail.
