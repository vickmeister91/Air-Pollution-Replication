The court has granted anticipatory bail to Vishal Gore, a director of a sugar factory, who was accused of involvement in illegal excavation of Murum from land owned by the Maharashtra State Farming Corporation. The applicant's counsel argued that Gore had no connection with the construction company involved in the excavation and had filed a complaint against other individuals named in a news item. The court ordered that Gore be released on a personal bond of Rs. 15,000 and one surety of the same amount, with conditions that he not tamper with prosecution evidence and attend the police station once a week. The case was adjourned until September 5, 2019.
