The petitioner challenged the order on the grounds that the Board had no power to issue directions to the Deputy Commissioner to seize the industry and that the Taluka Executive Magistrate had no jurisdiction to take action concerning water pollution.

The court found that the Board's direction to the Deputy Commissioner was without authority of law and that the Taluka Executive Magistrate had no jurisdiction to pass the order. The revision was allowed, and the impugned order was set aside.
