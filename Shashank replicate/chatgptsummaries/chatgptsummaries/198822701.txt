The case in question involves a criminal complaint filed against the accused petitioner under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act. The petitioner had taken a loan of Rs. 8 lakh and given a cheque in lieu of it, which was returned due to insufficient funds. The petitioner had filed an application under Section 91 Cr.P.C. for inspection of the complete cash-book and ledger of the non-petitioner complainant, which was dismissed by the trial court. The revision filed against this dismissal was also dismissed by the Additional Sessions Judge. The High Court upheld the decision of the lower courts, stating that the accused petitioner was only allowed to inspect entries in the ledger and cash-book that pertained to him and not other customers. The petition and stay application were dismissed.
