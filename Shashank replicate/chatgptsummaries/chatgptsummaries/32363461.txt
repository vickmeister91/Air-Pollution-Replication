The court order in question pertains to an application filed under Section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.) against an order passed by a Special Judge under the Schedule Castes & Schedule Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 in District-Dewas in Session Trial No.113/2013 dated 04.03.2017. The applicant is facing trial under various sections of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) and Section 3(2)(V) of the Schedule Castes & Schedule Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989. 

The applicant had filed an application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. stating that the complainant had filed an application before the District Magistrate, in which it was mentioned that acid was thrown on his face by his wife. The present application was filed against the order dismissing the application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. 

The court observed that the complainant was already examined in compliance with the order of the court in respect of his complaint filed before the District Magistrate and there was no ground for recalling him and confronting him with the fresh application. The application was dismissed.

To provide some context, the Schedule Castes & Schedule Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 is a law in India that aims to prevent atrocities against members of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes. The act lists various offences that are considered atrocities, including but not limited to, forcing a member of a Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe to drink or eat any inedible or obnoxious substance, sexual exploitation, and intentionally insulting or intimidating with intent to humiliate a member of a Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe in any place within public view.

Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. empowers the High Court to exercise its inherent powers to prevent abuse of the process of any court or to secure the ends of justice. This means that the High Court can intervene in cases where it deems fit to ensure that justice is served and the legal process is not being misused.

In this particular case, the applicant had filed an application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. This section deals with the power of the court to issue summons or order the production of any document or thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Cr.P.C. The applicant had claimed that the complainant had filed an application before the District Magistrate in which it was mentioned that acid was thrown on his face by his wife. The applicant had sought to recall the complainant and confront him with this fresh application.

However, the court observed that the complainant had already been examined in compliance with the order of the court in respect of his complaint filed before the District Magistrate. The court found that there was no ground for recalling him and confronting him with the fresh application. As a result, the application was dismissed.
