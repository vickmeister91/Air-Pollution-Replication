The case in question involves an application filed by the petitioner under Section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.) to challenge the order of the learned Special Judge, NDPS Act, Indore. The petitioner had previously filed an application under Section 91, 92 of Cr.P.C. and under Section 65 of the Information Technology (IT) Act, requesting that call details of certain mobile numbers be preserved as they may be required for his defense.

However, the trial judge dismissed the petitioner's application, prompting the petitioner to file the current application under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. The petitioner argued that the call details were crucial to his defense and that their preservation was necessary to ensure a fair trial.

After hearing the arguments of both parties, the court partially allowed the petitioner's application and directed the trial judge to send an intimation to the concerning company to preserve the record of the calls made by and on the mentioned mobile numbers and their locations at the relevant point of time.

This decision is significant as it highlights the importance of preserving evidence in criminal cases. The court recognized that call details could be crucial to the petitioner's defense and that their preservation was necessary to ensure a fair trial. By partially allowing the petitioner's application, the court has taken a step towards ensuring that justice is served in this case.

It is also worth noting that the court's decision was based on the provisions of the Cr.P.C. and the IT Act. These laws provide for the preservation of evidence and the protection of the rights of the accused. By invoking these laws, the petitioner was able to make a strong case for the preservation of the call details.

Overall, the court's decision in this case is a positive development for the Indian legal system. It demonstrates the importance of preserving evidence in criminal cases and upholding the rights of the accused. It also highlights the role that the Cr.P.C. and the IT Act play in ensuring a fair trial for all parties involved.
