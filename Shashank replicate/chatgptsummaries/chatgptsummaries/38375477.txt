The court order in question pertains to a petition filed under Section 482 Cr.P.C. for quashing an order passed by the Additional Sessions Judge, Faridabad, which set aside the order passed by the Judicial Magistrate, Palwal discharging the petitioners. The FIR was registered against the petitioners on the basis of a complaint made by Naresh K. Aggarwala, who alleged that the petitioners had cheated him by forging a special power of attorney.

The petitioners had approached the court seeking to quash the order passed by the Additional Sessions Judge, Faridabad, which had set aside the order passed by the Judicial Magistrate, Palwal discharging them. The court referred to the provisions of Section 227 Cr.P.C and held that the accused cannot approach the court at the stage of framing of charge and that the trial court had erred in accepting the evidence given by the accused at the stage of framing of charge. The court dismissed the petition.

The court order states that there is no need for the court to intervene and the case is dismissed. The order was given on June 1st, 2012 by Judge G. Arora and signed by Ritu Bahri.

The court order is significant as it highlights the importance of following due process of law and the need for the accused to present their evidence at the appropriate stage of the trial. The court held that the accused cannot approach the court at the stage of framing of charge and that the trial court had erred in accepting the evidence given by the accused at the stage of framing of charge.

The court order also highlights the importance of the role of the judiciary in upholding the rule of law and ensuring that justice is served. The court's decision to dismiss the petition reinforces the principle that the judiciary must remain impartial and independent in its decision-making.
