In a recent court order, a contempt of court case was dismissed due to lack of evidence of wilful disobedience on the part of the respondent. The petitioner had filed the case against the respondents for alleged disobedience of a judgment in a previous case. The Division Bench had issued a direction to issue a No Objection Certificate and a mining permit, but the petitioner claimed that the mining permit had not been granted. 

The Geologist, Mining & Geology District Office, Thodupuzha, filed an affidavit stating that subsequent events had occurred, due to which mining permission could not be granted. The court found that there was no wilful disobedience on the part of the respondent and closed the contempt proceedings. The petitioner was advised to challenge the action of not granting permission for mining in the appropriate forum, in accordance with the law.

The case highlights the importance of following court orders and the consequences of not doing so. In this case, the respondent was able to provide evidence that they were unable to grant the mining permit due to subsequent events. However, it is important to note that if there had been evidence of wilful disobedience, the consequences could have been severe.

It is also important to note that the court advised the petitioner to challenge the action of not granting permission for mining in the appropriate forum, in accordance with the law. This highlights the importance of following legal procedures and seeking redress through the appropriate channels.

Overall, this court order serves as a reminder of the importance of following court orders and the consequences of not doing so. It also highlights the importance of following legal procedures and seeking redress through the appropriate channels.
