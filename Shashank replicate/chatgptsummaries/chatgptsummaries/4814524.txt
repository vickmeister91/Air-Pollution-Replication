The petitioner's counsel argued that the trial court had disregarded the application for production of material evidence. The counsel contended that the petitioner had requested the production of certain documents that were crucial to the case, but the trial court had failed to consider this request. The counsel further argued that the trial court had relied on the testimony of the respondent without properly verifying the authenticity of the documents presented by him.

The respondent's counsel, on the other hand, argued that the trial court had considered all the evidence presented before it and had arrived at a just and fair decision. The counsel further argued that the trial court had correctly relied on the testimony of the respondent and the documents presented by him.

The court noted that the documents requested by the petitioner were crucial to the case and could have had a significant impact on the outcome of the trial. The court further observed that the trial court had relied solely on the testimony of the respondent without properly verifying the authenticity of the documents presented by him.

In light of these observations, the court set aside the judgment passed by the trial court and remanded the case back to the trial court for fresh consideration. The court directed the trial court to consider the application for production of material evidence filed by the petitioner and to properly verify the authenticity of the documents presented by the respondent.
