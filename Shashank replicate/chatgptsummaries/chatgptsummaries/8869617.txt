The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Ratanlal, who had been issued a notice under Section 41A Cr.P.C. by the Investigating Officer to appear before him at a specified location. However, since the allegations contained in the FIR pertained to non-bailable offenses, Ratanlal filed an application under Section 438 Cr.P.C. apprehending arrest. The court has directed that Ratanlal shall surrender before the concerned court on January 17, 2018, and shall be released on his furnishing personal bond for a sum of Rs.25,000/- with two sureties of like amount to the satisfaction of the Judicial Magistrate, Nawa Shahar.

The order is significant as it highlights the importance of following proper legal procedures when dealing with non-bailable offenses. The court has made it clear that Ratanlal cannot avoid arrest by simply filing a bail application under Section 438 Cr.P.C. and that he must surrender before the concerned court. This is in line with the provisions of the law, which require that a person accused of a non-bailable offense must be arrested and produced before a magistrate.

The court's decision to grant bail to Ratanlal is also noteworthy. The court has taken into account the fact that Ratanlal has filed a bail application and has agreed to surrender before the concerned court. The court has also considered the fact that Ratanlal has furnished a personal bond for a sum of Rs.25,000/- with two sureties of like amount. This indicates that the court has confidence in Ratanlal's ability to appear before the court and face trial.

The court's decision to grant bail to Ratanlal is also significant in light of the fact that the allegations against him pertain to non-bailable offenses. This indicates that the court has taken a lenient view of the matter and has considered the circumstances of the case before granting bail. This is in line with the principles of natural justice, which require that a person accused of a crime be given a fair trial.
