The Karnataka High Court recently issued three court orders pertaining to land ownership and transfer in the state. The first order dealt with the Karnataka Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Transfer of Certain Lands Act, 1978. The question at hand was whether a "granted land" (land granted by the government to a person belonging to a Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe) can still be considered a "granted land" if occupancy rights were subsequently granted to a tenant under the Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961. The court referred to various sections of the Act and concluded that such land cannot be considered a "granted land." The order also discussed provisions related to the prohibition of transfer of granted lands and the commencement of the Act.

The second court order pertained to the grant of occupancy rights under the Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, for a land that was earlier transferred or leased in violation of Section 4 of the Act. The order declared such grants as null and void and did not exempt such lands from the Act's provisions. The case in question involved a land in Alnavar village, which was leased to one Kedila Setty and subsequently granted to one Namdari Naik. The order confirmed the legal bars on the transfer of such lands and directed the resumption of the land to the government. The petitioners, who claimed to be the legal heirs of Namdari Naik, challenged the order.

The third court order declared the lease of a certain land to be invalid and ordered for the resumption of the land. The authorities had not recorded their existing rights over the land, and the matter was referred to the concerned commission for further consideration in accordance with the law. The order was set aside, and the matter was remanded for reconsideration.
