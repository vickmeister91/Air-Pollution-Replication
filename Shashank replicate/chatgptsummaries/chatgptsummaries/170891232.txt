The Wild Life (Protection) Act is a law in India that provides for the protection of wild animals and plants, and for matters connected therewith or ancillary or incidental thereto. The Act prohibits hunting of wild animals, including birds and reptiles, and also prohibits the trade in wildlife and wildlife products.

The IPC, or Indian Penal Code, is the main criminal code of India. It covers a wide range of offenses, including theft, fraud, assault, and murder.

Anticipatory bail is a provision in Indian law that allows a person to seek bail in anticipation of being arrested. This is typically done when a person believes that they may be arrested for a crime, but have not yet been charged.
