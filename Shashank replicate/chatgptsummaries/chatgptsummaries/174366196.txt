The court has granted anticipatory bail to Deepak Pratap Deo and Biku Kumar in connection with a case involving the alleged offence under Sections 39A, 51 and 52 of the Wild Life (Protection) Act. The case pertains to the apprehension of three spotted deers from their house. However, no harm was caused to the deers by the petitioners, and the deers were subsequently handed over to the forest department.

The court directed that the petitioners shall be released on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs.10,000/- each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Sub-Divisional Judicial Magistrate, Garhwa, subject to conditions as laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure.

Anticipatory bail is a provision in the Indian legal system that allows a person to seek bail in anticipation of an arrest on accusation of having committed a non-bailable offence. The provision is intended to protect the individual's personal liberty and prevent unnecessary detention.

In this case, the petitioners were apprehending their arrests in connection with the alleged offence under Sections 39A, 51 and 52 of the Wild Life (Protection) Act. Section 39A of the Act pertains to the prohibition of hunting in a sanctuary, national park or closed area. Section 51 deals with the penalty for hunting in a sanctuary, national park or closed area, while Section 52 pertains to the penalty for hunting in any other area.

The case pertains to the apprehension of three spotted deers from the petitioners' house. However, no harm was caused to the deers by the petitioners, and the deers were subsequently handed over to the forest department. The court took note of this fact and granted anticipatory bail to the petitioners.

The court directed that the petitioners shall be released on bail on furnishing bail bonds of Rs.10,000/- each with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of learned Sub-Divisional Judicial Magistrate, Garhwa, subject to conditions as laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure. Section 438(2) lays down certain conditions that must be satisfied by the person seeking anticipatory bail. These conditions include that the person must cooperate with the investigation, not tamper with evidence, and not commit any offence while on bail.
