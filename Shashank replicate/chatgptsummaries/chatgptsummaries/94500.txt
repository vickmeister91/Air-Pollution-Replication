The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Jeby John, who is accused of violating the Wild Life Protection Act by transporting parrots without proper authorization. According to reports, the petitioner had allegedly arranged for the transportation of 56 parrots from Bangalore to his establishment, Golden Aquarium, at Kunnamkulam. The second accused, who was transporting the parrots, had reportedly named the petitioner as the person to whom the consignment was sent.

Jeby John had sought anticipatory bail, claiming innocence and alleging that the second accused had been influenced by business rivals to implicate him. However, the court dismissed the bail application, stating that the petitioner must appear before the investigating officer or the magistrate and seek regular bail.

The Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, is an Indian law that provides for the protection of wild animals and plants and for matters connected therewith or ancillary or incidental thereto. The Act prohibits the hunting of wild animals, birds, and plants, except under certain circumstances, and provides for the establishment of protected areas, such as national parks and wildlife sanctuaries.

The transportation of parrots without proper authorization is a violation of the Wild Life Protection Act, and can result in imprisonment for a term of up to three years, or a fine of up to Rs. 25,000, or both.

Jeby John had claimed that he was innocent, and that the second accused had been influenced by business rivals to implicate him. However, the court found no evidence to support this claim, and dismissed his bail application.
