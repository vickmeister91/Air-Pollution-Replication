The court order pertains to the regulation of saw-mills in the state of Jharkhand for the protection and conservation of forests and the environment. The case was brought by Maa Bhawani Timber against the State of Jharkhand's policy decision to regulate the number of saw-mills in the state and directions issued regarding the grant and renewal of licenses for running saw-mills. The policy decision was made in compliance with the direction of the Hon'ble Supreme Court in T.N. Godavarman Thirumulkpad's case to assess the optimum distance from the forest for running saw-mills.

The court examined the relevant directions of the Supreme Court and upheld the State's policy decision for conservation of forests and protection of the environment. The court cited provisions of the law that allow the government to declare certain areas as prohibited for saw-mill operations and to refuse renewal or grant of licenses. The court emphasized the importance of protecting the environment and cited constitutional provisions and previous court rulings to support its decision.

The petitioner, Maa Bhawani Timber, claimed an absolute right to run a saw-mill and challenged the government's decision to restrict their license period and require the saw-mill to be relocated. However, the court dismissed the petitioner's claim and upheld the government's decision. The court concluded that the government's policy decision is in line with the directives of the Supreme Court and dismissed the writ petition.

The court's decision is significant as it upholds the importance of protecting the environment and conserving forests. The court's decision also highlights the government's power to regulate industries that may have a negative impact on the environment. The court's decision is in line with previous court rulings and constitutional provisions that prioritize the protection of the environment.

The court's decision also emphasizes the need for industries to comply with regulations and policies that are in place to protect the environment. The court's decision serves as a reminder to industries that they must operate in a manner that is sustainable and does not harm the environment.
