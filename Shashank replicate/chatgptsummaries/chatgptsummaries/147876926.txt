The Patna High Court has issued an order to the Bihar Pollution Control Board to prepare a comprehensive plan for the prevention, control, and abatement of air pollution. The court has also directed the Board to ensure the execution of the plan within 15 days. The order comes in response to a petition filed by a group of concerned citizens who sought the court's intervention in addressing the issue of air pollution in the state.

The court has further directed the Board to file an affidavit regarding the actions taken by them in discharge of their duties under Section 17 of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. This section mandates the State Pollution Control Board to take measures for the prevention, control, and abatement of air pollution.

The court has listed the case for June 28, 2016, to receive an update on the action taken by the State Government. This indicates that the court is closely monitoring the situation and expects the State Government to take swift action in addressing the issue of air pollution.

Air pollution is a major concern in Bihar, with several cities in the state ranking among the most polluted in the country. The state has been grappling with high levels of particulate matter, nitrogen dioxide, and sulfur dioxide, which have been linked to a range of health problems, including respiratory illnesses, heart disease, and stroke.

The court's order is a significant step towards addressing the issue of air pollution in Bihar. By directing the State Pollution Control Board to prepare a comprehensive plan and ensure its execution, the court has sent a strong message to the State Government that it must take concrete steps to address the issue of air pollution.

The court's order is also significant in that it highlights the role of the State Pollution Control Board in addressing the issue of air pollution. The Board has a crucial role to play in monitoring air quality, enforcing pollution control measures, and ensuring compliance with environmental regulations.

The court's order is likely to have a positive impact on the efforts to address air pollution in Bihar. It is expected to increase public awareness of the issue and put pressure on the State Government to take swift action. The order is also likely to encourage other states to take similar steps to address the issue of air pollution.

The court's order highlights the role of the State Pollution Control Board in addressing the issue of air pollution and is likely to have a positive impact on the efforts to address the issue in Bihar. The court's monitoring of the situation and its listing of the case for an update on the action taken by the State Government indicate that it expects swift action to be taken to address the issue of air pollution.
