The court order in question relates to a criminal revision petition filed by Rasul Bieg @ Nazim and others against an order passed by the Additional District and Sessions Judge in Chitradurga. The impugned order allowed an application filed by the Public Prosecutor under Section 91 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.). The petitioners had challenged the order on the grounds that it was not maintainable since it was not a final order.

The court, after considering the arguments put forth by both parties, dismissed the revision petition on the grounds that the impugned order was not a final order. The court held that since the order was merely an interlocutory order, it could not be challenged through a revision petition. The court also noted that the petitioners had failed to demonstrate how the impugned order had caused them any prejudice or harm.

Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. empowers a court to issue a summons or order for the production of any document or other thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. The Public Prosecutor had filed an application under this section seeking the production of certain documents that were in the possession of the petitioners.

The petitioners had argued that the application filed by the Public Prosecutor was not maintainable since it was not supported by any affidavit or other evidence. They had also contended that the documents sought by the Public Prosecutor were not relevant to the case.

The court, however, rejected these arguments and allowed the application filed by the Public Prosecutor. The court held that the Public Prosecutor was not required to file an affidavit or other evidence in support of the application since Section 91 did not require such a requirement. The court also held that the documents sought by the Public Prosecutor were relevant to the case and were necessary for the purposes of the trial.
