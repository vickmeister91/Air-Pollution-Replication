The court order in question pertains to a petition filed by Mohd. Sirajuddin Ansari, proprietor of M/s Ansari Retrading, seeking relief from an order to disconnect electricity supply to his business. The order to disconnect was issued by the Chhattisgarh Environment Protection Board, and as no challenge had been made to that order, the petitioner could not be granted any relief.

The petitioner had argued that the order to disconnect electricity supply was arbitrary and without any basis. He had also contended that the order had been issued without giving him an opportunity to be heard. However, the court noted that the petitioner had not challenged the order of the Environmental Board, and therefore, it could not grant him any relief.

The court observed that the petitioner had the right to challenge the order of the Environmental Board if he wished to do so. The court also noted that the petitioner had not produced any material to show that the order of the Environmental Board was arbitrary or without any basis. In the absence of any challenge to the order of the Environmental Board, the court could not interfere with the order to disconnect electricity supply.

The court also observed that the petitioner had not made any prayer for quashing the order of the Environmental Board. The court noted that the petitioner had only sought relief from the order to disconnect electricity supply. The court held that it could not grant relief to the petitioner in the absence of any challenge to the order of the Environmental Board.

The court further observed that the petitioner had not produced any material to show that he had suffered any loss or damage as a result of the order to disconnect electricity supply. The court held that in the absence of any material to show that the petitioner had suffered any loss or damage, it could not grant him any relief.

In conclusion, the court disposed of the petition with the observation that the petitioner could challenge the order of the Environmental Board if he wished to do so. The court held that in the absence of any challenge to the order of the Environmental Board, it could not grant any relief to the petitioner. The court also observed that the petitioner had not produced any material to show that he had suffered any loss or damage as a result of the order to disconnect electricity supply. The court held that in the absence of any material to show that the petitioner had suffered any loss or damage, it could not grant him any relief.
