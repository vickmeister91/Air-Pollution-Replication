The Punjab-Haryana High Court recently dismissed a petition filed by M/s Billa Bricks, which challenged an order passed by the Pollution Control Board. The court stated that there is an alternative remedy available to the petitioner, which is to file a statutory appeal under Section 31 of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The court hoped that the Appellate Authority would consider and decide the appeal expeditiously, within six weeks from the date of filing.

The case involved a dispute between M/s Billa Bricks and the Pollution Control Board regarding the pollution caused by the brick kiln operated by the petitioner. The Pollution Control Board had issued an order directing the petitioner to take certain measures to control the pollution caused by the kiln. The petitioner challenged this order before the High Court, arguing that the order was arbitrary and illegal.

The High Court, however, held that the petitioner had an alternative remedy available to them, which was to file a statutory appeal under Section 31 of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The court noted that the petitioner had not exhausted this remedy before approaching the High Court. The court also observed that the Appellate Authority would be better equipped to deal with the technical aspects of the case, as it would have the benefit of expert opinion.

The court further directed the Appellate Authority to consider and decide the appeal expeditiously, within six weeks from the date of filing. The court emphasized the importance of expeditious disposal of such appeals, as delay in deciding such matters could have serious consequences for the environment and public health.

The court's decision highlights the importance of exhausting all available remedies before approaching the High Court. It also underscores the need for expeditious disposal of appeals in environmental matters, as delay in deciding such matters could have serious consequences for the environment and public health.

In conclusion, the Punjab-Haryana High Court's decision in the case of M/s Billa Bricks serves as a reminder to litigants to exhaust all available remedies before approaching the High Court. It also emphasizes the importance of expeditious disposal of appeals in environmental matters, as delay in deciding such matters could have serious consequences for the environment and public health. The court's direction to the Appellate Authority to decide the appeal expeditiously within six weeks from the date of filing is a welcome step towards ensuring timely resolution of environmental disputes.
