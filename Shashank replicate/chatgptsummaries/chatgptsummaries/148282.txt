The court order pertains to a bail application filed by the petitioner under Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The petitioner is accused of violating the Wild Life Protection Act by killing a wild boar while working as a night watcher for Harrison Malayalam Plantations. The prosecution has alleged that the petitioner committed the offence with the intention of selling the meat.

The court has granted bail to the petitioner, subject to certain conditions. The petitioner has been directed to report to the Investigating Officer as and when required. The petitioner has also been instructed not to tamper with any evidence related to the case or commit any further offences while on bail.

The court has considered various factors while granting bail to the petitioner. The court has taken into account the fact that the petitioner has no previous criminal record and has cooperated with the investigation. The court has also noted that the petitioner has been in custody for a considerable period of time and that the investigation is still ongoing.

The court has further observed that the offence in question is bailable in nature and that the petitioner is entitled to bail as a matter of right. The court has also considered the fact that the petitioner has a family to support and that his continued detention would cause undue hardship to his family.

The court has imposed certain conditions on the petitioner while granting bail. The petitioner has been directed to furnish a personal bond and surety bond in the sum of Rs. 50,000 each. The petitioner has also been instructed to surrender his passport and not leave the jurisdiction of the court without prior permission.

The court has also directed the petitioner to attend all court hearings and cooperate with the investigation. The petitioner has been warned that any violation of the conditions of bail would result in the cancellation of bail and his arrest.

In conclusion, the court has granted bail to the petitioner, subject to certain conditions, in the case of alleged violation of the Wild Life Protection Act. The court has considered various factors while granting bail, including the nature of the offence, the petitioner's cooperation with the investigation, and the petitioner's family circumstances. The court has also imposed certain conditions on the petitioner to ensure that he does not tamper with evidence or commit any further offences while on bail.
