The Madras High Court recently passed a court order pertaining to a group of writ petitions filed by M/s. New Fly Ash Bricks Manufacturers Association against the Government of India and Tamil Nadu Electricity Board. The petitioners sought a writ of Mandamus directing the respondents to implement the Office Memorandum and notification issued by the first respondent and forbear them from collecting any service charge from the members of the petitioner association in respect of Fly Ash allotment to be used for manufacturing the Bricks, Blocks, and Tiles, etc.

The court heard all the writ petitions together and disposed of them by a common order. The court referred to a similar case where the Hon'ble Division Bench allowed the writ appeals filed by the writ petitioners. The court held that the issue involved in these batch of cases are squarely covered in favor of the petitioners by the decision of the Hon'ble Division Bench and allowed the writ petitions on the same lines.
