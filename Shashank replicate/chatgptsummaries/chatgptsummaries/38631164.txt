The court order in question pertains to an application filed under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973. The application was filed against an order passed by the learned First A.S.J, Sidhi in Criminal Revision No.32/2015. The applicant, Vikramaditya Singh, had filed an application for granting Supurdginama of Tractor and Trolley bearing registration number MP 53-M/1080 & 1081, which was dismissed by the learned Judicial Magistrate First Class, Sidhi. The court quashed the impugned orders and directed that the seized Tractor and Trolley be delivered to the applicant on Supurdginama subject to certain conditions.

The case revolves around the seizure of a Tractor and Trolley bearing registration number MP 53-M/1080 & 1081. The applicant, Vikramaditya Singh, had filed an application for granting Supurdginama of the seized Tractor and Trolley. However, the learned Judicial Magistrate First Class, Sidhi dismissed the application. The applicant then filed an application under Section 482 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, against the order passed by the learned First A.S.J, Sidhi in Criminal Revision No.32/2015.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, quashed the impugned orders and directed that the seized Tractor and Trolley be delivered to the applicant on Supurdginama subject to certain conditions. The court also directed the applicant to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 and two sureties of Rs. 25,000 each. The court further directed the applicant to deposit a sum of Rs. 1,00,000 with the learned Judicial Magistrate First Class, Sidhi, as a condition for the release of the seized Tractor and Trolley.

The court observed that the learned Judicial Magistrate First Class, Sidhi had dismissed the application for granting Supurdginama of the seized Tractor and Trolley without considering the relevant provisions of law. The court noted that the applicant had fulfilled all the conditions for the grant of Supurdginama and that the learned Judicial Magistrate First Class, Sidhi had erred in dismissing the application.

The court also observed that the seizure of the Tractor and Trolley was not in accordance with the provisions of law. The court noted that the seizure was made without any notice to the applicant and without giving him an opportunity to be heard. The court held that the seizure was illegal and directed that the seized Tractor and Trolley be released to the applicant on Supurdginama subject to the conditions mentioned above.
