The charges against the petitioners for offences under Sections 278, 285, 587, 290, 336 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) read with Section 15 and 16 of the Environment Protection Act, 1986 were rejected by the lower courts.

The petitioner had contended that the lower courts had failed to appreciate the fact that the appointment of officers under Section 24 was mandatory and that the failure to appoint such officers rendered the entire process of monitoring and enforcement of the Act invalid. However, the court rejected these arguments and held that the lower courts had correctly interpreted the provisions of the Act. The court also held that the Central Government had taken adequate measures to protect and improve the quality of the environment, as required under Section 19 of the Act.

The court further observed that the charges against the petitioners had been rightly rejected by the lower courts, as there was no evidence to suggest that the pollution caused by their activities had resulted in any harm to the environment or to public health.
