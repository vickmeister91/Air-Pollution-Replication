The court order pertains to three appeals filed by Rawat Mal Jain against the Delhi Development Authority, Municipal Corporation of Delhi, and the Union of India. The appellants had filed a suit for permanent injunction against the respondents, claiming that they had no right or interest in the land in question and had no authority to interfere with the appellants' possession of the land. The respondents argued that the land was designated as a green area in the Master Plan of Delhi Perspective 2001 and could not be used for any purpose other than the one shown in the plan.

The court found that the appellants were using the land for an unauthorized purpose and vacated the interim order. The Master Plan for Delhi Perspective 2001 emphasizes the preservation of ecology and creation of a physical and social environment for improved quality of life for Delhi's residents. The court order emphasizes the importance of preserving the ecology and environment of Delhi for the improved quality of life of its residents and as a model for the rest of the country.

The Master Plan and Zonal Development Plan have a statutory force, and land cannot be used for a purpose that is not permitted or authorized by them. The court also notes that the enforcement of planning controls is essential to protect the environment and prevent ecological imbalance. The court dismisses the appeals of the appellants who violated the Delhi Development Act, Master Plan, Zonal Development Plan, and the Delhi Lands (Restrictions on Transfer) Act, 1972, and used the land for commercial activity, which was not a permitted use.
