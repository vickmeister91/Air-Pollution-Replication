The court order in question pertains to an application for bail filed by two individuals, Billor Singh and Kailash Solanki, who were arrested on charges of possessing leopard skin, an offense punishable with up to three years of imprisonment. The defense counsel argued that the accused were innocent and falsely implicated in the case. The state counsel opposed the application, but the court allowed it, directing the release of the accused on bail upon furnishing a personal bond of Rs. 20,000 each with one solvent surety of the same amount. The court also directed the accused to abide by the conditions enumerated under section 437(3) of the Cr.P.C.
