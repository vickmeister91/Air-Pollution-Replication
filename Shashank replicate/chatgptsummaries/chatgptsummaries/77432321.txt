The case of M/S New Data Bus Service Pvt. Ltd. and the Government of NCT of Delhi involves the petitioner's buses being granted stage carriage permits by the State Transport Authority of the Government of the National Capital Territory of Delhi. However, the Assistant Commissioner of Police (Traffic) started challaning the buses of the petitioner on the ground of overtaking, picking up/dropping passengers without a valid bus stop. The documents of the petitioner including R.C. fitness certificate and permits had been seized. The petitioner chose to compound the same by paying fines ranging from Rs.2000/- to Rs.3100/-. The petitioner filed a writ petition stating that the acts of the respondents are arbitrary and unjustified and violate his fundamental rights. The court dismissed the petition stating that the show cause notices issued to the petitioner related to the violation of the permit conditions by the vehicles of the petitioner.

The court order discusses the directions issued by the Supreme Court in M.C. Mehta v. UOI regarding the operation of heavy and medium transport vehicles and light goods vehicles in Delhi. These directions include the requirement for speed control devices, segregation of bus lanes, and designated bus stops. The court also notes that the directions have been used by traffic police to harass bus operators, leading to concerns from both the petitioners and the police. The court suggests seeking clarifications from the Supreme Court regarding the subsequent judgment in the UPSRTC case.

The court order states that the conditions of every permit must be followed without any discretion. The court suggests that the petitioners seek clarification from the Supreme Court regarding a previous judgment. The court also notes that there is no urgency in the matter and that the interim directions are causing hardship to the respondents. The judgment of a previous case is binding and no further orders are necessary. The petition and pending applications are disposed of accordingly.
