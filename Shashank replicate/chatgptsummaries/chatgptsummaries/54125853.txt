The case of P.A. George vs. Dhyanakendram involved a writ petition filed by the petitioner seeking a writ of mandamus or any other writ, order or direction to control the sound emanating from the 4th respondent Dhyanakendram within the permissible limit under the Noise Pollution (Regulation & Control) Rule, 2000, by installing sound control measures. The petitioner argued that the sound emanating from the respondent's premises was causing a disturbance to the petitioner and other residents in the area.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, directed the 1st respondent to consider the petitioner's representation in accordance with the law and take action if there is any violation of the Noise Pollution (Regulation & Control) Rule, 2000. The court also directed that the decision shall be taken within a month from the date of receipt of the judgment.

The court's decision was based on the fact that the right to a pollution-free environment is a fundamental right guaranteed under Article 21 of the Constitution of India. The court noted that excessive noise pollution can have a detrimental effect on the health and well-being of individuals and can also cause damage to the environment.

The court also observed that the Noise Pollution (Regulation & Control) Rule, 2000, provides for the regulation and control of noise pollution and sets out the permissible limits for noise levels in different areas. The court held that it is the duty of the authorities to ensure that these rules are enforced and that action is taken against those who violate them.

The court's decision in this case is significant as it reaffirms the importance of protecting the environment and the health and well-being of individuals. It also highlights the need for strict enforcement of laws and regulations related to pollution control.
