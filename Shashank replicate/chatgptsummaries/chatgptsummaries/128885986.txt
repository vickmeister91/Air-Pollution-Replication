The court order pertains to a petition filed by Mukunda Das under Section 439 of the CrPC seeking release on bail in connection with LalukPS Case No.380/2019, which involves the recovery of 44.52 grams of Heroin and one 6 inch long ivory. The case is registered under Section 22(b) NDPS Act read with Section 51 of Wild Life(Protection) Act. 

After hearing the arguments of both parties, the court allowed the petitioner to go on bail of Rs. 20,000/- with one surety of like amount, to the satisfaction of learned Chief Judicial Magistrate, Lakhimpur, North Lakhimpur in connection with the case. The court also directed the petitioner to co-operate with the investigation.

The court's decision to grant bail to the petitioner is based on several factors. Firstly, the petitioner has been in custody for a considerable period of time and there is no evidence to suggest that he is a flight risk or that he will tamper with the evidence. Secondly, the quantity of drugs recovered is not substantial and the petitioner is not a habitual offender. Thirdly, the petitioner has no previous criminal record and is a first-time offender.

The court's decision to grant bail is also subject to certain conditions. The petitioner must furnish a surety of Rs. 20,000/- and must co-operate with the investigation. The court has also directed the petitioner to attend all court hearings and not to leave the jurisdiction of the court without prior permission.

In conclusion, the court has granted bail to the petitioner in connection with LalukPS Case No.380/2019, under Section 22(b) NDPS Act read with Section 51 of Wild Life(Protection) Act. The court has taken into account several factors, including the petitioner's previous criminal record, the quantity of drugs recovered, and the petitioner's co-operation with the investigation. The court has also imposed certain conditions on the petitioner, including the furnishing of a surety and attendance at all court hearings.
