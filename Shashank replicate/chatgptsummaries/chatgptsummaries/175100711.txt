The Supreme Court of India recently dismissed a civil appeal made by the Punjab Irrigation Department against Nishant Kumar Alag and others. The case involved allegations of violations of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980. The court ordered an investigation by an officer not below the rank of Addl. Chief Secretary to determine the officers responsible for the violations.

The case began when Nishant Kumar Alag and others filed a petition before the National Green Tribunal (NGT) alleging that the Punjab Irrigation Department had violated the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980. The NGT had ordered an investigation into the matter and had directed the Punjab Irrigation Department to submit a report. However, the department failed to comply with the NGT's order, and the matter was taken up by the Supreme Court.

The Supreme Court noted that the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 was enacted to protect forests and wildlife in the country. The court observed that the Act required that any diversion of forest land for non-forest purposes must be approved by the central government. The court also noted that the Act required that compensatory afforestation be carried out for any forest land that was diverted for non-forest purposes.

The court found that the Punjab Irrigation Department had violated the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 by diverting forest land for non-forest purposes without obtaining the necessary approvals from the central government. The court also found that the department had failed to carry out compensatory afforestation as required by the Act.

The court ordered an investigation by an officer not below the rank of Addl. Chief Secretary to determine the officers responsible for the violations. The Addl. Chief Secretary was instructed to consider the arguments made by the appellant and submit a report. If the report is favorable to the appellant, they are permitted to approach the Tribunal again.

The court's decision is significant as it underscores the importance of protecting forests and wildlife in the country. The Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 is a crucial piece of legislation that seeks to balance the need for development with the need to protect the environment. The court's decision sends a strong message that violations of the Act will not be tolerated and that those responsible will be held accountable.
