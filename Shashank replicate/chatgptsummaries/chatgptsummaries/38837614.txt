The Karnataka High Court recently passed a court order in a writ petition filed by Sri. S. Ramesh Kumar against the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board and Bangalore Electricity Supply Company Limited. The petitioner had sought to quash a corrigendum passed by the Pollution Control Board. The court observed that the corrigendum modified an earlier direction issued under the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. 

The court noted that the petitioner had the option to file a substantive appeal under sub-section (c) of Section 33-B of the said Act. As such, the court declined to entertain the petition and disposed of it, while keeping the remedy of the petitioner open. 

The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 is an important piece of legislation that seeks to prevent and control water pollution. The Act provides for the establishment of Pollution Control Boards at the central and state levels. These boards are responsible for enforcing the provisions of the Act and ensuring that industries and other entities do not pollute water bodies. 

In this case, the petitioner had challenged a corrigendum passed by the Pollution Control Board. The corrigendum had modified an earlier direction issued under the Act. The court observed that the petitioner had the option to file a substantive appeal under sub-section (c) of Section 33-B of the Act. This section provides for the filing of appeals against orders issued by the Pollution Control Board. 

The court's decision to decline to entertain the petition and dispose of it while keeping the remedy of the petitioner open is significant. It indicates that the court recognized the importance of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and the role of the Pollution Control Board in enforcing its provisions. At the same time, the court ensured that the petitioner's right to seek redressal was not curtailed. 

The court's decision also highlights the importance of following due process and exhausting all available remedies before approaching the court.
