The Madras High Court recently passed a judgment in a writ petition filed by P. Krishnan against the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board and other respondents. The petition was filed regarding the issuance of a Consent Order to South India Bottling Company to start an industry. The petitioner argued that the consent was given without proper inquiry and in contravention of Section 25 of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974.

The court examined the provisions of the Act and found that the consent was given after conducting an inquiry and complying with all procedures. The court also noted that the petitioner did not avail of the alternative remedy of appealing to the State Government and dismissed the writ petition.

The case highlights the importance of following due process and availing of alternative remedies before approaching the court. The court noted that the petitioner had not exhausted all available remedies before approaching the court. This is an important principle of law, as it ensures that the court's time is not wasted on cases that could have been resolved through other means.

The court's decision also underscores the importance of complying with environmental laws and regulations. The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, is a crucial piece of legislation that seeks to prevent and control water pollution. The Act lays down procedures for obtaining consent to establish and operate industries that may have an impact on water quality. The Act also provides for penalties for non-compliance.

In this case, the court found that the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board had followed due process and complied with all procedures before granting consent to the South India Bottling Company. The court's decision is a reminder to all stakeholders that environmental laws and regulations must be followed in letter and spirit.

The case also highlights the role of the judiciary in ensuring that environmental laws are enforced. The court's decision in this case is a testament to the judiciary's commitment to upholding the rule of law and protecting the environment.

Krishnan against the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board and other respondents is an important reminder of the need to follow due process and comply with environmental laws and regulations.
