However, when the cheque was presented for collection, it was dishonoured. The petitioner then filed a Petition under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. viz., C.M.P.No.3589 of 2014, for summoning the statement of accounts from the date of opening the SB Account No.10244, by the respondent in Canara Bank, Raj Nivas, No.207, Cauvery Road, Karungalpalayam. The Trial Court dismissed the petition, and the Criminal Original Petition was filed to set aside the said order. However, the court dismissed the Criminal Original Petition, stating that no useful purpose would be achieved by summoning the Bank accounts of the respondent.

In another case, the petitioner, R. Suresh Kumar, had filed a Criminal Original Petition to quash the proceedings in C.C.No. 107 of 2014 on the file of the Judicial Magistrate, First Class, Tirupur. The petitioner had issued a cheque for Rs. 1,00,000/- on 28.06.2013, which was dishonoured. The respondent had filed a complaint under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, and the Trial Court had taken cognizance of the offence. The petitioner had contended that the cheque was issued as a security deposit, and not towards any liability. The petitioner had also stated that the respondent had not given any notice as required under Section 138 of the Act. The court observed that the petitioner had not produced any evidence to prove that the cheque was issued as a security deposit. The court also noted that the respondent had sent a legal notice to the petitioner, which was returned with a postal endorsement "refused". The court held that the Trial Court had rightly taken cognizance of the offence, and dismissed the Criminal Original Petition.
