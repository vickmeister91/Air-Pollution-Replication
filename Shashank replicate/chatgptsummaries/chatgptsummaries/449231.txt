The Town Club had filed a petition under Article 226 of the Constitution of India against the District Collector and the Tahsildar for issuing a notice to pay arrears of rent for the period between 1994 to 1997. The petitioner argued that the order was passed without giving proper opportunity and violated the terms and conditions of the license. However, the court dismissed the petition, stating that the petitioner was given due opportunity to represent itself in the enquiry before re-fixing the rent and there was no violation of principles of natural justice.

The court also noted that the petitioner had violated the terms and conditions by putting up structures without approval and subletting to third parties, and the respondents had not taken steps for removal of such encroachments and had only demanded enhanced lease amount. This indicates that the petitioner was not entirely innocent in the matter and had violated the terms of the license.

The case highlights the importance of adhering to the terms and conditions of a license or agreement. The petitioner had violated the terms of the license by putting up structures without approval and subletting to third parties. This not only violated the terms of the license but also created encroachments that were not removed by the respondents. The respondents had only demanded an enhanced lease amount, which was not enough to address the issue of encroachments.

The court's decision to dismiss the petition was based on the fact that the petitioner was given due opportunity to represent itself in the enquiry before re-fixing the rent and there was no violation of principles of natural justice. This indicates that the court was satisfied with the process followed by the respondents in re-fixing the rent and issuing the notice to pay arrears of rent.

The case also highlights the importance of following the principles of natural justice. The court noted that there was no violation of principles of natural justice in the process followed by the respondents. This indicates that the respondents had followed a fair and just process in re-fixing the rent and issuing the notice to pay arrears of rent.
