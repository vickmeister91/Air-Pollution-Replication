The Patna High Court has recently issued an order for the release of Anil Kumar and Binod Kumar on bail in connection with Barun P.S. Case No. 116 of 2018. The case pertains to allegations of carrying sand without proper challan and fleeing from the police party. The court has set certain conditions for the bail, including a bail amount of Rs. 25,000 each with two sureties of the same amount. Additionally, one of the bailors must be a local person with sufficient immoveable properties and must cooperate in the investigation.

The order comes after the accused had been in custody for a considerable period of time. The court took into account the fact that the investigation in the case had been completed and the chargesheet had already been filed. The court also noted that the accused had no previous criminal record and were not likely to abscond or tamper with evidence.

The court's decision to grant bail to the accused is in line with the principles of natural justice and the presumption of innocence until proven guilty. The court has also taken into account the fact that the accused have a right to liberty and freedom, which cannot be curtailed without sufficient cause.

The conditions set by the court for the bail are aimed at ensuring that the accused cooperate with the investigation and do not abscond. The requirement for a local person with sufficient immoveable properties as a bailor is aimed at ensuring that the accused do not flee the jurisdiction of the court. The cooperation of the bailor in the investigation is also expected to ensure that the accused do not tamper with evidence.

The order of the Patna High Court is a reminder of the importance of the principles of natural justice and the presumption of innocence until proven guilty. It is also a reminder of the need for the courts to balance the rights of the accused with the need for justice to be served.
