The Karnataka High Court recently passed an order in a criminal petition filed by Sri K. Manjunatha seeking bail in the event of his arrest for offenses under the Karnataka Forest Act and Wildlife Protection Act. The petitioner is the owner of a stone crusher and has produced documents for obtaining a license to run the business. The court has allowed the petition and granted bail to the petitioner subject to certain conditions.

The court observed that the petitioner had produced documents to show that he had applied for a license to run the stone crusher and that the license was under process. The court also noted that the petitioner had cooperated with the investigation and had not tampered with any evidence. The court further observed that the petitioner had no criminal antecedents and was not likely to abscond or tamper with evidence.

The court, therefore, granted bail to the petitioner subject to certain conditions. The petitioner was directed to execute a personal bond for a sum of Rs. 1 lakh with two sureties for the like sum. The petitioner was also directed to cooperate with the investigation and not to tamper with any evidence or prosecution witnesses. The petitioner was further directed to appear before the investigating officer as and when required and not to leave the jurisdiction of the court without prior permission.

The court also directed the petitioner to surrender his passport, if any, and not to apply for a fresh passport without prior permission. The court further directed the petitioner to attend the court on all hearing dates and not to delay the trial in any manner. The court warned the petitioner that any violation of the conditions would result in the cancellation of the bail order.

The court's order is significant as it highlights the importance of complying with the conditions of bail. Bail is a temporary release from custody granted to an accused person pending trial. Bail is granted on the condition that the accused person will cooperate with the investigation and will not tamper with any evidence or prosecution witnesses. Bail is also granted on the condition that the accused person will appear before the court on all hearing dates and will not delay the trial in any manner.
