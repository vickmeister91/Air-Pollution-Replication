The court has granted anticipatory bail to Ashok Kumar, a Junior Engineer under the Rural Works Department, Koderma, in connection with a case registered under Sections 33 of the Indian Forest Act and Forest Conservation Act 1980. The court considered the fact that the petitioner was deputed to look after the construction of the road and was least concerned with the boulders brought by the contractor from the forest area. The petitioner was released on bail on furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Judicial Magistrate, 1st class, Koderma, subject to the conditions laid down under section 438 (2) Cr. P.C.

The case against Ashok Kumar was registered under Sections 33 of the Indian Forest Act and Forest Conservation Act 1980. These sections deal with the punishment for contravention of the provisions of the Act. The petitioner was accused of being involved in the illegal transportation of boulders from the forest area. However, the court considered the fact that the petitioner was only responsible for the construction of the road and was not involved in the transportation of the boulders. The court also noted that the petitioner had no knowledge of the illegal transportation of the boulders.

The court granted anticipatory bail to the petitioner on the grounds that he was not directly involved in the transportation of the boulders and that he had no knowledge of the illegal activity. The court also considered the fact that the petitioner was a government employee and that he had cooperated with the investigation. The court imposed certain conditions on the petitioner, including the requirement to furnish a bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each to the satisfaction of the learned Judicial Magistrate, 1st class, Koderma.

The conditions laid down under section 438 (2) Cr. P.C. include the requirement that the petitioner shall make himself available for interrogation by a police officer as and when required, that he shall not, directly or indirectly, make any inducement, threat or promise to any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such facts to the court or to any police officer, and that he shall not leave India without the previous permission of the court.
