To understand the court order in detail, it is important to first understand the background of the case. The petitioner, Madanraj Nahar, is accused in a case pending under sections 1208, 420 IP53' W of the Prevention of Conupfidsxiet, the Act. In this case, the petitioner had filed an application under section 91 of Cr.P.C. to issue summons to I.B.P.L. authority letter No.2032_I43ii for cross-examination. However, this application was not admitted in evidence on the ground that it is a copy.

The petitioner then filed a criminal petition under section 482 Cr.P.C. to squash the order passed by the Addl. City Civil & Sessions Judge for CE Cases, Bangalore. The petitioner argued that the order passed by the lower court was erroneous and that the petitioner had a right to cross-examine the witness.

The court, however, observed that the investigating agency is not in possession of the document sought to be produced and, therefore, the accused cannot make an application to obtain it. The court further stated that the petitioner cannot rely on a copy of the document as evidence and that the original document must be produced in court.

The court order highlights the importance of producing original documents in court as evidence. It also emphasizes the need for investigating agencies to be in possession of all relevant documents in a case. The court's decision to not admit a copy of the document as evidence is in line with the principles of fair trial and due process.
