The court order pertains to a bail application filed by Makhan Adiwasi under Section 439 of the Cr.P.C. in connection with a case registered against him for the offence punishable under Sections 9, 39, 41 & 51 of Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The allegation against him is that he slaughtered a wild blue bull (nilgai) in his field by electric wire and cut its body in three parts and thereafter, thrown it in the nearby canal.

The court allowed the bail application and directed the applicant to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000/- with one solvent surety in the like amount to the satisfaction of the Trial Court for his appearance before the concerned Court on all the dates of hearing fixed in this behalf by the Court concerned during trial. The court also imposed certain conditions on the applicant.

The court order is significant as it deals with the issue of bail in a case involving the alleged violation of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The Act is aimed at protecting wild animals and plants and their habitats. The Act provides for the establishment of protected areas, regulation of hunting and trade in wild animals and plants, and conservation of biodiversity.

The court order highlights the seriousness of the offence alleged against the applicant. The slaughter of a wild blue bull (nilgai) is a serious offence under the Act. The Act provides for punishment of imprisonment for a term which shall not be less than three years but which may extend to seven years and also with fine.

Bail is granted on the condition that the accused person will appear for trial and will not tamper with evidence or influence witnesses.
