In a recent court order, M/S Vgn Developers Pvt. Ltd filed a criminal original petition against the Tamilnadu Pollution Control Board. The petitioners were accused of violating the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 by commencing construction activities without obtaining clearance from the Tamil Nadu State Level Environment Impact Assessment Authority. The court allowed the petition and quashed the proceedings against the petitioners in C.C.No.58 of 2014 on the file of the Judicial Magistrate, Ambattur.

The case revolved around the construction activities carried out by M/S Vgn Developers Pvt. Ltd without obtaining clearance from the Tamil Nadu State Level Environment Impact Assessment Authority. The Tamilnadu Pollution Control Board had initiated proceedings against the petitioners under the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986. The petitioners challenged the proceedings and filed a criminal original petition in the court.

The court examined the facts of the case and observed that the petitioners had indeed commenced construction activities without obtaining clearance from the Tamil Nadu State Level Environment Impact Assessment Authority. However, the court also noted that the petitioners had subsequently obtained the necessary clearance and had taken steps to rectify the violation.

The court further observed that the petitioners had not caused any harm to the environment and had taken steps to ensure that the construction activities did not have any adverse impact on the environment. The court also noted that the petitioners had cooperated with the authorities and had taken steps to rectify the violation.

Based on these observations, the court allowed the petition and quashed the proceedings against the petitioners in C.C.No.58 of 2014 on the file of the Judicial Magistrate, Ambattur. The court also directed the Tamilnadu Pollution Control Board to release the bank guarantee furnished by the petitioners.

The court order is significant as it highlights the importance of obtaining clearance from the Tamil Nadu State Level Environment Impact Assessment Authority before commencing construction activities. It also emphasizes the need to take steps to rectify any violations and cooperate with the authorities to ensure that the environment is not harmed.

In conclusion, the court order in the case of M/S Vgn Developers Pvt. Ltd vs Tamilnadu Pollution Control Board serves as a reminder to all stakeholders to comply with the provisions of the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 and obtain the necessary clearances before commencing any construction activities. It also highlights the importance of taking steps to rectify any violations and cooperate with the authorities to ensure that the environment is not harmed.
