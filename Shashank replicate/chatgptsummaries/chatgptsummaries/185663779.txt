The Allahabad High Court has recently quashed an order issued by the Uttar Pradesh Pollution Control Board, which had levied environmental compensation on M/S M.D. Hide Traders for alleged violation of provisions of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The court found that the order was issued without jurisdiction and in violation of principles of natural justice, as the petitioner was not given any notice or opportunity to be heard. The court directed the Pollution Control Board to take appropriate decision afresh only after giving opportunity of hearing to the petitioner in the matter relating to levying of environmental compensation.

The case pertains to the alleged violation of provisions of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 by M/S M.D. Hide Traders. The Uttar Pradesh Pollution Control Board had issued an order levying environmental compensation on the petitioner for the alleged violation. The petitioner had challenged the order before the Allahabad High Court on the grounds that it was issued without jurisdiction and in violation of principles of natural justice.

The Allahabad High Court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, found that the order was indeed issued without jurisdiction and in violation of principles of natural justice. The court observed that the petitioner was not given any notice or opportunity to be heard before the order was issued. The court held that the principles of natural justice require that a person should be given an opportunity to be heard before any adverse order is passed against him.

The court also observed that the order was issued without jurisdiction. The court noted that the Uttar Pradesh Pollution Control Board had issued the order under Section 31A of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. However, the court found that Section 31A was not applicable in the present case, as it pertains to the power of the Central Pollution Control Board to issue directions to State Pollution Control Boards. The court held that the Uttar Pradesh Pollution Control Board had no power to issue the order under Section 31A.

The court, therefore, quashed the order issued by the Uttar Pradesh Pollution Control Board and directed it to take appropriate decision afresh only after giving opportunity of hearing to the petitioner in the matter relating to levying of environmental compensation. The court also observed that the petitioner had already deposited the amount of environmental compensation with the Pollution Control Board, and directed the Pollution Control Board to refund the amount to the petitioner.
