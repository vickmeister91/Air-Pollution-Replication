The court dismissed the petition under Article 226 of the Constitution of India, citing the National Green Tribunal Act and the existence of a statutory forum for redressal of grievances. The court also referred to a previous judgment in Sterlite Industries (India) Limited Vs. Union of India and others, which held that companies operating without valid permission for a long period must compensate by paying damages.

Chidambaranar Port Trust had violated environmental laws by dumping dredged material in the sea without obtaining necessary clearances from the authorities. The petitioner also claimed that the port trust had caused damage to the environment and marine life in the area.
