The Kerala High Court recently dismissed an appeal filed by the State Government against a petition filed by the Institute of Social Welfare. The petition was filed against a scheme to link the main land of Cochin with islands in the archipelago beyond the western backwaters. The scheme involves construction of bridges and reclamation of an area in the Vembanad lake. The Institute of Social Welfare had filed a petition against the reclamation work, which was initially dismissed by a single judge and later by a division bench. The State Government filed a review petition, and the case was heard afresh.

The court dismissed the appeal, stating that the proposed land reclamation would not interfere with the natural flow of sea water and that the Central Government had granted sanction subject to conditions for constructing bridges and carrying out the reclamation work. The court also noted that the project was of great public importance and would benefit the people of the region. The court further observed that the project had been planned after taking into account the environmental concerns and that the State Government had taken all necessary steps to ensure that the project was implemented in an environmentally sustainable manner.
