The court order pertains to a bail application filed under Section 439 of the Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, on behalf of two applicants, Kalu and Sachin, who had been in jail since 5th April 2019 in connection with a crime registered at Police Station-Forest Department, Uday Nagar, District-Dewas. The applicants were accused of storing chemicals and distributing them to villagers, which is punishable under various sections of the Indian Forest Act, 1927 and Biological Diversity Act, 2002.

The court allowed the bail application and directed the applicants to furnish a personal bond of Rs.1,00,000/- each with one solvent surety each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the learned Trial Court. The bail was granted on the condition that they shall remain present before the Court concerned during trial and also comply with the conditions enumerated under Section 437 (3) of Criminal Procedure Code, 1973.

The case against the applicants was registered under the Indian Forest Act, 1927 and Biological Diversity Act, 2002. The Indian Forest Act, 1927 is an act to consolidate the law relating to forests, the transit of forest-produce and the duty leviable on timber and other forest-produce. The Biological Diversity Act, 2002 is an act to provide for the conservation of biological diversity, sustainable use of its components and fair and equitable sharing of the benefits arising out of the use of biological resources.

The applicants were accused of storing chemicals and distributing them to villagers. This act is punishable under various sections of the Indian Forest Act, 1927 and Biological Diversity Act, 2002. The Indian Forest Act, 1927 prohibits the storage of chemicals in forests without permission. The Biological Diversity Act, 2002 prohibits the distribution of chemicals that may harm the biological diversity of the area.

The court granted bail to the applicants on the condition that they shall remain present before the Court concerned during trial and also comply with the conditions enumerated under Section 437 (3) of Criminal Procedure Code, 1973. Section 437 (3) of Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 lays down the conditions for granting bail. The section states that the court may impose any condition which it considers necessary for ensuring that the accused shall attend in accordance with the conditions of the bond executed under this Chapter, or for any other reason which the court considers it necessary to impose.

The court directed the applicants to furnish a personal bond of Rs.1,00,000/- each with one solvent surety each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the learned Trial Court. A personal bond is a written promise by the accused to appear before the court on the specified date and time. A solvent surety is a person who guarantees the payment of the bail amount in case the accused fails to appear before the court.
