Md. Innus Ali, the petitioner, filed an application under Section 482 Cr.P.C. to set aside and quash the order dated 16.09.2019 and subsequent orders passed in PRC No. 410/2019, under Section 120B/379 of IPC R/W Section 51 of the WildLife Protection Act pending in the Court of learned JMFC, Kaliabor, Nagaon, Assam, whereby warrant of arrest has been issued against the petitioner. The court has considered the submissions made by both parties and has ordered that the petitioner shall appear before the court below on or before 18.01.2020 and till his appearance, the execution of the NBWA shall be kept in abeyance. If a bail application is filed, the same shall be considered in accordance with law. The criminal petition stands disposed of.

The case pertains to the alleged violation of the WildLife Protection Act by the petitioner, Md. Innus Ali. The court had issued a warrant of arrest against the petitioner, which he sought to quash through his application under Section 482 Cr.P.C. The court heard the arguments of both parties and ordered that the petitioner shall appear before the court below on or before 18.01.2020. The court also directed that the execution of the NBWA shall be kept in abeyance till the petitioner's appearance. If a bail application is filed, the same shall be considered in accordance with law. The criminal petition stands disposed of.

The court's order is significant as it provides relief to the petitioner by allowing him to appear before the court below and seek bail if necessary. The court has also kept the execution of the NBWA in abeyance, which means that the petitioner will not be arrested till his appearance before the court below. The court's decision is in accordance with the principles of natural justice and upholds the petitioner's right to a fair trial.
