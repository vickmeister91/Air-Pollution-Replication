Abdul Kayum, a habitual offender, filed an application for anticipatory bail under section 438 of the Code of Criminal Procedure in Forest Crime No. 39185/23 registered by P.S. Forest Division, Range Silvani, District-Raisen under sections 9, 39 & 51 of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The prosecution case was that on 20.10.2016, Abdul Kayum had driven pegs all around his paddy field and stretched GI wires along those pegs. He had released stolen electric current in the GI wires. As a result, two blue bulls were entangled in the trap and were electrocuted and died.

The court dismissed the application for anticipatory bail as the act of the petitioner was inherently dangerous and could even have led to loss of human lives. The petitioner had been absconding since the date of the offence i.e. 20.10.2016. The court also noted that the petitioner was a habitual offender and there were at least 10 offences under the Forest Act registered against him.

The Act also provides for the establishment of national parks, sanctuaries, and other protected areas for the conservation of wild animals and plants.

The Act prohibits hunting, poaching, and capturing of wild animals. It also prohibits the destruction of their habitats. The Act provides for penalties for offences committed under it. The penalties include imprisonment and fines.
