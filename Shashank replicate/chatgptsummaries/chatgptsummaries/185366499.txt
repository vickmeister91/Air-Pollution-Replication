The court order in question pertains to a petition filed by Sanjay Behal seeking directions to the Pollution Control Board to grant consent to operate his hotel, M/s. Bye Pass Hotel on Churat Road near Dhali Tunnel, Shimla. The petitioner had failed to renew his license after March 31, 2017, despite repeated notices. As a consequence, the power supply to the hotel was disconnected.

The court directed the petitioner to file a representation to the Secretary, Pollution Control Board, within two days, detailing the steps taken by him for renewal of consent to operate the hotel. The representation must be filed within the stipulated time, and if it is, the Pollution Control Board is directed to grant/renew consent to operate the hotel within one day, subject to compliance with provisions contained in the Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act 1994.

The electricity board is also directed to restore the electricity to the petitioner's hotel immediately after issuance of consent to operate the hotel by the Pollution Control Board.

The court's decision is significant as it highlights the importance of compliance with environmental regulations and the consequences of failing to do so. The petitioner's failure to renew his license and comply with the provisions of the Water (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act 1994 resulted in the disconnection of power supply to his hotel. The court's decision to direct the petitioner to file a representation to the Pollution Control Board and to grant/renew consent to operate the hotel within one day, subject to compliance with environmental regulations, emphasizes the need for businesses to comply with environmental regulations and obtain the necessary licenses and permits.
