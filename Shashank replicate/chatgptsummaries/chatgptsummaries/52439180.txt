On March 12, 2015, the Gujarat High Court dismissed a writ petition filed by Hindusthan Chemicals Company against the Gujarat Pollution Control Board. The company had filed the writ petition seeking relief against the Gujarat Pollution Control Board's order to shut down its manufacturing unit in Vatva, Ahmedabad. The Gujarat Pollution Control Board had issued the order on the grounds that the company was violating the provisions of the Water (Prevention and Control and Pollution) Act, 1974.

The Gujarat High Court dismissed the writ petition on the grounds that the company had an alternative remedy of appeal under Section 33B of the Water (Prevention and Control and Pollution) Act, 1974 to the National Green Tribunal (Western Bench), Pune. The court held that the company could not bypass the statutory remedy provided under the Act and approach the High Court directly.

The court observed that the National Green Tribunal was a specialized forum established under the National Green Tribunal Act, 2010 to deal with environmental disputes. The court noted that the Act provided for an appeal to the National Green Tribunal against any order or decision of the State Pollution Control Board or the Pollution Control Committee or any other competent authority under the Water (Prevention and Control and Pollution) Act, 1974.

The court held that the National Green Tribunal had the expertise to deal with environmental disputes and had the power to pass appropriate orders to protect the environment. The court observed that the National Green Tribunal had the power to issue directions for the closure, prohibition or regulation of any industry, operation or process, which was causing environmental pollution.

The court also noted that the National Green Tribunal had the power to impose penalties on any person or industry, which was causing environmental pollution. The court held that the National Green Tribunal was a more appropriate forum to deal with the issues raised by the company in its writ petition.
