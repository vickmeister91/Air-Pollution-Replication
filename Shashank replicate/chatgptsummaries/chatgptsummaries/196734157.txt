The Himachal Pradesh High Court has issued a court order regarding the non-compliance of environmental laws by industrial units in Parwanoo. The Pollution Control Board has been directed to disconnect water and electricity connections of defaulting units and recover a cost of Rs. 25,000 from perpetual defaulters. The court has also ordered an inspection of establishments in Dharamshala, Mcleodganj, and Nadi for non-compliance of environmental laws. The next hearing is scheduled for December 15, 2017.

The court order is a significant step towards ensuring compliance with environmental laws in the state of Himachal Pradesh. The order directs the Pollution Control Board to take strict action against defaulting units, including disconnecting their water and electricity connections. This will ensure that the units are unable to operate until they comply with environmental laws.

The court has also ordered an inspection of establishments in Dharamshala, Mcleodganj, and Nadi for non-compliance of environmental laws. This is a positive step towards ensuring that all establishments in the state comply with environmental laws. The inspection will help identify establishments that are not complying with environmental laws and take appropriate action against them.
