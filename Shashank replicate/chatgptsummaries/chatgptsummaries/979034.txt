The recent court order pertains to a proposal by the State Government to construct a Forest Lodge in the buffer zone of the Parambikulam Wildlife Sanctuary. The appellant, who is a bird watcher and committed to conservation, filed a petition to prohibit the construction of the lodge, citing violation of Section 2 of the Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980. The court held that the construction of the lodge would involve the clearance of forest land and would require prior approval of the Central Government. 

The court also rejected the contention that a communication sent by the Ministry of Tourism constituted prior approval. The court issued a writ of prohibition against the respondents from proceeding with the proposal for the construction of the Forest Lodge, but clarified that the judgment was without prejudice to the right of the respondents to obtain the approval of the Central Government as envisaged in Section 2 of the Act.

The Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to protect forests and wildlife in India. The Act requires that any diversion of forest land for non-forestry purposes must be approved by the Central Government. The Act also mandates that any such diversion must be compensated by afforestation on an equivalent area of non-forest land.
