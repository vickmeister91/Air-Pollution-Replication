The court order pertains to a case where Ram Pyari filed a suit for permanent injunction against the South Delhi Municipal Corporation (SDMC) to prevent them from demolishing her property. The SDMC argued that the suit was barred under the Delhi Municipal Corporation Act and that the appropriate forum for appeal was the Ld. Appellate Tribunal MCD. The court agreed with the SDMC and dismissed the application under Order XXXIX Rule 1 and 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure. The court cited a previous judgment in support of its decision and noted that the interim order against demolition had been extended until December 13, 2018.

The court also stated that the plaintiff's suit was not maintainable under Section 347 (E) read with Sections 347 (B) and 343 (4) of DMC Act, 1957. The court further stated that no order as to costs was given and the decree sheet was to be prepared accordingly.

The court order also stated that the interim order in the appeal filed by the plaintiff against orders of MCD Authority has been extended till 13.12.2018. The defendant's application under Order XXXIX Rule 4 CPC stands allowed and the ad-interim stay order granted in this case stands vacated.
