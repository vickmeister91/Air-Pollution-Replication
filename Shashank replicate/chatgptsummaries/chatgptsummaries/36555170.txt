The Allahabad High Court recently disposed of a writ petition filed by Abrar Ahmad against an order passed by the Regional Officer of the U.P. Pollution Control Board. The court granted liberty to the petitioner to approach the statutory Appellate Authority within three weeks for preferring an appeal in accordance with the law. The order was passed on 18 October 2019.

The writ petition was filed by Abrar Ahmad, who was aggrieved by an order passed by the Regional Officer of the U.P. Pollution Control Board. The order directed the petitioner to pay a sum of Rs. 1,00,000 as environmental compensation for the violation of environmental norms. The petitioner contended that the order was passed without giving him an opportunity of being heard and without considering the relevant facts and circumstances of the case.

The Allahabad High Court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, observed that the petitioner had an alternative remedy available to him under the law. The court noted that the petitioner could prefer an appeal before the statutory Appellate Authority against the order passed by the Regional Officer of the U.P. Pollution Control Board. The court further observed that the petitioner had not exhausted his statutory remedy before approaching the court.

The court, therefore, disposed of the writ petition and granted liberty to the petitioner to approach the statutory Appellate Authority within three weeks for preferring an appeal in accordance with the law. The court also directed the Appellate Authority to decide the appeal on its merits and in accordance with the law.

The order passed by the Allahabad High Court highlights the importance of exhausting statutory remedies before approaching the court. The court has reiterated that the availability of an alternative remedy is a bar to the exercise of writ jurisdiction under Article 226 of the Constitution of India. The court has also emphasized that the statutory Appellate Authority is the appropriate forum for deciding appeals against orders passed by the U.P. Pollution Control Board.

In conclusion, the Allahabad High Court has disposed of a writ petition filed by Abrar Ahmad against an order passed by the Regional Officer of the U.P. Pollution Control Board. The court has granted liberty to the petitioner to approach the statutory Appellate Authority within three weeks for preferring an appeal in accordance with the law. The court has emphasized the importance of exhausting statutory remedies before approaching the court and has reiterated that the statutory Appellate Authority is the appropriate forum for deciding appeals against orders passed by the U.P. Pollution Control Board.
