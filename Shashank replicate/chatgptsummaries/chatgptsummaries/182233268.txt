The Madras High Court issued a court order on April 23, 2018, in response to a writ petition filed by M/s.Prashanth Multi Specialty Hospital. The petitioner requested a writ of mandamus directing the 3rd respondent to dispose of their application for the renewal of consent to operate under AIR Act for a period of five years. The court directed the respondents to consider the petitioner's application and pass appropriate orders on or before May 31, 2018, in accordance with the law. The writ petition was disposed of with no costs.

The writ petition filed by M/s.Prashanth Multi Specialty Hospital was in response to the delay in the renewal of their consent to operate under the AIR Act. The petitioner had applied for the renewal of their consent to operate for a period of five years, but the 3rd respondent had not taken any action on the application. The petitioner had approached the court seeking a writ of mandamus directing the 3rd respondent to dispose of their application for the renewal of consent to operate.

The Madras High Court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, directed the respondents to consider the petitioner's application and pass appropriate orders on or before May 31, 2018, in accordance with the law. The court emphasized that the respondents should consider the application on its merits and not delay the process any further. The court also made it clear that the respondents should follow the due process of law while considering the application.

The court's decision was based on the principle of natural justice, which requires that every person should be given a fair hearing and that decisions should be made based on the merits of the case. The court recognized that the petitioner had a legitimate expectation that their application for the renewal of consent to operate would be considered in a timely manner and that the delay in processing the application had caused them hardship.

The court's decision was also in line with the provisions of the AIR Act, which requires that every person who operates an industrial plant should obtain consent to operate from the State Pollution Control Board. The Act also provides for the renewal of consent to operate for a period of five years, subject to certain conditions. The court's decision ensured that the petitioner's application for the renewal of consent to operate was considered in accordance with the provisions of the AIR Act.

The court's decision was a significant victory for the petitioner, as it ensured that their application for the renewal of consent to operate was considered in a timely manner.
