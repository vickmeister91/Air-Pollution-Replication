The Patna High Court has granted anticipatory bail to four petitioners who were apprehending their arrest in a case registered under various sections of the Indian Penal Code, Bihar Minor Mineral Concession Rules, Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, Indian Forest Act, Water (Prevention and Control) Pollution Act, Air (Prevention and Control) Pollution Act, and Environment (Protection) Act. The FIR was lodged against 19 persons who were running a crusher without a valid license. The court considered the nature of seizure and granted bail to the petitioners on the condition of furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 each with two sureties.

The case in question pertains to the illegal operation of a crusher by 19 persons without a valid license. The FIR was lodged against all 19 persons, including the four petitioners who were granted anticipatory bail. The charges against them include various sections of the Indian Penal Code, Bihar Minor Mineral Concession Rules, Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, Indian Forest Act, Water (Prevention and Control) Pollution Act, Air (Prevention and Control) Pollution Act, and Environment (Protection) Act.

The Patna High Court considered the nature of seizure and granted anticipatory bail to the four petitioners who were apprehending their arrest in the case. The court directed the petitioners to furnish a bail bond of Rs. 10,000 each with two sureties. The court also imposed certain conditions on the petitioners, including that they shall not tamper with the evidence or influence the witnesses in any manner.

The court observed that the petitioners were not named in the FIR and that their names were added later during the investigation. The court also noted that the petitioners were not present at the site when the crusher was seized. The court further observed that the petitioners were not the owners of the crusher and that they were merely employees of the owner.

The court also took into consideration the fact that the petitioners had no criminal antecedents and that they were ready to cooperate with the investigation. The court noted that the petitioners had already been interrogated by the investigating officer and that they had cooperated fully with the investigation.
