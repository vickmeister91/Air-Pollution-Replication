The accused filed an appeal, which was dismissed by the Additional Sessions Judge. The accused then filed a revision petition challenging the judgment of conviction and sentence on various grounds.

The court order discusses the case of accused persons charged with illegal possession of forest produce. The court found discrepancies in the identification of the seized material and non-compliance with mandatory provisions of the Forest Act. The court also noted that the prosecution failed to produce a certificate proving that the person who certified the seized material had undergone training or was authorized by the government to issue such certificates. Therefore, the court set aside the judgment of conviction and acquitted the accused persons.
