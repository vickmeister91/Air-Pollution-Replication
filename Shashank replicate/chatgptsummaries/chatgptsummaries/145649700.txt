In a recent court order, the petitioner Sukhjinder Singh had filed a petition against an order directing him to produce a vehicle in the office of State Crime Branch. The court order pertains to this petition and states that the order is in accordance with Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The court dismissed the petition, stating that no interference can be made at this stage.

Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure empowers a court to issue a summons or order to produce any document or thing that is necessary for the purpose of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. In this case, the State Crime Branch had issued an order directing Sukhjinder Singh to produce a vehicle in their office. The petitioner had challenged this order, stating that it was illegal and arbitrary.

However, the court held that the order was in accordance with Section 91 of the Code of Criminal Procedure and was therefore legal. The court also noted that the petitioner had not challenged the order on any specific ground and had merely stated that it was illegal and arbitrary. The court held that such a vague and general challenge was not sufficient to warrant interference at this stage.

The court further noted that the petitioner had an alternative remedy available to him, which was to challenge the order before the appropriate forum. The court held that the petitioner could not bypass the statutory remedy and approach the court directly. The court also observed that the petitioner had not made out any case for interference at this stage and dismissed the petition.
