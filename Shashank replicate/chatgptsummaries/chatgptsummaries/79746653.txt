The Madras High Court issued a court order on April 23, 2018, in response to a writ petition filed by M/s.Prashanth Fertility Research Centre Pvt Ltd. The petitioner requested a writ of mandamus directing the 3rd respondent to dispose of their application for the renewal of consent to operate under AIR Act to renew for the period of five years. The court directed the respondents to consider the petitioner's application and pass appropriate orders on or before May 31, 2018, in accordance with the law.

The petitioner had submitted an application for the renewal of consent to operate under the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981, and the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, for the period of five years. However, the 3rd respondent had not disposed of the application, and the petitioner had not received any communication from the respondent regarding the status of the application.

The petitioner contended that the delay in disposing of the application was causing them financial loss and was affecting their business. The petitioner also argued that the delay was a violation of their fundamental right to carry on their business under Article 19(1)(g) of the Constitution of India.
