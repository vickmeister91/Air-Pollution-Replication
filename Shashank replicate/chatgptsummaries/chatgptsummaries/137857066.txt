The court order pertains to a bail application filed by two individuals, Pooran and Baddri, under section 439 of the Criminal Procedure Code (Cr.P.C.) in connection with a case registered under sections 9, 39, 50 & 51 of the Wild Life Protection Act. The applicants were accused of illegal hunting of a wild animal, Nil Gai. The counsel for the applicants argued that they were falsely implicated and nothing was recovered from their possession.

After hearing the arguments of both sides, the court allowed the bail application and directed the applicants to furnish a personal bond of Rs. 50,000 each with one solvent surety each of the like amount. The court also imposed certain conditions on the applicants. They were directed to appear before the Investigating Officer/concerned Range Officer, Forest Range, Gourjhamar, District Sagar, on 2nd and 4th Sunday of every month between 11 A.M., to 2 P.M. for six months during the trial.

The court warned that in case of breach of bail conditions, the trial court could take coercive action against the applicants. This means that if the applicants fail to comply with the conditions imposed by the court, they could be arrested and sent to jail.

The Act also provides for the establishment of national parks, wildlife sanctuaries, and other protected areas for the conservation of wildlife.
