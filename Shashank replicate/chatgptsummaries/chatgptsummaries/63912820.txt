In the first court order, the petitioner, P. Murugan, claimed ownership of a vehicle that was seized by the Forest Range Police Station in connection with a case under the Wild Life Protection Act. The petitioner sought interim custody of the vehicle, but the Judicial Magistrate denied the request. The High Court allowed the petition and directed the Magistrate to return the vehicle to the petitioner subject to certain conditions, while the confiscation proceedings continue independently.

The High Court ordered the petitioner to deposit the original Registration Certificate of the vehicle and a sum of Rs. 25,000 to the credit of the case. The petitioner was also directed not to make any alterations to the vehicle and to produce it before the court as and when required. The Authority under the Tamil Nadu Prohibition Act was allowed to proceed with the confiscation proceedings.
