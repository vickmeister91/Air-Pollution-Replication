The court order in question pertains to a petition filed by Jay Cylinders Ltd. against the U.P. Pollution Control Board. The petitioner had appealed against an assessment order passed by the Prescribed Authority under the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Cess Act, 1977. The appeal was dismissed by the appellate authority on the grounds of limitation. The petitioner had filed an application for condonation of delay, which was also dismissed.

The court found that the appellate authority had erred in counting the limitation period and had not considered whether there was good and sufficient reason for the delay in filing the appeal. The court quashed the order and remanded the case to the appellate committee for decision afresh.
