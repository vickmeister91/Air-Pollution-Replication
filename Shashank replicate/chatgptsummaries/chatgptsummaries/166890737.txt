The Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, is a crucial piece of legislation that aims to protect wildlife and their habitats in India. The Act provides for the establishment of protected areas, the regulation of hunting and trade in wildlife, and the prevention of poaching and illegal trade in wildlife and their products.

Sections 29/51 of the Act deal with offences related to hunting, killing, or capturing any wild animal or bird, or attempting to do so. These offences are punishable with imprisonment for a term of up to three years, or with a fine, or with both.
