The case at hand involves allegations against Smt Manimala @ Roopa. The petitioner, K Satish Kumar, filed a petition under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. seeking to set aside an order passed in cc: No. 29450/2006 and quash the CC.No. 29460/2006. The court order pertains to this petition.

Section 482 of Cr.P.C. provides for the inherent powers of the High Court to make orders necessary to give effect to any order under the Code, or to prevent abuse of the process of any court or otherwise to secure the ends of justice. In this case, the petitioner sought to exercise this power to set aside an order and quash another.

However, the court observed that prima facie material was against the accused, Smt Manimala @ Roopa. This means that there was enough evidence to suggest that the accused had committed the alleged offense. As a result, the court held that the trial should proceed.

The dismissal of the petition means that the order passed in cc: No. 29450/2006 and the CC.No. 29460/2006 will not be set aside or quashed. The trial will continue as per the normal course of law.

It is important to note that the court's observation of prima facie material against the accused does not mean that the accused is guilty. It simply means that there is enough evidence to suggest that the accused may have committed the alleged offense. The trial will provide an opportunity for both the prosecution and the defense to present their case and for the court to make a final determination.

In conclusion, the court order in the case filed by K Satish Kumar against Smt Manimala @ Roopa under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. sought to set aside an order and quash another. However, the court observed that prima facie material was against the accused and the trial should proceed. As a result, the petition was dismissed, and the trial will continue as per the normal course of law.
