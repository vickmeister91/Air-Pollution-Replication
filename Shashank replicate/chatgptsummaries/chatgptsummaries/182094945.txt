In a recent court order, three individuals who had filed a criminal petition seeking bail in a case registered against them for killing an animal listed in the Schedule to the Wild Life Protection Act and sharing its meat were granted bail subject to certain conditions. The petitioners had filed a criminal petition seeking bail in the case. The court allowed the petitioners to be released on bail subject to certain conditions. The conditions included executing a personal bond and not influencing prosecution witnesses.

The court noted that the offense is triable by a magistrate and the maximum punishment for the offense is three years. The court also noted that the petitioners had been in custody for a considerable period of time and that there was no evidence to suggest that they would abscond or tamper with evidence.

The court further noted that the petitioners had no criminal antecedents and that they were not likely to commit any offense while on bail. The court also observed that the petitioners had cooperated with the investigation and had not attempted to influence any witnesses.

In light of these factors, the court granted bail to the petitioners subject to the conditions mentioned above. The court also directed the petitioners to cooperate with the investigation and to appear before the court as and when required.
