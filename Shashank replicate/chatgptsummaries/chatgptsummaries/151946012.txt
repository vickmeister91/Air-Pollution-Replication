The court order in question pertains to two petitions that challenged the rejection of applications for grant of immunity under Section 40-A of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 and the issuance of ownership certificates to retain wildlife articles. The adjudication order and consequential orders were challenged on the ground that they were contrary to the notification issued by the Ministry of Environment and Forests.

The petitioners argued that the orders were in violation of the notification issued by the Ministry of Environment and Forests, which provided for the grant of immunity to persons who had acquired wildlife articles before the enactment of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972. The notification also provided for the issuance of ownership certificates to persons who had acquired wildlife articles before the enactment of the Act.

The court disposed of the petitions in terms of a judgment rendered in a similar case. The court held that the notification issued by the Ministry of Environment and Forests was valid and that the orders passed by the authorities were in violation of the notification. The court directed the authorities to reconsider the applications for grant of immunity and issuance of ownership certificates in accordance with the notification.

The court observed that the notification issued by the Ministry of Environment and Forests was intended to provide relief to persons who had acquired wildlife articles before the enactment of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972. The court held that the notification was a valid exercise of the power conferred on the Ministry under the Act.

The court also observed that the authorities had failed to consider the notification while passing the orders. The court held that the orders were therefore in violation of the notification and directed the authorities to reconsider the applications in accordance with the notification.
