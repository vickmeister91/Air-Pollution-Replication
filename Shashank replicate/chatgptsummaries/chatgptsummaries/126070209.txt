The court has granted anticipatory bail to Jitender Nath Kisku, Dhiraj Kumar Singh, and Abdul Salim Sk. in connection with a case registered under various sections of the Indian Penal Code, Jharkhand Minor Mineral Concession Rules, M.M.D.R. Act, Environment (Protection) Act, and Explosive Substance Act. The petitioners had moved the court apprehending their arrest and submitted that they were falsely implicated in the case based on anonymous information. The court has ordered their release on bail upon furnishing cash security and bail bond with the condition that they cooperate with the investigation and appear before the Investigating Officer as and when noticed.

The case in question involves allegations of illegal mining and transportation of minerals in Jharkhand. The accused were charged under various sections of the Indian Penal Code, including criminal conspiracy, cheating, and forgery, as well as under the Jharkhand Minor Mineral Concession Rules, M.M.D.R. Act, Environment (Protection) Act, and Explosive Substance Act. The charges relate to the alleged illegal mining and transportation of minerals, as well as the use of explosives without proper authorization.

The accused had approached the court seeking anticipatory bail, as they feared arrest based on anonymous information. They argued that they were falsely implicated in the case and that there was no evidence to support the charges against them. The court considered their arguments and granted them anticipatory bail, subject to certain conditions.

The court ordered the accused to furnish cash security and bail bond, and to cooperate with the investigation. They were also directed to appear before the Investigating Officer as and when noticed. The court emphasized that the grant of anticipatory bail did not mean that the accused were acquitted of the charges against them. It merely meant that they would not be arrested for the time being, and that they would have to cooperate with the investigation.

The grant of anticipatory bail is a significant relief for the accused, as it allows them to avoid arrest and detention while the investigation is ongoing. It also gives them the opportunity to defend themselves against the charges in a fair and impartial manner. However, the conditions attached to the bail order mean that they will have to cooperate with the investigation and appear before the Investigating Officer as and when required.
