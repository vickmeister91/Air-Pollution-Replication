The court order in question pertains to a petition filed under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. for quashing an order passed by the IInd Additional Sessions Judge, Sidhi, confirming the order of JMFC, Rampur, Naikin, District Sidhi in Forest Crime no. 538/22. The petitioner had sought the release of a seized vehicle bearing registration no. MP. 53 A.A. 6436, but the order was rejected on the ground that the Magistrate had no jurisdiction to release the vehicle after receiving information about the initiation of proceedings for confiscation under Section 52 (c) of the Forest Act.

The petitioner had argued that the vehicle was not involved in any forest offence and that the seizure was illegal. However, the court rejected this argument and cited a judgment of the Apex Court in The State of Madhya Pradesh v. Uday Singh, which held that the Magistrate has no powers to release the vehicle after receiving information about the initiation of proceedings for confiscation under Section 52 (c) of the Forest Act.

The court noted that the petitioner had not challenged the legality of the seizure before the Magistrate and had only sought the release of the vehicle. The court also observed that the petitioner had not produced any evidence to show that the vehicle was not involved in any forest offence.

The court further noted that the Magistrate had passed the order for confiscation of the vehicle under Section 52 (c) of the Forest Act after considering the report of the Forest Officer and after giving an opportunity to the petitioner to be heard. The court held that the Magistrate had acted within his jurisdiction and that the order of the Magistrate was confirmed by the Additional Sessions Judge.

The court also observed that the petitioner had an alternative remedy of filing an appeal against the order of the Magistrate under Section 52 (d) of the Forest Act. The court held that the petitioner had not availed of this remedy and had instead filed the present petition under Section 482 of Cr.P.C. The court held that the present petition was not maintainable and dismissed the same.
