The Punjab-Haryana High Court recently dismissed a writ petition filed by M/S Dev Shri Krishna Stone Crusher, which challenged the order passed by the Haryana State Pollution Control Board withdrawing the consent granted to the petitioner to set up a stone crushing unit. The court stated that the petitioner has a statutory alternative remedy of filing an appeal against the said order and since it has not been availed, the writ petition is dismissed on the ground of existence of statutory alternative remedy.

The petitioner, M/S Dev Shri Krishna Stone Crusher, had challenged the order passed by the Haryana State Pollution Control Board withdrawing the consent granted to the petitioner to set up a stone crushing unit. The petitioner had contended that the order was arbitrary and illegal and that the Board had not given any reasons for withdrawing the consent.

The court, however, dismissed the writ petition on the ground of existence of statutory alternative remedy. The court held that the petitioner has a statutory alternative remedy of filing an appeal against the said order and since it has not been availed, the writ petition is dismissed.

The court observed that the petitioner has not availed the statutory alternative remedy of filing an appeal against the order passed by the Board. The court stated that the petitioner cannot bypass the statutory remedy and approach the court directly. The court held that the writ petition is not maintainable in view of the existence of the statutory alternative remedy.

The court further observed that the petitioner has not shown any exceptional circumstances which would warrant the exercise of the extraordinary jurisdiction of the court under Article 226 of the Constitution of India. The court held that the petitioner has not made out a case for interference by the court in the matter.

The court also noted that the petitioner has not challenged the validity of the provisions of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 and the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981, under which the Board had passed the impugned order. The court held that the petitioner cannot challenge the validity of the provisions of the Acts in a writ petition filed under Article 226 of the Constitution of India.

In conclusion, the Punjab-Haryana High Court dismissed the writ petition filed by M/S Dev Shri Krishna Stone Crusher challenging the order passed by the Haryana State Pollution Control Board withdrawing the consent granted to the petitioner to set up a stone crushing unit. The court held that the petitioner has a statutory alternative remedy of filing an appeal against the said order and since it has not been availed, the writ petition is dismissed on the ground of existence of statutory alternative remedy. The court also held that the petitioner has not shown any exceptional circumstances which would warrant the exercise of the extraordinary jurisdiction of the court under Article 226 of the Constitution of India.
