A recent court order pertains to a petition filed by Savla & Associates, a law firm, against car mechanics who were operating from the pavements/road in the close vicinity of their office premises, causing noise pollution and hindering their work. The petitioner sought the removal of encroachments made by the mechanics and cited Article 48A and Article 51A of the Constitution of India, as well as the Noise Pollution (Regulation and Control) Rules, 2000.

The court directed the respondents to take necessary action to remove encroachments and prevent them from happening in the future. The writ petition was disposed of with the assurance that the respondents would take necessary action as and when called for.

The court order is significant as it highlights the importance of protecting the environment and ensuring that individuals and businesses do not engage in activities that cause pollution or hinder the work of others. The court's decision to direct the respondents to take necessary action to remove encroachments and prevent them from happening in the future is a step in the right direction towards ensuring a cleaner and healthier environment.

The court's decision is also in line with Article 48A and Article 51A of the Constitution of India, which state that it is the duty of every citizen to protect and improve the environment and to safeguard the forests and wildlife of the country. The Noise Pollution (Regulation and Control) Rules, 2000, further reinforce this duty by providing guidelines for the prevention and control of noise pollution.
