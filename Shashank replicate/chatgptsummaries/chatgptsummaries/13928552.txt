The Kerala High Court recently dismissed a writ appeal against an order dated 29.09.2015 passed in R.P. No.657 of 2015 as well as the judgment dated 03.07.2015 passed in W.P.(C) No. 14393 of 2015. The appellant was the writ petitioner who had filed the writ petition challenging Exts.P1, P4, P6 and P7. The learned Single Judge observed that the petitioner has a remedy to file appeal as per the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 where all the issues can be raised. Thus learned Single Judge refused to exercise his jurisdiction to entertain the writ petition.

The case in question pertains to a writ petition filed by the appellant challenging certain orders passed by the authorities under the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The appellant had challenged Exts.P1, P4, P6 and P7, which were orders passed by the authorities under the said Act. The appellant had contended that the orders were passed without giving him an opportunity of being heard and without considering his objections.

The learned Single Judge, while dismissing the writ petition, observed that the petitioner had a remedy to file an appeal under the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The learned Single Judge further observed that all the issues raised by the petitioner could be raised in the appeal and that the appellate authority would be in a better position to consider the matter.

The appellant had filed a writ appeal against the order of the learned Single Judge. The appellant had contended that the learned Single Judge had erred in dismissing the writ petition and that the orders passed by the authorities were illegal and arbitrary. The appellant had further contended that the orders were passed without giving him an opportunity of being heard and without considering his objections.

The Division Bench of the Kerala High Court, while dismissing the writ appeal, observed that the learned Single Judge had rightly dismissed the writ petition. The Division Bench observed that the petitioner had a remedy to file an appeal under the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 and that the appellate authority would be in a better position to consider the matter. The Division Bench further observed that the writ petition was not maintainable and that the learned Single Judge had rightly refused to exercise his jurisdiction to entertain the writ petition.

In conclusion, the Kerala High Court has dismissed a writ appeal against an order passed by the authorities under the Air (Prevention & Control of Pollution) Act, 1981. The Court has observed that the petitioner has a remedy to file an appeal under the said Act and that the appellate authority would be in a better position to consider the matter. The Court has further observed that the writ petition was not maintainable and that the learned Single Judge had rightly refused to exercise his jurisdiction to entertain the writ petition.
