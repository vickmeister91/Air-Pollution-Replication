The order in question was passed by the JMFC, Sidhi in an unregistered case, forest crime No.535/2008. The applicant challenged this order in criminal revision No.64/2014, which was dismissed by the First Additional Sessions Judge, Sidhi.

The applicant then filed a petition under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. in the court, challenging the order passed by the First Additional Sessions Judge, Sidhi. The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, found no illegality or perversity in the order passed by the JMFC, Sidhi and dismissed the petition filed by the applicant.

The court's decision was based on the fact that the order passed by the JMFC, Sidhi was in accordance with the provisions of the Wild Life Protection Act and Indian Forest Act. The court found that the forest officers had seized the applicant's vehicle for an offense under these acts, and that there was no illegality or perversity in the order passed by the JMFC, Sidhi.

The court also noted that the applicant had not raised any specific grounds for challenging the order passed by the JMFC, Sidhi. The court found that the applicant had merely made general allegations of illegality and perversity, without providing any specific instances of such illegality or perversity.

In light of these findings, the court dismissed the petition filed by the applicant under Section 482 of the Cr.P.C. The court's decision reaffirms the importance of following the provisions of the Wild Life Protection Act and Indian Forest Act, and highlights the need for specific grounds for challenging court orders.
