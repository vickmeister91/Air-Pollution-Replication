The Gujarat High Court recently upheld the acquittal of accused respondents in a criminal case related to water pollution. The case was brought by the Gujarat Pollution Control Board, which had appealed against the acquittal. However, the court found that the prosecution had not complied with Rule 27 of the Gujarat Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Rules 1976, which led to the accused being entitled to acquittal.

The case centered around allegations of water pollution by a company, and the accused respondents were allegedly in charge of and responsible for the conduct of the business of the company under Section 47 of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. However, the court found that there was no evidence to support this claim.

The Gujarat Pollution Control Board had argued that the accused respondents had failed to take adequate measures to prevent water pollution, and that they had not obtained the necessary permits and approvals from the authorities. However, the court found that the prosecution had not provided sufficient evidence to support these claims.

The court's decision to uphold the acquittal of the accused respondents was based on several factors. Firstly, the court found that the prosecution had not complied with Rule 27 of the Gujarat Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Rules 1976, which requires the prosecution to provide a copy of the complaint to the accused. The court found that the prosecution had failed to do so, which meant that the accused were entitled to acquittal.

Secondly, the court found that there was no evidence to indicate that the accused were in charge of and responsible for the conduct of the business of the company under Section 47 of the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974. The court noted that the prosecution had not provided any evidence to show that the accused had any direct involvement in the day-to-day operations of the company, or that they had any control over the company's policies and practices.

The court's decision to uphold the acquittal of the accused respondents is significant, as it highlights the importance of complying with procedural rules and providing sufficient evidence to support criminal charges.
