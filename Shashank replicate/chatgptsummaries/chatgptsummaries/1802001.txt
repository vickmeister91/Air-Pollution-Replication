The Andhra Pradesh High Court recently issued a court order in response to a writ application filed by K. Purushotham Reddy and Anr. against the Union of India and the Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation. The writ application alleged that the authorities were not following the Hazardous Waste (Management & Handling) Rules, 1989 while handling and disposing of used oil for re-refining or re-processing. The petitioners claimed that unauthorized persons were purchasing used oil from the users of lubricating oils, including the A.P. State Road Transport Corporation and A.P. State Electricity Board, for refining with various chemicals, causing serious threat to the environment, including fresh and underground water.

The court order directed all authorities, including the A.P. State Pollution Control Board, to strictly comply with the provisions of the said Rules and take strict action against any unauthorized handling of hazardous waste products. The court emphasized the importance of following the rules and regulations related to hazardous waste management and handling to prevent any harm to the environment and public health.

The Hazardous Waste (Management & Handling) Rules, 1989 were enacted to regulate the management and handling of hazardous waste in India. The rules provide for the safe and environmentally sound management of hazardous waste from generation to disposal. The rules also require the authorities to take necessary measures to prevent and control pollution caused by hazardous waste.

The court order highlights the importance of following the rules and regulations related to hazardous waste management and handling. The unauthorized handling of hazardous waste products can cause serious harm to the environment and public health. The court order directs all authorities to take strict action against any unauthorized handling of hazardous waste products.

The court order also emphasizes the role of the A.P. State Pollution Control Board in ensuring compliance with the Hazardous Waste (Management & Handling) Rules, 1989. The Pollution Control Board is responsible for monitoring and enforcing the rules related to hazardous waste management and handling. The court order directs the Pollution Control Board to strictly comply with the provisions of the rules and take necessary action against any violations.
