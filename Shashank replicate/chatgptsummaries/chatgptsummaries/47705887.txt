The court has issued an order in response to a Public Interest Litigation (PIL) filed by Saurin Rasiklal Shah, which alleges large-scale corruption in the High Speed Diesel (HSD) scam involving government officials and employees. The investigating officer found that some factories supplied with HSD did not exist, and only six firms/persons were allotted HSD quota by the Technical Evaluation Committee (TEC) of the Oil Companies. However, nearly 50 firms/persons were lifting HSD without allocation by the TEC, and even the six firms/persons allotted quota were not utilizing the HSD for their own manufacturing use and were diverting the same in the open market for their illegal gains.

The court has directed the Sales Tax Department to give all assistance and co-operation to the investigating officer. The court has also stated that the investigating officer shall not be restrained by any agency when he is investigating a cognizable offence involving a huge amount. This order is significant as it empowers the investigating officer to carry out a thorough investigation without any hindrance.
