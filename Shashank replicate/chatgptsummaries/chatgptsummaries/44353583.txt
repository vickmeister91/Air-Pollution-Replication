The court order in question pertains to a criminal petition filed by Smt. Asha E.U. against Sri Kumaraswamy E. Hiremath. The petitioner had filed a petition under Section 12 of the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, which is currently pending before the IV Metropolitan Magistrate Traffic Court in Bengaluru. The petitioner had also filed an application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. to call for records of the respondent's bank account for the years 2011 to 2015, which was refused to be received by the court.

The court observed that no litigant can be prevented from filing an interlocutory application or an application in any pending proceedings, and the petitioner is at liberty to file the application before the court. The trial court would examine the application and proceed to adjudicate the same on merits and in accordance with the law. The petition stands dismissed subject to this observation.

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, is a comprehensive legislation that seeks to protect women from domestic violence and abuse. The Act defines domestic violence as any act of commission or omission that results in harm or injury to the aggrieved person. The Act provides for a wide range of reliefs and remedies that can be sought by the aggrieved person, including protection orders, residence orders, monetary relief, custody orders, and compensation orders.

Section 12 of the Act provides for the application for protection orders. Any aggrieved person or a protection officer on behalf of the aggrieved person can file an application for a protection order. The protection order can be sought against the respondent, who is the person against whom the aggrieved person has sought the relief. The protection order can prohibit the respondent from committing any act of domestic violence, entering the shared household, or communicating with the aggrieved person.

However, the court had refused to receive the application.

Section 91 of Cr.P.C. provides for the power of the court to issue a summons or order to produce any document or thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial, or other proceeding under the Code. The section empowers the court to call for any document or thing from any person, including a bank, for the purposes of any proceeding under the Code.
