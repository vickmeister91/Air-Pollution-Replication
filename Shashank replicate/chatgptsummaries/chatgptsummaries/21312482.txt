The court order in question pertains to a writ petition filed by a petitioner seeking the quashing of the acquisition proceedings of a piece of land owned by her. The petitioner has raised several contentions to substantiate her claim and has sought the quashing of the preliminary notification issued under section 28(1) of the KIAD Act. The court notes that objections have not been filed by the petitioner, and there has to be a decision under section 28(3) of the KIAD Act.

The court observes that the petitioner has not filed any objections to the acquisition proceedings, and therefore, it is premature to seek the quashing of the preliminary notification. The court permits the petitioner to file objections before the Special Land Acquisition Officer and dispose of the writ petition with the observation that it is premature.

The court's decision is based on the provisions of the KIAD Act, which governs the acquisition of land for industrial development purposes. Section 28(1) of the Act provides for the issuance of a preliminary notification by the government, indicating its intention to acquire a particular piece of land. Section 28(3) of the Act provides for the filing of objections by the affected parties within 30 days of the issuance of the preliminary notification.

The court notes that the petitioner has not filed any objections to the preliminary notification, and therefore, the acquisition proceedings have not progressed beyond the preliminary stage. The court observes that it is premature to seek the quashing of the preliminary notification without filing objections and allowing the Special Land Acquisition Officer to consider them.

The court's decision is significant as it emphasizes the importance of following due process in land acquisition proceedings. The court has directed the petitioner to file objections before the Special Land Acquisition Officer, who will consider them and make a decision under section 28(3) of the KIAD Act. This decision ensures that the petitioner's objections are heard and considered before any further action is taken in the acquisition proceedings.
