The High Court recently issued a court order in response to a petition filed by the owner of a vehicle that had been seized by the police for allegedly committing various offenses. The petitioner had filed an application under Section 457 of the Cr.P.C. for interim custody of the vehicle, which had been dismissed by the lower courts. However, the High Court allowed the petition and directed that the vehicle be released to its registered owner on certain conditions.

The court order specified that the petitioner must furnish a personal bond and an undertaking not to transfer or sell the vehicle without the permission of the trial court. Additionally, the petitioner was directed to produce the vehicle at his own expense as and when directed to do so. These conditions were put in place to ensure that the vehicle would not be used for any illegal activities and that it would be available for further investigation if necessary.

The court's decision to grant interim custody of the vehicle to the petitioner was based on several factors. Firstly, the court noted that the petitioner was the registered owner of the vehicle and had provided evidence to support his claim. Secondly, the court found that the police had not provided sufficient evidence to justify the continued seizure of the vehicle. Finally, the court considered the fact that the petitioner had not been charged with any offenses related to the vehicle and had no prior criminal record.
