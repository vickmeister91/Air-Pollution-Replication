The court recently issued an order in relation to two criminal petitions filed by five accused persons who were arrested for the illegal transportation of red sandalwood logs. The court observed that the accused were habitual offenders and had been involved in similar crimes of red sandalwood smuggling in the past. The court referred to the law laid down by the Supreme Court in TN Godavaraman Thirumulpad Vs. Union of India and Others, which held that red sanders wood is an endangered species and a national wealth, and those indulging in illegal trade are not entitled to bail.

The court noted that the accused had been arrested by the forest department officials while they were transporting red sandalwood logs in a vehicle. The accused had failed to produce any valid documents or permits for the transportation of the logs. The court also observed that the accused had been involved in similar crimes in the past and had been arrested on several occasions.

The court further noted that the accused had been granted bail in some of the previous cases, but they had continued to indulge in the illegal trade of red sandalwood. The court held that the accused were habitual offenders and were likely to continue with their illegal activities if released on bail.

The court also referred to the provisions of the Wildlife Protection Act, 1972, which provides for the protection of endangered species of flora and fauna. The court noted that red sanders wood is an endangered species and is protected under the Act. The court held that those indulging in the illegal trade of red sandalwood were not entitled to bail.

The court dismissed both the criminal petitions filed by the accused and ordered the closure of any pending miscellaneous petitions. The court held that the accused were not entitled to bail and should remain in custody until the trial is completed.
