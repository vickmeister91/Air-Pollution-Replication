The High Court of Madhya Pradesh recently granted bail to two individuals, Raju @ Rajesh and Imran, who were accused of violating the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The case was registered by Police Station-Forest Range Seoni, District Seoni, and the accused were charged under Sections 9, 39, (1) Aa, Da & 52 of the Act. The allegations against them were that they had wild boar meat in their cultivated field.

After hearing the arguments of both sides, the court found that the applicants deserved the benefit of bail. The court directed them to furnish a personal bond of Rs.30,000/- with one solvent surety each in the like amount to the satisfaction of the trial court.

The Wild Life Protection Act, 1972, is a comprehensive legislation that provides for the protection of wild animals and plants. The Act aims to regulate hunting, poaching, and trade in wildlife and their products. It also provides for the establishment of protected areas, such as national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, where wildlife can be conserved and protected.

The Act has been amended several times since its enactment, with the latest amendment being in 2006. The Act has been successful in protecting several endangered species, such as tigers, elephants, and rhinoceroses.
