The court order in question pertains to a criminal revision case filed by Sushil Agrawal against the State of Chhattisgarh. The revision was filed against the order passed by the 1st Additional Sessions Judge, Raipur in Sessions Trial No.341 of 2013. The applicant had requested for the call details of two mobile numbers to establish his innocence, which were submitted along with the charge-sheet but not supplied to him. The court directed the Trial Court to supply a copy of the call details to the applicant within three days. The revision was disposed of with this direction.

The case in question involves a criminal trial in which Sushil Agrawal was accused of committing a crime. During the course of the trial, Agrawal had requested for the call details of two mobile numbers to establish his innocence. These call details were submitted along with the charge-sheet but were not supplied to Agrawal. As a result, Agrawal filed a criminal revision case against the State of Chhattisgarh, seeking the call details.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, directed the Trial Court to supply a copy of the call details to Agrawal within three days. The court held that Agrawal had a right to access the call details as they were crucial to his defense. The court also noted that the call details were submitted along with the charge-sheet and therefore, should have been supplied to Agrawal.

The court's decision is significant as it upholds the principle of natural justice, which requires that a person accused of a crime be given a fair opportunity to defend himself. In this case, Agrawal had requested for the call details to establish his innocence, and the court has now directed that he be given access to these details. This decision is also important as it ensures that the prosecution does not withhold crucial evidence from the accused, which could result in an unfair trial.
