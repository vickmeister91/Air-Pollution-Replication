The court order pertains to a criminal complaint filed against Sanjay Baichen for offences punishable under Sections 420, 465, 467, 468 of IPC. The complaint alleges that Baichen, who was given the agency for selling Navbharat Newspaper, collected money from people for advertisements even after his agency was terminated and forged receipts in the name of the complainant. Baichen filed an application under Section 245 (2) of Cr.P.C. to challenge the order taking cognizance of the complaint, which was rejected by the court. Baichen also filed an application under Section 91 of Cr.P.C. for production of certain documents, which was also dismissed. The court held that the rejection of the application filed under Section 245 (2) of Cr.P.C. was justified and that the complaint cannot be dismissed merely because it is also of a civil nature. The court also listed principles to be considered for proper exercise of jurisdiction in quashing criminal proceedings.
