Smt Jahida Kallar, the petitioner, filed a criminal misc. petition under Section 482 Cr.P.C. against the order passed by the Additional Chief Metropolitan Magistrate No.6, Jodhpur Metropolitan in Criminal Case No.823/2016 (306/2010) dismissing the application under Section 91 read with Section 311 Cr.P.C. filed by the petitioner for summoning the ITR of the financial year 1999-2000 of respondent No.2/complainant.

The petitioner had filed an application under Section 91 read with Section 311 Cr.P.C. before the learned court below for summoning the ITR of the financial year 1999-2000 of respondent No.2/complainant. However, the learned court below dismissed the application, which led to the petitioner filing a criminal misc. petition under Section 482 Cr.P.C.

The court allowed the petition and ordered the learned court below to call for the document in concern and deal with the same strictly in accordance with law. This means that the ITR of the financial year 1999-2000 of respondent No.2/complainant will now be summoned and dealt with in accordance with the law.

This order is significant as it upholds the right of the petitioner to access relevant documents in a criminal case. It also ensures that the learned court below follows the law while dealing with such documents.

In conclusion, the court's decision to allow the petition and order the learned court below to call for the document in concern and deal with the same strictly in accordance with law is a positive step towards ensuring justice in the case.
