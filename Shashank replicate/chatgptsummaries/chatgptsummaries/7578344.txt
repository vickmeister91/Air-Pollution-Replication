In a recent court order, Ganesh and Rawta Ram were granted bail after being arrested for offences under Section 9/51 of the Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 in FIR No. 8649/2019 of Police Station Regional Forest Officer, Sirohi, District Sirohi. The bail was granted under Section 439 Cr.P.C. and each petitioner was required to execute a personal bond of Rs.50,000/- with two sureties of Rs.25,000/- each for their appearance before the trial court on every date of hearing and whenever called upon to do so till the completion of the trial.

The Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 is a comprehensive legislation that provides for the protection of wild animals and plants and for matters connected therewith or ancillary or incidental thereto. The Act aims to ensure the protection of wild animals and plants and their habitats, as well as to regulate hunting, poaching, and trade in wildlife and their products. Section 9/51 of the Act deals with the punishment for hunting or destroying any wild animal or bird, or attempting to do so, without a license or permit.

In this case, Ganesh and Rawta Ram were arrested for allegedly hunting or destroying a wild animal or bird without a license or permit. The court granted them bail under Section 439 Cr.P.C., which empowers the court to grant bail to a person who is in custody, pending trial or investigation, on such terms and conditions as it deems fit.
