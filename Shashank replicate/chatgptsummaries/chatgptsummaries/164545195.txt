The court order pertains to a petition filed against an order passed by the National Green Tribunal in O.A. No. 140/2013. The National Green Tribunal had directed the deposit of 50% of the cost in the estimated plan for restoration of the environment. The State Government argued that the petitioner had not come with clean hands and that the writ petition had been filed in 2017 against an order passed in 2016. The court referred to an identical case and the orders passed by the National Green Tribunal in that case. The court also noted the details of the restoration plan and the liability of the mining lease holders and stone crushers. The court imposed a fine on each mining lease holder.

The restoration plan for the affected area has already been prepared, and the court has directed that it be put into operation as soon as possible. The mining leaseholders are required to pay an amount of Rs. 1 lakh per hectare, and the stone crushers are required to deposit an amount of Rs. 1 lakh initially. The court has directed that the amount of Rs. 5 lakhs by each mining leaseholder be deposited with the DFO Dew as at the rate of Rs. 1 lakh per hectare.
