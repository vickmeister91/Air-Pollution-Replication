In a recent court order, Sri Rajegowda, who was accused of taking an illegal electric connection that led to an elephant's electrocution, sought anticipatory bail and surrendered to the court. The court considered the nature of the allegations and granted bail for a period of less than two days.

The case pertains to an incident where an elephant was electrocuted due to an illegal electric connection taken by the accused. The incident had caused outrage among animal rights activists and the public at large. The accused, Sri Rajegowda, had been on the run since the incident and had finally surrendered to the court seeking anticipatory bail.

The court, while considering the nature of the allegations, granted bail to the accused for a period of less than two days. The court order stated that the accused had surrendered to the court voluntarily and had cooperated with the investigation. The court also noted that the accused had no previous criminal record and was not likely to abscond or tamper with evidence.

The court order also stated that the accused had to furnish a bond of Rs. 50,000 and two sureties of the same amount. The accused was also directed to cooperate with the investigation and not to leave the jurisdiction of the court without prior permission.
