The High Court of Gujarat at Ahmedabad recently issued a court order pertaining to a writ petition filed regarding illegal mining activities in the Balaram Ambaji Sanctuary. The petitioner had requested the court to issue a writ of mandamus or any other appropriate writ, order or direction, directing the respondents to take action against the person concerned for the activities carried out in the area of Sanctuary. The respondent had denied the allegations and stated that no mining activities were permitted or carried out within the sanctuary. After hearing both sides, the court found no merit in the writ petition and dismissed it.

The Balaram Ambaji Sanctuary is a protected area located in the Aravalli Range of Gujarat, India. It is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including leopards, hyenas, and sloth bears. The sanctuary is also an important source of water for the surrounding communities.

The writ petition filed in the High Court of Gujarat at Ahmedabad alleged that illegal mining activities were taking place within the sanctuary, which was causing damage to the environment and endangering the wildlife. The petitioner requested the court to issue a writ of mandamus or any other appropriate writ, order or direction, directing the respondents to take action against the person concerned for the activities carried out in the area of Sanctuary.

The respondent, on the other hand, denied the allegations and stated that no mining activities were permitted or carried out within the sanctuary. The respondent argued that the allegations were baseless and without any merit.

After hearing both sides, the court found no merit in the writ petition and dismissed it. The court observed that the petitioner had failed to provide any evidence to support the allegations of illegal mining activities within the sanctuary. The court also noted that the respondent had denied the allegations and had provided evidence to support their claim that no mining activities were taking place within the sanctuary.

The court order is significant as it highlights the importance of protecting the environment and wildlife in protected areas such as the Balaram Ambaji Sanctuary.
