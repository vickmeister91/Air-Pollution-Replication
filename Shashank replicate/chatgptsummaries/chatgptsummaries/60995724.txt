The court recently dismissed an appeal filed by Bhim Sain against an order passed by the Sale Officer/Assistant Registrar, Cooperative Societies, Samana, auction notice dated 05.11.2012 and order dated 29.07.2013 by which the revision petition filed by the appellant was dismissed by the Joint Registrar, Patiala. The court found that the recovery proceedings initiated by the respondents were confined and related only to Rs.32,00,325.45 paise, and therefore, the recovery for a sum of Rs. 1,87,76,984/- from the appellant by retaining the entire sale proceeds from the sale of the appellant's land admeasuring 47 Kanals 7 Marlas in village Biyrak, Tehsil Samana, District Patiala, is contrary to law.

The court also found that the appellant was aware of the fact that the respondents' authorities intended to recover the entire amount of Rs. 1,87,76,984/- and had contested this issue before both the authorities, namely, the Assistant Registrar as well as the Joint Registrar, and therefore, had not been prejudiced in any manner in taking up this issue or challenging the auction proceedings on this ground.

The case revolves around the recovery proceedings initiated by the respondents against Bhim Sain. The respondents had initiated recovery proceedings against Bhim Sain for a sum of Rs. 32,00,325.45 paise. However, they retained the entire sale proceeds from the sale of Bhim Sain's land admeasuring 47 Kanals 7 Marlas in village Biyrak, Tehsil Samana, District Patiala, which amounted to Rs. 1,87,76,984/-. The court found that this action was contrary to law as the recovery proceedings were only related to the sum of Rs. 32,00,325.45 paise.

The court also noted that Bhim Sain was aware of the fact that the respondents intended to recover the entire amount of Rs. 1,87,76,984/- and had contested this issue before both the Assistant Registrar and the Joint Registrar. Therefore, the court found that Bhim Sain had not been prejudiced in any manner in taking up this issue or challenging the auction proceedings on this ground.

The court's decision is significant as it upholds the principle of law that recovery proceedings should be confined to the amount owed by the debtor. The court's decision also highlights the importance of due process and the right of the debtor to challenge recovery proceedings if they are not in accordance with the law.

In conclusion, the court's decision in this case is a reminder that recovery proceedings must be in accordance with the law and should not exceed the amount owed by the debtor. The decision also emphasizes the importance of due process and the right of the debtor to challenge recovery proceedings if they are not in accordance with the law.
