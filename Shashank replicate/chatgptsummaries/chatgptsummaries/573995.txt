The court order in question pertains to a bail application filed by Bhavabhai Lakhmanbhai Rabari under Section 439 of the Code of Criminal Procedure. The applicant had been charged with offenses punishable under Sections 66(B), 65(A) (E), 98 and 116 (2) of the Bombay Prohibition Act. After considering the facts of the case, nature of allegations, role attributed to the accused, and punishment prescribed for the alleged offenses, the court allowed the application and ordered the applicant to be released on bail on certain conditions.

The court's decision to grant bail was based on a number of factors. Firstly, the court considered the nature of the allegations against the accused. Secondly, the court considered the role attributed to the accused in the alleged offenses. The court found that the applicant's role was not central to the offenses and that he was not a flight risk. Finally, the court considered the punishment prescribed for the alleged offenses. The court found that the punishment was not so severe as to warrant the denial of bail.

The court's decision to grant bail was subject to certain conditions. The applicant was required to execute a bail bond before the lower court having jurisdiction to try the case. The bond was to be executed in the amount specified by the court and was to be accompanied by one or more sureties. The sureties were required to be residents of the local area and to have sufficient means to ensure that the applicant would appear before the court as and when required.

In addition to the bail bond, the court imposed certain other conditions on the applicant. The applicant was required to surrender his passport, if any, to the lower court.
