The case in question involves a petitioner who is facing trial for an offence punishable under Section 498-A read with Section 34 of the IPC. The petitioner has challenged an order passed by the JMFC, Sagar in Criminal Case No.1991/2015. The petitioner claims that the respondent No.2 has lodged the report under Section 498-A of the IPC against the petitioner with ulterior motive and to wreak vengeance against the petitioner. 

The petitioner filed an application under Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. to obtain certain documents, but the application was dismissed. The petitioner preferred revision before learned 5th ASJ, Sagar, which was also dismissed. The petitioner has assailed the order by filing this petition before this Court stating that the purpose of Section 91 of the Cr.P.C. is defeated if the documents are not called. 

The case is at the stage of recording of prosecution evidence before learned trial Court. After due investigation, charge-sheet has been filed. This report, if called, will not be helpful to the petitioner. With this note that after the prosecution evidence, the petitioner may produce the documents as may be deemed fit in his defence, this petition is dismissed.
