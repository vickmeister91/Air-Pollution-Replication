The court order pertains to a petition filed by Hotel Pride Resort, seeking permission to operate their hotel premises in the Gir area. The petitioner had filed an undertaking before the court, stating that they would take necessary measures to protect wildlife in the area and not breach any provisions of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The court has now stated that the petitioner has abided by the undertaking and the grievances made in the petition have been addressed.

The court has granted permission to the petitioner to run/operate the hotel premises from October 16, 2019, subject to the condition that they file the undertaking before the Chief Conservator of Forest, Junagadh, within a week. The undertaking must state that the petitioner will take necessary care to protect wildlife in the Gir area and not breach any provisions of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972.

The court has disposed of the petition and has not ordered any costs. This means that the petitioner can now operate their hotel premises in the Gir area, provided they comply with the conditions set by the court.
