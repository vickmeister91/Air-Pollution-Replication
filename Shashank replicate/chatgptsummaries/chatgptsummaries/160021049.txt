The case in question involves a Criminal Original Petition filed by the petitioners under Section 482 of the Criminal Procedure Code. The petitioners sought to set aside an order passed by the learned IX Additional Special Judge for CBI Cases in Chennai in Crl.M.P.No.3552 of 2015. The order in question dismissed the petitioners' send for petition filed under Section 91 Cr.P.C in Crl.M.P.No.3552/2015 in C.C.No.30/2011 on the file of the IX Additional Special Judge for CBI Cases, Chennai.

The petitioners had requested the tender of pardon proceedings under Section 306 Cr.P.C issued by the learned XI Metropolitan Magistrate in Crl.M.P. No.2537 of 2011 dated 02.05.2011 in C.C.No.1/2013 from the file of the XIII Additional Special Judge for CBI Cases, Chennai for effective cross-examination of PW29 Mr. Hanifa. However, the trial court had dismissed their request.

The petitioners argued that the trial court's order was erroneous and that they were entitled to the documents sought for in their petition filed under Section 91 Cr.P.C in Crl.M.P.No.3552 of 2015. They contended that the documents were necessary for the effective cross-examination of PW29 Mr. Hanifa.

The court considered the petitioners' arguments and ultimately set aside the impugned order. The court directed the trial court to send for the entire tender of pardon proceedings in Crl.M.P.No.2537/2011 from the XIII Additional Special Judge, CBI cases, Chennai. This would allow the petitioners to access the documents they had requested and would enable them to effectively cross-examine PW29 Mr. Hanifa.

Overall, the court's decision was based on the principle of fairness and the importance of ensuring that all parties have access to the necessary documents and information in order to present their case effectively. The court recognized the petitioners' right to a fair trial and took steps to ensure that this right was protected.
