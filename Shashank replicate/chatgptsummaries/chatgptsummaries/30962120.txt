The case in question involves a petitioner who filed a petition challenging the order passed by the Judicial Magistrate No.II, Trichirappalli, dismissing the petition filed by the petitioner to produce the income tax particulars including PAN number of the complainant/respondent. The respondent had filed a private complaint for the offence under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instrument Act, alleging that the petitioner borrowed a sum of Rs.50,00,000/- from the respondent by way of cash. The petitioner had filed a petition under Section 91 of Cr.P.C., to summon the documents relating to income tax bills, returns and PAN card details of the respondent. The learned Judicial Magistrate dismissed the said petition recording the reasons. The petitioner sought to set aside the order passed by the learned Judicial Magistrate.

The case was heard by the court, which dismissed the criminal original petition and directed the learned Judicial Magistrate to complete the trial and dispose of the case within a period of six months from the date of receipt of a copy of this order. The court's decision was based on the fact that the petitioner's request for the respondent's income tax particulars was not relevant to the case at hand. The court held that the petitioner's request was an attempt to delay the proceedings and that the documents requested were not necessary for the trial.

The court's decision was based on the provisions of Section 91 of the Cr.P.C., which allows a court to issue a summons to produce a document or other thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. The court held that the documents requested by the petitioner were not necessary for the trial and that the petitioner's request was an attempt to delay the proceedings.

The court's decision was also based on the fact that the respondent had already produced the necessary documents related to the case. The court held that the respondent had produced the necessary documents and that the petitioner's request for additional documents was not necessary for the trial.
