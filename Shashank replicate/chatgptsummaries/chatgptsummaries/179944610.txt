A recent court order in Karnataka pertains to a criminal petition filed by J Maheshgoud against the State of Karnataka. The petition seeks to set aside an order allowing the prosecution's applications under Sections 91 and 311 of the CR.P.C. The VI Additional District and Special Judge at Mysuru had passed an order under these sections, seeking the summoning of certain documents from witnesses.

Section 91 of the CR.P.C. empowers a court to issue a summons to any person to produce any document or thing that is necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under the Code. Section 311 of the CR.P.C. empowers a court to summon any person as a witness or examine any person in attendance, though not summoned as a witness, or recall and re-examine any person already examined, if it appears to the court that such person is able to throw light upon the matter in issue.

The court partially allowed the petition, setting aside the direction to produce birth and school documents from one witness but confirming the direction to produce an application and transfer certificate from another witness. The court's decision was based on the facts and circumstances of the case and the relevance of the documents sought to be produced.

The petitioner had challenged the order of the VI Additional District and Special Judge at Mysuru on the grounds that the order was passed without considering the relevance and necessity of the documents sought to be produced. The petitioner had also argued that the order was passed without giving the petitioner an opportunity to be heard.

The court, after considering the submissions made by the petitioner and the prosecution, partially allowed the petition. The court held that the direction to produce birth and school documents from one witness was not relevant to the case and therefore set aside the direction. However, the court confirmed the direction to produce an application and transfer certificate from another witness as it was relevant to the case.

The court's decision is significant as it highlights the importance of relevance and necessity in the production of documents under Sections 91 and 311 of the CR.P.C. The court has emphasized that the power to summon documents or witnesses should be exercised judiciously and only when it is necessary for the fair and just disposal of the case.

The court's decision also underscores the importance of giving an opportunity to be heard to the parties concerned before passing any order under Sections 91 and 311 of the CR.P.C.
