The National Insurance Company filed an appeal against the judgment and award passed by the Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal in a motor accident claim petition. The claimant had sustained injuries in an accident caused by the driver of a jeep insured by the National Insurance Company. The Tribunal held that the accident occurred due to the driver's rash and negligent driving and awarded compensation to the claimant. The National Insurance Company denied the occurrence of the accident and filed a written statement. The Tribunal rejected the company's application under Section 170 of the Motor Vehicles Act, which allows the insurer to contest the claim on all or any of the grounds available to the person against whom the claim has been made. The company filed an appeal challenging the rejection of its application.

The court order discusses the rejection of an application under Section 170 of the Act filed by an insurance company. The section allows insurers to contest claims on certain grounds, but in this case, neither of those grounds applied. The court found that the insurer could only challenge the award on the grounds mentioned in sub-section (2) of Section 149 of the Act, which it failed to do. The court also discussed the requirements of policies and limits of liability under Sections 146 and 147 of the Act, and the insurer's duty to satisfy judgments or awards given in respect of liability covered by the policy.

The court order discusses the duty of an insurer to satisfy a judgment or award in case of liability incurred by the insured person. The insurer can contest the claim on certain grounds and may participate in the determination of liability. However, if permission is denied, the insurer cannot contest the fact of occurrence of the accident but can contest the findings on other grounds. The court dismissed the appeal filed by the insurer and ordered the deposited amount to be adjusted towards the amount payable under the award.
