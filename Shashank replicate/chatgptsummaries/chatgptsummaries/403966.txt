The Jharkhand High Court recently allowed a writ petition filed by Suresh Prasad Jaiswal against the State of Jharkhand and others. Jaiswal had applied for a No Objection Certificate (NOC) to set up an industry, but the Pollution Control Board rejected his application without specifying the shortcomings in his documents. The court quashed the rejection order and directed the Board to inform Jaiswal of the exact shortcomings in writing and give him a reasonable time to rectify them.

The court's decision is significant as it emphasizes the need for government officers to help the industrial climate in the country and not impede it by rejecting applications on undisclosed grounds. The court's ruling is a reminder to government officials that they must act in a transparent and accountable manner when dealing with applications for NOCs and other permits.
