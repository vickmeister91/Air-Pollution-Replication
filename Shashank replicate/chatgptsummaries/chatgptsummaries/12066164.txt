The Madras High Court recently dismissed a writ petition filed by S. Vijayalakshmi against the Tamil Nadu Generation and Distribution Corporation Limited (TANGEDCO) and the Tamil Nadu Pollution Control Board. The petitioner had sought a writ of mandamus to direct TANGEDCO to reconnect electricity to a specific connection and to prevent disconnection in the future. However, the court dismissed the petition as the disconnection had been made pursuant to a direction from the Pollution Control Board.

The petitioner, S. Vijayalakshmi, had approached the court seeking a writ of mandamus to direct TANGEDCO to reconnect electricity to a specific connection and to prevent disconnection in the future. The petitioner claimed that the disconnection was illegal and arbitrary, and that it had caused her great hardship and inconvenience. She argued that TANGEDCO had disconnected the electricity supply without any prior notice or intimation, and that this had caused her great financial loss and mental agony.

However, the court dismissed the petition, stating that the disconnection had been made pursuant to a direction from the Pollution Control Board. The court noted that the Pollution Control Board had issued a notice to the petitioner, directing her to disconnect the electricity supply to her premises, as she had failed to comply with certain environmental norms and regulations. The court observed that the Pollution Control Board had the power to issue such notices, and that TANGEDCO was duty-bound to comply with them.

The court further noted that the petitioner had the option to challenge the notice issued by the Pollution Control Board in accordance with the law. The court stated that the petitioner could approach the appropriate forum and seek appropriate relief, if she felt that the notice was illegal or arbitrary. The court also observed that TANGEDCO had acted in accordance with the law, and that it had no discretion in the matter.
