The court has granted anticipatory bail to Vijay Kumar Jain in connection with a police case against him for allegedly running a crusher illegally. The case against him was filed under various sections of the Indian Penal Code, Jharkhand Mineral Dealer Rules, Air Pollution Act, and M.M.R.D.A. Act. Jain was apprehending his arrest for these alleged offenses.

The court directed that Jain shall be released on bail on furnishing a bail bond of Rs. 10,000/- with two sureties of the like amount each, subject to conditions as laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure. This section lays down the conditions for granting anticipatory bail.

Anticipatory bail is a provision in the Indian legal system that allows a person to seek bail in anticipation of an arrest. This provision is available to a person who apprehends arrest for a non-bailable offense. The purpose of this provision is to protect the person from unnecessary harassment and detention.

In this case, Jain was apprehending his arrest for allegedly running a crusher illegally. The court has granted him anticipatory bail, which means that he can avoid arrest if he complies with the conditions laid down by the court.

The conditions for granting anticipatory bail are laid down under Section 438(2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure. These conditions include that the person shall make himself available for interrogation by the police as and when required, shall not tamper with the evidence or influence the witnesses, and shall not commit any offense while on bail.
