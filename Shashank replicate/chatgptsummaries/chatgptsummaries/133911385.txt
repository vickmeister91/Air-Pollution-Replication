The court has granted regular bail to Maheshbhai Gebabhai Nana Garjiya and five others in connection with a case registered under the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. The charge sheet has been filed and the offences are triable by a Magisterial Court. The court has ordered the applicants to execute a bond of Rs.10,000/- each with one surety of the like amount and follow certain conditions, including not leaving the territory of India without prior permission of the Sessions Judge concerned and appearing before the Investigation Officer concerned as and when required. The trial court shall not be influenced by the observations made by the High Court while granting bail.

The case in question pertains to a violation of the Wild Life Protection Act, 1972. Maheshbhai Gebabhai Nana Garjiya and five others were arrested and charged with offences under the Act. The charge sheet has been filed and the case is now triable by a Magisterial Court. The accused had applied for bail, which has now been granted by the court.

The court has ordered the applicants to execute a bond of Rs.10,000/- each with one surety of the like amount. The applicants have also been directed to follow certain conditions, including not leaving the territory of India without prior permission of the Sessions Judge concerned and appearing before the Investigation Officer concerned as and when required. These conditions are standard for bail applications and are meant to ensure that the accused do not abscond or tamper with evidence.

The court has also made it clear that the trial court shall not be influenced by the observations made by the High Court while granting bail. This is an important point as it ensures that the trial court will make its decision based on the evidence presented before it and not be swayed by any observations made by the High Court.
