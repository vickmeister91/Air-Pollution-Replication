The court order in question pertains to a case between S.R. Janartanan and F. Sebastian. The petitioner, S.R. Janartanan, had filed a petition challenging the order of the court below passed under Sections 91 and 311 of CrPC to recall PW.1. The respondent, F. Sebastian, wanted to establish his defense by summoning certain documents from Thiru Kumara finance in which the petitioner was a partner from 2009 onwards. 

The court allowed the recall petition filed under Section 311 of CrPC. The court found no illegality or infirmity in the orders passed by the court below and dismissed the criminal original petition. The Judicial Magistrate-I, Triurchirappalli was directed to dispose of the CC No.201 of 2014 within a period of three months from the date of receipt of a copy of this order and report compliance to the Registry, immediately after completion of the proceedings.

To understand the court order in detail, it is important to understand the sections of CrPC that were invoked in this case. Section 91 of CrPC deals with the power of the court to issue summons or order for the production of any document or thing necessary or desirable for the purposes of any investigation, inquiry, trial or other proceeding under this Code. Section 311 of CrPC deals with the power of the court to summon any person as a witness or examine any person in attendance, though not summoned as a witness, or recall and re-examine any person already examined, if it appears to the court that such person is able to throw light upon the matter in dispute.

Janartanan, was a partner from 2009 onwards.
