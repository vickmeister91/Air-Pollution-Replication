Namendra Singh Chauhan, a government servant, filed an application under Section 438 Cr.P.C. seeking bail in the event of his arrest in a wildlife protection case. Chauhan claimed that his jeep was stolen while he was away from his headquarters and that he had no involvement in the case. The court found his defense to be prima facie convincing and granted him bail on the condition of furnishing a personal bond of Rs. 30,000 and one surety. The order is valid for 60 days, within which Chauhan must seek regular bail.
