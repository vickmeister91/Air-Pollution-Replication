In a recent court order, the Rajasthan High Court declared an amendment to the Rajasthan Minor Mineral Concession Rules as illegal and set it aside. The court order pertains to a case where a Mining Engineer had cancelled the petitioner's application for a mining lease. The petitioner had appealed the decision, but the appeal was rejected due to the amendment in the rules.

However, the court found the amendment to be illegal and allowed the appellate authority to examine the merits of the appeal. The order cancelling the petitioner's application was declared illegal and quashed, and the appellate authority was required to adjudicate the appeal on its own merits.

The case highlights the importance of following due process and adhering to the law. The court's decision to declare the amendment as illegal and set it aside shows that even if a rule or regulation is amended, it must be done in accordance with the law and cannot be used to deny someone their legal rights.

The court's decision also underscores the need for transparency and fairness in the mining industry.
