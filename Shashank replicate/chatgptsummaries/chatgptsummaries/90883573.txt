The court order pertains to a criminal revision petition filed by East West Linkers and Smt. Preet Singh against Kuldeep Singh Tomar and the Delhi Pollution Control Committee. The complaint alleged that the accused were carrying out illegal industrial activity in a residential area without obtaining consent from the DPCC, causing air pollution and violating the Environment Protection Act. The Court of ld. M.M took cognizance of the offence and decided to summon the accused persons to face trial.

However, the petitioners challenged the jurisdiction of the Court of ld. M.M, arguing that only the special designated Court of Addl. Chief Metropolitan Magistrate (Special Act), Tis Hazari Courts, Delhi had exclusive jurisdiction to deal with cases pertaining to environmental pollution and ecological destruction. The court order discusses the dispute over jurisdiction in the case, with the complainant arguing that the court has jurisdiction and the trial court accepting this argument. 

The court order states that a Special Court was created to deal with all cases related to environmental issues, and only the Court of ld. ACMM (Special Act), Tis Hazari Courts, Delhi had jurisdiction to try cases pertaining to environmental pollution and ecological destruction. The impugned order passed by ld. M.M is without any jurisdiction and is liable to be set aside. The revision petition is allowed, and the Trial Court record is to be sent to ld. M.M with directions to transfer the case to the specially designated Court of ld. ACMM (Special Act), Central District, Tis Hazari Courts, Delhi, for proceedings in accordance with the law.
