The case of K. Murugada involves a writ petition filed under Article 226 of the Constitution of India, seeking a Writ of Certiorari to call for the records pertaining to the impugned order in Ref.No.PV2/12162/2016 dated 29.08.2016 on the file of the first respondent and quash the same. The petitioner had purchased land to an extent of nine acres in S.No.785/1C situated at Azhagiyapandipuram Village, Thovalai Taluk, Kanyakumari District. The said land has been classified as Private Forest under the Tamil Nadu Preservation of Private Forests Act, 1949 and the Tahsildar, Thovalai has issued patta to the afore-said land in his favour.

The petitioner had applied before the second respondent Committee on 21.11.2015 in accordance with the Tamil Nadu Preservation of Private Forests Act, 1949 for carrying out maintenance work in the road situated in S.No.785/1C1C4, Azhagiyapandipuram Village, Thovalai Taluk, Kanyakumari District with certain conditions. The second respondent after careful consideration of the application and based on the reports of the respondents 3 and 4 had passed an order in Mu.Mu.M1/45049/2014 dated 29.02.2016 granting permission for carrying out maintenance work in the road situated in S.No.785/1C1C4, Azhagiyapandipuram Village, Thovalai Taluk, Kanyakumari District with certain conditions.

However, to the shock and surprise of the petitioner, the first respondent has issued the impugned order dated 29.08.2016 directing the second respondent to withdraw the permission order and has directed the petitioner to seek permission under the Forest Conservation Act,1980. The petitioner contends that the impugned order is illegal, arbitrary, and without jurisdiction. The petitioner further contends that the impugned order is in violation of the principles of natural justice and the Tamil Nadu Preservation of Private Forests Act, 1949.

The petitioner argues that the impugned order is illegal as it has been passed without jurisdiction. The impugned order has been passed by the first respondent, who is not authorized to pass such an order. The first respondent is only authorized to issue directions to the second respondent, who is the competent authority to pass orders under the Tamil Nadu Preservation of Private Forests Act, 1949. The petitioner further argues that the impugned order is arbitrary as it has been passed without any basis or reason.

The petitioner also contends that the impugned order is in violation of the principles of natural justice. The petitioner was not given an opportunity to be heard before the impugned order was passed. The petitioner was not given any notice or intimation before the impugned order was passed. The petitioner argues that the impugned order is in violation of the Tamil Nadu Preservation of Private Forests Act, 1949. The Act provides for the preservation of private forests and the maintenance of ecological balance. The impugned order is in violation of the objectives of the Act.

The petitioner seeks a Writ of Certiorari to quash the impugned order and to direct the first respondent to withdraw the same. The petitioner also seeks a direction to the second respondent to consider the petitioner's application for maintenance work in the road situated in S.No.785/1C1C4, Azhagiyapandipuram Village, Thovalai Taluk, Kanyakumari District in accordance with the Tamil Nadu Preservation of Private Forests Act, 1949.

In conclusion, the case of K. Murugada involves a writ petition filed under Article 226 of the Constitution of India, seeking a Writ of Certiorari to call for the records pertaining to the impugned order in Ref.No.PV2/12162/2016 dated 29.08.2016 on the file of the first respondent and quash the same. The petitioner contends that the impugned order is illegal, arbitrary, and without jurisdiction. The petitioner further contends that the impugned order is in violation of the principles of natural justice and the Tamil Nadu Preservation of Private Forests Act, 1949. The petitioner seeks a Writ of Certiorari to quash the impugned order and to direct the first respondent to withdraw the same.
