The value of the cheques involved in the case is Rs. 27 lakhs.

The court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, allowed the revision and set aside the order of the Metropolitan Magistrate. The case involves a private complaint filed by IIT Capital Services Limited under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act. The complaint alleges that the accused had issued cheques to the complainant, which were dishonored due to insufficient funds. The complainant had sent a legal notice to the accused, but the accused failed to make the payment. Hence, the complainant filed a private complaint under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act.

During the course of the trial, the complainant had filed an application to recall and examine a witness and mark certain documents. However, the Metropolitan Magistrate had dismissed the application on the ground that the complainant had not made out a case for recalling the witness and marking the documents.

Aggrieved by the order of the Metropolitan Magistrate, the complainant filed a revision before the Sessions Court. The Sessions Court, after hearing the arguments of both parties, allowed the revision and set aside the order of the Metropolitan Magistrate.

The Sessions Court held that the documents sought to be marked were relevant to the case and could not be ignored. The court also held that the witness sought to be recalled was a material witness and his testimony was necessary for the just decision of the case.

The court cited previous judgments to support its decision. The court relied on the judgment of the Supreme Court in the case of State of Rajasthan v. Shambhu Kewat, where it was held that the court has the power to recall a witness if it is necessary for the just decision of the case. The court also relied on the judgment of the Delhi High Court in the case of Rakesh Kumar v. State, where it was held that the court has the power to mark documents if they are relevant to the case.
