The Kerala High Court recently heard two writ petitions jointly and disposed of them by a common judgment. The petitions were related to the implementation of waste management projects in Thrissur, Kerala. The court had previously directed the authorities to ensure the implementation of three waste management projects, including the Laloor Model Project for Solid Waste Management (LAMPS). The petitioners in these writs challenged the setting up of waste treatment plants in certain areas, including Sakthan Thampuran Nagar. The court held that the petitioners cannot oppose the implementation of the projects as directed by the previous judgment, but the question to be decided is whether the plants can be set up in the identified areas.

The court order discusses the implementation of three waste management projects in Thrissur, Kerala. The petitioner is not against the projects but objects to the choice of location for the plants, particularly Sakthan Thampuran Nagar, without following proper procedures. The court notes that authorization from the Pollution Control Board and notification of the location are required for setting up the plants. The respondents have stated that they will obtain all necessary permits and licenses before establishing the plants. The court orders the respondents to implement the projects in accordance with the relevant laws and procedures.

The court's decision is significant as it highlights the importance of following proper procedures while implementing waste management projects. The court has directed the authorities to obtain all necessary permits and licenses before establishing the plants. This is crucial as it ensures that the plants are set up in a manner that is environmentally sustainable and does not harm the health of the local population.
